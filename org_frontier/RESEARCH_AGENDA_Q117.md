# Agenda: the structural law — characterizing triadicity without Φ (Q117–Q120)

A new angle. The program has computed the verdict with exact IIT-4.0 Φ throughout, treating the oracle as
the ground truth and reading off its consequences. It has never asked whether the oracle is necessary: is
there a property of the coordination, computable without Φ, that decides the verdict exactly? If so, the
classifier could be cheap and exact at once, and the structural law would have a closed form rather than a
black-box.

This wave looks for a Φ-free characterization. The probe loop supplied the ingredients: triadic forms carry a
feedback cycle (probes 25, 39), have a 4-edge minimum at n=3 (probe 35), and resist every cheap dependence
proxy (probes 45-47). The question those leave open is whether a structural predicate, read off the truth
tables and the connectivity, reproduces the exact verdict with no error. The strict-mediation family of 256
forms (Q93) is the test bed: small enough to enumerate exhaustively, rich enough to hold both dyadic and
triadic forms.

The wave asks whether topology alone decides the verdict, what logical condition closes the gap when it does
not, whether the verdict is an exact automorphism under role-symmetry, and whether a form can be triadic with
no single party pivotal. The questions use the existing oracle as ground truth and search for the Φ-free law
that matches it.

The payoff is a test that does not call Φ: a structural law with a closed form, validated against the exact
oracle on the complete family.

## Q117 — A Φ-free necessary-and-sufficient test

**Question.** Is there a predicate, computed without Φ from the form's truth tables, that reproduces the exact
verdict on the whole strict-mediation family? Does the feedback cycle suffice, or is more needed?

**What it reads.** The exact verdict against a Φ-free predicate over all 256 forms. *Discriminator:* whether
topology alone decides the verdict, and whether a logical composition condition closes any gap to zero error.

## Q118 — The law beyond strict mediation

**Question.** Does the Φ-free test generalize past the strict-mediation family, to forms where the mediator
does not read both outer parties, or to a broader n=3 sample?

**What it reads.** The predicate's error rate on a wider family. *Discriminator:* whether the law is a feature
of strict mediation or of triadicity as such.

## Q119 — Role-symmetry as an exact automorphism

**Question.** Is the verdict exactly invariant under relabeling the two outer parties (worker ↔ counterpart)?
Probe 22 showed it behaviorally; this asks for an exact automorphism over the family.

**What it reads.** The verdict of each form against its worker-counterpart relabeling, over all 256.
*Discriminator:* whether any form flips under the swap, or the symmetry is exact.

## Q120 — Pure higher-order binding

**Question.** Is there a triadic form in which no single party is pivotal, the integration carried only by the
joint determination? Probe 13's holistic residual hints it may exist.

**What it reads.** The verdict against each party's pivotality (does its defection collapse the form) across
the triadic forms. *Discriminator:* a triadic form whose every single-party knockout leaves it triadic, the
binding purely higher-order.
