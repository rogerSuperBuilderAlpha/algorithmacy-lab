# Q52 — phi-ladder mechanism · Stage 1 review

**Question.** What structural feature of the implication commit indices {2, 4, 11, 13} determines an n=3
form's rung on the one-sided back-channel Φ ladder (0.830075 vs 0.415037 vs dyadic), and why does
symmetric two-sided coupling collapse the ladder to a single value?

**Agenda id.** Grows from Q51 / probes #156 and #159. No dedicated agenda slot; the open thread is the
mechanism behind the commit-index ladder that one-sided worker back-channels leave and symmetric
two-sided back-channels erase.

## The gap

Q51 (#156, #159) documented the ladder but did not name the structural predictor. The eight matched-read
implication forms split into three Φ bands under one-sided worker back-channel wiring, while symmetric
two-sided coupling unifies all eight at 0.830075. No probe has tested whether the rung tracks commit
truth-table features (W-centric vs C-centric violation states, party-read polarity alignment), whether
mid-tier commits {4,11} form an invariant plateau, or whether symmetric coupling lifts the dyadic endpoint
forms. The party-read rule from Q50 is settled; the commit-index ladder mechanism is not.
