## The gap

Q55 (#178) reported that every symmetric bijective ceiling pair prints MIP `3 parts: {W,S,C}` while every
aligned one-sided ceiling pair prints an outer-party singleton seam (`{W,SC}` or `{WS,C}`). The split is
documented but unexplained. No probe has read the official MIP tie set (`sia.ties`) on the thirty-two
ceiling pairs, compared partition-Phi landscapes across symmetric and one-sided topologies, tested whether
directional coupling symmetry forces tied outer-party cuts that IIT-4.0 resolves to the complete partition,
or verified the normalized-phi tie-break rule that selects among at-system-Phi partitions. The bijective
parity enumeration, topology constructors, and seam helpers from Q49/Q54/Q55 are available; the structural
explanation is not.
