# Q73 — Stage 3 hypotheses (the law's pillars, fixed before re-derivation)

Five pillars, each a keystone result re-derived from a representative form. Written and committed before
the re-derivation runs.

## P1 — Joint determination
- **Claim:** read_recipient (agent reads sender and recipient) is triadic; broadcast (agent ignores
  recipient) is dyadic.

## P2 — Liveness decides
- **Claim:** read_recipient (recipient live) is triadic and one_shot (same wiring, recipient frozen) is
  dyadic; liveness, not wiring, decides.

## P3 — Substitutability collapses
- **Claim:** all_required (recipients bound jointly) is triadic; substitutable (either recipient suffices)
  is dyadic.

## P4 — Closure binds the whole
- **Claim:** a closed ring's major complex is the full element set at Φ > 2.0.

## P5 — Structural, not cheap
- **Claim:** the verdict is robust to noise (Q71) and not recoverable by any cheap proxy (Q72); the exact
  computation is required. Established in those studies; carried here as a pillar.

H0 for each: the stated relation fails. Predicted: all five hold, and the law stands.
