# The outreach-coordination law: when agent-mediated outreach demands algorithmacy

## Abstract

Ten studies (Q63-Q72) read agent-mediated outreach with exact IIT-4.0 Φ. This paper draws them into one
stated law and re-derives its pillars in a single reproducible probe. The law: agent-mediated outreach
demands algorithmacy if and only if every party in the exchange is bound into a single irreducible joint
determination that stays live end to end. Five conditions govern the verdict — joint determination,
liveness and reciprocity, non-substitutability, the core's localization under depth and delegation, and
the verdict's structural (not cheap) character. The keystone re-derivation confirms all five; the synthesis
stands on results each pre-registered and CI-backed in its own study.

## Introduction

The line began from a question about agent-mediated outreach: when both a sender and a recipient delegate
to agents, is the coordination still the people's, or the machines'? Q63 established the single-mediator
verdict and Q64-Q72 extended it across breadth, depth, reciprocity, triage, two-sided delegation, agent
substitutability, noise, and the proxy frontier. The pieces cohere into one law, stated and re-derived
here.

## The law

**Agent-mediated outreach demands algorithmacy iff every party in the exchange is bound into a single
irreducible joint determination that stays live end to end.** Five conditions govern it.

1. *Joint determination.* The agent must read all parties; an agent that ignores the recipient broadcasts
   and the form factors (Q63, Q64).
2. *Liveness and reciprocity.* Each party must stay live to the commit; a frozen recipient leaves the form
   dyadic, and reciprocity binds at a threshold, not by degrees (Q63, Q67).
3. *Non-substitutability.* Interchangeability of any party — counterpart, recipient, or agent — collapses
   the triad; only an all-required determination is irreducible (Q64, Q70).
4. *Core and delegation.* Depth preserves the triad but localizes the core to a symmetric end pair; a
   closed loop binds the whole; a bidirectional triage agent displaces the sender; full delegation moves
   the core to the two agents, while they are specific and required (Q65, Q66, Q68, Q69, Q70).
5. *Structural, not cheap.* The verdict is robust to noise and unrecoverable by any cheap proxy; the exact
   computation is required (Q71, Q72).

## Methods

One probe (`probe_law_pillars.py`) re-derives pillars P1-P4 from keystone forms with exact IIT-4.0 Φ;
pillar P5 is carried from the registered Q71 and Q72 checks. Keystone forms: read_recipient, broadcast,
one_shot, all_required, substitutable, and a closed ring.

## Results

P1: read_recipient is triadic (Φ = 2.0); broadcast is dyadic. P2: read_recipient is triadic and one_shot,
the same wiring with a frozen recipient, is dyadic. P3: all_required is triadic (Φ = 3.0); substitutable is
dyadic. P4: the closed ring's major complex is the full set {E, A1, A2, R} at Φ = 4.0. P5: the registered
Q71 and Q72 checks establish noise robustness and proxy irrecoverability. All five pillars hold.

## Discussion

The law gives a single criterion for when agent-mediated outreach demands algorithmacy, and it is
deflationary about the intuition that started the line. Cost, disclosure, party count, and connectivity do
not decide the verdict. What decides it is whether the agent binds every party into one joint
determination that stays live and is not substitutable. The delegation results give the sharp social
reading: when both sides hand the exchange to agents, the irreducible coordination is the machines', with
the humans at the edges as a source and a sink — unless a human stays live in the loop, or the agents are
interchangeable enough that the coordination factors back to a broadcast. Whether to keep a human in the
core is then a design choice about liveness and specificity, and the instrument says exactly which
arrangements keep one.

## Limitations

In-silico throughout: exact Φ on small Boolean models, evidence about the models, separated from claims
about real inboxes by the validation gap [axtell1996aligning]. The law is a structural account; it does
not say whether outreach is welcome, ethical, or worth a reply. Φ magnitude is read ordinally. Each pillar
is a representative re-derivation; the full evidence is in Q63-Q72. Hypotheses were fixed before computing
throughout [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
