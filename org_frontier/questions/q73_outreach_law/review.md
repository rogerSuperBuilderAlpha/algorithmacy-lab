# Q73 — Stage 1 review: the outreach-coordination law

## The question

Ten studies (Q63-Q72) read agent-mediated outreach with exact Φ. This study draws them into one stated
law: when does agent-mediated outreach demand algorithmacy, and what governs the answer? It re-derives the
keystone results in one place and states the law they support.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Q63** outreach is triadic iff the agent jointly determines from sender and a live recipient; disclosure
  is a label; cost does not recover the verdict.
- **Q64** breadth does not dilute an all-binding campaign (Φ = n−1); substitutable/pooled factor.
- **Q65** depth preserves the triad (chain Φ = 2.0); a relay-gap agent breaks it; the core localizes.
- **Q66** the open-chain core is a symmetric end pair; a closed loop binds the whole at Φ = 4.0.
- **Q67** reciprocity binds at a threshold; partial reciprocity seeds a local core but the whole still
  factors.
- **Q68** a recipient-side triage agent joins the core only when bidirectional, and displaces the sender.
- **Q69** when both sides delegate, the two agents are the core; both humans are displaced.
- **Q70** but only if the agents are non-substitutable; multi-homing collapses it.
- **Q71** the verdict is robust to noise (graceful decay, no spurious triads).
- **Q72** no cheap proxy recovers the verdict; the exact computation is required.

## The gap

Each study stated a piece. No single artifact states the law they jointly support or re-derives its
pillars in one reproducible place. This study closes that.
