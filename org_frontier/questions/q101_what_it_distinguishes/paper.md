# Q101 — What a coordination distinguishes: the joint-success state

## Question

Q99 and Q100 read the shape of the cause-effect structure. A distinction specifies more than a mechanism
and a purview, though: it specifies a state, the configuration the mechanism tells apart. This study reads
that state, asking not whether a coordination integrates nor what shape it has, but what it is about — which
joint configuration of the parties it distinguishes.

## Method

For three triadic forms (read_recipient, the all-required market, the required market) and two dyadic
controls (broadcast, one_shot), the binding distinction is found — a single-party mechanism whose purview
spans two or more parties — and its specified cause and effect states are read at the integrating state.

## Results

The read-recipient triad's binding distinction has the two parties as its purview and specifies the state
(1, 1) for both cause and effect. The all-required market's binding distinction specifies (1, 1, 1) over the
worker and both recipients. The dyadic forms have no binding distinction. The required market, a symmetric
triad, has no single-party binding distinction either, but its joint distinction {E, C} → {M1, M2} specifies
(1, 1).

| form | binding purview | specified effect state |
|---|---|---|
| read_recipient | E, R | (1, 1) |
| all_required (k=2) | E, R1, R2 | (1, 1, 1) |
| required market (N=2) | — (joint distinction {E,C}→{M1,M2}) | (1, 1) |
| broadcast / one_shot | none | — |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 binding distinction specifies a joint state | confirmed |
| H2 the state is the determination-firing configuration | confirmed |
| H3 no dyadic form distinguishes a joint state | confirmed |
| H4 every triad distinguishes it via a binding distinction | refuted |

## Interpretation

The content of coordination is the joint-success state. The mediator's distinction does not tell apart the
worker's state or the recipient's state; it tells apart the joint configuration in which both are present,
the state where the determination fires and the coordination succeeds. The cause-effect structure says, in
its specified states, that the system is about that configuration. This is a semantic statement the scalar
verdict and even the structural fingerprint do not make: the verdict says the form integrates, the
fingerprint says what shape it has, and the specified state says what it is about.

The dyad is about nothing joint. The broadcast and one_shot have no distinction with a joint purview, so
they distinguish only single-party states and there is no joint-success configuration for them to be about.
The difference between coordination and its absence is, at the level of content, the difference between a
system that distinguishes a joint-success state and one that does not.

The content is uniform across kinds, while the structure that carries it is not. The required market, being
symmetric, has no single mediator distinguishing the joint state; it distinguishes the all-present
configuration through two mutually-binding groups instead, the joint distinction {E, C} → {M1, M2}
specifying (1, 1). The mediated triad and the symmetric triad are about the same thing — the state where
every party is present — but one states it through a hub and the other through a pair of groups. That the
content is invariant while the structure varies by kind is the complement to Q100: the structure carries
both a form, which differs, and a content, which here does not.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with the exact cause-effect structure at the integrating state. The forms read
here have conjunctive determinations that fire at the all-ones state; a determination firing at another
configuration would specify that configuration, which is untested. Three triadic forms and two dyadic
controls, so the content is demonstrated rather than swept over a population. H4 is refuted by the
mediated/symmetric split, which Q99 established, not by a triad distinguishing a different state.
