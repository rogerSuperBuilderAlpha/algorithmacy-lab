# Q101 literature — the content of coordination

## From shape to content

Q99 and Q100 read the shape of the cause-effect structure: a joint mechanism carries the integration, and
the structure fingerprints the kind. A distinction has more than a mechanism and a purview, though: it
specifies a state, the configuration the mechanism tells apart. Reading that state asks not whether a
coordination integrates, nor what shape it has, but what it is about — which joint configuration of the
parties it distinguishes.

## The expected content

For a conjunctive determination, the mediator commits when the parties are jointly present, so the binding
distinction should specify the all-present state, the configuration where coordination succeeds. If this
holds across forms, the cause-effect structure says that coordination is, in content, the distinguishing of
the joint-success state — a semantic statement the scalar verdict and even the structural fingerprint do not
make. A dyadic form, which does not bind the parties jointly, should distinguish no such joint state.

## Method context

The specified state is read from the distinction's `purview_state`, at the integrating state, on forms
already on the record.
