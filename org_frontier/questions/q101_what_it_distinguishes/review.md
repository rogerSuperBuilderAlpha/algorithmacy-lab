# Q101 — Stage 1 review: what does a coordination distinguish?

## The question

Q99 and Q100 read the shape of the cause-effect structure. A distinction specifies a state of its purview,
the configuration it tells apart. Reading that state asks what a coordination is about — which joint
configuration of the parties it distinguishes — not whether it integrates or what shape it has.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **A joint mechanism carries the integration (Q99).** The binding distinction's purview is the parties it
  binds. Its specified state is the configuration of those parties the coordination distinguishes.
- **The law: triadic iff the parties are bound into one joint determination (Findings).** A conjunctive
  determination fires at the all-present state, so the binding distinction should specify that state.
- **The structure is orthogonal to the scalar (Q100).** The content read here is a third axis, beyond the
  verdict and the fingerprint.

## The gap

No study reads the specified states of a coordination's distinctions. Whether a triad distinguishes a joint
configuration, and whether that configuration is the determination-firing state, is uncomputed.
