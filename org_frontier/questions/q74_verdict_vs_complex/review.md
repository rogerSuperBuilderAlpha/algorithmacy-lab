# Q74 — Stage 1 review: whole-system verdict versus the maximal complex

## The question

The review of the outreach program found that the deepest results (Q65's core localization, Q68/Q69's
displacement) read on the maximal complex in ways that can overclaim, because the whole-system verdict and
the maximal complex diverge. This study characterizes that divergence and states a rule for which reading
to report. When the major complex differs from the whole-system verdict, what structural feature decides
the difference, and which verdict is the one to report?

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Read the verdict on the major complex when there are spectators (convention; Q63 H3, Q68, Q69).** A
  form with a spectator node is read on the maximal complex, because the spectator is outside the
  coordination. But the convention does not say when the whole-system verdict should instead govern.
- **Core membership needs bidirectional coupling, which is necessary but not sufficient (Finding 8).** A
  party joins the major complex only if it both feeds and is fed by the coordination; among bidirectionally
  coupled parties, pivotality decides. Q65 found interior chain agents that are bidirectionally coupled yet
  outside the complex.
- **Two divergence cases appear in the record.** Type A: whole-system dyadic, major complex triadic
  (spectator or frozen node, e.g. Q63 disclosed, Q68 monitoring, Q69 delegated). Type B: whole-system
  triadic, major complex a proper triadic subset (Q65 chain localization).

## The gap

The lab applies the major-complex reading case by case but has not built the divergence cases side by side
and stated the rule that decides which verdict to report, as a function of the excluded elements' coupling.
No prior probe pins this, so the question is open.
