# Q74 — Stage 2 deep research: which system exists

This is a question internal to integrated information theory, so the relevant prior work is the structure
of complexes (shared `references.bib`).

## The maximal complex as the system that exists

Integrated information theory holds that the system that exists is the maximal complex: the subset of
elements whose integrated information is locally maximal, with surrounding elements feeding it without
belonging to it [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4]. PyPhi computes the maximal complex exactly
for small systems [mayner2018pyphi]. A larger wired system can contain a smaller maximal complex; the
whole-system Φ over the minimum-information partition is a different quantity from the Φ of the maximal
complex, and the two can disagree on the dyadic/triadic verdict.

## Spectators, sources, and sinks

A party that only emits (a constant source) or only reads (a sink) is not bidirectionally coupled and, by
the lab's core-membership finding, stays out of the maximal complex. Such a party makes the whole system
factor while the core remains irreducible. A party that is bidirectionally coupled but non-pivotal also
stays out, but the whole system can still be irreducible. The distinction between these two exclusions is
the structural feature this study isolates. The coordination framing carries from Q63-Q73 [malone1994interdisciplinary;
rao2012economics; hancock2020aimc].

## The open gap

No source states, for the lab's forms, the rule that decides which verdict to report when the whole system
and the maximal complex diverge. The gap is open: build the divergence cases, read both verdicts, and tie
the divergence type to the coupling of the excluded elements. In-silico, with the validation gap
[axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
