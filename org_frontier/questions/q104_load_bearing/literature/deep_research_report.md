# Q104 literature — the load-bearing element of the structure

## From fragility to the carrier

Q93 found the verdict fragile: every triadic form collapses under at least one single-bit flip, and the
margin is near-binary. That result is about the verdict. Its cause-effect analogue asks which element of
the structure carries the integration — which distinction holds the φ, and which edge, removed, collapses
it. Q99 found the integration lives in a binding mechanism with φ_d equal to the system Φ; this reads
whether that mechanism is the load-bearing one and confirms it by knockout.

## The expected load

For the read-recipient triad the binding distinction carries the system Φ, and the singletons carry less,
so the integration concentrates in the binding mechanism. The edge that enables it — the mediator reading a
party — should be load-bearing, its removal collapsing the verdict. Whether the downstream reads are also
load-bearing, or whether the triad has redundancy, follows Q93's result that the minimal triad sits one
edit from collapse, sharpened here to which edits.

## Method context

The distinctions are read with Q99's `ces`; the knockouts remove a read by collapsing its rule and recompute
the verdict, as in Q93's perturbation but targeted at named edges.
