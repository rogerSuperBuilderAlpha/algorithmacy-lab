# Q104 — Stage 1 review: the load-bearing distinction

## The question

Q93 found the verdict fragile to a single bit-flip. Its cause-effect analogue asks which element of the
structure carries the integration — which distinction holds the φ, and which edge, removed, collapses it.
This closes the structure wave on the question Q99 opened: the integration lives in a binding mechanism, but
is that mechanism the load-bearing one, and does the minimal triad have any redundancy?

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The integration lives in a binding mechanism with φ_d = Φ (Q99).** The candidate load-bearing
  distinction. Whether it is the carrier, and how much the parts carry, is read here.
- **The triad is one edit from collapse (Q93).** Every triadic form collapses under at least one single-bit
  flip. Whether every edge of the minimal triad is load-bearing sharpens this.
- **The law: a triad is one irreducible joint determination (Findings).** The φ_d of the joint mechanism
  against the parts is the cause-effect form of that law.

## The gap

No study reads which distinction carries the integration or which edges are load-bearing. Whether the
binding distinction holds the system Φ, how much the parts carry, and whether the minimal triad has
redundancy, is uncomputed.
