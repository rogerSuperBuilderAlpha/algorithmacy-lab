# Q116 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on whether strategic value and structural contribution are the same quantity. The
political-economy wave measured each party's strategic value, its Shapley value of integration (Q111); the
cause-effect wave measured each party's structural contribution, the φ_d its own distinction carries (Q99,
Q104). This closing study asks whether the two coincide. Written and committed before the run; the instrument
was validated.

The panel runs both measures across four forms: the read-recipient triad, the bidirectional principal (Q114),
and the all-required market at N = 2 and N = 3 (Q85).

## H1 — The two coincide on the minimal triad

- **Claim:** On the read-recipient triad, each party's normalized singleton φ_d equals its normalized Shapley
  share (within 0.01).
- **H0:** They differ on the triad.
- **Predicted outcome:** they coincide. H0 refuted. In the minimal case, structural depth and strategic
  value are the same number.

## H2 — The two rank the parties identically on every form

- **Claim:** On every form in the panel, the two measures induce the same ordering of parties: no party pair
  is ranked oppositely by Shapley value and singleton φ_d.
- **H0:** Some form ranks a pair oppositely.
- **Predicted outcome:** concordant everywhere. H0 refuted. The structurally deepest party is always the most
  strategically valuable; structure predicts the order of value.

## H3 — The two diverge cardinally on the principal

- **Claim:** On the bidirectional principal, the two distributions differ by more than 0.05 in some party's
  share.
- **H0:** They coincide on the principal too.
- **Predicted outcome:** they diverge. H0 refuted. The identity of H1 is not universal; the two measures are
  the same number only in special cases. This is the study's genuinely uncertain claim — the identity could
  equally have held everywhere.

## H4 — The structural measure over-concentrates where the two diverge

- **Claim:** On the forms where they diverge (principal, market at N = 3), the most strategically valuable
  party's structural share exceeds its strategic share.
- **H0:** The structural measure does not systematically over-concentrate.
- **Predicted outcome:** structural > strategic for the top party. H0 refuted. The Shapley value spreads
  credit to every party for its marginal contribution, while the φ_d localizes it in each party's own
  distinction, so structure concentrates value on the dominant party that strategy redistributes outward.
