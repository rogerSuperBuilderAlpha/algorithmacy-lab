# Q116 — Stage 1 review: strategic value against structural contribution

## The question

The political-economy wave (Q111-Q115) measured each party's strategic value, its Shapley value of
integration. The cause-effect wave (Q99-Q104) measured each party's structural contribution, the φ_d its own
distinction carries. This closing study asks whether the two are the same quantity, and where they part.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Value concentrates at the productive position (Q111); the principal captures by creating (Q114); scale
  pays the scarce (Q115).** The strategic side of the comparison is fully mapped, with Shapley values on
  record for the triad, the principal, and the market.
- **The mediator's distinction carries φ_d = system Φ (Q104); the binding distinction is the joint
  determination (Q99).** The structural side is mapped too, with per-party singleton φ_d available from the
  cause-effect structure.
- **The read-recipient numbers already hint at the answer.** The mediator's singleton φ_d (2.0) against the
  outer parties' (0.5) normalizes to 2/3, 1/6, 1/6, the same as the Shapley shares. Whether the coincidence
  survives asymmetric coupling and scale is the open question.

## The gap

The two measures were computed in separate waves and never compared. Whether strategic value equals
structural depth, ranks with it, or coincides only in the minimal case is untested, and the wave does not
close until the two halves are put side by side.
