# Q50 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Five working hypotheses on the party-read structure that lets OR-labelled strict-mediation forms bind
triadically at Φ=2.0 on the four-edge floor. Written and committed before any test runs. Probe #148
(Q45) supplies the two binding OR labels and the fourteen dyadic counterexamples.

## H1 — Matched non-constant party reads bind OR
- **Claim:** An OR-commit strict-mediation form (S-index 7) is triadic at Φ=2.0 if and only if the worker
  and counterpart use the same non-constant one-input read of S (W-index = C-index ∈ {1, 2}).
- **H0:** At least one triadic OR form violates the matched-read rule, or at least one dyadic OR form
  satisfies it.
- **Predicted outcome:** Exactly two triadic OR forms (W1_S7_C1, W2_S7_C2); zero triadic OR forms off
  the matched diagonal. H0 is refuted.

## H2 — Constant party reads exclude OR binding
- **Claim:** Every OR-commit form where either party has a constant read of S (W-index or C-index in
  {0, 3}) is dyadic, regardless of the other party's read.
- **H0:** At least one OR form with a constant party read is triadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** Zero triadic OR forms among the eight constant-read combinations (four with
  W-index 0, four with W-index 3). H0 is refuted.

## H3 — Asymmetric party reads collapse OR
- **Claim:** Every OR-commit form with mismatched party-read indices (W-index ≠ C-index) is dyadic.
- **H0:** At least one OR form with W-index ≠ C-index is triadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** Zero triadic OR forms among the twelve asymmetric-read combinations. H0 is
  refuted.

## H4 — Binding OR forms share the canonical seam tie
- **Claim:** The two triadic OR forms at Φ=2.0 carry the same MIP tie pattern as the canonical conjunctive
  triad: system Φ=2.0 with a {W,SC}/{WS,C} seam tie, not a worker-unique cut.
- **H0:** At least one binding OR form has a different Φ magnitude, structure label, or seam set than the
  instrument control.
- **Predicted outcome:** Both W1_S7_C1 and W2_S7_C2 read triadic with max_phi=2.0 and a non-empty seam
  set matching the control's {W,C} symmetric tie. H0 is refuted.

## H5 — Commit symmetry splits the party-read rule
- **Claim:** Among the sixteen monotone strict-mediation forms at Φ=2.0, symmetric mediator commits (AND,
  OR, NOR, NAND: S-index in {1, 7, 8, 14}) bind only with matched party reads (W-index = C-index ∈ {1, 2}),
  while asymmetric implication commits (S-index in {2, 4, 11, 13}) bind only with complementary reads
  (W-index ≠ C-index, one identity and one NOT).
- **H0:** At least one symmetric-commit triadic form uses mismatched reads, or at least one implication
  triadic form uses matched reads.
- **Predicted outcome:** Eight symmetric-commit forms on the matched diagonal; eight implication forms on
  the complementary anti-diagonal; zero cross-rule violations. H0 is refuted.
