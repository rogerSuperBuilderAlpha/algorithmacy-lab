# Q50 — OR triadic seam · Stage 1 review

**Question.** What party-read structure lets an OR-labelled strict-mediation triad bind irreducibly (Φ=2.0)
at the four-edge floor, while otherwise-similar OR forms collapse to a dyad?

**Agenda id.** Grows from Q45 / probe #148 (agenda #48 follow-on). No dedicated agenda slot; the open
thread is the party-read wiring that separates the two binding OR forms (W1_S7_C1, W2_S7_C2) from the
fourteen dyadic OR-labelled strict-mediation forms at the same edge floor.

## The gap

Probe #148 showed OR is not categorically excluded from triadic binding in strict mediation, but it
listed only two binding labels among sixteen OR-labelled forms without explaining the party-read wiring
that separates them. The fourteen dyadic OR forms share the same mediator commit (S'=W∨C) and the same
four-edge floor; the difference must lie in how W and C read S. No probe has tested whether matched versus
asymmetric one-input party reads, constant versus live reads, or commit symmetry (OR versus implication)
predicts which monotone forms bind at Φ=2.0. This question asks for that party-read structure.
