# Q105 — Stage 1 review: construction distance

## The question

The program reads whether a form is triadic. Q93 read the collapse side of the boundary: a triad is one edit
from dyadic. The build side is unread. A designer needs the inverse — how far a dyadic form is from triadic,
and where the crossing edit sits — which opens the prescriptive turn from diagnosis to design.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **A triad is one edit from collapse (Q93).** The fragility margin is near-binary. The construction
  distance is its dual; whether the boundary is thin from both sides is the question.
- **The law has two conditions (Findings).** The mediator reading all parties (binding) and the parties
  keeping themselves live (liveness, Finding 3). The build supplies whichever is missing, and which one that
  is, is the open question.
- **The strict-mediation family is the 256 forms (corpus).** Each an 8-bit point, so the construction
  distance is a Hamming distance to the 24 triadic forms.

## The gap

No study reads the build side of the dyad/triad boundary. How far a dyadic form is from triadic, and whether
the building edit sits at the mediator or a party, is uncomputed.
