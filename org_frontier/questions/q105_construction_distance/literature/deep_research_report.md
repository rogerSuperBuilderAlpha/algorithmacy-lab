# Q105 literature — the build side of the boundary

## From diagnosis to design

The program reads whether a form is triadic. Q93 read the collapse side of the dyad/triad boundary: a
triadic form is one bit-flip from dyadic, and the margin is near-binary. The build side is unread. A
designer who wants a coordination to demand the competency, or to avoid it, needs the inverse: how far a
dyadic form is from triadic, and where the edit that crosses the boundary sits.

## The expected boundary

If the boundary is thin from both sides, most dyadic forms should be a small number of edits from a triadic
one, mirroring Q93's finding that triads are one edit from collapse. The law says a triad needs the mediator
reading all parties into one joint determination, so the building edit should be at the mediator's function
rather than at the parties' reads — though liveness, the parties keeping themselves live to the mediator,
lives in the parties' reads, so building could happen there. The construction distance is the design dual of
the fragility margin, and the two together map the boundary from both sides.

## Method context

The construction distance is a Hamming distance over the 8-bit form encoding to the 24 triadic forms, which
the verdict identifies, with no perturbation simulation needed.
