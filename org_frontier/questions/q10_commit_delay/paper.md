# Commit-response delay: graded Φ, stable verdict

<code + data: org_frontier/questions/q10_commit_delay/ ; probes #160–164 (this study), context Q9, #62, #43, #57, #60, #112, #32 in probes/PROBES.md>

## Abstract

A triadic conjunctive mediated form (worker W, mediator S, counterpart C) runs under a fixed commit-response delay: the parties respond to S's commit from d steps earlier, for d = 0, 1, 2, 3. Two constructions carry the delay. The buffer pipeline carries S's committed value forward through d pass-through nodes; the lagged read composes the synchronous map so the parties read S as it was d steps back, with no added nodes. The study asks whether the added delay flips the dyadic/triadic verdict or grades Φ down. The buffer pipeline holds the verdict triadic at every d, with Φ_MIP(d) = [2.0, 1.0, 2.0, 2.0], a dip at d=1 and recovery above, so the verdict stays robust while the magnitude moves non-monotonically (H1, refuted). The buffer nodes stay inside the major complex: at d=1 the core is {S,C,B1} and ejects W, and at d=2 and d=3 the interior buffers remain inside the core with φ_{B_i}=0.0 (H2, refuted). The two constructions disagree at d=1 and d=2. At d=2 the lagged read factors to dyadic Φ=0 while the buffer pipeline reads triadic Φ=2.0, so the implementation carries the result (H3, confirmed). A forward-difference discontinuity appears at d=2 for both forms, traced to the d=1 dip-and-recovery, so the fixed collinearity rule fails the no-d=2-threshold claim (H4, refuted). Transport delay and the Q9 slowed clock leave distinct fingerprints: transport stays triadic on W-S-C party cores and avoids the self-absorbed {S} core the hold-for-k produces at k*_det=2 (H5, confirmed). Three of five fixed predictions are refuted. Transport delay regrades Φ and relocates the complex while the verdict holds, and the verdict a delay produces depends on how the delay is built.

## Introduction

A coordination form holds together only if the parties act on each other within one update of the analyst's clock. IIT evaluates integration over single-step transitions: cause and effect repertoires run from t to t+1 through the transition matrix, with no instantaneous causation [Oizumi2014IIT3; Mayner2018PyPhi]. A fixed transport delay therefore lives in the structure of the network. Explicit structure carries it: d intermediate nodes chained in a pipeline, or the parties reading the mediator at lag d, each lag costing one network step [Mayner2018PyPhi].

The substantive question places a commit-response delay against the other timing manipulations already on the corpus. Some flip the verdict; added depth leaves it. Probe 57 found that lengthening a mediator chain held the verdict triadic. Grain coarsening (#32, #60) and sequential update (#62) factored a triadic form to dyadic. Q9 found that holding the mediator for k steps flipped the verdict at k*_det=2 and collapsed the major complex onto a self-absorbed {S} core. A transport delay sits between these. It adds depth like a chain while it desynchronizes the loop like grain and hold. Which behavior wins, and whether the answer turns on how the delay is constructed, stood open.

The corpus anchor is two_sided_match: W' = S, S' = W ∧ C, C' = S, triadic at Φ_MIP = 2.0 with MIP {W,SC} under synchronous update, the form Probes 57 and 62 and Q9 used. The delay is built two ways on it. The buffer pipeline inserts d pass-through nodes B_1…B_d carrying S's value forward, with no self-OR, verified as transport free of stickiness. The lagged read composes the map so the parties read S from d steps back, expressible as a single 3-node TPM. The grid runs d over 0, 1, 2, 3, the synchronous baseline through three steps of transport, spanning the forms' attractor periods and the Q9 flip point.

No prior probe ran a commit-response delay on the corpus, and the deep-research survey found no source that computes Φ for a mediated social triad under such a delay. The literature supplies the machinery and a directional prediction; the measured verdict was open.

## Prior work

No surveyed primary source computes Φ_MIP, the dyadic/triadic verdict, or the major complex for a mediated triad under a commit-response delay across d. The probe stands open, and the literature fixes the formalism and a strong directional prediction about where the answer should land.

IIT evaluates integration over single discrete-step transitions, with elements influencing one another only from one timestep to the next, so a fixed delay of d steps lives in explicit structure, never a free parameter [Oizumi2014IIT3; Albantakis2023IIT4; Mayner2018PyPhi]. The canonical model of a propagation delay is a COPY element relaying one input forward; a chain of d COPY elements implements a d-step delay line [Marshall2018Blackboxing]. The buffer-pipeline construction states this construction in IIT's own terms.

The published precedent for what a delay does to Φ is direct. Marshall, Albantakis and Tononi model an XOR system with a one-step propagation delay as COPY buffers and compute Φ at two grains: the micro system with the delay has Φ = 0.25 with first-order mechanisms, and black-boxing the COPY delays over two steps yields the same logic at Φ = 1.875 with second-order mechanisms [Marshall2018Blackboxing]. Both values stay positive. The delay relocates cause-effect power between grains while preserving it, and the COPY buffers fold inside the macro complex once the coarser grain is taken. The general result reinforces this: integrated information peaks at the grain fitting the system's dynamics. A two-element system with second-order Markov dynamics has Φ rise from 0.07 at the micro step to 0.12 at a macro step spanning two micro steps [Hoel2016MacroMicro; Oizumi2014IIT3]. A fixed transport delay still permits recovery of the interaction timescale under probabilistic jitter; Φ stays maximal at the scale matching the system's interaction delay, reduced in magnitude with the peak location preserved [Leung2022MacroTimescales].

Where the buffer nodes sit follows from the bidirectional requirement. Each part of a complex specifies both selective causes and selective effects about the rest; a subset that specifies one direction only integrates weakly and reads as an appendix [Oizumi2014IIT3; Albantakis2023IIT4]. A purely feed-forward chain has Φ = 0 and falls outside any complex, the feed-forward "zombie" prediction IIT makes against recurrent systems [Oizumi2014IIT3; Tononi2015HereTheres; Hanson2019Zombies]. A pass-through buffer passes a commit forward and takes no integrated feedback, so the prediction placed the buffers outside the major complex with the verdict recovered on the W-S-C core. Integrated information responds to fault lines, the partitions separating parts that interact directionally, so a pipeline cut comes cheap and drives Φ down while leaving the verdict in place [Albantakis2023IIT4].

The construct also has a coordination-theory home. A precedence dependency, where a consuming task needs resources from a producing one, runs either by inserting buffers between the processes or by tying the production rate to the consumption rate [Crowston1994Taxonomy]. The transport buffer and the rate match are the two coordination fixes, and they map onto the two cases the question separates: a pure transport delay against a slowed update clock. The sources licensed a directional prediction: transport delay grades Φ and relocates the complex while the verdict holds, and a flip waits on the slowed clock. The survey's own verification refuted one supporting claim. The buffer COPY nodes contribute no first-order power proved unsustained, so the buffers' φ called for measurement rather than an assumed zero.

## Hypotheses

Five hypotheses were fixed before any computation, each with the structural prior its null rests on. The verdict is the binary structure read off Φ_MIP over the most-integrated reachable state, with PHI_EPS = 1e-9 the zero threshold and TOL = 1e-6 the numerical tolerance; Φ magnitude serves as an ordinal hint.

**H1 — verdict-robust grade.** A buffer pipeline of length d holds the verdict triadic at every d = 0, 1, 2, 3, lowering Φ_MIP at the synchronous grain while staying on the triadic side of the boundary, the way depth (#57) and reliability (#27) did. The decision rule confirms H1 only if Φ_MIP(d) either falls monotonically (dΦ/dd ≤ 0) or holds at the #57 chain value of 2.0. The null reads transport delay like the other timing axes: at some d the joint determination factors and the W-C pair reads dyadic, the collapse #32, #62 and the Q9 hold produced. A Φ_MIP that rises with d also refutes.

**H2 — buffers outside the complex.** The pass-through buffers fall outside the W-S-C major complex at the micro grain, with the verdict object recovered on the W-S-C core and the interior buffers excluded at φ_{B_i} ≈ 0, since a feed-forward element carries Φ = 0. The null is the #57 chain-intermediary reading, where the buffers sit inside the complex with φ_{B_i} > 0. Black-boxing the buffers over d steps may absorb them at higher Φ, recorded as compatible with the micro-grain claim.

**H3 — construction disagreement.** The explicit buffer pipeline and the lagged read part ways on the verdict or the major complex at some d: the lagged read compiles to a composed map and behaves like a grain change, flipping while the buffer pipeline holds. The null has IIT respond to the lag itself, so both return the same verdict and the same membership at every d. The implementation-is-load-bearing claim is the confirmed branch.

**H4 — no d=2 threshold.** Transport delay carries an ordinary d=2 status, so Φ_MIP(d) on the buffer pipeline runs through d=2 with no kink, jump, or verdict change unique to it for either form, with the d=2 reading on the smooth line through d=1 and d=3 within TOL. The null reproduces the Q9 k=2 flip and the grain-2 collapse: a d=2 delay composes two transitions and produces a kink or flip at d=2. A forward-difference discontinuity at d=2 refutes.

**H5 — distinguishable fingerprints.** Transport delay and the Q9 slowed clock are mechanistically distinct, visible in the surviving structure. Where transport delay touches the verdict it leaves a major complex on {W,S,C} or {W,C} with the buffers excluded and steers clear of the self-absorbed {S} core the hold-for-k produced, with a MIP cut differing from the hold's and no buffer self-OR. The null collapses the two into one factorization under two parameters: the delay reproduces the hold's {S}-core collapse with the identical MIP cut.

## Methods

The corpus anchor is two_sided_match: rules `[lambda x: x[1], lambda x: x[0] & x[2], lambda x: x[1]]`, node 0 = W, node 1 = S, node 2 = C. A second form of differing synchronous attractor period is carried for H4 and H5 to separate a d-trend from an attractor effect: gig_false_dyad, `[lambda x: 1 - x[1], lambda x: x[0] & x[2], lambda x: x[2] & (1 - x[1])]`, synchronous period 1. Φ, the verdict, and the major complex are read by `org_frontier/classifier/classifier.py` and `org_frontier/probes/lib.py`; the maximal complex over reachable states by `new_big_phi.maximal_complex`.

The buffer pipeline inserts d pass-through nodes on the path from S's commit to the parties' read: the system has n = 3 + d nodes, B_1 copies S, each B_{i+1} copies B_i, the parties read the tail B_d in place of S, and S keeps its own conjunctive commit W ∧ C. Each buffer is pure pass-through with no B_i → B_i self-edge, verified by `cm_from_rules` as clean transport, clear of any #43 stickiness cell. The lagged read carries the same d-step transport with no added nodes: the parties read S as it was d synchronous steps earlier, built as the d-fold composition of the synchronous map with the party read delayed by d, expressible as a single 3-node TPM. The grid runs d over 0, 1, 2, 3. The forward difference is dΦ/dd|_d = Φ(d+1) − Φ(d). A verdict flip is the smallest grid d at which Φ_MIP ≤ PHI_EPS.

The instrument control ran first and passed. Both constructions reduce to the bare 3-node synchronous map at d=0: triadic, Φ_MIP = 2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`, state (1,1,1), the Probe 57/62/Q9 anchor. `buffer_pipeline_rules(rules, 0)` equals the bare 3-node TPM, and for d ≥ 1 no B_i → B_i self-edge appears at any d, confirming pass-through. The W-S-C major complex under strict mediation is the full {W,S,C} at φ = 2.0. A form whose d=0 endpoint failed to reproduce would have had its swept values discarded unread.

## Results

**H1 — refuted.** The buffer pipeline holds the verdict triadic at every d = 0, 1, 2, 3, so the verdict never flips. The magnitude moves non-monotonically. Φ_MIP(d) = [2.0, 1.0, 2.0, 2.0] over n = 3, 4, 5, 6 nodes, with MIPs {W,SC}, {WS,CB1}, {WS,B1,CB2}, {WS,B1B2,CB3}, and the forward differences dΦ/dd = [−1.0, +1.0, +0.0]. Φ dips at d=1 and recovers, missing both a monotone fall and the #57 chain value of 2.0. The +1.0 rise at d=1→2 refutes by the fixed rule. The pass-through control passed, with no B_i → B_i self-edge at any d, so depth drives the Φ change, with inertia ruled out. The verdict-robust half of the prediction held; the magnitude-monotone half failed (results/q10_h1_buffer_verdict.csv, probe_buffer_verdict.py).

**H2 — refuted.** The buffers stay inside the major complex, and the {W,S,C} core fails to recover at every d. At d=1 the micro-grain core is {S,C,B1}, which ejects W, with Φ_MIP = 1.0; {W,S,C} falls outside the core, φ_{B1} = 0.0, and the {W,S,C} subsystem itself reads φ = 0.0. At d=2 the core is the full {W,S,C,B1,B2} and at d=3 it is {W,S,C,B1,B2,B3}: the interior buffers remain inside, even with φ_{B2} = φ_{B3} = 0.0 < PHI_EPS. A φ-zero element sitting inside the maximal complex is the refuting reading: the predicted exclusion by zero first-order power failed. Black-boxing the buffers over d steps recovers triadic Φ = 2.0 on {W,S,C} at d=1 but collapses to a dyadic {S} core at d ≥ 2 in the grain branch, so the higher grain absorbs the buffers only at d=1 (results/h2_buffer_membership.csv, probe_buffer_membership.py).

**H3 — confirmed.** The two constructions disagree at d=1 and d=2. At d=0 both read triadic Φ = 2.0 on the {W,S,C} core with MIP {W,SC}. At d=1 the buffer pipeline reads triadic Φ = 1.0 with the W-S-C major complex projecting to {C,S}, while the lagged read reads triadic Φ = 2.0 with the full {W,S,C} complex, two different major complexes at the same d. At d=2 the lagged read factors to dyadic Φ = 0.0 while the buffer pipeline reads triadic Φ = 2.0, the cleanest instance of the split, with the same transport lag giving opposite verdicts. At d=3 both read triadic Φ = 2.0 on {W,S,C} and agree. The lagged read stays a single composed 3-node TPM at every d, while the buffer pipeline adds nodes (3 + d). The disagreement d-values are [1, 2] (results/construction_split.csv, probe_construction_split.py).

**H4 — refuted.** Both forms give the identical profile Φ_MIP(d) = [2.0, 1.0, 2.0, 2.0] with forward differences [−1.0, +1.0, +0.0]. The slope changes from −1.0 entering d=2 to +1.0 leaving d=1, a forward-difference discontinuity at d=2. The line through the d=1 and d=3 readings evaluated at d=2 gives 1.5, while the d=2 reading is 2.0, a residual of 0.5 ≫ TOL, so d=2 lies off the smooth d=1→d=3 trend. The fixed collinearity rule refutes for both forms. The verdict holds triadic at every d for both, so the discontinuity marks the recovery point of a d=1 Φ dip, with no Q9-style verdict collapse at d=2. The literal kink the rule flags is real, while the verdict-collapse the null predicted stays absent (results/no_d2_threshold.csv, probe_no_d2_threshold.py).

**H5 — confirmed.** Transport delay and the slowed clock leave distinct fingerprints, and all three discriminators fire. The transport-delay fingerprint stays triadic at every d: d=1 Φ_MIP = 1.0, MIP {WS,CB1}, core (S,C,B1), W-S-C projection (S,C); d=2 Φ_MIP = 2.0, MIP {WS,B1,CB2}, core (W,S,C,B1,B2), projection (W,S,C); d=3 Φ_MIP = 2.0, MIP {WS,B1B2,CB3}, projection (W,S,C); no self-edges at any d. The slowed-clock fingerprint (Q9 hold-for-k at k*_det = 2) is dyadic Φ_MIP = 0.0 with the major complex collapsed to the self-absorbed (S,) core at read_state (0,0,0), φ_s = 1.0. The six sequential orders are all dyadic Φ = 0 on cores {S,C}, {S}, {W,C}, {W,C}, {S}, {W,S}. The discriminators: the {S}-core watch passes, with transport staying on {W,S,C}/{W,C} party cores and clear of {S}; the MIP cut differs from the hold's None-cut at every d; the pass-through carries no B_i → B_i self-OR. Transport delay stands as a distinct, verdict-stable timing axis in its own right, separate from the slowed clock (results/probe_factorization_fingerprint.csv, probe_factorization_fingerprint.py).

## Discussion

The five results separate two claims that the directional prediction had run together. Transport delay holds the verdict: the buffer pipeline reads triadic at every d, and the Q9 {S}-core collapse stays away (H1, H5). The prediction's verdict-robust half held. Its magnitude half failed. Φ_MIP dips to 1.0 at d=1 and recovers to 2.0 above, so the delay grades Φ non-monotonically where the chain theorem suggested a monotone fall (H1, H4). The dip-and-recovery is a single feature read three ways: the +1.0 forward difference that refutes H1, the off-line d=2 point that refutes H4, and the same recovery that keeps the verdict triadic throughout. The kink at d=2 is real, a magnitude artifact of the d=1 dip rather than the verdict threshold the Q9 sibling produced at k=2.

The membership prediction missed widest. Feed-forward Φ = 0 placed the buffers outside the complex, yet the maximal complex absorbs them: at d=2 and d=3 the φ-zero interior buffers sit inside the core, and at d=1 the core ejects W ahead of any buffer (H2). A φ-zero element inside the major complex is the exclusion postulate selecting the larger substrate once adding the buffer leaves the system's irreducibility intact. The buffers read as full members of the complex here.

The construction carries the result, the affirmative finding among the refutations. The buffer pipeline and the lagged read encode the same transport lag and disagree on the verdict at d=2: dyadic Φ=0 against triadic Φ=2.0 (H3). The lagged read compiles to a single composed 3-node TPM and behaves like a grain change; the buffer pipeline adds depth and behaves like a chain. A delay reported with its construction is fully specified, and the two arms travel separately. This places transport delay fourth in the #112 timing catalogue, alongside grain (#32, #60), sequential update (#62), and the Q9 hold: the one of the four that holds the verdict robust under the explicit-depth construction while a second construction of the same delay reproduces the collapse.

## Limitations

The verdict is grain- and schedule-relative, and Q10 makes that relativity its central finding: the same transport lag reads triadic as buffer depth and dyadic as a lagged read, and black-boxing the buffers moves the membership again (H2, H3). The Φ magnitude carries no absolute scale, serving as an ordinal hint, and the d=1 dip reads as a verdict-neutral feature within the measured coordination. The system sizes stay small (n = 3 to 6), and the grid runs to d=3, three steps of transport spanning the forms' attractor periods and the Q9 flip point; longer delays and larger forms stay untested. The black-boxed reading uses a single grain branch and a single worked form, so the absorption-versus-ejection question is settled for two_sided_match at d ≤ 3 and stands open in general. Φ magnitudes across d are version-dependent between IIT 3.0 and 4.0; the qualitative result holds stable, verdict-robust under depth and construction-dependent under the lagged read. The corpus carries no measured worker. The probe explores irreducibility under a delay; whether any field coordination form carries the Φ the corpus anchor does stays open.
