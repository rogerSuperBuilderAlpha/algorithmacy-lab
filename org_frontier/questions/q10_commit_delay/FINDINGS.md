## Reading across (probes #160–#164)

A fixed transport delay between the mediator's commit and the parties' response leaves the verdict where it was. The buffer-pipeline form reads triadic at every delay d=0..3 and never produces the self-absorbed {S} core that the Q9 slowed clock collapsed to, so pure transport delay and a slowed update clock are categorically different operations (H5, H1 on the verdict). The magnitude tells a subtler story: Φ_MIP does not glide down, it dips to 1.0 at d=1 and recovers to 2.0 at d=2 and d=3, a non-monotone profile that refutes the simple grading prediction (H1) and registers as a forward-difference kink at d=2 that does not coincide with any verdict change (H4). The buffer nodes do not sit outside the complex: a single buffer at d=1 ejects the worker and joins the {S,C,B1} core, and deeper buffers are inside the major complex despite carrying zero stand-alone integration (H2). The implementation choice is load-bearing, as in Q9. A buffer pipeline (3+d explicit nodes) and a lagged read (one composed 3-node TPM) are different objects and disagree on the verdict at d=2, where the lagged read factors to dyadic Φ=0 while the buffer pipeline stays triadic at Φ=2.0 (H3). Transport delay keeps the coordination integrated; what changes with the delay is the partition and the Φ magnitude, and which model of "delay" is used decides the d=2 verdict.
