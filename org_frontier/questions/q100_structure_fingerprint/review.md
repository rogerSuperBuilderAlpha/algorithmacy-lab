# Q100 — Stage 1 review: does the structure fingerprint the kind?

## The question

Q99 found that triads carry different cause-effect structures the binary verdict collapses. A taxonomy
invites a fingerprint: if the kinds differ, a profile of the structure should separate them, finer than the
scalar verdict. The sharpest test is a pair of coordinations with equal Φ that the structure tells apart.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Triads come in structural types (Q99).** Mediated, symmetric, distributed. This study turns the
  taxonomy into a fingerprint and tests its resolution across coordination kinds.
- **The cause-effect-structure counts classify the verdict (probes 36, 93, 102).** The counts separate
  triadic from dyadic; whether they separate kinds *within* triadic is the new question.
- **Depth, breadth, the ring, the market, and delegation are all triadic (Q64, Q66, Q85, Q68).** The scalar
  gives them one label. Whether the structure gives them different ones is open.

## The gap

No study reads a structural fingerprint across coordination kinds, and none tests whether two equal-Φ forms
carry different structure. Whether the cause-effect structure is a finer classifier than the verdict, and
whether its richness is the scalar or something orthogonal, is uncomputed.
