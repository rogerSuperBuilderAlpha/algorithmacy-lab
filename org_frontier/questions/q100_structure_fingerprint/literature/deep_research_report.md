# Q100 literature — does the structure fingerprint the kind?

## From a taxonomy to a fingerprint

Q99 found that triads carry different cause-effect structures — mediated, symmetric, distributed — that the
binary verdict collapses. A taxonomy invites a fingerprint: if the kinds differ, a profile of the structure
(distinction and relation counts, maximal order, dual type) should separate them, and the separation should
be finer than the scalar verdict, which calls them all triadic.

## Why this matters

The dissertation's case is that integrated information is a rich instrument. The clearest demonstration is a
pair of coordinations the scalar verdict cannot tell apart — equal Φ — that the cause-effect structure
does. Such a Φ-degenerate pair shows the structure carries content the number discards, which is the
property that distinguishes IIT from a complexity index. Whether the structure also scales with the
coordination's magnitude, or is set only by its kind, is the secondary question.

## Method context

The fingerprint reuses the `structure_suite` summary (the same `phi_structure` call) and Q99's dual-type
classification, read at the integrating state on keystone forms already on the record.
