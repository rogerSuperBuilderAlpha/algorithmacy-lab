# Q100 — The cause-effect structure fingerprints the kind, and is orthogonal to the verdict

## Question

Q99 found that triads carry different cause-effect structures (mediated, symmetric, distributed) that the
binary verdict collapses. This study reads a fingerprint of the structure (distinction and relation counts,
maximal order, dual type) for a keystone of six coordination kinds, and asks whether the fingerprint
separates the kinds, whether two forms with equal Φ carry different structure, and whether the richness of
the structure tracks the scalar.

## Method

Six keystone triads, one per kind: joint determination (read_recipient), a breadth market, a depth chain, a
ring, a required market, and a delegation triage. For each, the Φ-structure suite is read at the integrating
state, and the dual type is taken from Q99. The fingerprints are compared for distinctness, dual-type
coverage, Φ-degeneracy, and the correlation of relation count with Φ.

## Results

Five of the six keystones carry distinct fingerprints, spanning all three dual types. Joint determination
and the chain share Φ = 2 but differ in structure. The relation count does not track Φ: its Spearman
correlation with Φ is 0.14, and the breadth market carries 128 relations at Φ = 3 against the ring's 14 at
Φ = 4.

| kind | Φ | distinctions | relations | dual type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| joint determination | 2.0 | 4 | 8 | mediated |
| breadth market (k=2) | 3.0 | 8 | 128 | mediated |
| depth chain (d=2) | 2.0 | 6 | 14 | distributed |
| ring (d=2) | 4.0 | 6 | 14 | symmetric |
| required market (N=2) | 4.0 | 6 | 14 | symmetric |
| delegation triage | 1.0 | 5 | 17 | distributed |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 four or more distinct fingerprints | confirmed (5/6) |
| H2 at least two dual types | confirmed (all three) |
| H3 equal Φ, different structure | confirmed |
| H4 relation count scales with Φ | refuted (ρ = 0.14) |

## Interpretation

The cause-effect structure is a finer instrument than the verdict, and it is orthogonal to it. Five of six
coordination kinds carry distinct fingerprints, so the structure classifies what the verdict calls one
thing. Two kinds with equal Φ carry different structure, so the structure is not recoverable from the
scalar. And the richness of the structure does not track the scalar at all: a breadth market with one
mediator binding several recipients explodes to 128 relations at Φ = 3, while a symmetric ring of higher Φ
holds 14. The number measures how irreducible a coordination is; the structure measures what shape it has,
and the two vary independently.

This is the property that distinguishes integrated information from a complexity index. A complexity index
returns one number, and two systems with the same number are indistinguishable to it. The cause-effect
structure returns a form, and two coordinations of equal Φ can have entirely different forms — a sparse
mediated triad and a combinatorially rich mediated market, a hub and a pair of mutually-binding groups. The
program's earlier results read the number; the structure carries content the number discards, which is the
case for using the full theory rather than its scalar summary.

The fingerprint's one collapse is informative. The ring and the required market share an identical
fingerprint and an identical dual type, because they are the same coordination shape: a four-element ring
and a two-agent required market are isomorphic, two groups mutually determining each other. The fingerprint
reports them as one kind, correctly, while separating the other four. A finer fingerprint, the distinction
set itself, would label the parties differently but would not find a deeper difference, because there is
none.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with the exact cause-effect structure at the integrating state. The fingerprint
is coarse (counts, maximal order, dual type) and collapses the ring and market though their distinctions
carry different labels. One keystone per kind, six in all, so the H4 correlation rests on six points and is
indicative. A population sweep over many forms per kind would tighten the claim that richness is independent
of Φ.
