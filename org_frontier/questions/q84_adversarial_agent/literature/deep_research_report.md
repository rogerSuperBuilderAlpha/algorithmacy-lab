# Q84 — Stage 2 deep research: outside influence on a coordination

This study frames membership as an adversarial question and draws on the same literatures (shared
`references.bib`).

## Membership and influence

Integrated information theory locates the coordination that exists in the maximal complex
[oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4; mayner2018pyphi]. An element outside the maximal complex may
read it or feed it without belonging. The lab's core-membership finding (Finding 8) holds that membership
requires bidirectional coupling plus pivotality. The adversarial reading asks whether a party that wants to
change the coordination can do so from outside, or must join the core, where it becomes a detectable member.

## Adversaries on the receiving and sending sides

The economics of spam frames adversarial senders and the filters that resist them [rao2012economics]; AI-
mediated communication places agents that may act against a party's interest on both sides [hancock2020aimc;
jakesch2019aimc]. Whether such an agent can move the verdict from outside the core is the structural form of
the manipulation question. The framing carries from Q63-Q83 [malone1994interdisciplinary].

## The open gap

The membership conditions are known; their adversarial reading is uncomputed. The gap is open: add an agent
in read-only, emit-only, and bidirectional-pivotal couplings and test whether the core verdict moves only
when the agent joins. In-silico, with the validation gap [axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before
computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
