# Q72 — Stage 2 deep research: cost as a proxy for structure

This study tests cost proxies on the outreach corpus and draws on the same literatures (shared
`references.bib`).

## Cost signals value, but does it signal structure

Costly-signaling theory holds that an expensive message can carry information a cheap one cannot
[spence1973job; zahavi1975mate; grafen1990biological], and the economics of spam turns on the cost
asymmetry of sending [rao2012economics]. The motivating intuition was that invested cost makes outreach
worth answering. The structural question is narrower: does a cheap measure of cost or connectivity recover
whether the coordination is irreducible.

## Proxies for integrated information

A standing problem in integrated-information work is that exact Φ is expensive, so cheaper proxies are
sought [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4; mayner2018pyphi]. The lab's Finding 7 showed cheap
time-series proxies separate dyadic from triadic only near chance. This study tests the cheapest possible
proxies, structural counts, on the outreach forms.

## The open gap

Finding 7 is general; the outreach-specific proxy frontier, with explicit collisions, is uncomputed. The
gap is open: compute structural cost proxies over the labelled outreach forms and test whether any
separates the verdict. In-silico, with the validation gap [axtell1996aligning]; four hypotheses fixed
before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
