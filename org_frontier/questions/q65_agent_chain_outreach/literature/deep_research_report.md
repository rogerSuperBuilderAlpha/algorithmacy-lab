# Q65 — Stage 2 deep research: chained mediation in outreach

This study extends Q63–Q64 along the depth axis, agents between the sender and the recipient. It draws on
the same literatures (shared `references.bib`) and the question of layered intermediation.

## Layered intermediation

Coordination theory treats each mediating mechanism as part of the coordination structure, and chains of
mediators as nested coordination [malone1994interdisciplinary]. Agent-to-agent outreach adds a concrete
case: a sender's agent composing for a recipient's agent that filters before a human reads. The economics
of spam already shows automated intermediaries on the receiving side, filters and triagers, as a standard
part of the email channel [rao2012economics]; outreach agents add an automated intermediary on the
sending side too.

## AI-mediated communication, chained

AI-mediated communication is defined as communication in which an agent modifies, augments, or generates
messages on a person's behalf [hancock2020aimc]. When both sides delegate to agents, the message passes
through two such agents in series. Whether recipients trust a message changes with the perception of
automation [jakesch2019aimc], but the structural question, untouched by that literature, is whether the
chain of agents is one irreducible coordination or a sequence that factors.

## Signaling through a chain

Costly-signaling theory says a signal is informative when its cost makes faking unprofitable
[spence1973job; zahavi1975mate; grafen1990biological]. A chain of agents can either preserve the costly
joint determination end to end or insert a cheap relay that breaks it. The structural counterpart, tested
here, is whether each agent's joint read of its neighbours is what carries irreducibility along the chain.

## The open gap

The lab's mediation-chain result establishes that depth preserves irreducibility in the abstract
[albantakis2023iit4; oizumi2014phenomenology; mayner2018pyphi]. No source computes the verdict for
agent-to-agent outreach, contrasts depth against breadth, or tests a relay-gap agent. The gap is open:
build the agent chain E → A1 → … → Ad → R, read the verdict and Φ as depth grows, test a relay-gap agent,
and combine depth with breadth. In-silico, with the usual validation gap [axtell1996aligning]; five
hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
