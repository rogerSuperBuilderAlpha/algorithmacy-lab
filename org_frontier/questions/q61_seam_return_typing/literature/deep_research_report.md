# Q61 — Stage 2 deep research

## Question

On aligned one-sided back-channel forms held at uniform triadic max_phi=2.0, does the official MIP singleton
seam ({W,SC} vs {WS,C}) track return-path sequential/reciprocal typing even though max_phi offers no subpanel
discrimination?

## MIP singleton seam and recipient direction

IIT 4.0 reads integrated information at the minimum-information partition (MIP) at the max-Phi reachable
state (`albantakis2023iit4`; `mayner2018pyphi`). For n=3 triadic forms, two-part partitions name a severed
singleton: {W,SC} isolates W, {WS,C} isolates C. Q49 operationalized the seam as the set of singletons
appearing across tied two-part MIPs. Q57 (#185) showed that on sixteen aligned one-sided back-channel ceiling
pairs, the back-channel recipient always determines which outer-party singleton cut co-enters the official
tie set: worker recipient→{W,SC}, counterpart recipient→{WS,C}. Partition normalization and directed cut
geometry (#186–#199) explain why recipient direction fixes the favored seam, but none of those probes
cross-tabulates seam against interdependence typing.

## Return-path Thompson typing and uniform verdict

Thompson (1967) distinguished sequential and reciprocal interdependence as escalating coordination demand.
Q43 (#135–#139) operationalized these labels on small Boolean triples: sequential splits on whether the
return path closes through the mediator; reciprocal requires direct cross-party coupling. Q60 (#200) applied
the same return-path convention to the back-channel panel and found perfect recipient→type mapping
(worker→sequential, counterpart→reciprocal) while every form reads triadic at max_phi=2.0 (#201, #204).
The verdict therefore offers no sequential-vs-reciprocal discrimination; typing survives only at the
partition-template and connectivity level.

## Co-extensiveness as a partition question

Malone and Crowston (1994) frame coordination as managing dependencies among activities — a partition
question when two labels classify the same finite panel. Q60 established recipient→type; Q57 established
recipient→seam on the identical panel. The open test is direct: whether seam and return-path type are
co-extensive partitions, or whether one is strictly finer or coarser than the other. If co-extensive, the MIP
singleton seam recovers at the verdict level the typing max_phi magnitude loses. If not, recipient party is a
common cause without identical partition geometry.

## Pre-registration and validation gap

Hypotheses and decision rules are committed before probe code (`brodeur2024preregistration`;
`chamberlin1890multiple`). Results apply to deterministic n=3 Boolean models at finest grain with synchronous
update. Seam extraction follows Q49/Q57; return-path typing follows Q43/Q60. Real organizations remain beyond
reach.
