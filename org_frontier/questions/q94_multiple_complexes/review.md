# Q94 — Stage 1 review: can coordination hold more than one core?

## The question

Every membership result reads the single major complex. IIT 4.0 allows a substrate to split into several
non-overlapping complexes. Whether a coordination form fragments into two coexisting cores, and what
coupling merges them, is unasked.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Finding 8 and Q90 read the major complex.** The program locates which parties join the one core; it
  has never asked whether there is more than one.
- **Bidirectional coupling is necessary for membership (Finding 8).** Lifted to whole cores, the prediction
  is that two cores merge only when they constrain each other both ways.
- **Depth and breadth scale Φ within one determination (Q64, Q65).** Whether coupling two separate cores
  scales the same way, or differently, is open.

## The gap

No study reads more than the major complex. Whether a form holds two disjoint complexes, and the coupling
that fragments or merges them, is uncomputed.
