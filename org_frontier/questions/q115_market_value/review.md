# Q115 — Stage 1 review: the value of a required market at scale

## The question

Q113 found an all-required market at N=2 splits its value equally between the two unique outer parties and
the two agents. This scales the market to N=3 and N=4 and asks who captures the gains: the unique parties or
the required agents. The agents are all required, so the question is whether requiredness shares in the
growth, or whether scale commoditizes the agents regardless.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **An all-required market at N=2 is egalitarian (Q113).** All four parties capture equal value;
  substitutability destroys all value. Whether the equality survives scale is open.
- **Value concentrates at the productive position (Q111); the principal captures by creating, not owning
  (Q114).** The wave reads value capture as structural. A scaling law for markets would complete it.
- **The Q85 market scales cleanly to N agents (all-required, substitutable, mixed).** The machinery to
  rescale is in place; only the value computation is new.

## The gap

No study scales the value distribution of a market. Whether a growing required market shares its growth among
the agents or concentrates it in the unique parties, and whether requiredness or scarcity commands the share,
is uncomputed.
