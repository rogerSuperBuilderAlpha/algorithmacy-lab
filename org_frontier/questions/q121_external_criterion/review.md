# Q121 — Stage 1 review: an external criterion for the verdict

## The question

The critical review (CRITICAL_REVIEW_Q111_Q117.md, T1) found the construct's behavioral validation unmet and
made "produce one non-circular criterion on which triadic forms are distinct" the first defense question. This
study tests the weak form of that question: a criterion from a formalism independent of IIT, on the family
where the verdict is hardest.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Behavioral-difficulty validation failed (Probes 98, 107, 108); Φ is orthogonal to coordination success
  (Probe 48, ρ=0.00).** The construct was tested against difficulty and success and did not track them. The
  open question is whether it tracks anything non-Φ at all on the hard cases.
- **The verdict is logic, not wiring (Q117).** Forty full-cycle forms split 24/16; an observational criterion
  should fail there, an interventional one might succeed.
- **Observational dependence measures are weak (Probes 45-47).** MI, transfer entropy, and O-information do
  not cleanly separate the verdict, pointing away from output statistics and toward intervention.

## The gap

The program tested observational and behavioral criteria, and one weak interventional one (resilience), but
never set an observational and an interventional criterion side by side on the hard subset. Which kind of
non-Φ criterion recovers the verdict where topology and observation both fail, and so which kind of
measurement could ground the construct, is untested.
