# Q121 — Stage 2 literature: an external criterion, and why observation fails

## The question in context

The critical review's T1 asks for a non-circular criterion on which triadic forms are distinct. The program's
own record shows the strong form of the question is unmet: agent-difficulty validation failed (Probes 98, 107
at rank-AUC 0.567, 0.541) or was weak (Probe 108 at 0.618), and Probe 48 found Φ orthogonal to coordination
success (ρ = 0.00). The weak form, consilience with an independent formalism, has a sharper handle once two
facts from the corpus are taken seriously.

## What the corpus and the field establish

- **The verdict is interventional, not observational.** Q117 showed the verdict lives in the determination's
  logic, not its wiring: 40 forms of identical connectivity split 24/16. IIT itself is a causal,
  interventional theory; Φ is built on the cause-effect repertoire under do-interventions, not on observed
  correlations. This predicts that observational dependence measures will fail to separate the verdict where
  connectivity cannot, and that an interventional probe may succeed.
- **Observational dependence measures already failed.** Probes 45-47 found MI, transfer entropy, and
  O-information do not cleanly separate the verdict; a dyadic back-channel form can be the most synergistic.
  This is the expected signature of a verdict that is not a function of the output distribution.
- **Damage spreading is the canonical interventional probe for Boolean dynamics.** Kauffman's random Boolean
  networks (1969) and the Derrida-Pomeau annealed approximation (1986) measure how a single-bit perturbation
  propagates: ordered regimes dissipate damage, chaotic regimes amplify it. Damage spreading is a do-
  intervention measure defined entirely within dynamical-systems theory, with no reference to IIT, so its
  agreement with the verdict is consilience, not circularity.
- **Class imbalance inflates rank-AUC.** With 24 triadic among 256 forms, a criterion that merely detects the
  feedback cycle scores AUC ≈ 0.97 on the full family. So "AUC well above 0.5 on the family" is a weak bar;
  the severe test is the 40 full-cycle forms where the cycle is uninformative.

## The gap Q121 fills

The program tested observational and behavioral criteria and a single weak interventional one (resilience),
but never put an observational and an interventional criterion side by side on the hard subset where the
verdict is hardest. Q121 does, asking which kind of non-Φ criterion recovers the verdict where topology and
observation both fail, and so which kind of external measurement could ground the construct.
