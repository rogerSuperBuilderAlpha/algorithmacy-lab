# Q60 — Stage 2 deep research

## Question

Among aligned one-sided back-channel forms held at triadic Φ=2.0, does Thompson's sequential/reciprocal
interdependence label track the recipient party's mediator-severance template, or does the uniform triadic
verdict collapse interdependence typing once the back-channel is present?

## Thompson interdependence and coordination demand

Thompson (1967) distinguished pooled, sequential, and reciprocal interdependence as escalating patterns of
coordination demand among interdependent tasks. Malone and Crowston (1994) reframed coordination as a
cross-disciplinary study of dependencies among activities, aligning with the lab's Boolean coordination
forms. Q43 (#135–#139) operationalized Thompson's types as small n=3 AND-family dynamical systems and
tested them against the IIT-4.0 dyadic/triadic verdict. Sequential interdependence split on the return
path: a propagating mediator chain reads triadic while an acyclic hand-off reads dyadic. Reciprocal
interdependence required a closed feedback cycle, not bidirectional labels alone. The naive Thompson
ordering broke because sequential and reciprocal both reached Φ=2.0 at n=3.

## Back-channel panel and uniform triadic ceiling

The Q51–Q59 thread adds a single cross-edge between outer parties on implication-commit forms. Q54 (#173)
showed one-sided bijective parity forms reach Φ=2.0 only under commit-topology alignment. Q59 (#198, #199)
fixed recipient-class mediator-severance templates: two mediator-incident edges in the recipient-only cut
difference, with worker and counterpart templates stable under XOR and XNOR. Every aligned one-sided ceiling
pair reads triadic at Φ=2.0, so the verdict no longer separates Thompson types the way Q43's baseline triple
did. The open question is whether return-path sequential/reciprocal typing still tracks recipient-class
partition geometry or collapses with the uniform verdict.

## IIT-4.0 verdict and partition templates

IIT 4.0 classifies a system's integrated information at the minimum-information partition (MIP)
(`albantakis2023iit4`; `mayner2018pyphi`). The binary dyadic/triadic verdict is read from whether Φ_MIP
exceeds the zero threshold; Φ magnitude is treated as ordinal only. Q57–Q59 showed that once triadic, MIP
tie-breaking follows recipient-specific cut geometry rather than outer-party connectivity symmetry. The
present study asks whether Thompson's sequential/reciprocal label aligns with that recipient geometry or
with the collapsed verdict.

## Pre-registration and validation gap

Hypotheses and decision rules are committed before probe code (`brodeur2024preregistration`;
`chamberlin1890multiple`). Results apply to deterministic n=3 Boolean models at finest grain with synchronous
update. Thompson typing follows Q43 return-path conventions; mediator templates follow Q59 edge inventories.
Real organizations remain beyond reach.
