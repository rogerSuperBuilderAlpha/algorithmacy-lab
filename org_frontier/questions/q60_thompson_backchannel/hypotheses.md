# Q60 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Five working hypotheses reconnecting Q43 Thompson interdependence typing to the Q59 back-channel
mediator-severance templates. Written and committed before any test runs.

## H1 — Return-path typing tracks recipient
- **Claim:** Q43's return-path operationalization for sequential vs reciprocal interdependence — sequential
  when the non-recipient outer party reads the mediator but not the cross-party edge directly, reciprocal
  when the recipient outer party reads the cross-party edge directly — perfectly predicts back-channel
  recipient class (worker W vs counterpart C) on the sixteen aligned one-sided ceiling pairs.
- **H0:** Return-path Thompson typing misclassifies at least one pair's recipient party.
- **Predicted outcome:** 8/8 worker-recipient pairs read sequential; 8/8 counterpart-recipient pairs read
  reciprocal; 0 mismatches.

## H2 — Uniform triadic verdict collapses ordering
- **Claim:** Every form in the panel reads triadic at max_phi=2.0, so the IIT verdict gives no
  sequential-vs-reciprocal discrimination even though Q43's naive Thompson ordering requires sequential
  below reciprocal in Φ.
- **H0:** At least one pair reads dyadic or carries max_phi ≠ 2.0.
- **Predicted outcome:** 16/16 triadic at max_phi=2.0; Thompson Φ-ordering step collapses across the panel.

## H3 — Feedback-cycle predicate fails recipient split
- **Claim:** Q43's connectivity-matrix feedback-cycle predicate assigns every back-channel ceiling form
  reciprocal, so cycle presence alone cannot track the worker/counterpart recipient halves that Q59 #198
  separates by mediator-severance template.
- **H0:** The cycle predicate splits the panel into both sequential and reciprocal classes.
- **Predicted outcome:** 16/16 cycle=True; 0/16 sequential by cycle predicate; recipient split absent.

## H4 — Thompson type tracks mediator-severance template
- **Claim:** Return-path sequential/reciprocal label aligns with Q59 recipient edge templates: sequential
  forms carry the worker template {S→W, W→C, W→S} vs {C→S}; reciprocal forms carry the counterpart
  template {C→S, C→W, S→C} vs {W→S}.
- **H0:** At least one pair's Thompson type disagrees with its recipient-class template.
- **Predicted outcome:** 16/16 template matches; sequential/reciprocal and worker/counterpart templates
  are the same partition.

## H5 — No Φ discrimination between Thompson subpanels
- **Claim:** Sequential and reciprocal subpanels carry identical structure and max_phi on every pair, so
  interdependence typing survives only at the partition-template level, not at the verdict or Φ level.
- **H0:** The two subpanels differ in structure or max_phi on at least one pair.
- **Predicted outcome:** All eight sequential and all eight reciprocal rows read triadic at max_phi=2.0;
  zero spread within or between subpanels.
