# Q66 — Stage 2 deep research: the boundary of the irreducible core

This study refines Q65 and draws on the same literatures (shared `references.bib`). The specific question,
where the irreducible core of a chain of mediators sits, is internal to the integrated-information
framework, so the relevant prior work is the structure of complexes and the lab's own findings.

## Complexes and the maximal complex

Integrated information theory identifies the system that exists as the maximal complex, the subset of
elements whose integrated information is locally maximal [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4]. A
larger wired structure can contain a smaller maximal complex, with the remaining elements feeding it
without belonging to it. PyPhi computes the maximal complex exactly for small systems [mayner2018pyphi].
Q65 found exactly this in an agent chain: the wired chain is four elements, but the maximal complex is the
recipient-facing pair.

## Coordination, mediation, and feedback

Coordination theory treats chained mediators as nested coordination [malone1994interdisciplinary]. The
lab's program adds that a directed path through mediators behaves differently from a closed feedback loop:
the verdict lives in the causal structure, where a closed loop binds elements that an open path does not.
This study tests that distinction directly by closing the agent chain into a ring. The signaling and
AI-mediated-communication framing carries over from Q63–Q65 [spence1973job; hancock2020aimc;
jakesch2019aimc; rao2012economics], with the structural reading by exact Φ.

## The open gap

Q65 localized the core of one chain depth. Whether the localization holds across depth, whether the core
stays a two-element recipient-facing pair as agents are added, and whether closing the chain into a loop
enlarges the core, are uncomputed. The gap is open: map the core boundary across depth and under loop
closure. In-silico, with the validation gap [axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before computing
[chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
