# Q113 literature — substitutability and value

## Substitutability in the structure and in value

Finding 5 makes substitutability fatal to the verdict: an interchangeable party collapses the triad. Q111
gave a value measure, the Shapley value of subsystem-Φ. Putting the two together asks what substitutability
does to value, not just to the verdict. Because the substitutable market is dyadic, its total value is zero,
so substitutability does not lower a party's share within a productive coordination; it removes the
coordination's value entirely.

## The expected contrast

The all-required market is symmetric — every agent is equally needed — so its value should distribute
equally among the parties, unlike the mediated triad of Q111 where one party was uniquely essential and
captured the most. The substitutable market should capture nothing. The lesson would be that value
concentrates only under asymmetric essentiality, and that interchangeability is not a discount on a party's
value but a destruction of all of it: a platform that makes its agents replaceable zeroes the rent for
everyone, including itself.
