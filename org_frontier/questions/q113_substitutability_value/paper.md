# Q113 — Substitutability destroys value; a required set shares it

## Question

Finding 5 makes substitutability fatal to the verdict. Q111 gave a value measure. This study asks what
substitutability does to value (lower a party's share, or remove the coordination's value entirely), and
whether an all-required market distributes value as the mediated triad did or differently.

## Method

The Q85 agent market at N = 2 in two configurations: all-required (triadic, Φ = 4) and substitutable
(dyadic, Φ = 0). For each, the Shapley value of every party is computed with the coalition value the
subsystem's Φ.

## Results

The all-required market distributes its Φ of 4 equally: each of the four parties has Shapley value 1.0. The
substitutable market captures zero: every party's value is zero. The value lost in substitution is the
entire Φ of 4.

| market | Φ | Shapley | total |
|---|---|---|---|
| all-required | 4.0 | 1, 1, 1, 1 | 4.0 |
| substitutable | 0.0 | 0, 0, 0, 0 | 0.0 |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 all-required captures value | confirmed |
| H2 substitutable captures zero | confirmed |
| H3 required parties share equally | confirmed |
| H4 substitutability destroys the whole value | confirmed |

## Interpretation

Value concentrates under asymmetric essentiality and distributes under symmetric. The mediated triad of
Q111 had one uniquely essential party, and it captured two-thirds. The all-required market has four equally
essential parties, and each captures a quarter. The Shapley value tracks essentiality, so the shape of the
value distribution is the shape of who cannot be done without: a hub concentrates it, an all-required set
shares it. A coordination's political economy is set by whether one party is essential or all are.

Substitutability is destruction, not discount. The substitutable market captures nothing, because being
dyadic it has no integrated information to value. The value lost in making the agents interchangeable is the
whole market's worth, borne by every party. A worker and a counterpart who would each have a quarter of a
market worth four are left with nothing once the agents between them become substitutable, though they
themselves were never replaceable. Interchangeability does not move value from the replaceable to the rest;
it eliminates the coordination, and the value goes to zero for all.

The reading for platform strategy is direct. Making agents substitutable, the standard move to discipline a
workforce or cut its rents, does not redirect the agents' value to the platform. It dissolves the
coordination that produced the value, and the platform's own share falls to zero with everyone's. A
required set, where no party can be replaced, is the configuration that sustains value, and the value it
sustains is shared among its members. The pursuit of replaceability, taken to its end, is the pursuit of a
worthless coordination.

## Limitations

In-silico; exact subsystem Φ and Shapley value. Two market configurations at N = 2; the mixed market and
larger N are not tested, though Finding 5 makes any substitutable pair dyadic, so the zero-value result
should hold wherever substitutability appears. The coalition value is the subsystem Φ at the all-ones state.
