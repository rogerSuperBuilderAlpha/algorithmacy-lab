# Q113 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on substitutability and value. Q111 valued each party by its Shapley share of the
coordination's integrated information, and found the value concentrated at the mediator of a triad. This
applies the measure to a market of agents: an all-required market against a substitutable one. Finding 5
makes the substitutable market dyadic. Written and committed before the run; the instrument was validated.

## H1 — The all-required market captures value

- **Claim:** The all-required market's Shapley values sum to a positive total (its Φ).
- **H0:** They sum to zero.
- **Predicted outcome:** positive. H0 refuted.

## H2 — The substitutable market captures zero value

- **Claim:** The substitutable market's Shapley values sum to zero.
- **H0:** They sum to a positive total.
- **Predicted outcome:** zero. H0 refuted. The dyadic market has no value to distribute.

## H3 — The required parties share value equally

- **Claim:** In the all-required market, the parties have equal Shapley value.
- **H0:** Their values are unequal.
- **Predicted outcome:** equal. H0 refuted. A symmetric required set distributes value egalitarianly, in
  contrast to the mediated triad of Q111, where the mediator concentrated it.

## H4 — Substitutability destroys the whole value

- **Claim:** The value lost in making the agents substitutable equals the all-required market's Φ — the
  entire coordination's value, not just the substitutable agents' share.
- **H0:** The loss is only the agents' share.
- **Predicted outcome:** the loss is the whole value. H0 refuted. Substitutability is not a redistribution
  but a destruction: making any agent interchangeable zeroes everyone's value.
