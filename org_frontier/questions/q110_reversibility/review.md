# Q110 — Stage 1 review: reversibility of the boundary

## The question

Q93 read the collapse side of the dyad/triad boundary and Q105 the build side. Hamming distance is
symmetric form to form, so the boundary's shape is distributional: are triads as far from dyads as dyads are
from triads, or is the boundary thin from one side and thicker from the other? This closes the design wave.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **A triad is one edit from collapse (Q93).** The fragility margin is near-binary across the triadic set.
- **Building a triad costs 1 to 3 edits (Q105).** The construction distance varies, with most dyadic forms
  one or two edits from a triad.
- **Triadic forms are the 9.4% minority (corpus census).** Their rarity should make every triad adjacent to
  a dyad while leaving many dyads several edits from a triad.

## The gap

No study compares the fragility margin and the construction distance directly. Whether the boundary is
symmetric, or cheaper to cross toward the dyad than toward the triad, is uncomputed.
