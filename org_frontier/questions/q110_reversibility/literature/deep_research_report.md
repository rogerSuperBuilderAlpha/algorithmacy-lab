# Q110 literature — the symmetry of the boundary

## The two sides

Q93 read the collapse side of the dyad/triad boundary: a triad is one bit-flip from dyadic. Q105 read the
build side: a dyadic form is some edits from triadic, and the distance varies. Hamming distance is symmetric
form to form, so a triad one edit from a dyad means that dyad is one edit from a triad. The boundary's shape
is therefore a distributional question: how the fragility margins of the triadic forms compare to the
construction distances of the dyadic forms.

## The expected asymmetry

Triadic forms are rare — the 9.4% census — and sit among dyadic ones, so every triad should have a dyad one
edit away, and the fragility margin should be uniformly 1. Dyadic forms are the majority and are not all
adjacent to the rare triads, so the construction distance should vary, with some dyads two or three edits
from any triad. The boundary would then be thin from the triad side and thicker from the dyad side, and the
mean cost of building a triad would exceed the mean cost of breaking one. The design reading is that
coordination is easy to break and harder to build, which the wave's earlier results — every edge
load-bearing (Q104), repair lever-specific (Q107) — already suggest.

## Method context

Both distances are Hamming distances over the 8-bit encoding, between the triadic and dyadic forms the
verdict identifies.
