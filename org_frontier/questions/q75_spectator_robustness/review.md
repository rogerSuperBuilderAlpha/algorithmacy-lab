# Q75 — Stage 1 review: spectator robustness of the core

## The question

Q74 stated the rule for which verdict to report when the whole system and the maximal complex diverge: a
non-bidirectionally-coupled excluded element is a spectator, and the maximal-complex reading governs. This
study tests the robustness of that core. If genuine spectators are added to a triadic form, does the
maximal complex stay fixed, or can a spectator perturb it? A stable core under spectator addition is what
makes the major-complex reading trustworthy.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Excluded elements that are non-bidirectionally coupled stay out (Q74, Finding 8).** Read-only and
  emit-only elements are spectators. This study confirms they stay out as more are added.
- **The read-recipient triad is triadic at Φ=2.0 with core {E, M, R} (Q63).** The baseline whose
  robustness is tested.
- **Spectators do not change the verdict in single cases (Q63 H3 disclosure, Q68 monitoring).** This study
  scales to several spectators and several coupling classes at once.

## The gap

Q74 classified the divergence; it did not test whether the core is stable as spectators accumulate. Whether
the maximal complex stays {E, M, R} at Φ=2.0 when one, two, or three uncoupled, read-only, or emit-only
parties are added is uncomputed. No prior probe pins this, so the question is open.
