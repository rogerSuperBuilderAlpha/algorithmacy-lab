# Q75 — Stage 2 deep research: stability of the maximal complex

This study tests robustness of the maximal complex and draws on the same literatures (shared
`references.bib`).

## Spectators and the system that exists

Integrated information theory holds that the maximal complex is the system that exists, with surrounding
elements feeding it without belonging [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4; mayner2018pyphi]. A
robust maximal complex is one that does not shift when non-member elements are added: idle parties,
observers that read without being read, or constant sources that feed without reading. The lab's
core-membership finding (Finding 8) predicts these stay out, and Q74 confirmed it case by case; the
question here is stability as such elements accumulate.

## Why robustness matters for the application

Real coordination is surrounded by parties that watch or feed without participating: regulators that
monitor, logs that record, sources that supply context. If the irreducible core shifted whenever such a
party were added, the verdict would be fragile. The coordination framing carries from Q63-Q74
[malone1994interdisciplinary; rao2012economics; hancock2020aimc].

## The open gap

Q74 stated the rule; its stability under accumulating spectators is uncomputed. The gap is open: add
spectators of each coupling class to the triad and confirm the core holds. In-silico, with the validation
gap [axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong;
brodeur2024preregistration].
