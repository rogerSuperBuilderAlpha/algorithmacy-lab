# Q99 findings — the integration lives in a joint mechanism, but the binder is not always one party

Two hypotheses confirmed, two refuted. The cause-effect structure gives the verdict a face: in every triad
the integration lives in a joint mechanism, the whole carrying the system Φ. But the dual pair of a single
party binding a group is the signature of mediated triads only; chains, rings, and markets are triadic
through structures with no single binder. The structure reveals a taxonomy of triads the scalar verdict
collapses into one.

## What it says

The universal cause-effect signature of a triad is a joint mechanism that carries the integration. In every
triadic form, the maximal-φ_d distinction is a higher-order mechanism, a group of two or more parties,
and its φ_d equals the system Φ (H2). The integration lives in a whole, never in a single party. This is
the structural face of irreducibility, and it holds across joint determination, depth, and a market. The
dyadic forms have no such distinction: their maximal mechanism is not a joint one carrying Φ, and the
broadcast and one_shot have no structure at all.

The dual pair of a single party binding a group is not universal. The read-recipient triad and the
mediator-led market carry it: a spanning distinction whose mechanism is the mediator and whose purview is
the others, paired with a joint distinction whose mechanism is the others and whose purview is the mediator.
But the chain, the ring, and the required market do not (H1 refuted). The structure gives the verdict a face,
and the face is not always a single binder.

The cause-effect structure reveals a taxonomy of triads that the scalar verdict hides. Three shapes appear.
A mediated triad has a single party binding a group, the spanning/joint dual. A symmetric triad has two
groups binding each other: the ring's structure is {E, A2} ↔ {A1, R} and the market's is {E, C} ↔ {M1, M2},
two joint mechanisms each the other's purview, with no single binder. A distributed triad, the chain, has
overlapping joint distinctions ({E, A2} → {A1}, {A1, R} → {A2}) that bind no single global dual. The binary
verdict calls all three triadic; the structure tells them apart.

The dual pair, where it exists, does not span the whole form (H4 refuted). In the k=2 market the dual is the
mediator against the worker and both recipients, which does cover the parties; but the chain, ring, and
market that carry no single-party dual leave H4 false by construction. The result is that the dual pair is a
mediated-coordination signature, not a complete account of every triad's structure.

## What this opens

The finding launches the cause-effect-structure wave with a concrete payload: the verdict hides at least
three structural kinds of triad. Q100 asks whether these structures fingerprint the five pillars; this study
already shows the fingerprint exists, separating mediated from symmetric from distributed coordination.

## Caveats

- **Mixed result.** H2 and H3 confirmed; H1 and H4 refuted, the refutations revealing the taxonomy.
- **Curated set.** Five triadic and three dyadic forms, chosen to span topologies. The taxonomy is
  demonstrated on these, not proven exhaustive.
- **One state.** The structure is read at the integrating state; a fuller account would read it across
  states. A dyadic form can carry stray higher-order distinctions (substitutable's "distributed" label)
  without a dual pair or a Φ-carrying joint mechanism, so the triadic signature is the dual plus H2, not any
  higher-order distinction alone.
- **In-silico.** Boolean models, exact cause-effect structure.
