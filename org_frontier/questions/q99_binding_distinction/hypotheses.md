# Q99 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on the cause-effect structure of coordination, read for its dual binding pair. The
read-recipient triad's structure carries a spanning distinction (a single party's mechanism with a
multi-party purview) and a joint distinction (a multi-party mechanism with that party's purview), each the
other's dual. This study asks whether that dual pair is the common shape of irreducible coordination across
structurally different triads. Written and committed before the chain, ring, market, and substitutable
forms are read. The read-recipient triad (dual present) and the broadcast (no structure) are the
instrument control. The cause-effect structure is read once at the integrating state.

## H1 — Every triad carries the dual binding pair

- **Claim:** Each triadic form — joint determination, a chain, a ring, a required market — has a dual pair:
  a single-party spanning distinction and a higher-order joint distinction over each other's purviews.
- **H0:** Some triadic form lacks the dual pair.
- **Predicted outcome:** all carry it. H0 refuted. The dual pair is the structural face of irreducibility,
  the same shape across different topologies. This is the study's central and genuinely uncertain claim;
  a ring or market could carry a different structure that no single dual pair captures.

## H2 — The integration lives in a joint mechanism

- **Claim:** In every triadic form, the maximal-φ_d distinction is a higher-order (multi-party) mechanism,
  and its φ_d equals the system Φ.
- **H0:** Some triadic form's maximal distinction is a single party, or its φ_d differs from the system Φ.
- **Predicted outcome:** the maximal distinction is joint, with φ_d = Φ. H0 refuted. The whole carries the
  integration, not a part.

## H3 — The dual pair is exclusive to triadic forms

- **Claim:** No dyadic form carries a dual pair.
- **H0:** Some dyadic form carries one.
- **Predicted outcome:** none do. H0 refuted.

## H4 — The dual pair spans the whole coordination

- **Claim:** In every triadic form, the mechanisms and purviews of the dual pair together cover every party
  of the form.
- **H0:** Some triadic form's dual pair leaves a party uncovered.
- **Predicted outcome:** the dual pair spans every party — the structure accounts for the whole core, not
  part of it. Genuinely uncertain for the larger forms, where the dual pair may cover only a sub-part.
