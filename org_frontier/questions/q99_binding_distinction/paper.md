# Q99 — The cause-effect structure of a triad: a joint mechanism, and a taxonomy of binders

## Question

The program has read coordination through the scalar verdict and through major-complex membership, and has
used the cause-effect structure only to count distinctions and relations as a predictor. This study reads
the structure as meaning. The read-recipient triad carries a spanning distinction — the mediator's mechanism
with the two outer parties as its purview, and a joint distinction (the outer parties' mechanism with the
mediator as its purview), the two being duals. The question is whether this dual pair is the common shape of
irreducible coordination across structurally different triads.

## Method

The cause-effect structure is read with PyPhi's `phi_structure` at the integrating state, for five triadic
forms spanning topologies (joint determination, a chain, a ring, a required market) and three dyadic
controls. For each, the distinctions are read as (mechanism, purview, φ_d), the dual pair is searched, and
the maximal distinction is checked for being a joint mechanism carrying the system Φ.

## Results

In every triadic form the maximal-φ_d distinction is a joint mechanism with φ_d equal to the system Φ (H2):
the integration lives in a whole. No dyadic form carries a dual pair (H3). But the single-party dual pair is
not universal: the read-recipient triad and the mediator-led market carry it, while the chain, ring, and
required market do not (H1 refuted), and the dual pair does not span every party of the larger forms (H4
refuted).

## Interpretation

The cause-effect structure gives the verdict a face, and the face is a joint mechanism. Every triad's
integration resides in a higher-order mechanism, a group of parties, whose φ_d is the system Φ. The
irreducible coordination is, structurally, a whole that specifies and is specified, and the scalar verdict
is the φ of that whole. This is what integrated information adds over a binary classifier: it names the
mechanism that carries the coordination, not just its presence.

The structure reveals a taxonomy of triads that the verdict collapses. A mediated triad has a single party
binding a group — the spanning distinction is a lone mediator, paired with the joint distinction of the
parties it binds. A symmetric triad has two groups binding each other: the ring's structure is {E, A2} ↔
{A1, R} and the market's is {E, C} ↔ {M1, M2}, two joint mechanisms each the other's purview, with no single
binder. A distributed triad, the chain, has overlapping joint distinctions that bind no single global dual.
The binary verdict calls all three triadic and cannot tell them apart; the structure does.

The mediated and symmetric shapes correspond to a real difference in coordination. A mediated triad has a
party through which the others coordinate, and that party is the spanning distinction. A symmetric triad has
no such party: the parties coordinate as two groups that mutually determine each other, the way a ring or a
required market does. The structure recovers the difference between coordination through a hub and
coordination among equals, which the verdict, reading only whether the form integrates, discards.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with the exact cause-effect structure. The set is five triadic and three dyadic
forms chosen to span topologies, so the taxonomy is demonstrated rather than proven exhaustive. The
structure is read at the integrating state; a fuller account would read it across states. A dyadic form can
carry stray higher-order distinctions without a dual pair or a Φ-carrying joint mechanism, so the triadic
signature is the dual together with the joint-mechanism result, not any higher-order distinction alone.
