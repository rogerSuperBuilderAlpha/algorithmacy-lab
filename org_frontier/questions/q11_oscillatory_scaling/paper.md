# Oscillatory coordination forms: a constant ring law, a period term, no triadic guarantee

<code + data: org_frontier/questions/q11_oscillatory_scaling/ ; probes #165–169 in probes/PROBES.md>

## Abstract

Coordination forms that settle onto a limit cycle are built at sizes n=3,4,5,6 and read with exact Φ_MIP: a ring with a rotating update (each node copies its left neighbor, a traveling wave of period n) and a mediated form with a periodic commit (a conjunctive hub that commits every p micro-steps). The rotating ring carries Φ_MIP=2.0 at every n with a triadic verdict and the full node set in the major complex, a constant law that the AND-neighbor ring's cap at Φ=4 for n≥4 separates from at every matched n (gap −2.0), placing the rotating ring as a fifth shape (H1, confirmed). Cycle period enters the law as a term: in the periodic-commit form Φ_MIP moves whenever only p changes at fixed n, from Φ=n−1 at p=1 to Φ=0 at p=2 and p=3 (Δ_p Φ = −2,−3,−4,−5 at n=3,4,5,6), a term the static fixed-point families lack (H2, confirmed). Distributing a parity flip around a ring breaks the parity hub's decaying law: the flip ring holds Φ=2.0 with a full core while the hub falls to 2^(2−n), and the gap to the floor widens (1.5, 1.75, 1.875, 1.9375) with no halving (H3, confirmed). The triadic-persistence claim fails: the rotating ring stays triadic with a full core, while the periodic-commit form at p=2 reads dyadic Φ=0 with the core shed to a single node at every n, so a built-to-cycle form can still factor (H4, refuted). The detectable band stays pinned to the parity of n: the grain at which the rotating ring flips to dyadic is g*=3,2,5,2 for n=3,4,5,6, tracking parity and ignoring the period (H5, refuted). Three of five fixed predictions confirm. An oscillatory attractor adds a Φ(n) law the fixed-point zoo lacked, period is a variable in it, and triadicity and a period-scaled grain band both fail to follow from cycling alone.

## Introduction

The probe zoo reads fixed-point coordination forms and reports their Φ(n) scaling laws by direct Φ_MIP computation: the conjunctive hub at Φ=n−1 (Probe 116), the pool at Φ=n(n−1), the parity hub decaying as Φ=2^(2−n) (#115/#120), and an AND-neighbor ring capped at Φ=4 for n≥4 (#132). These are properties of systems that hold a single state. The open question is whether a form that settles onto a limit cycle instead carries a Φ(n) law of a different shape, and whether the cycle period enters it.

The question matters for how a coordination form is characterized. A static law fixes Φ from n and topology alone. If a sustained cycle adds a term that depends on how many steps the global state takes to return, then the timing of a coordinated process sets its irreducibility alongside its membership and wiring. That would place period beside size as a variable the analyst reads, and it would separate forms that look identical at a single transition but cycle at different rates.

The Stage 2 gap was concrete. The zoo had four fixed-point laws and treated the cycle period as a sampling caveat (#32, #60), so it stayed outside Φ(n). No prior probe computed Φ_MIP on a limit-cycle form across n and read off a law, and none tested whether period is a term. Whether the four laws are complete, and whether an oscillatory attractor adds a fifth, stood open.

## Related work

No surveyed source computes Φ_MIP on a limit-cycle coordination form across n=3,4,5,6 and reads off a scaling law to compare against the fixed-point families. The literature supplies the pieces and points in a definite direction, and leaves the computation undone.

Attractor-based IIT admits periodic orbits as Φ-carriers in principle. A dynamical-systems recasting of integrated information over the global attractor, the Informational Structure, builds its invariant sets from stationary points, periodic orbits, or chaotic sets on the same footing [esteban2018informational; kalita2019informational]. The warrant is never cashed out. Every explicit Φ calculation in that framework runs on equilibrium attractors, and the periodic case is flagged as admissible and left undeveloped; the framework derives no Φ(N) law and tests only systems of about 2–5 nodes [esteban2018informational]. A parallel continuous-dynamics proposal indexes integrated information by the topological dimensionality of a reconstructed shared attractor and contains no treatment of periodic orbits at all [kitazono2017dimensionality].

The empirical-Φ oscillator results show a distinct oscillatory signature with a different measure. In coupled Kuramoto oscillators Φ_R is near zero for desynchronized and for fully synchronized regimes and peaks only in the metastable transition region, so oscillatory metastable dynamics are where Φ is nonzero [mediano2021common; mediano2016metastability]. Two cautions keep this from answering the question. It is shown for one large network of N=8 communities, leaving the Φ(n) law across small n unmeasured, and it uses Φ_R, a time-series measure in a different framework than the geometric Φ_MIP of the zoo [mediano2021common]. The timescale result is the most direct evidence on the period question. Φ_R is an explicit function of an integration timescale τ and grows with it, and the thermodynamic-limit work generalizes IIT's single-step effect to ϕ(τ) over an arbitrary number of updates [mediano2021common; aguilera2019thermodynamic]. Both tie the value to the temporal span, the analogue of cycle period. Neither computes Φ as a function of an actual limit-cycle period.

The fixed-point side carries its own non-trivial scaling. In kinetic Ising models with a mean-field approximation, integrated information diverges in the thermodynamic limit at certain critical points instead of converging to a cap, and the divergence is metric-dependent between Wasserstein and KL distance [aguilera2019thermodynamic; aguilera2019scaling]. Fixed-point attractor states themselves suppress Φ: in Hopfield networks each element independently rules out most prior states and the interactions add little [balduzzi2008framework]. This is a structural reason to expect a limit cycle, where no single state is held, to behave differently, and it is a hypothesis to test rather than a result on cycle scaling.

The construction side has direct mathematical support. In asynchronous random Boolean networks, rhythmic behavior originates from a closed-ring core that supports a traveling wave, and the ring size corresponds to the period [rohlfshagen2004circular]. A unidirectionally coupled ring of delayed oscillators supports rotating-wave solutions in which each node is a fixed phase-lag copy of one common periodic profile, and the ring-closure condition forces an integer winding relation T₂·N=nP among ring size, per-node lag, and period [kashchenko2023travelling]. The period is tied to size through an integer constraint. The strict reading is a commensurability rather than a clean period∝n law, since P and T₂ are themselves parameters.

The remaining sources do not touch the question. A random-search study optimizing Φ over transition matrices as node count grows makes no fixed-point-versus-limit-cycle distinction [cardillo2022optimizing]. The standard critiques treat Φ as a static state-discrimination property and contain no oscillatory content [cerullo2015problem]. The foundational measure is fixed: Φ compares whole-system effective information against its parts under the minimum information partition, presupposing discrete states and Markovian discrete-time dynamics, so a continuous limit cycle has to be discretized first, and the graining sets both the verdict and how period appears [balduzzi2008framework; barrett2019notwelldefined].

## Hypotheses

Five hypotheses were fixed before any computation, each with the structural prior its null rests on. The verdict is the dyadic/triadic structure read off Φ_MIP over the most-integrated reachable state, with PHI_EPS=1e-9 the zero threshold and TOL=1e-6 the numerical tolerance; Φ magnitude serves as an ordinal hint.

**H1: a fifth ring law.** The rotating-update ring carries a Φ(n) law separable from the AND-neighbor ring's cap, so over n=3,4,5,6 the two come apart at matched n. A traveling wave keeps every node informative about the global phase at all times, where the AND-collapse holds one fixed state. The null reproduces the cap: closing a chain into a loop and adding rotation changes the attractor type while leaving the integration the loop carries intact, so Φ_MIP settles to Φ=4 for n≥4 and the zoo's four laws are complete. Confirmation requires Φ_rot(n)≠4 at some n≥4 and Φ_rot(n)≠Φ_and(n) at some matched n, both beyond TOL.

**H2: period as a term.** Φ_MIP for the limit-cycle family depends on the attractor period p as well as on n. Holding n fixed and varying the commit cadence p in the periodic-commit form moves Φ_MIP, a term the static fixed-point families lack. The null reads grain-1 Φ as blind to how many steps the global state takes to return, so the period stays a sampling caveat outside Φ(n) and two forms with the same n and different p take the same Φ. Confirmation requires |Δ_p Φ| > TOL at some n.

**H3: distributed flip breaks the decay.** A ring of per-node flips, each node reading only its left neighbor with no shared hub, escapes the parity hub's decaying law Φ=2^(2−n). Distributing the flip keeps Φ bounded away from the parity floor where the hub has already fallen below it. The null makes the flip rule set the law wherever it sits, so a ring of flips decays the same way the XOR hub does and the two stay law-equivalent. Confirmation requires Φ_flip(5) and Φ_flip(6) above the floor by more than TOL, a constant law class, and no per-step halving.

**H4: triadic across n.** Both cyclers, the rotating ring and the periodic-commit form at p=2, return a triadic verdict with the full node set in the major complex at every n=3,4,5,6, even though cycling fails as a general predicate for triadicity (#130). A sustained cycle holds no single state and keeps the joint determination irreducible. The null has the constructed cyclers behave like the general population: at least one reads dyadic at some n, or its major complex sheds nodes. Refutation requires a dyadic verdict or a shed core at some n for either form.

**H5: period-scaled grain band.** Reading the rotating ring at a coarser temporal grain flips its verdict to dyadic only near grain ≈ p, and since p=n the n=6 ring tolerates a coarser grain than the n=3 ring before collapsing. The grain-flip threshold g*(n) scales with period and floats above k=2. The null fixes the threshold near grain 2 regardless of period, so building a longer cycle leaves the detectable band where it was. Confirmation requires g*(n) non-decreasing in n with g*(6) > g*(3).

## Methods

The forms are built as discrete TPMs at grain 1. The rotating ring rot_ring(n) gives each node the value of its left neighbor, x_k' = x_{k−1}, a pure rotation whose synchronous attractor period is exactly n. The periodic-commit form commit_ring(n,p) has a mediator node 0 commit the conjunction of the members and hold that commit for p micro-steps, built with the hold_k_tpm pattern at s_idx=0, k=p, then composed into a single (tpm, cm) pair. The flip ring flip_ring(n) gives each node the negation of its left neighbor, x_k' = ¬x_{k−1}, the distributed parity construction. Baselines are the AND-neighbor ring and_ring(n) (#132), the conjunctive hub, and the parity hub (#115/#120). Φ_MIP, the verdict, the MIP location, and the major complex are read by the org_frontier classifier and probe library; the major complex over reachable states by `pyphi.compute.major_complex()`. The attractor period is the length of the longest reachable synchronous cycle.

The decision rules were fixed before any run. H1: Φ_rot(n)≠4 at some n≥4 and Φ_rot(n)≠Φ_and(n) at some matched n, both beyond TOL. H2: |Δ_p Φ| > TOL at some n in the commit-cadence sweep p∈{1,2,3}; the rotating ring carries a corroborant sub-sweep over period p=n. H3: Φ_flip above the floor 2^(2−n) at n=5 and n=6, a non-decay law class, and step ratios above 0.5. H4: a dyadic verdict or a shed core at some n refutes. H5: g*(n), the smallest grain reading dyadic, non-decreasing in n with g*(6) > g*(3).

The instrument controls ran first and passed across all five probes. The strict-mediation triad reads triadic Φ_MIP=2.000000 with MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`. The AND-neighbor ring reads and_ring(3)=6.000000 (period 1, full core) and and_ring(4)=4.000000 (period 2, full core), the #132 cap, and matches its baseline Φ=4.0 at n=5,6. The parity hub reads parity_hub(5)=0.125000 with a full 5-node core, the #115/#120 decay value. A form whose control endpoint failed to reproduce would have had its swept values discarded unread.

## Results

**H1, confirmed.** The rotating ring traces a constant law distinct from the cap. rot_ring(n) reads Φ_MIP=2.000000 at n=3,4,5,6, triadic at every n, with the full node set in the major complex (core sizes 3,4,5,6) and synchronous period exactly n (3,4,5,6). The AND-neighbor ring sits at its #132 cap, and_ring=4.000000 at n=4,5,6 with periods 2,1,2. The per-n gap Φ_rot(n)−Φ_and(n) = −2.000000 at n=4, n=5, and n=6. Both decision clauses fire: Φ_rot(n)≠4 at some n≥4 beyond TOL is true, and Φ_rot(n)≠Φ_and(n) at some matched n beyond TOL is true. The rotating ring is a fifth shape, a constant Φ=2 ring separate from the capped Φ=4 ring at matched n (results/q11_rotating_law.csv, probe_probe_q11_rotating_law.py).

**H2, confirmed.** Period enters the law as a term. In the periodic-commit form, holding n fixed and moving only the commit cadence p moves Φ_MIP at every n. At p=1 the form reduces to the no-stretch synchronous conjunctive hub and reads triadic with Φ=n−1: Φ=2.000000 at n=3 (MIP {x0x1,x2}), Φ=3.000000 at n=4, Φ=4.000000 at n=5, Φ=5.000000 at n=6, each with a full core and period 2. At p=2 and p=3 the form reads dyadic Φ=0.000000 with period 1. The within-n move Δ_p Φ = Φ(p=3)−Φ(p=1) is −2.000000 at n=3, −3.000000 at n=4, −4.000000 at n=5, and −5.000000 at n=6, so |Δ_p Φ| > TOL at every n. The corroborant sub-sweep on the rotating ring is flat, Φ=2.000000 at n=3,4,5,6 with a full core at each, consistent with H1's constant ring and adding no corroboration; the decision rests on the commit-cadence sweep, which confirms (results/q11_h2_commit_ring_period_sweep.csv, results/q11_h2_rot_ring_corroborant.csv, probe_probe_q11_period_term.py).

**H3, confirmed.** Distributing the flip breaks the decay. flip_ring(n) reads Φ_flip=2.000000 at n=3,4,5,6 with a full core at every n (core sizes 3,4,5,6), a constant law class. The parity hub falls along its floor 2^(2−n) = 0.5, 0.25, 0.125, 0.0625. The gaps Φ_flip(n)−2^(2−n) = 1.500000, 1.750000, 1.875000, 1.937500 widen with n, and the step ratios Φ_flip(n+1)/Φ_flip(n) = 1.0 at every step show no halving. The decision checks fire: Φ_flip(5) and Φ_flip(6) sit above the floor beyond TOL, the law class holds constant, and the flip ring holds level. The flip ring's measured synchronous attractor period is 6,4,10,6 at n=3,4,5,6, the longest reachable cycle, which is 2n for odd n and n for even n and departs from the nominal 2n at n=4 and n=6; this is reported as measured and leaves the Φ-based decision untouched (results/q11_flip_ring_decay.csv, probe_probe_q11_flip_ring_decay.py).

**H4, refuted.** Cycling leaves triadicity unsecured. The rotating ring confirms its half: rot_ring(n) reads triadic Φ_MIP=2.000000 with a full core at n=3 (x0,x1,x2), n=4 (x0..x3), and n=5 (x0..x4). The periodic-commit form at p=2 fails it. commit_ring(n,2) reads dyadic Φ_MIP=0.000000 with the core shed to {x0} alone, size 1 of n, at n=3,4,5, with the MIP factoring. The mechanism is a collapse: with cadence p=2 the composed hold-k map reduces to copying x0 to every node, so the next-state connectivity has only x0 as a parent (CM `[[1,1,1],[0,0,0],[0,0,0]]` at n=3), a reducible map. The same form is triadic at p=1 (Φ=2.0) and dyadic at p=2 and p=3 (Φ=0.0). A dyadic verdict and a shed core appear at n=3 already, so n=6 cannot change the verdict. A built-to-cycle form can read dyadic across n (probe_probe_q11_triadic_persistence.py).

**H5, refuted.** The detectable band holds its width as period grows. Sweeping the observation grain g=1..n+1 on rot_ring(n) at period p=n, the smallest grain reading dyadic (Φ_MIP ≤ PHI_EPS) is g*=3 at n=3, g*=2 at n=4, g*=5 at n=5, and g*=2 at n=6, the sequence g*=[3,2,5,2]. At grain 1 every ring is triadic Φ_MIP=2.000000, the genuine-crossing baseline. The decision rule fails on both counts: g*(n) falls as well as rises, and g*(6)=2 < g*(3)=3, so a longer period leaves the flip point where it was. The structure is parity of n: odd n (3,5) survive to the full period g=p, even n (4,6) factor as early as g=2. The ring flips, so it falls outside the never-flips branch, and its flip location follows the parity of n (results/q11_grain_band.csv, probe_probe_q11_grain_band.py).

## Discussion

The five results draw one line. An oscillatory attractor adds a Φ(n) law beyond the four the fixed-point zoo held, and period is a variable in it, while triadicity and a period-scaled grain band both fail to follow from cycling alone.

Two results establish the new law and its term. The rotating ring is a fifth shape, constant at Φ=2 across n, separable from the capped Φ=4 ring at every matched size (H1). And period is a term: the periodic-commit form moves Φ from n−1 at p=1 to 0 at p≥2 when only the commit cadence changes, the τ/update-count dependence the oscillator literature found with a time-series measure [mediano2021common; aguilera2019thermodynamic] realized here as an actual cadence in a geometric Φ_MIP law (H2). The flip ring closes the separation from the other side: distributing the parity flip around a loop drops the parity hub's decay, holding Φ=2 with a full core while the hub falls to 2^(2−n), so the decay belongs to the single shared XOR commit and disappears once the flip is spread around the loop (H3). These three confirm that the construction rule, more than the loop topology, sets which law a ring carries.

The two refutations bound the claim. A built cycle leaves the triadic verdict open: the rotating ring is triadic with a full core, while the periodic-commit form at p=2 collapses to copying one node and reads dyadic with a shed {x0} core at every n, so a form built to cycle can still factor (H4). This tracks #130, where no attractor predicate separates the verdict. The grain band, in turn, stays flat as period grows. The rotating ring flips at g*=[3,2,5,2], a parity-of-n effect with no growth in p (H5). The same flat Φ=2 reading that makes H1 clean hides the period-scaling of H5: a pure rotation is constant in both n and p, so the period dependence H2 reports comes from the mediated commit form, where p is set independently of member count.

## Limitations

The verdict is grain- and schedule-relative, and H5 makes that relativity concrete: the rotating ring reads triadic at grain 1 and dyadic at grains tied to the parity of n, so the dyadic/triadic call depends on the observation grain. Φ magnitude carries no absolute scale and serves as an ordinal hint. The sizes stay small, n=3 to 6, and the cadence sweep runs p=1,2,3, so longer cycles and larger forms stay untested. The period term is shown for the periodic-commit mediated form, where the commit cadence is set independently of member count; the rotating ring is flat in both n and p, so whether other ring constructions carry a period term is not settled here. The flip ring's measured attractor period departs from the nominal 2n at even n, reported as measured. The forms are constructed to integrate or to commit, so the laws are properties of these constructions and do not generalize to arbitrary cyclers. The corpus carries no measured worker. The probe explores irreducibility on built limit-cycle forms; whether any field coordination form carries the Φ these constructions do stays open.
