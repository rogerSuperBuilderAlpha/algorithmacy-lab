## What it says

Breadth does not dilute an all-binding outreach campaign. When the agent's commit jointly determines a
message from the sender and every recipient, the form stays triadic at Φ = n−1 as recipients are added
from one to four, the conjunctive-hub law holding in the outreach frame, and every recipient sits in the
irreducible core. Two ways of scaling outreach factor at every breadth: a pooled broadcast that ignores
recipients, and a substitutable campaign where any recipient can be swapped. A single substitutable
recipient inside an otherwise all-binding campaign collapses the whole form. Breadth itself carries no
verdict; the joint determination does.

## Caveats

- **Confirmatory.** The five predictions followed from the conjunctive-hub law (probe 116) and the
  substitutability finding applied to multi-recipient outreach; none was refuted. The contribution is the
  explicit breadth-scaling verdicts.
- **In-silico.** Boolean models give evidence about the models, leaving real campaigns to the validation
  gap. Φ magnitude is encoding-dependent; the n−1 pattern is a structural signature, read ordinally.
- **Specific structure.** This scales the all-binding campaign, which escapes breadth dilution by
  construction. Whether randomly-wired multi-recipient outreach dilutes with breadth (Finding 6) is left
  to a later cycle.
