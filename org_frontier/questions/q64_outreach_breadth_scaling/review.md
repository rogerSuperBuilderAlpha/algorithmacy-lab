# Q64 — Stage 1 review: outreach breadth scaling

## The question

Q63 found that single-recipient agent-mediated outreach is triadic when the agent binds sender intent and
a live recipient into one joint determination, and dyadic otherwise. This study asks the breadth
question. When one sender's agent coordinates with several recipients at once, does the form stay triadic
as recipients are added, and how does the verdict scale with their number? Parties: sender intent (E),
agent/mediator (M), recipients (R1 … Rk). The agent's commit is M's rule.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The single-recipient outreach verdict (Q63, probes 215–219).** read_recipient is triadic at Φ=2.0,
  substitutable recipients collapse the form, and liveness is required. Q64 extends k=1 to k=4.
- **The conjunctive-hub law, Φ = n−1 (probe 116, q45).** An AND-all hub where every party feeds a
  mediator that commits their conjunction stays triadic at Φ = n−1 for n=4 to 7. An all-required campaign
  (M'=E∧R1∧…∧Rk) is that hub with the sender as one input, so it should stay triadic with Φ = n−1 at
  every breadth.
- **Substitutability collapses the triad (Finding 5).** A counterpart bound through a disjunction factors;
  only the all-required conjunction stays triadic. A campaign with any substitutable or optional recipient
  should be dyadic at every breadth.
- **Breadth dilutes irreducibility for random wiring (Finding 6).** The random strict-mediation triadic
  rate falls 9.4% (n=3) → 2.3% (n=4) → 0% (n=5). Specific structures (the conjunctive hub) escape this,
  while random multi-recipient outreach should not.

## The gap

The lab has the single-recipient verdict and the conjunctive-hub scaling law, but has not built the
multi-recipient outreach forms and read the verdict and the major complex as breadth grows. Whether an
all-binding campaign stays triadic at Φ=n−1 to k=4, whether any substitutable recipient collapses it at
every breadth, and whether all recipients sit in the core, are uncomputed. No prior probe answers the
breadth question for outreach, so it is open.
