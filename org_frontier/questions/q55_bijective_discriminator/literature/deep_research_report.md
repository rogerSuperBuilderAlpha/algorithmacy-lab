# Q55 — Stage 2 deep research

The question asks what separates sixteen bijective parity-channel pairs below Phi=2.0 from thirty-two that
reach the ceiling on matched-read implication forms. The literature covers IIT partition geometry, Boolean
parity gate bijectivity, seam conditional entropy, commit-class coordination structure, and the Q54 residual
gap. Eleven sources, reusing verified entries from Q52, Q53, and Q54.

## Integration, MIP partitions, and seam geometry

Tononi (2004) introduced integrated information as irreducibility under the minimum information partition
(`tononi2004information`). Balduzzi and Tononi (2008) formalized the MIP as the weakest cut of cause-effect
structure (`balduzzi2008integrated`). IIT 3.0 and 4.0 carry the measure forward (`oizumi2014phenomenology`;
`albantakis2023iit4`). The Scholarpedia entry summarizes how partition notation maps to party-line cuts
(`tononi2015scholarpedia`). Q54 (#170) left sixteen bijective pairs below the ceiling without naming their
MIP seam class.

## Boolean parity bijectivity and the below-ceiling half

Carlet (2010) surveys Boolean functions, separating affine parity gates from monotone commits
(`carlet2010boolean`). Q54 (#170, #174) showed bijective XOR/XNOR gates are necessary for Phi=2.0 but
insufficient: sixteen invertible pairs stay below the ceiling. The present study tests whether those sixteen
share commit misalignment, mid-rung magnitude, or a distinct MIP singleton.

## Seam distinguishability

Cover and Thomas (2006) define conditional entropy H(Y|X) (`cover2006information`). Q54 (#172) documented
higher H(W,C|S) under symmetric_xor than symmetric-AND. Q55 tests whether seam entropy also grades the
bijective parity panel between below-ceiling and ceiling halves.

## Commit-class coordination

Malone and Crowston (1994) treat coordination as managing dependencies among activities
(`malone1994interdisciplinary`). Q52 (#161–#162) documented the 0.415037 mid rung on C-centric commits under
one-sided AND. Q54 (#173) showed XOR one-sided ceiling hits require commit-topology alignment. Q55 extends
alignment to XNOR with predicted polarity inversion.

## Computation and pre-registration

PyPhi implements exact IIT 4.0 Phi (`mayner2018pyphi`). Brodeur et al. (2024) and Chamberlin (1890/1965)
support fixing five discriminator hypotheses before computing (`brodeur2024preregistration`;
`chamberlin1890multiple`).

## Gap

Q54 established bijective parity gates as necessary and named alignment for one-sided XOR success. No probe
has partitioned the forty-eight bijective parity pairs into a complete below-vs-ceiling signature across
topology sidedness, Phi magnitude, seam entropy, MIP singleton class, and XNOR alignment inversion.
