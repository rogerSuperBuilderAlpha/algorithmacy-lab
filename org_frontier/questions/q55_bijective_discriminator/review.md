**Question.** What distinguishes the sixteen bijective parity-channel pairs that remain below Φ=2.0 from the
thirty-two that reach the ceiling on matched-read implication forms?

## The gap

Q54 showed channel-gate bijectivity is necessary for Φ=2.0 but insufficient: sixteen invertible parity pairs
stay below the ceiling while thirty-two reach it. Commit alignment (#173) names one-sided success but does
not characterize the failing half as a complete partition. MIP seam geometry, mid-rung Φ uniformity, seam
entropy thresholds, and XNOR alignment inversion are untested discriminators. No probe has asked whether the
sixteen below-ceiling bijective pairs share a single structural signature separable from every ceiling pair.
