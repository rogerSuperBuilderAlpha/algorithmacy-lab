# Q96 — Stage 1 review: state-contingent membership

## The question

Finding 5 collapses the triad when a party is substitutable or optional, but participation in the corpus is
always unconditional. Whether a party bound into the core only in some states counts as bound, or whether
the conditionality factors it out, is unasked.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Substitutability and optionality collapse the triad (Finding 5).** A party that need not participate
  factors the form. Contingent participation is the matched test: does sometimes-participation factor it
  too?
- **The reciprocity gradient binds at a threshold (Q67).** A partial reciprocal loop seeds a local core.
  Contingent reading is a related partial-binding question.
- **Liveness is necessary (Finding 3, Q92).** The recipient must be bound through some path; Q92 showed a
  tracking memory suffices. Whether a contingent read suffices is open.

## The gap

No form gates a party's participation by state. Whether contingent participation integrates into a triad,
factors the whole, or binds the party only locally is uncomputed.
