# Q96 literature — conditional participation

## Unconditional binding in the corpus

Finding 5 collapses the triad when a party is substitutable or optional. Every form the program has built
binds its parties unconditionally: a bound party is read in every state. Real coordination is often
conditional — a platform consults a counterpart only when a flag is set, an agent escalates only in some
regimes. Whether a party that participates in only some states counts as bound, or whether the conditionality
factors it out the way optionality does, is unasked.

## The question

Two readings are possible. Contingent participation could integrate: the recipient is bound on average,
and the form is triadic with the recipient in the core, weaker than full participation but irreducible. Or
the conditionality could factor the form: a recipient read only sometimes is, structurally, an optional
party, and Finding 5 would make the form dyadic. The gate that controls the contingency is itself a party,
and whether it joins the core is part of the question. The distinction matters for whether conditional
coordination — the common case — is structurally a triad or a dyad with an intermittent third party.

## Method context

The form gates the mediator's read of the recipient by a state that tracks the worker, so the contingency
is endogenous and both gate values are reachable. The verdict and major complex are read by the program's
standard instrument.
