# Q96 — State-contingent participation: a factored whole with a local core

## Question

Finding 5 collapses the triad when a party is substitutable or optional, but every form in the corpus binds
its parties unconditionally. Real coordination is often conditional — a platform consults a counterpart only
when a flag is set. Whether a party that participates in only some states counts as bound, or whether the
conditionality factors it out the way optionality does, is the question.

## Method

The read-recipient triad with a gate. The gate T tracks the worker (T'=E), and the mediator reads the
recipient only when the gate is on: M' = E ∧ (R ∨ ¬T), with E'=M and R'=M. Across reachable states the gate
takes both values, so the recipient is in the determination contingently. The verdict and major complex are
read, against the always-on triad and the never-on broadcast.

## Results

The contingent form is whole-system dyadic, with major complex {M, R} at Φ = 2.0. The always control is
triadic with core {E, M, R}; the never control is dyadic with core {E, M}.

| form | whole-system | Φ | major complex |
|---|---|---|---|
| contingent | dyadic | 0.00 | {M, R} |
| always | triadic | 2.00 | {E, M, R} |
| never | dyadic | 0.00 | {E, M} |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 contingent participation integrates | refuted |
| H2 contingency weakens it (Φ < 2.0) | confirmed |
| H3 the recipient is in the contingent core | confirmed |
| H4 the gate is in the contingent core | refuted |

## Interpretation

Contingency is closer to optionality than to participation. A recipient read only when a gate is on does
not bind into a single joint determination of the whole, and the form factors, exactly as Finding 5 makes
substitutable and optional roles factor. The whole-system verdict is dyadic, so conditional participation
does not produce a triad.

The recipient is bound, but only locally and only to the mediator. The major complex is the pair {M, R},
not the worker-mediator-recipient triad: making the read conditional moves the localized core from the
worker side, where the never control sits at {E, M}, to the recipient side. The recipient is bound weakly,
in a pair, rather than as one of three parties in an irreducible whole.

The controller of the contingency is excluded. The gate that decides whether the recipient is read is not
in the core, and neither is the worker. The party that gates participation is not itself bound into the
coordination it gates, which is a clean structural statement: a switch over a coordination is outside the
coordination it switches.

For conditional coordination, the lesson is that gating a party's participation does not preserve the
triad. A platform that reads a counterpart only under a flag has a platform-counterpart pair with the
worker and the flag factored out, not a three-party coordination. The unconditional triad and the
conditional pair are different structures, and the conditionality is what separates them.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with exact Φ and major complex. The gate tracks the worker; other gating laws,
and a gate that more of the form reads, could bind the gate into the core and are untested. The contingent
form is whole-system dyadic with a triadic {M, R} complex, the Q74 situation, so both readings are
reported. One contingency construction is tested, not a family.
