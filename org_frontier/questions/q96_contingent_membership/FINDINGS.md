# Q96 findings — contingent participation factors the whole, but keeps a local core

One hypothesis confirmed in the form predicted, two in a sharper form, one refuted. A recipient read only
in some states does not give a triad: the form factors as a whole, the way an optional party does. But the
recipient stays bound to the mediator in a localized core, while the worker and the gate drop out.

| form | whole-system | Φ | major complex |
|---|---|---|---|
| contingent | dyadic | 0.00 | {M, R} |
| always (control) | triadic | 2.00 | {E, M, R} |
| never (control) | dyadic | 0.00 | {E, M} |

| H | Result | Verdict |
|---|--------|---------|
| H1 | contingent participation integrates (triadic) | refuted (whole-system dyadic) |
| H2 | contingency weakens it (Φ < 2.0) | confirmed |
| H3 | the recipient is in the contingent core | confirmed |
| H4 | the gate is in the contingent core | refuted |

From `probe_contingent_membership.py`.

## What it says

Conditional participation does not make a triad. When the mediator reads the recipient only when a gate is
on, the form is whole-system dyadic (H1 refuted): the gated read behaves like an optional party, and
Finding 5's collapse of substitutable or optional roles applies. A party read only sometimes is,
structurally, not bound into a single joint determination of the whole. Contingency is closer to optionality
than to participation.

The recipient stays bound locally. The major complex of the contingent form is {M, R} at Φ = 2.0 (H3): the
mediator and the contingently-read recipient form an irreducible pair, even though the whole system factors.
This separates the contingent form from the never control, whose core is {E, M}: making the read conditional
on a gate moves the localized core from the worker side to the recipient side. The recipient is bound,
weakly, to the mediator alone.

The worker and the gate are both excluded. The worker, bound in the always-on triad, leaves the contingent
core, and the gate that controls the contingency is not in it (H4 refuted). The party that decides whether
the recipient participates is not itself bound into the coordination it gates. The contingency restructures
the form into a mediator-recipient pair with everything else, including its own controller, factored out.

The reading for conditional coordination is that gating a party's participation does not preserve the
triad. A platform that consults a counterpart only under a flag has not built a worker-platform-counterpart
triad; it has a platform-counterpart pair that the worker and the flag sit outside. Conditional
coordination is structurally a dyad with an intermittently-bound third party, set against the
unconditional triad of full participation.

## Caveats

- **Mixed result.** H2 and H3 confirmed; H1 and H4 refuted, the refutations being the substantive finding.
- **Whole-system vs major complex.** The contingent form is whole-system dyadic with a triadic {M, R}
  complex; the Q74 rule governs which to report, and both are reported here.
- **One gating model.** The gate tracks the worker (T'=E); other gating laws, and a gate read by more of
  the form, could bind the gate into the core and are untested.
- **In-silico.** Boolean models, exact Φ and major complex.
