# Q96 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on a recipient bound into the determination only in some states. Participation in the
corpus is always unconditional; Q96 gates the mediator's read of the recipient by a state that tracks the
worker, so the recipient is read only when the gate is on. Written and committed before the contingent form
is read. The always-on (triadic, Φ = 2) and never (dyadic) forms are the instrument control.

## H1 — Contingent participation integrates

- **Claim:** The contingent form, where the mediator reads the recipient only when the gate is on, is
  triadic.
- **H0:** It is dyadic; the conditionality factors the recipient out.
- **Predicted outcome:** triadic. The recipient is bound on average even though read only conditionally.
  This is the study's central and genuinely uncertain claim; the gating could equally factor the form.

## H2 — Contingency weakens the binding

- **Claim:** The contingent form's Φ is below the always-on triad's 2.0.
- **H0:** Its Φ is at least 2.0.
- **Predicted outcome:** below 2.0 — partial participation integrates partially.

## H3 — The recipient is in the contingent core

- **Claim:** The recipient is in the major complex of the contingent form.
- **H0:** The recipient is outside the core.
- **Predicted outcome:** in the core — a contingently-read party still counts as bound.

## H4 — The gate is in the contingent core

- **Claim:** The gate, which decides whether the recipient is read, is in the major complex.
- **H0:** The gate is outside the core.
- **Predicted outcome:** in the core — the party that controls the contingency is bound into it.
