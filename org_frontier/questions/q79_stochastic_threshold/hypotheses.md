# Q79 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on the stochastic emergence of the triad. The agent's commit reads the recipient with
probability p (M'=E∧R) and ignores it with 1−p (M'=E); p is swept over {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0}. Written
and committed before any test runs.

## H1 — The deterministic endpoints

- **Claim:** At p=1 the form is triadic at Φ=2.0; at p=0 it is dyadic at Φ=0.
- **H0:** An endpoint departs from its deterministic value.
- **Predicted outcome:** Φ=2.0 at p=1, Φ=0 at p=0. H0 refuted.

## H2 — Φ rises monotonically with the reading probability

- **Claim:** Φ is non-decreasing as p rises from 0 to 1.
- **H0:** Φ is non-monotone in p.
- **Predicted outcome:** Φ non-decreasing. H0 refuted.

## H3 — Partial reading already yields a positive Φ

- **Claim:** For 0 < p < 1 the form has Φ > 0; partial joint determination yields partial irreducibility.
- **H0:** Φ stays at 0 for some interior p.
- **Predicted outcome:** Φ > 0 at p = 0.25, 0.5, 0.75. H0 refuted.

## H4 — The emergence is graded, not a threshold

- **Claim:** Φ is positive at the smallest tested p > 0 (p = 0.25); there is no flat-zero region followed
  by a jump.
- **H0:** Φ is 0 at p = 0.25.
- **Predicted outcome:** Φ > 0 at p = 0.25. H0 refuted.
