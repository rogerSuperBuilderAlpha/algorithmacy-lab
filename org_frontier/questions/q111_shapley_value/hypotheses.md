# Q111 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on the Shapley value of integration — how a coordination's value is distributed among its
parties. The value of a coalition is the integrated information of its subsystem; a party's Shapley value is
its average marginal contribution to that value. This opens the political-economy wave: does structural
membership confer strategic value, and who captures it? Written and committed before the run; the instrument
was validated on the triad and the spectator form. Predictions follow from Finding 8 (the mediator binds the
parties) and Q98 (influence requires membership).

## H1 — The mediator captures more than each outer party

- **Claim:** In the read-recipient triad, the mediator's Shapley value exceeds each outer party's.
- **H0:** An outer party's value is at least the mediator's.
- **Predicted outcome:** the mediator captures more. H0 refuted. The party essential to every productive
  coalition captures the most value.

## H2 — The values sum to the system Φ

- **Claim:** The Shapley values sum to the system's Φ (efficiency: the value is fully distributed).
- **H0:** They do not sum to Φ.
- **Predicted outcome:** they sum to Φ. H0 refuted.

## H3 — The mediator's share is super-egalitarian

- **Claim:** The mediator's Shapley value exceeds the egalitarian share Φ/n.
- **H0:** It is at most Φ/n.
- **Predicted outcome:** above Φ/n. H0 refuted. The mediator captures a disproportionate share — the
  structural basis of platform power.

## H4 — A non-core party captures non-positive value

- **Claim:** A read-only spectator added to the triad has a Shapley value at most zero.
- **H0:** The spectator captures positive value.
- **Predicted outcome:** non-positive. H0 refuted. Value tracks core membership: a party outside the core
  captures none, and a spectator that factors the whole system can even hold negative value. This is the
  study's genuinely uncertain claim on the sign.
