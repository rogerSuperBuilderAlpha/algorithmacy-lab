# Q111 literature — the value of the core

## From structure to value

The program reads which parties are bound into the coordination. It has not asked what a party is worth.
Cooperative game theory answers exactly this: given a value for each coalition, the Shapley value
distributes the grand coalition's worth among the players by their average marginal contribution (Shapley
1953). Taking the value of a coalition to be the integrated information of its subsystem turns the
structural account into an economic one — the Φ a set of parties can sustain is the worth they create
together, and the Shapley value is each party's share.

## The expected distribution

The law makes the mediator the party that binds the others. In the read-recipient triad the two outer
parties produce nothing without the mediator — their coalition has value zero — while either pairs with the
mediator to produce the full value. The mediator is therefore essential to every productive coalition and
should capture a disproportionate share of the value, which is the structural basis of platform power and
rent. A party outside the core, a spectator, contributes nothing and should capture no value, and a
spectator that factors the whole system may even subtract from it.

## Method context

The coalition value is the subsystem Φ from `sia`; the Shapley value is the exact average over orderings,
feasible at these sizes.
