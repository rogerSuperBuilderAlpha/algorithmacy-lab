# Q111 — Stage 1 review: the value of the core

## The question

The program reads which parties are bound into a coordination, never what a party is worth. Cooperative game
theory distributes a coalition's worth among its players by marginal contribution. Taking the worth of a
coalition to be the integrated information of its subsystem, the question is who captures the value — does
the mediator take a disproportionate share, and does a non-core party take any?

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The mediator binds the parties (Finding 8).** The two outer parties produce nothing without it, so the
  mediator should be essential to every productive coalition and capture the most value.
- **Influence requires membership (Q98); a coalition can capture the mediator (Q97).** Both are about power;
  neither quantifies value. The Shapley value does.
- **A read-only spectator factors the whole system (Q74, Q75).** Its value should be non-positive, and the
  whole-system reading may make it negative.

## The gap

No study assigns a value to a party. Whether structural membership confers strategic value, and how that
value distributes between the mediator and the parties, is uncomputed.
