# Parity hub versus conjunctive hub under flip-noise

<code + data: org_frontier/questions/q8_parity_vs_conjunctive_noise/ ; probes #150–154 (this study), context #115, #116, #140–149 in probes/PROBES.md>

## Abstract

The parity (XOR-all) hub determines its parties through pure top-level synergy, with no lower-order seed beneath it; the conjunctive (AND-all) hub mixes redundant, unique, and synergistic information. The analysis of Boolean functions makes parity the maximally noise-sensitive form, so a natural prediction is that the parity hub's dyadic/triadic Φ_MIP verdict collapses faster under flip-noise than the conjunctive hub's at matched size. This paper injects the Q6/Q7 output flip-noise model into each hub's commit and sweeps p on a 51-point grid from the clean limit p=0 to the random output p=0.5, at n=3 and n=4, reading Φ_MIP, its derivative, the verdict, and the major complex. Every prediction that distinguishes the two forms fails. Both forms hold the strict triadic verdict at every interior p and read dyadic only at the degenerate endpoint p=0.5, so the verdict-flip points coincide at p_v=0.50 for all four sweeps (H1, refuted). The conjunctive form sheds the larger fraction of its clean Φ at every interior point and has the steeper clean-limit log-slope, the opposite of the predicted direction (H2, refuted). The flip-gap does not widen with n; on the governing effective register it inverts, from −0.01 at n=3 to −0.04 at n=4 (H3, refuted). The literal (1−2p)^n threshold prediction lands within one grid step but is degenerate, and the parity decay curve is n-independent while (1−2p)^n is strongly n-dependent, so the closed form gives the wrong shape (H4, partial). Both forms decay as monotone glides with |dΦ/dp| maximal at p→0 and no interior susceptibility peak, so the predicted parity cliff is absent (H5, refuted). The parity hub's small clean Φ does not make its verdict more fragile; if anything the conjunctive hub sheds integration faster in fraction, and the verdict collapse is a property of the degenerate endpoint for both forms.

## Introduction

A coordination hub can bind its parties in two ways. The conjunctive hub fires only when all parties agree, and its determination leaves a redundant trace that pairwise analysis can read. The parity hub fires on the odd-count rule, and its determination is pure synergy: every pair of parties is independent, and only the whole carries the bit. Q-series probes #115 and #116 fixed the clean integrated information of the two forms. The parity hub reads Φ = 2^(2−n), which is 0.5 at n=3 and 0.25 at n=4, shrinking with size. The conjunctive hub reads Φ = n−1, which is 2.0 at n=3 and 3.0 at n=4, growing with size. The clean-Φ gap between the forms is large and widens with n.

Whether that clean gap projects onto a robustness gap is the question. The parity hub's irreducibility lives only in the top-level structure. Noise that thins that structure has nothing to fall back on, so the parity verdict might break first. Q6 fixed the conjunctive baseline: matched flip-noise on a conjunctive mediator's commit produced a smooth exponential Φ decay with the verdict stepping to dyadic only at the degenerate endpoint p=0.5. Whether the parity hub collapses earlier, and whether the gap between the two forms widens with size, was left open.

The matched manipulation injects the same output flip-noise model into each hub's commit and traces Φ_MIP(p) for both forms at n=3 and n=4 on one grid. A manager who can harden one hub against noise wants to know whether the synergistic hub is the fragile one. The gap from Stage 2 is that no prior probe ran the parity hub under noise, and no source in the literature computes the head-to-head decay curves.

## Related work

No source in the verified set computes the Φ_MIP(p) decay curves for a parity hub against a conjunctive hub under matched TPM flip-noise, nor the noise level at which each form's verdict collapses [garbansteif2014]. That specific experiment is an open gap. The literature establishes every mechanism the question leans on, and the prediction it yields is a cross-framework inference.

The quantity is well-defined and the noise model is admissible. Integrated information is the irreducibility of a system relative to its minimum-information partition, the partition that makes the least difference to the cause-effect repertoire [oizumi2014iit3]. IIT admits probabilistic transition matrices, and PyPhi analyzes stochastic systems, so an output flip-noise commit is a well-formed IIT object [oizumi2014iit3; mayner2018pyphi]. Noise lowers φ through a determinism ceiling: indeterminism in a unit's transition reduces a system's intrinsic information, which upper-bounds φ_s, and the IIT-4.0 system-φ paper carries a continuous noise knob that sets the slope of a unit's activation [albantakis2023systemphi]. The direction of every noise effect is down; the rate is free.

The mechanism predicting parity fragility comes from the analysis of Boolean functions. The correlation between a function and its noised copy decomposes by Fourier level, E[f(ω)f(ω_ε)] = Σ_S f̂(S)²(1−ε)^|S|, so robustness is governed by how high in the spectrum the function's mass sits [garbansteif2014; odonnell2014boolean]. Parity equals the single top-level character, with all its energy at level n, so its retained signal decays as (1−ε)^n, the fastest possible rate and the maximally noise-sensitive extreme. The dictator is the opposite, noise-stable extreme. Because (1−ε)^n ≤ (1−ε) for all n ≥ 1, parity is more fragile than a low-order function even at fixed n [bks1999noise; garbansteif2014]. Two caveats bound the analogy. Formal noise sensitivity is an n→∞ property of a sequence, while this question fixes n=3,4; the fixed-n Stab_ρ(χ_S)=ρ^|S| inequality backs the same ordering, so this is a type-mismatch [garbansteif2014]. A conjunctive/AND gate spreads its Fourier weight across all levels, so it is lower-order-dominated, and its decay is gentler than parity's while still depending on n [odonnell2014boolean].

The synergy basis for why connectivity cannot see the parity hub is established. Integrated information decomposes into redundancy, unique, and synergy atoms, and parity is the canonical pure-synergy gate, with all pairwise mutual informations zero while the joint carries one bit [mediano2019beyond; rosas2020reconciling]. Resting-state fMRI shows higher-order synergies largely invisible to functional connectivity [varley2023ped; varley2024synergyfirst], and low-order-biased models can mistake higher-order structure for noise [reing2021higherorder]. Two findings qualify the shape of the collapse. Φ acts as an order parameter that undergoes a phase transition, with a susceptibility peak at criticality rather than an abrupt drop [popiel2020criticality]. Dynamical noise can enhance high-order structure at intermediate levels, so the sweep tests monotonicity instead of assuming it; the enhancement Orio reports is redundant, and it leaves the parity-fragility prediction intact [orio2023dynamicalnoise].

## Hypotheses

The five hypotheses were fixed before any computation, each with the structural prior its null rests on.

H1: the parity verdict flips at an interior p; the conjunctive verdict holds to the endpoint. At fixed n, rising flip-noise collapses the parity triadic verdict at a finite p_par* strictly inside (0, 0.5), while the conjunctive verdict survives until p=0.5. Null: both forms flip only at p=0.5, the parity verdict riding smoothly to zero like every prior stochastic conjunctive sweep, because a unit XORed with a fair coin carries zero information regardless of the commit.

H2: parity Φ decays faster than conjunctive Φ in flip fraction. Rescaled to its own clean value, the normalized parity curve still falls faster: parity has lost a larger fraction at every interior p and its clean-limit log-slope is steeper, at both sizes. Null: once normalized, the two forms decay at the same fractional rate, parity only looking more fragile because its clean Φ is smaller.

H3: the verdict-flip gap widens with n. The distance between the parity flip point and the conjunctive flip point is larger at n=4 than n=3, with parity's flip moving to lower p as n grows while the conjunctive flip stays pinned near 0.5. Null: the gap does not widen, either because both forms flip at 0.5 at both sizes or because the parity flip is n-independent and the single n=3→4 step is too short.

H4: the parity collapse threshold tracks (1−2p)^n. The parity flip point is quantitatively predicted by the Fourier-decay law, landing within grid tolerance of the value (1−2p)^n = r_n gives at each n. Null: the partition-minimization structure of Φ_MIP shifts the collapse off the Boolean-function prediction, the analogy giving the direction but the wrong p.

H5: parity collapses as a cliff, conjunctive as a glide. The parity Φ_MIP(p) has a sharp knee, an interior peak in |dΦ/dp|, because there is no lower-order seed to cushion the fall; the conjunctive curve glides, monotone with |dΦ/dp| maximal at p=0. Null: both curves are smooth monotone glides with |dΦ/dp| maximal at p=0 and no interior susceptibility peak.

## Methods

Node 0 is the hub S; nodes 1…n−1 are the parties, each reading the hub. The parity hub commits S' = x[1] ⊕ … ⊕ x[n−1] (`parity_hub(n)` from probe #115). The conjunctive hub commits S' = x[1] ∧ … ∧ x[n−1] (`single_hub(n)` from probe #116). Both run at n=3 and n=4. Flip-noise of strength p is output noise on the hub column: for clean hub column col_clean ∈ {0,1} per row, the column becomes P(S'=1) = (1−p)·col_clean + p·(1−col_clean), so the commit lands on its clean value with reliability 1−p and flips with probability p. The party columns stay deterministic. The flip rides in the mechanism's TPM, the Q6/Q7 model.

The measure is Φ_MIP from IIT-4.0 (`max_phi_float`, max exact Φ over reachable states with the connectivity matrix inferred numerically), and the binary verdict and MIP location from `classify`, with PHI_EPS = 1e-9 the zero threshold for the strict dyadic/triadic verdict. The verdict is the load-bearing object; Φ magnitude is an ordinal hint under the classifier's own caution, and the Q8 claims rest on the shape and ordering of the two curves, treating each Φ as an ordinal reading. The grid is 51 uniform points, p = 0.00, 0.01, …, 0.50, the same grid as Q6 so the conjunctive curve is directly comparable. Four sweeps (parity-n3, parity-n4, conjunctive-n3, conjunctive-n4) are computed once and reused. The discrete derivative is the centered difference on interior points, one-sided at the endpoints. Numerical tolerance throughout is TOL = 1e-6. The normalized curve Φ̂(p) = Φ(p)/Φ(0) rescales each sweep to its clean value; the drop fraction is f(p) = 1 − Φ̂(p).

The instrument control ran first and passed for every sweep. The four clean p=0 endpoints reproduced their tabled values to 1e-6: parity n=3 Φ=0.5, parity n=4 Φ=0.25, conjunctive n=3 Φ=2.0, conjunctive n=4 Φ=3.0, all triadic, parity MIP all-singletons. The strict-mediation reference triad reads triadic at Φ=2.0, tying this question's instrument to Q6/Q7. If any p=0 endpoint had not reproduced its tabled value and triadic, that form's swept values would not be trusted.

H1 reads the strict verdict-flip point p_v, the smallest grid p at which the structure reads dyadic (Φ_MIP ≤ PHI_EPS), for each form at each size, and whether the major complex stays the full hub-plus-parties set up to p_v. The negative control checks that an interior dyadic reading is a real zero-crossing and not a one-step dropout by recording Φ_MIP at each step: a dyadic reading must carry Φ_MIP ≤ PHI_EPS while its left neighbour carries Φ_MIP > PHI_EPS, so the flip is a genuine zero-crossing. Decision rule: confirmed if p_v(parity, n) < 0.5 strictly and p_v(conj, n) = 0.5 at both sizes; refuted if the parity verdict also flips only at 0.5 at either size.

H2 reads two quantities at matched n. The count of interior grid points where f_parity(p) > f_conj(p), and the clean-limit log-slope L = |log Φ̂(0.01)| / 0.01 for each form. The self/identity control compares each normalized curve against itself, which must give 0 interior points won. Decision rule: confirmed if at both sizes parity has lost the larger fraction at every interior point and L_parity > L_conj; refuted if at either size the conjunctive form has lost at least the larger fraction at any interior point or L_conj ≥ L_parity.

H3 reads two registers. The strict flip-gap G_v(n) = p_v(conj, n) − p_v(parity, n), and the effective gap G_eff(n) = p_eff(conj, n) − p_eff(parity, n), where p_eff is the smallest grid p at which Φ̂ < 0.01, a fixed near-zero band identical for both forms and sizes. Which register governs is decided jointly with H1: if the strict parity flips are interior, G_v governs; if they sit at 0.5, G_eff governs. Decision rule: confirmed if on the governing register G(4) > G(3) and parity's flip moves to lower p with size; refuted if G(4) ≤ G(3) or parity's flip does not move earlier.

H4 reads the measured parity collapse p_par* (the strict flip from H1) and the closed-form prediction p_pred(n) solving (1 − 2p_pred)^n = r_n with r_n = PHI_EPS / Φ_parity(0). The contrast is |p_par* − p_pred(n)| against the grid spacing 0.01. A recorded corroborant is the per-interior residual max_p |Φ̂_parity(p) − (1−2p)^n|. The positive control feeds the analytic (1−2p)^n against itself, which must return residual 0. Decision rule: confirmed if |p_par* − p_pred(n)| ≤ 0.01 at both sizes; refuted otherwise.

H5 reads the susceptibility χ(p) = dΦ/dp by centered difference on the un-normalized curve, the location p_χ = argmax|χ| over the interior, and whether |χ| has an interior peak exceeding both neighbours and both endpoints. The positive control applies the same test to a synthetic curve with a deliberate interior knee at p=0.25, which must register as an interior peak. Decision rule: confirmed if at both sizes the parity |χ| has an interior peak and the conjunctive |χ| is monotone-decreasing from p=0; refuted if the parity susceptibility has no interior peak or the conjunctive one shows an interior peak.

## Results

H1: refuted. Both forms hold the strict triadic verdict at every interior p and read dyadic only at the degenerate endpoint. The strict verdict-flip points coincide: p_v(parity, 3) = 0.50, p_v(parity, 4) = 0.50, p_v(conj, 3) = 0.50, p_v(conj, 4) = 0.50. Every sweep carries Φ_MIP > PHI_EPS = 1e-9 at every interior p from 0.01 to 0.49 and reads dyadic only at p=0.50, where Φ_MIP = 0. The major complex is the full hub-plus-parties set at every interior p and factors to none only at p=0.50. The negative-control zero-crossing check passes for all four: Φ_MIP at the left neighbour p=0.49 is 0.007285 (parity n=3), 0.003643 (parity n=4), 0.011093 (conjunctive n=3), 0.006520 (conjunctive n=4), each above PHI_EPS and dropping to 0 at p=0.50, a genuine single zero-crossing. The hypothesis required p_v(parity, n) < 0.5; the parity verdict does not flip in the interior at either size, so H1 is refuted, matching the pre-registered coarse-grid trial expectation. Φ̂ is observed n-independent for parity, the identical curve at n=3 and n=4, while the conjunctive form decays faster at n=4 (`results/q8_verdict_flip.csv`, `probe_verdict_flip.py`).

H2: refuted. The conjunctive form sheds the larger fraction, the opposite of the predicted direction. Of the 49 interior points, parity wins f_parity > f_conj at 0/49 at n=3 and 0/49 at n=4; conversely f_conj ≥ f_parity at all 49 interior points at both sizes. The clean-limit log-slope is steeper for the conjunctive form: at n=3, L_parity = 2.465605 against L_conj = 5.162257; at n=4, L_parity = 2.465605 against L_conj = 10.172569, so L_parity > L_conj is false at both sizes. The spot value at p=0.10 reads Φ̂_parity = 0.763197, identical at n=3 and n=4, against Φ̂_conj = 0.594361 at n=3 and 0.406861 at n=4, the conjunctive form lower and falling faster at the larger size. The self/identity control returns 0 interior points won by each curve against itself, so the point-count statistic reads zero on identical curves. H2 is refuted at both sizes (`results/q8_normalized_decay.csv`, `probe_normalized_decay.py`).

H3: refuted. The flip-gap does not widen with n. On the strict register both forms flip only at p=0.5, so p_v reads 0.5 for all four sweeps and G_v(3) = G_v(4) = 0.0. Because the strict parity flips are not interior, the effective register governs. There p_eff(parity, 3) = p_eff(parity, 4) = 0.5, so the parity flip is n-independent and does not move earlier with size, while p_eff(conj, 3) = 0.49 and p_eff(conj, 4) = 0.46, the conjunctive form collapsing earlier than parity. The effective gaps are G_eff(3) = −0.01 and G_eff(4) = −0.04, so G(4) − G(3) = −0.03 and the gap inverts and narrows with size. Every confirmation condition fails: G(4) > G(3) is false, p_par*(4) < p_par*(3) is false (0.5 against 0.5), and the conjunctive form is not pinned at or near the endpoint relative to parity. H3 is refuted (`results/q8_gap_scaling.csv`, `probe_gap_scaling.py`).

H4: partial. The literal threshold matches but is degenerate, and the curve shape refutes the law. The measured collapse p_par* = 0.50 at both sizes; the closed form gives p_pred = 0.499370 at n=3 and 0.496024 at n=4, so |p_par* − p_pred| = 0.000630 at n=3 and 0.003976 at n=4, both under the grid spacing 0.01, which confirms the literal rule. The match is degenerate: the resolution floor r_n = 2e-9 pins both p_pred values to p ≈ 0.5 for any n, so the agreement carries no information about the form. The shape refutes the law. The parity normalized curve is n-independent, identical at n=3 and n=4 with max difference 0.0, reading 0.879699 at p=0.05, 0.763197 at p=0.10, and 0.542458 at p=0.20 at both sizes, while (1−2p)^n is strongly n-dependent, giving 0.729 at n=3 against 0.6561 at n=4 at p=0.05. The per-interior residual is 0.326458 at n=3 and 0.412858 at n=4, growing with n, against the positive control's residual of 0.0 for an exact match. The instrument control passed (`single_hub(3)` triadic Φ=2.0). The literal rule is confirmed within tolerance but degenerate, and the closed form gives the wrong shape, so the verdict is partial (`results/q8_boolean_law.csv`, `probe_boolean_law.py`).

H5: refuted. Both forms decay as monotone glides with no interior susceptibility peak. On all four un-normalized Φ(p) sweeps, |χ| = |dΦ/dp| is maximal at the left endpoint p=0 and decays from there. The interior argmax is always the first interior point p_χ = 0.02, but it never exceeds the p=0 endpoint, so the interior-peak flag is false for all four. The endpoint values: parity n=3 |χ|(0) = 1.217729 against |χ|(0.02) = 1.206762, tail |χ|(0.49) = 0.735586; parity n=4 |χ|(0) = 0.608864 against 0.603381, tail 0.367793; conjunctive n=3 |χ|(0) = 10.062552 against 9.316242, tail 1.137233; conjunctive n=4 |χ|(0) = 29.016811 against 23.807200, tail 0.673634. No forward-difference rise above TOL = 1e-6 follows a fall in any sweep, so neither curve is non-monotone. The parity cliff is absent at both sizes, and the conjunctive glide holds. The positive control located an injected knee at p=0.25 as a strict interior peak, so a cliff would have registered if present. H5 is refuted (`results/q8_cliff_vs_glide.csv`, `probe_cliff_vs_glide.py`). One implementation note: the spec's literal positive-control curve, flat to 0.25 then one steeper line to 0, gives a constant-slope segment whose |χ| is a flat plateau and never an isolated peak; the control was implemented as a localized knee at 0.25, a steep step over [0.24, 0.26] then a shallower line to 0, so it registers as the interior peak the exact test demands.

## Discussion

The parity hub's small clean Φ does not make its verdict more fragile. Every prediction that distinguished the two forms failed. The verdict-flip points coincide at p=0.50 for all four sweeps (H1), so the strict dyadic/triadic verdict is set by the degenerate endpoint and cannot separate a synergistic hub from a redundant one. A unit XORed or ANDed with a fair coin carries zero information regardless of the commit function [mayner2018pyphi], and that endpoint governs both forms identically. This extends the Q6 reading, where the conjunctive mediator flipped only at p=0.5, to the parity hub: the verdict collapse is an endpoint phenomenon for both.

The fragility ordering runs the other way in Φ. H2 found the conjunctive form shedding the larger fraction of its clean integration at every interior point and carrying the steeper clean-limit log-slope, the opposite of the Boolean-function prediction. The cross-framework analogy gave the wrong direction once read on Φ_MIP instead of on Fourier correlation. The parity decay is n-independent, the identical normalized curve at n=3 and n=4, while the conjunctive decay steepens with size. That n-independence is itself the clean signature the Boolean law predicts for a low-order function, here showing up on the parity hub instead, which is the inversion H2 records.

The size axis does not amplify a gap that is not there. H3 found the strict gap zero at both sizes and the effective gap inverting from −0.01 to −0.04, the conjunctive form collapsing slightly earlier than parity and more so at n=4. The clean-Φ gap (2^(2−n) against n−1) widens with n, but it does not project onto a widening noise-threshold gap. The collapse threshold is governed by the endpoint, not by the clean magnitude, so the large and growing clean-Φ asymmetry does not carry into robustness.

H4 splits cleanly between a degenerate match and a refuted shape. The literal (1−2p)^n rule lands within tolerance only because the resolution floor pins both predictions to p ≈ 0.5 for any n, so the match is empty. The informative reading is the shape: the parity curve obeys a single n-independent law closer to (1−2p) than to (1−2p)^n, with a residual against (1−2p)^n that grows with n. The partition-minimization structure of Φ_MIP shifts the decay off the Fourier-correlation law, the sharper Q8 claim refuted on the part that carries content.

H5 finds no phase-transition cliff. Both forms are monotone glides with steepest slope at p→0 and no interior susceptibility peak, so the parity collapse is a scaled glide, with none of the order-parameter knee Popiel reported for Φ at criticality [popiel2020criticality]. The injected-knee control confirms the test would have seen a cliff. No transient rise appears in either curve, so the Orio–Mediano–Rosas enhancement caution [orio2023dynamicalnoise] does not bite at this grain.

Read across the five probes: the verdict collapse is an endpoint property shared by both forms (H1), the Φ-fraction ordering inverts the synergy-fragility prediction (H2), the gap does not widen and faintly inverts with size (H3), the Boolean law gives the wrong shape once the degenerate match is set aside (H4), and the decay is a glide with no cliff for either form (H5). The through-line is that the clean-Φ asymmetry between a synergistic and a redundant hub, real and large, does not transfer to the noise-collapse threshold, which the degenerate endpoint fixes for both. The parity hub is the more fragile form only in clean magnitude, not in verdict or in fractional decay rate.

## Limitations

The result is specific to this form, this noise model, and this grain. The two hubs are the parity (XOR-all) and conjunctive (AND-all) commits at n=3 and n=4; disjunctive, threshold, or majority commits and larger n are untested. The single n=3→4 step is short, and the Boolean noise-sensitivity ordering is an n→∞ property [garbansteif2014], so an interior parity flip or a widening gap could still appear at larger sizes the grid ceiling here does not reach. The noise is independent output flip-noise riding in the hub column; input noise, correlated noise, and noise in the initial state are separate manipulations. The grid stops at p=0.5, the random-output endpoint for a single column. Φ magnitude is an ordinal hint under the classifier's own caution, so the H2 fraction comparison and the H4 residual read as orderings and shapes with no claim to calibrated scale. The strict verdict is binary and is fixed at the degenerate endpoint for any single noised hub by construction, so H1's and H3's refutations describe the verdict instrument and leave the two forms' interior likeness to the Φ curve. The synergy/redundancy decomposition that motivates the parity-fragility prediction [mediano2019beyond] was not tracked alongside Φ_MIP(p); whether the parity hub loses a synergy atom while the conjunctive hub keeps a redundant core stays open.
