## Reading across (probes #150–#154)

The parity hub's small clean Φ does not make its verdict more fragile. Both forms hold the strict triadic verdict at every interior p and read dyadic only at the degenerate endpoint p=0.5, so all four verdict-flip points coincide at p_v=0.50 (H1). On the magnitude axis the prediction inverts: the conjunctive hub sheds the larger fraction of its clean Φ at every interior point and carries the steeper clean-limit log-slope, while the parity decay curve is the same at n=3 and n=4 (H2). The flip-gap does not widen with size; on the governing effective register it goes the other way, from −0.01 at n=3 to −0.04 at n=4, because the conjunctive form reaches the near-zero band earlier than the size-independent parity form (H3). The (1−2p)^n closed form lands within one grid step of the parity threshold but only degenerately, and it predicts an n-dependent curve where the measured parity decay is flat in n, so its shape is wrong (H4). Neither form has an interior susceptibility peak; both are monotone glides with |dΦ/dp| maximal at the clean corner (H5). The verdict's robustness to noise until the degenerate endpoint is a property of the verdict, holding for a pure-synergy hub with clean Φ=0.25 as much as for a conjunctive hub with clean Φ=3.0, not an artifact of only ever measuring high-Φ conjunctive forms.
