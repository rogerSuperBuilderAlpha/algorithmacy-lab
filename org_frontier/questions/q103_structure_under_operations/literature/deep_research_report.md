# Q103 literature — the structure under the operations

## The operations and their verdict effects

The program established what its operations do to the verdict. Substitution collapses the triad: a
multi-homing worker or an interchangeable counterpart factors the form (Q70, Finding 5). A tracking memory
preserves the triad and shifts the core to {M, R, Mem} (Q92). Recipient-side delegation puts the triage
agent in the core when it is bidirectionally coupled (Q68). These are verdict-and-membership results. What
the operations do to the cause-effect structure is unread.

## The question

Q100 found the structure orthogonal to the scalar in richness. At the collapse boundary, though, the two
should move together: an operation that drives Φ to zero should empty the integrated structure, and an
operation that preserves Φ while changing the core should restructure the joint mechanism so the new party
enters. Whether the structure follows the operations this way — collapsing under substitution, restructuring
under memory and delegation — is the integrative question that closes the loop between the structure wave
and the operation sweeps.
