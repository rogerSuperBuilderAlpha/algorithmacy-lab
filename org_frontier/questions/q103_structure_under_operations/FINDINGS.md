# Q103 findings — the whole-system structure collapses under substitution but diverges from membership

One hypothesis confirmed, three refuted in a way that closes the loop with Q74. Substitution empties the
integrated structure, as the verdict predicts. But the operations that move the major complex — a tracking
memory, delegation — do not move the whole-system joint mechanism. The new parties appear in the structure
as relays and in spanning purviews, not as members of a joint mechanism. The whole-system cause-effect
structure is not a faithful readout of major-complex membership.

## What it says

Substitution collapses the structure. The substitutable form has system Φ = 0 and no joint mechanism: an
operation that factors the verdict empties the integrated structure (H1). This is the clean case where
structure and verdict move together.

The other operations diverge from membership. Q92 found that a tracking memory shifts the core to
{M, R, Mem}, and Q68 that delegation puts the triage agent in the core. The whole-system cause-effect
structure does not show this. The tracking-memory form keeps Φ = 2 but carries no joint mechanism at all —
its structure is a spanning distinction {M} → {E, R} and the memory enters only as a relay, in the
singletons {R} → {Mem} and {Mem} → {M} (H2 refuted). The delegation form keeps a joint mechanism, but it is
the original {E, R}; the triage agent appears in the mediator's spanning purview {M} → {E, T} and as a
singleton, not in the joint mechanism (H3 refuted). The new parties are in the structure, but elsewhere
than the major complex puts them.

The whole-system structure and the major complex diverge, which is Q74 at the cause-effect level. Q74
established that the whole-system verdict and the major-complex verdict can disagree, and that membership is
read on the complex. The same holds for the structure: the whole-system cause-effect structure stays
anchored to the original parties or dissolves to a spanning distinction, while the major complex absorbs the
new party. H4 fails because the tracking-memory form is triadic with Φ = 2 yet carries no joint mechanism on
the whole system — a triad whose integration sits in a sub-complex the whole-system reading does not
surface. The structure wave's earlier results (Q99–Q102) read mediated and symmetric forms where the
whole system is the complex; this finding marks the boundary of that reading.

## Caveats

- **Mixed result.** H1 confirmed; H2, H3, H4 refuted, the refutations revealing the whole-system/complex
  divergence rather than a failure of the operations to restructure the major complex, which Q92 and Q68
  established.
- **Whole-system reading.** The structure is read on the whole system, not the major complex; the new
  parties' membership in the complex is a Q92/Q68 result and is not contradicted. A major-complex CES read
  would surface them, and is the natural follow-up.
- **Four operations.** Substitution, memory, delegation, read at the integrating state. In-silico.
