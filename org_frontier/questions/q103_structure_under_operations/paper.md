# Q103 — The cause-effect structure under the operations

## Question

The program established what its operations do to the verdict and to membership: substitution collapses the
triad, a tracking memory shifts the core to {M, R, Mem}, delegation puts the triage agent in the core. What
they do to the cause-effect structure is unread. This study asks whether the structure follows — whether
substitution empties it and whether memory and delegation restructure the joint mechanism to admit the new
party.

## Method

The read-recipient triad and three operations: substitution (the Q64 substitutable form), a tracking memory
(Q92), and delegation (the Q68 triage bidirectional form). For each, the integrated structure is read at the
integrating state, returning the system Φ, the dual type, and the parties appearing in any joint
distinction. A factored form (Φ = 0) carries no joint mechanism.

## Results

Substitution empties the structure: the substitutable form has Φ = 0 and no joint mechanism. The other
operations keep Φ positive but do not admit the new party to a joint mechanism. The tracking-memory form
carries no joint mechanism at all — a spanning distinction {M} → {E, R}, with the memory in the singletons
{R} → {Mem} → {M}. The delegation form keeps the original joint mechanism {E, R}, with the triage agent in
the mediator's spanning purview {M} → {E, T}, not in the joint mechanism.

| operation | Φ | joint-mechanism parties | the new party |
|---|---|---|---|
| read_recipient | 2.0 | {E, R} | — |
| substitution | 0.0 | (none) | structure empty |
| tracking memory | 2.0 | (none) | Mem as a relay |
| delegation | 1.0 | {E, R} | T in a spanning purview |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 substitution collapses the structure | confirmed |
| H2 memory admits Mem to the joint mechanism | refuted |
| H3 delegation admits T to the joint mechanism | refuted |
| H4 a joint mechanism is present iff triadic | refuted |

## Interpretation

The whole-system cause-effect structure tracks the verdict at the collapse boundary but not at the
membership boundary. Substitution drives Φ to zero and the structure empties with it, so the clean case
holds: an operation that factors the form leaves no integrated structure. But the operations that preserve
the triad while changing the core do not move the whole-system joint mechanism to match. The memory and the
triage agent enter the structure, as a relay and in a spanning purview, while the joint mechanism stays
anchored to the original parties or dissolves.

This is Q74 at the level of structure. Q74 found that the whole-system verdict and the major-complex verdict
can disagree, and that membership is read on the complex. The cause-effect structure inherits the same
split: the major complex of the tracking-memory form is {M, R, Mem}, but the whole-system structure carries
no joint mechanism over those three, only a spanning distinction over the original two. A triad can have its
integration in a sub-complex that the whole-system reading does not surface, which is why a tracking-memory
form is triadic with Φ = 2 yet shows no joint mechanism on the whole system.

The result marks the boundary of the structure wave's earlier readings. Q99 through Q102 read mediated and
symmetric forms where the whole system is the complex, so the whole-system structure was the right object.
For forms where the major complex is a proper subset, the structure must be read on the complex, not the
whole system, to recover the membership that Q92 and Q68 established. Reading the structure as meaning
carries the same reporting rule the scalar verdict needed.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with the exact cause-effect structure at the integrating state. The structure is
read on the whole system, not the major complex; the new parties' membership in the complex is a Q92/Q68
result, not contradicted here, and a major-complex structure read would surface them. Four operations, read
at one state. The refutations reveal the whole-system/complex divergence, not a failure of the operations to
restructure the core.
