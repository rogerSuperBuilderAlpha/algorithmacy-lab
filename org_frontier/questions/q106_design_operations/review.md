# Q106 — Stage 1 review: the design operations

## The question

Q105 found the dyad/triad boundary thin and the build usually at a party's read. A distance is a measure; a
designer needs named operations with known effects. What is the catalog, and does it follow from the law's
conditions?

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The law has three conditions (Findings).** Binding (the mediator reads every party), liveness (each
  party stays live, Finding 3), and requirement (parties jointly needed, not substitutable, Finding 5).
  Each is a candidate lever.
- **Building usually restores liveness (Q105).** The construction distance most often turns the liveness
  lever. The operations should name that lever and the others.
- **Substitution collapses the triad (Q70); a missing read collapses it (Q63).** These are break operations
  already on the record; the catalog names them and their inverses.

## The gap

No study names the design operations or shows they partition into the law's conditions. Whether the
operations are reversible levers, and whether they are exactly the law's conditions read as moves, is
uncomputed.
