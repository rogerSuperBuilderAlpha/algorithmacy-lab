# Q106 — The design operations: the law's conditions as reversible levers

## Question

Q105 found the dyad/triad boundary thin and the build usually at a party's read. A distance is a measure; a
designer needs operations — named moves with known effects. This study builds the catalog: a set of
operations, each moving the verdict in a stated direction, grounded in the law's conditions.

## Method

The law gives three conditions a coordination must meet to be triadic: the mediator binds every party
(binding), each party keeps itself live to the commit (liveness), and the parties are jointly required
rather than substitutable (requirement). Each condition is a lever with a build operation that supplies it
and a break operation that removes it, the two constructed as inverses. The verdict is read before and
after each of the six operations.

## Results

Every build operation takes its dyadic input to triadic, and every break operation takes its triadic input
to dyadic. The operations span exactly three levers, each with one build and one break, and the build and
break of each lever are inverses.

| operation | lever | direction | before → after |
|---|---|---|---|
| add_binding / remove_binding | binding | build / break | dyadic ⇄ triadic |
| restore_liveness / break_liveness | liveness | build / break | dyadic ⇄ triadic |
| require_all / substitute | requirement | build / break | dyadic ⇄ triadic |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 builds build | confirmed |
| H2 breaks break | confirmed |
| H3 inverse pairs | confirmed |
| H4 three levers | confirmed |

## Interpretation

The design vocabulary is the law restated as moves. The descriptive program read the verdict; the law said a
coordination is triadic exactly when three conditions hold. Reading those conditions as operations gives a
designer three reversible levers: supply or remove the mediator's read of a party, close or open a party's
loop to the mediator, require all parties or make one optional. Each lever turns the verdict, and turning it
back undoes the move. The catalog is three knobs, not six tricks, and the knobs are the conditions of the
law.

This is the account the dissertation's third paper needs. A design-operations chapter requires a vocabulary
of operations with stated effects, and the vocabulary cannot be invented separately from the diagnostic
criterion without risking incoherence. Here it is not invented separately: the operations are the
conditions, so the design account and the diagnostic account are the same account read in two directions.
The verdict says whether a coordination demands the competency; the operations say how to build the
coordination that does. They are dual readings of one law.

The catalog also frames the wave's other results. Q105's finding that builds usually restore liveness is a
statement about which lever the construction distance most often turns; Q107's finding that repair is
lever-specific is a statement that a broken lever must be the one restored. Both are about the levers this
study names, so the catalog is the vocabulary in which the design wave's results are stated.

## Limitations

In-silico; exact verdict on keystone before-and-after forms. The operations move the verdict on these
forms; that every form is moved by these operations is Q105's construction-distance result, not re-derived
here. The three levers match the law's conditions on the three-node and small-market forms; a richer family
could expose a lever these forms do not, and is untested.
