## What it says

When both parties delegate, the irreducible coordination can become the two agents. In a live exchange
where the humans stay coupled, the core is a human and the adjacent agent, a size-two end pair {Ar, R}.
When the humans recede to a frozen intent source and a passive readout while the agents mutually couple,
the core is exactly the two agents {As, Ar}, and both humans fall outside it. The agent-to-agent
coordination is irreducible on its own, triadic at Φ = 2.0, with no human in the core. Closing the whole
exchange into a loop binds all four at Φ = 4.0.

The reading for fully agent-mediated outreach is direct. When a sender and a recipient both hand the
exchange to agents and recede to setting intent and reading the outcome, the irreducible coordination is
between the agents. The humans are a source and a sink at the edges of a coordination whose core is the
two machines. A live human at either end keeps a human in the core; full delegation moves the core to the
agents.

## Caveats

- **Confirmatory.** The four predictions followed from Q66 and Q68; none was refuted. The agents-as-core
  result under full delegation is the headline.
- **In-silico.** Boolean models, evidence about the models. Φ read ordinally.
- **Frozen humans.** "Delegation" is modelled as a frozen intent source and a passive readout; a partly
  live human is a separate gradient, untested here.
