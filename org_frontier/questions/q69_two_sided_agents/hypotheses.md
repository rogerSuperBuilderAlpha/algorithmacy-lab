# Q69 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on the two-sided agent exchange (sender E, sender's agent As, recipient's agent Ar,
recipient R). Written and committed before any test runs.

## H1 — A live two-sided chain keeps a human in the core

- **Claim:** In the live chain E↔As↔Ar↔R (E'=As, As'=E∧Ar, Ar'=As∧R, R'=Ar), the major complex is a
  size-two human-agent end pair (a human is in the core), not the two agents.
- **H0:** The live-chain major complex is the two agents {As, Ar}.
- **Predicted outcome:** core is a human-agent end pair. H0 refuted.

## H2 — Delegating humans are displaced; the agents become the core

- **Claim:** When the humans are a frozen intent source and a frozen readout (E'=E, R'=R) while the agents
  mutually couple (As'=E∧Ar, Ar'=As∧R), the major complex is the two agents {As, Ar}; both humans are
  displaced.
- **H0:** The delegated major complex includes a human, or is not {As, Ar}.
- **Predicted outcome:** core = {As, Ar}. H0 refuted.

## H3 — Agent-to-agent coordination is irreducible on its own

- **Claim:** The delegated configuration's major complex is triadic (Φ_MIP > 0): the two agents form an
  irreducible coordination without a human in the core.
- **H0:** The delegated major complex is dyadic (Φ = 0).
- **Predicted outcome:** Φ > 0 on the agent core. H0 refuted.

## H4 — Closing the loop binds all four

- **Claim:** A closed four-element ring (each element reads both cyclic neighbours) has a major complex
  equal to the full set {E, As, Ar, R} at Φ > 2.0.
- **H0:** The ring's major complex is a proper subset, or Φ ≤ 2.0.
- **Predicted outcome:** core = all four at Φ = 4.0. H0 refuted.
