# When both sides delegate, the irreducible coordination is the two agents

## Abstract

> **Clarification (post-review).** The "both humans displaced" result is read on the maximal complex. The
> delegated form's whole-system verdict is dyadic (Φ_MIP = 0): modelling delegation as a frozen intent
> source (E'=E) and a passive readout (R'=R) makes the humans non-integrating stubs by construction, so
> their exclusion from the core is partly built into the form rather than discovered. The substantive,
> non-trivial content is that the two agents form a maximal complex at Φ = 2.0, equal in strength to the
> live exchange's human-agent core {Ar, R}. Read "the irreducible coordination is the agents" as a
> statement about the maximal complex of a whole-system-reducible form, contingent on this frozen-human
> model of delegation.

This study reads the two-sided agent exchange, where a sender and a recipient each delegate to an agent,
with exact IIT-4.0 Φ and the major complex. Four pre-registered hypotheses are tested over four elements:
sender intent (E), sender's agent (As), recipient's agent (Ar), recipient (R). In a live exchange the
core is a size-two human-agent end pair {Ar, R}. When the humans recede to a frozen intent source and a
passive readout while the agents mutually couple, the core becomes exactly the two agents {As, Ar}, with
both humans displaced, and that agent-to-agent coordination is triadic at Φ = 2.0. Closing the exchange
into a loop binds all four at Φ = 4.0. All four hypotheses confirmed: full delegation moves the
irreducible coordination from the humans to the agents.

## Introduction

Q63-Q68 read outreach with one mediating agent and a one-sided triage agent. The limit is both sides
delegating: a sender's agent composing for a recipient's agent. This study asks where the irreducible
coordination sits when everyone has an agent, and in particular whether the two agents can form the core
with both humans displaced.

## Related work

AI-mediated communication anticipates both sides delegating [hancock2020aimc; jakesch2019aimc]; the
economics of spam already has automated senders and filters [rao2012economics]. Coordination theory
locates coordination in the mediating mechanism [malone1994interdisciplinary]. Integrated information
theory decides membership by the major complex [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4;
mayner2018pyphi], building on Q66's chain-core result and Q68's displacement of the sender by a
bidirectional triage agent.

## Hypotheses

Fixed before computation (`hypotheses.md`). H1: a live two-sided chain keeps a human in the core. H2:
delegating humans are displaced and the agents become the core. H3: agent-to-agent coordination is
irreducible on its own. H4: closing the loop binds all four.

## Methods

Four elements (E, As, Ar, R). Three configurations: live_chain (E↔As↔Ar↔R), delegated (humans frozen as
source E'=E and readout R'=R while the agents mutually couple), closed_ring (each element reads both
cyclic neighbours). Each is read on the major complex (`probes.lib.major_complex`) with exact IIT-4.0 Φ;
the instrument is validated on a decoupled relay and a fully-coupled triad first. Exact forms and
decision rules are in `methods.md`.

## Results

The control passed (relay Φ = 0.000; triad Φ = 0.830).

**H1 (confirmed).** The live chain's major complex is {Ar, R} at Φ = 2.0, a recipient-and-agent end pair.
A human is in the core.

**H2 (confirmed).** The delegated configuration's major complex is exactly {As, Ar}. Both humans, the
frozen intent source and the passive readout, are outside the core.

**H3 (confirmed).** The delegated major complex is triadic at Φ = 2.0. The two agents form an irreducible
coordination with no human in the core.

**H4 (confirmed).** The closed ring's major complex is the full set {E, As, Ar, R} at Φ = 4.0.

## Discussion

Full delegation moves the irreducible coordination from the humans to the agents. When the humans stay
live, a human and the adjacent agent are the core. When the humans recede to setting intent and reading
the outcome, the core is the two agents, and the humans are a source and a sink at the edges. The
agent-to-agent coordination is irreducible by itself. For fully agent-mediated outreach this is the sharp
form of the worry the program started from: when a sender and a recipient both delegate, the coordination
that the instrument reads as irreducible is between the machines, and the people are outside it. Keeping a
human in the core requires keeping a human live in the exchange; closing the loop so the recipient's
state flows back to the sender binds all four into one core at twice the integration.

## Limitations

The study is confirmatory; the agents-as-core result under full delegation is the headline. Results are
in-silico: Boolean models, evidence about the models [axtell1996aligning]. Φ is read ordinally.
Delegation is modelled as a frozen source and a passive readout; a partly live human is a separate
gradient, untested. Hypotheses were fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong;
brodeur2024preregistration].
