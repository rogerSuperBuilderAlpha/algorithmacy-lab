# Q69 — Stage 2 deep research: both sides delegating

This study completes the two-sided case of Q63-Q68 and draws on the same literatures (shared
`references.bib`).

## Agents on both sides

AI-mediated communication anticipates both sides delegating: an agent composes for one party while
another agent processes for the other [hancock2020aimc; jakesch2019aimc]. The economics of spam already
has automated senders and automated filters [rao2012economics]; the two-sided agent exchange is the limit
where the humans set intent and the agents do the coordinating.

## Whose coordination is it

Coordination theory locates coordination in the mechanism that manages dependencies
[malone1994interdisciplinary]. When two agents mediate, the question is whether the irreducible
coordination is between the agents, between a human and an agent, or across all four. Integrated
information theory answers through the major complex [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4;
mayner2018pyphi]: the subset whose integration is locally maximal is the coordination that exists, and a
human reduced to a source or sink falls outside it.

## The open gap

No source computes whether two delegating agents form the irreducible core with the humans displaced. The
gap is open: build the four-element exchange under a live chain, a delegated configuration with the humans
as source and sink, and a closed loop, and read the core. In-silico, with the validation gap
[axtell1996aligning]; four hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong;
brodeur2024preregistration].
