# Q89 — Stage 1 review: heterogeneous agents in a market

## The question

Q85's agent market was symmetric (every agent read both outer parties) and collapsed all-or-nothing.
Real agent markets are heterogeneous: agents differ in what they read and in whether they are read.
Whether a heterogeneous market selects a stable core, and which agents enter it, is the open question its
caveat marks.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The interchangeable market collapses (Q85).** A substitutable market is a broadcast at every size;
  only an all-required set stays triadic with the full core. The symmetric baseline.
- **Core membership needs bidirectional coupling and pivotality (Finding 8, Q90).** A read-only or
  emit-only party stays out; pivotality is graded. The rule this study applies to unlike agents.
- **Substitutability collapses the form for any role (Finding 5).** Whether heterogeneity changes this is
  part of the question.

## The gap

Q85 tested only interchangeable agents. A market of agents that differ in what they read — full, partial,
or passive — is uncomputed, as is whether such a market forms a stable selected core or collapses. Whether
a partial reader that is bidirectional and required enters the core is the sharp uncomputed case.
