# Q89 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on a market of heterogeneous worker-side agents. Q85's market was symmetric and collapsed
all-or-nothing; this asks which agents enter the core when they differ in what they read and in whether
they are read forward. Worker E, agents M1.., counterpart C. Written and committed before the major
complexes are read. The all-required full pair (triadic, full core) and the substitutable full pair
(dyadic) reproduce Q85 and serve as the instrument control.

## H1 — A passive (read-only) agent is excluded

- **Claim:** In a market where one full agent reads both outer parties but is not read forward, that
  agent is not in the major complex, and the core is {E, M1, C}.
- **H0:** The passive agent is in the major complex.
- **Predicted outcome:** excluded. H0 refuted. (Bidirectional coupling is necessary; an unread agent is
  not bidirectional — Finding 8, Q90.)

## H2 — A partial-reading required agent enters the core

- **Claim:** A required agent that reads only the sender (not the counterpart) is in the major complex,
  and the market stays triadic.
- **H0:** The sender-only agent is excluded, or the market is dyadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** included and triadic. H0 refuted. Reading a single outer party suffices for
  membership when the agent is required and bidirectional; reading both is not necessary. This is the
  study's genuinely uncertain claim.

## H3 — Heterogeneous agents form one stable selected core

- **Claim:** A market of a full agent, a sender-only agent, and a recipient-only agent, all required, is
  triadic with a single core containing every agent plus E and C; and the recipient-only required agent
  is in its core.
- **H0:** Some required heterogeneous agent is excluded, or the market is dyadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** one stable mixed core of all five. H0 refuted. The heterogeneous market selects a
  stable core rather than collapsing all-or-nothing as the interchangeable market did.

## H4 — Substitutability still collapses the market

- **Claim:** A heterogeneous market whose outer parties read the agents through OR (substitutable) is
  dyadic.
- **H0:** The substitutable heterogeneous market is triadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** dyadic. H0 refuted. Heterogeneity does not rescue substitutability (Finding 5).
