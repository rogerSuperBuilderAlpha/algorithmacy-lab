# Q114 literature — the principal's rent

## Ownership and the core

Finding 8 established that a principal who owns the mediator joins the irreducible core only when
bidirectionally coupled — reading the mediator and being read by it. Ownership alone, monitoring or gating
without reciprocity, leaves the principal outside. Q111 valued the parties by their Shapley share. The
question here is what the principal's coupling is worth, and whether the value it captures is taken from the
parties or added to the whole.

## The two readings

A cynical reading expects rent: the owner inserts itself into the coordination and captures value at the
parties' expense, their shares falling as the principal's rises. A productive reading expects creation: the
principal that joins the joint determination adds to it, enlarging the coordination's value, so the
principal captures a share of a larger whole and the parties are no worse off. The discriminator is whether
the parties' absolute values fall or hold when the principal enters. A monitor-only principal, outside the
core, should capture nothing by either reading, and a read-only one that factors the whole system should
capture negative value.
