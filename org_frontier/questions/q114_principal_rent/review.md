# Q114 — Stage 1 review: the principal's rent

## The question

Finding 8 found a principal joins the core only when bidirectionally coupled. What is joining worth, and
does the principal's value come at the parties' expense (rent) or add to the total (creation)? The
discriminator is whether the parties' values fall or hold when the principal enters.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **A principal joins the core iff bidirectionally coupled (Finding 8).** Ownership without reciprocity
  leaves it outside. The bidirectional and monitor-only forms are on the record.
- **Value concentrates at the productive position (Q111).** The mediator captures two-thirds. Whether a
  principal captures by creating or extracting is the open question.
- **A read-only element factors the whole system and captures negative value (Q111).** A monitor-only
  principal should do the same.

## The gap

No study values the principal. Whether a coupled principal extracts rent from the parties or grows the
coordination, and whether ownership without coupling captures anything, is uncomputed.
