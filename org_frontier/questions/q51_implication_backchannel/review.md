# Q51 — implication back-channel · Stage 1 review

**Question.** Does adding a minimal worker–counterpart back-channel edge let implication-commit triads bind
irreducibly (Φ=2.0) with matched party reads, instead of the complementary reads that strict mediation
requires?

**Agenda id.** Grows from Q50 / probe #154 (H5). No dedicated agenda slot; the open thread is whether
back-channel coupling can swap the party-read rule for implication commits.

## The gap

Probe #154 split monotone Φ=2.0 forms by commit symmetry: symmetric commits need matched party reads,
implication commits need complementary reads. All tests ran under strict mediation (no W↔C edge). A
back-channel adds coupling between the outer parties beyond the four-edge floor and may restore pivotality
for matched-read implication forms that strict mediation excludes. No probe has asked whether implication
commits can bind at Φ=2.0 with matched reads once a minimal back-channel is present, or whether
back-channels preserve or disrupt the complementary-read binding that strict mediation requires.
