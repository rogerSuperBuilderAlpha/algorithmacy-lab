# Q51 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Five working hypotheses on whether a minimal worker–counterpart back-channel overturns the implication
party-read rule from Q50 probe #154. Written and committed before any test runs.

## H1 — Matched implication binds at Φ=2.0 with worker back-channel
- **Claim:** Adding a minimal worker-side back-channel (W'=f_W(S)∧C) to at least one matched-read
  implication-commit strict-mediation form makes it triadic at Φ=2.0.
- **H0:** Zero matched-read implication forms with the worker back-channel reach Φ=2.0.
- **Predicted outcome:** At least one of the eight matched forms (W1_S{2,4,11,13}_C1, W2_S{2,4,11,13}_C2)
  reads triadic with max_phi=2.0. H0 is refuted.

## H2 — Worker back-channel enables triadic binding for most matched implication forms
- **Claim:** The worker-side back-channel makes at least six of eight matched-read implication forms
  triadic (Φ>0), even if none reach the ceiling.
- **H0:** Fewer than six matched-read implication forms with the worker back-channel are triadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** Six or more of eight matched forms read triadic. H0 is refuted.

## H3 — Strict mediation control: matched implication stays dyadic
- **Claim:** Without a back-channel, all eight matched-read implication-commit strict-mediation forms are
  dyadic (Φ=0), replicating the Q50 H5 negative arm.
- **H0:** At least one matched-read implication form without back-channel is triadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** Zero of eight matched forms triadic under strict mediation. H0 is refuted.

## H4 — Back-channel preserves complementary implication at Φ=2.0
- **Claim:** The worker-side back-channel leaves all eight complementary-read implication forms that bind
  at Φ=2.0 under strict mediation still triadic at Φ=2.0.
- **H0:** At least one complementary-read implication form with the worker back-channel drops below Φ=2.0
  or becomes dyadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** All eight complementary forms stay triadic at max_phi=2.0. H0 is refuted.

## H5 — Symmetric two-sided back-channel unifies matched implication binding
- **Claim:** A symmetric two-sided back-channel (W'=f_W(S)∧C, C'=f_C(S)∧W) makes all eight matched-read
  implication forms triadic at a single shared max_phi value.
- **H0:** Fewer than eight matched forms are triadic, or their max_phi values differ across forms.
- **Predicted outcome:** All eight forms triadic with identical max_phi. H0 is refuted.
