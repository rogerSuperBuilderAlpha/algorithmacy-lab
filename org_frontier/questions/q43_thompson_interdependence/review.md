# Q43 — Thompson's interdependence types vs the dyadic/triadic verdict · Stage 1 review

**Question.** Does the IIT-4.0 dyadic/triadic verdict map onto Thompson's (1967) three types of
organizational interdependence — pooled to a dyadic/factorable coordination form, sequential to a
mediator chain, and reciprocal to an irreducible triad — when each type is modeled as a small Boolean
dynamical system and read on its exact-Φ verdict and major complex?

**Agenda id.** #43 from RESEARCH_AGENDA_50_V2.md ("Does the verdict correspond to Thompson's
interdependence types — pooled dyadic, sequential the chain, reciprocal triadic?").

## The gap

The three structures Thompson's typology names have each been modeled and given a verdict, but never
under Thompson's framework and never tested against his predicted ordering as a single mapping. Two
specific things are unanswered. First, the mapping does not hold as stated: a mediator chain (the
natural model of sequential interdependence) is already triadic at Φ=2.0 (#57), not an intermediate
category, and the all-required pool (a natural model of pooled interdependence) is the *most* strongly
irreducible structure of all (#116), the opposite of the predicted pooled=dyadic. So the open question
is which Boolean reading of each Thompson type is the faithful one — pooled as independent contributions
sharing a resource (which factors, #25/#40) versus pooled as an all-required joint output (which is
triadic, #116); sequential as a pass-through chain (triadic, #57) versus sequential as a one-way
acyclic hand-off with no return (dyadic, #39) — and whether, under a defensible single modeling
convention applied uniformly to all three, the verdict reproduces Thompson's pooled < sequential <
reciprocal ordering of "coordination demand" or breaks it. Second, no probe has built the three types
as a matched triple and read them together: the chain, the pool, and the reciprocal triad were studied
in different waves for different purposes, so the comparison Thompson's typology actually asks for —
same node count, same determination family, only the interdependence topology varied — has not been run.
The pieces exist; the controlled head-to-head and the resolution of the pooled/sequential modeling
ambiguity do not. No prior probe answers the question; together they make it answerable and already
suggest the naive mapping fails.
