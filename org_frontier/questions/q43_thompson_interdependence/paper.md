# Thompson's interdependence types against the dyadic/triadic verdict

<code + data: org_frontier/questions/q43_thompson_interdependence/ ; probes #135–#139 in probes/PROBES.md>

## Abstract

Thompson (1967) sorts organizational interdependence into three types of rising coordination demand: pooled, sequential, reciprocal. This paper asks whether the IIT-4.0 dyadic/triadic verdict reproduces that ordering when each type is built as a small Boolean dynamical system, matched on node count and determination family, and read on its exact-Φ verdict and major complex. The verdict breaks the ordering. Under one uniform convention at n=3 the verdict ranks pooled at Φ=0.0 (dyadic) and both sequential and reciprocal at Φ=2.0 (triadic), so the top step of the ordering collapses (H1, confirmed). Two of the three types come out verdict-ambiguous: a pooled form is dyadic when contributions are independent and triadic when one node is jointly determined by all contributors (H2, confirmed), and a sequential form is triadic when a return edge closes the chain and dyadic when the hand-off is acyclic (H3, confirmed). Reciprocal reads triadic only when its coupling closes an actual feedback cycle, and bidirectional labels without a cycle read dyadic (H4, confirmed). The proposed two-primitive replacement, verdict as a function of {joint determination, feedback cycle}, fails: four forms share the pair (True, True), three of them with identical connectivity matrices, yet they split across both verdicts (H5, refuted). The verdict turns on closed loops in the cause-effect structure, finer than Thompson's labels and finer than the coarse primitive pair.

## Introduction

Coordination theory needs a way to say when an arrangement is reducible to independent pieces and when it is not. Thompson's typology is the standard answer in organization theory. Pooled interdependence is parties drawing on and contributing to a common pool with no direct contact, coordinated by standardization. Sequential interdependence is a one-way chain where one unit's output is another's input, coordinated by plans. Reciprocal interdependence is mutual, each unit's output feeding back as another's input, coordinated by mutual adjustment. Thompson ranks the coordination burden pooled < sequential < reciprocal.

The IIT-4.0 classifier built in this program gives an independent reading of the same question. A Boolean form is dyadic when its most-integrated reachable state factors into independent parts, triadic when it stays bound. Prior probes had already modeled each of Thompson's three structures, in separate waves and for separate purposes, and the readings sat awkwardly against his ordering. The mediator chain that models sequential interdependence reads triadic at Φ=2.0 (#57), a top-of-scale verdict for a middle type. An all-required pool is the most strongly irreducible structure measured, with Φ rising with size (#116), the opposite of the predicted pooled=dyadic.

The gap from Stage 1 is that the three structures had never been built as a matched triple, holding node count and determination family fixed while only the interdependence topology varied, and read against Thompson's ordering as one mapping. The pieces existed. The controlled head-to-head and the resolution of the modeling ambiguity in "pooled" and "sequential" remained open.

## Related work

Thompson (1967) fixes the three interdependence types and their coordination-demand ordering used as the null throughout. The IIT-4.0 measure and its exact-Φ computation come from Albantakis et al. (2023); the dyadic/triadic verdict is the binary structure read off Φ over the most-integrated reachable state. The structural priors each hypothesis rests on are this program's own probes: chains and trees are triadic at Φ=2.0 regardless of depth (#57, #58); an independent-contribution relay factors to a {W,S} core while a joint commit binds the triad (#25, #40); the all-required pool scales as Φ≈n−1 with group surplus at n≥4 (#44, #52, #65, #105, #116); irreducibility needs a feedback cycle, an acyclic source→sink determination being dyadic (#5, #39); and the irreducible triad's MIP cuts at {W,SC} with the worker the weakest seam (#16, #26, #33).

The methodology this question follows is surveyed in the program's deep-research run. Stage 3's commitment to five falsifiable hypotheses with their nulls before any computation descends from Chamberlin's method of multiple working hypotheses, the structural defense against the unconscious selection that single-hypothesis work invites (Chamberlin 1890/1965). The "before any computation" clause is a pre-registration move (Nosek et al. 2018; Hardwicke and Wagenmakers 2023). The credibility comes from the per-hypothesis method plan rather than the bare act of pre-committing: an analysis of 15,992 RCT test statistics found the reduction in p-hacking only where a complete pre-analysis plan accompanied registration (Brodeur et al. 2024). Stage 5's reading against a ground-truth instrument is the docking standard, where forcing models into equivalence exposes hidden assumptions and where cross-model equivalence leaves the gap to the real system open (Axtell et al. 1996). Reporting refutations as first-class results answers publication and novelty bias (Lakens et al. 2024). H5's refutation here is such a result.

## Hypotheses

The five hypotheses were fixed and committed before any test ran. Each is stated with its null.

**H1, the naive ordering fails.** Under one uniform convention at fixed n, the verdict departs from pooled < sequential < reciprocal, with at least one ordering step inverting or collapsing. *Null:* the three verdicts come out strictly monotone in Thompson's order, pooled lowest and dyadic, sequential intermediate, reciprocal highest.

**H2, "pooled" is verdict-ambiguous.** Two defensible Boolean encodings of pooled over the same parts land on opposite verdicts at fixed node count, and the deciding feature is whether one downstream node is jointly determined by all contributors. *Null:* "pooled" has one verdict regardless of how the shared resource is wired.

**H3, "sequential" is verdict-ambiguous.** A pass-through propagating chain is triadic while a one-way acyclic source→sink hand-off factors, and the deciding feature is the return path. *Null:* "sequential" has one verdict invariant to how the chain is read.

**H4, reciprocal needs a cycle.** Reciprocal reads triadic only when the coupling closes an actual feedback cycle through both parties, and a bidirectional-but-acyclic variant reads dyadic. *Null:* any model with arrows drawn in both directions reads triadic.

**H5, only reciprocal is type-robust, and the verdict is a two-primitive function.** The verdict floats across verdicts for pooled and sequential, while reciprocal stays triadic when its cycle is intact, so the verdict tracks the primitive pair {joint determination, feedback cycle} over the Thompson label. *Null:* the verdict is a clean function of Thompson's three-way type, each type mapping to a single verdict stable across its reasonable encodings.

## Methods

Every form is a deterministic Boolean dynamical system. Each party's next state is a fixed function of the current node-state tuple `x`, little-endian (`x[0]` first party). Labels follow the classifier default: `W` worker, `S` system/mediator, `C` counterpart, `C1, C2` further parties. The matched triple holds node count and determination family fixed: every primary form uses AND of its inputs (`&`) at n=3 unless a size sweep is named. The verdict is the binary structure (dyadic vs triadic) read off Φ over the most-integrated reachable state, with `PHI_EPS = 1e-9` the zero threshold; Φ magnitude is an ordinal hint. Verdict, Φ, and major complex come from `org_frontier/classifier/classifier.py` and `org_frontier/probes/lib.py`; exact Φ from `proxy_audit/exact_phi.py`; the Python is `~/iit-playground/venv-4.0/bin/python`.

**Instrument control (run first).** The pass-through mediator chain at n=3, with `W'=S`, `S'=W&C`, `C'=S`, rules `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[1]]`, is the established triad (#57). It must reproduce triadic at max_phi=2.0 with MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}` before any comparison verdict is trusted. It passed in every probe: structure=triadic, max_phi=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`.

**H1.** A matched triple at n=3, AND family. Pooled (independent-contribution relay) `[x[1], x[0], x[1]]`; sequential (pass-through chain) the control form; reciprocal (feedback cycle) `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[1]]`. Measure each form's structure and max_phi and rank the three Φ values. Decision rule: H1 confirmed if pooled is not the strict Φ-minimum, or sequential does not fall strictly between pooled and reciprocal; refuted only if the three Φ values are strictly monotone in Thompson's order with pooled dyadic. Script: `probe_thompson_ordering.py`.

**H2.** Two pooled encodings over the same parts. Independent-contribution pool `[x[1], x[0], x[1]]`; all-required pool (the `pool(n)` form, `S'=AND of all non-S parties`, every non-S party `=S`) at n=3 and n=4. Measure structure and max_phi for both; major complex for the independent pool; whole-minus-best-proper-subset Φ surplus for the all-required pool. Decision rule: confirmed if the two encodings land on opposite verdicts at n=3. Script: `probe_thompson_pooled.py`.

**H3.** Two sequential encodings at n=3, AND family. Propagating chain (the control form); acyclic source→sink hand-off `[x[0], x[0]&x[2], x[2]]` (each stage sources the next, nothing returns). Measure structure, max_phi, and the propagating chain's MIP. Decision rule: confirmed if the two split, propagating triadic and acyclic dyadic. Script: `probe_thompson_sequential.py`.

**H4.** Two reciprocal encodings at n=3, AND family, both nominally two-way. Cyclic reciprocal `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[1]]` (coupling closes a loop); bidirectional-acyclic `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[2]]` (a return arrow is drawn but `C'=C` is a self-loop that closes no cycle). Measure structure and max_phi for both, plus major complex and MIP for the cyclic form. Decision rule: confirmed if cyclic reads triadic and bidirectional-acyclic reads dyadic. Script: `probe_thompson_reciprocal.py`.

**H5.** The full 2×3 panel from H2–H4 as one table, all n=3, AND family. Each form is coded on two primitives: joint determination present (some node's rule is an AND over ≥2 parties, read off the rule body and cross-checked against `cm_from_rules`) and feedback cycle present (the connectivity matrix contains a directed cycle through ≥2 parties). Two functional-dependence checks: (a) verdict is not a function of Thompson type (some type maps to both verdicts); (b) verdict is a function of the primitive pair (no two forms share a pair yet differ). Decision rule: confirmed if pooled and sequential each span both verdicts and every form with a primitive present reads triadic while every form with neither reads dyadic; refuted if Thompson type predicts verdict cleanly or if two forms with the same primitive pair disagree. Script: `probe_thompson_primitives.py`.

## Results

**H1, confirmed.** The matched triple at n=3 gives Φ in Thompson order 0.0, 2.0, 2.0. Pooled (independent-contribution relay) is dyadic, Φ=0.000000, MIP `(factors)`. Sequential (pass-through chain) is triadic, Φ=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`. Reciprocal (feedback cycle) is triadic, Φ=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`. The decision booleans: pooled is the strict Φ-minimum (True) and is dyadic (True), but sequential fails to fall strictly between pooled and reciprocal (False, 2.0 ties 2.0), so the verdict departs from strict monotonicity in Thompson's order (False). The top step pooled < sequential < reciprocal collapses, with sequential tying reciprocal. The null is refuted and H1 holds. (`results/thompson_ordering.csv`.)

**H2, confirmed.** Two pools at the same size n=3 in the same AND family land on opposite verdicts. The independent-contribution pool (`W'=S, S'=W, C'=S`) is dyadic, max_phi=0.000000, MIP `(factors)`, with major complex core {W,S} at Φ=2.000000. The all-required pool (#116) is triadic at every size: n=3 gives max_phi=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC1}`, best-proper-subset Φ=2.000000, group surplus +0.000000; n=4 gives max_phi=3.000000, MIP `4 parts: {W,S,C1,C2}`, best-proper-subset Φ=2.000000, group surplus +1.000000. Φ≈n−1 holds and the group surplus turns positive at n=4. The deciding feature is joint determination, isolated by holding node count and family fixed. H2 holds. (`results/thompson_pooled.csv`.)

**H3, confirmed.** The two sequential encodings split. The propagating chain (`W'=S, S'=W&C, C'=S`) is triadic, max_phi=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`. The acyclic hand-off (`W'=W, S'=W&C, C'=C`) is dyadic, max_phi=0.000000, MIP `(factors)`. The connectivity matrices differ only in the return edge: the propagating chain reads C back into the chain via `S'=W&C` and `C'=S`, while the hand-off's stages carry their own prior state forward (`W'=W`, `C'=C`) with no return. The return edge decides the verdict. H3 holds. (`results/h3_sequential.csv`.)

**H4, confirmed.** The cyclic reciprocal `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[1]]` is triadic, max_phi=2.000000, MIP `2 parts: {W,SC}`, max_phi_state (1,1,1), major complex core {W,S,C} at Φ=2.000000; its CM edges W→S, S→W, S→C, C→S close a loop. The bidirectional-acyclic `[x[1], x[0]&x[2], x[2]]` is dyadic, max_phi=0.000000, MIP `(factors)`; its CM edges W→S, S→W, C→S, C→C carry a `C'=C` self-loop that closes no cycle. Both forms carry arrows in both directions by label; only the one whose return edge closes a feedback cycle is triadic. The worker sits on the weakest seam, MIP `{W,SC}`. H4 holds. (`results/h4_reciprocal.csv`.)

**H5, refuted.** Claim (a) holds: pooled spans {dyadic, triadic} and sequential spans {dyadic, triadic}, so the verdict floats free of Thompson type. Claim (b) fails. The primitive pair leaves the verdict undetermined. Four forms share the pair (joint_determination=True, feedback_cycle=True), namely pooled all-required, sequential propagating-chain, reciprocal cyclic, and reciprocal bidirectional-acyclic, and three of them carry identical connectivity matrices, yet they split: the cyclic form is triadic at Φ=2.0 while the bidirectional-acyclic form is dyadic at Φ=0.0. The cycle predicate also fires on the dyadic independent-contribution pool, which the panel codes (joint_determination=False, feedback_cycle=True) at Φ=0.0. Two forms with the same primitive pair disagree, so the primitive pair fails as a function for the verdict. The proposed two-primitive replacement for Thompson's typology collapses. H5 is refuted. The instrument control passed (triadic, Φ=2.0, {W,SC}). (`results/thompson_primitives.csv`.)

## Discussion

Thompson's three labels track the verdict loosely, and the failure is specific. The ordering probe locates the break at the top: pooled coordination is dyadic at Φ=0, while sequential and reciprocal both reach Φ=2.0, so the predicted final step is absent. The pooled and sequential probes then show that two of the three types take both verdicts. Pooled is dyadic with independent contributions and triadic when one node is jointly determined. Sequential is triadic with a return edge and dyadic as an acyclic hand-off. In each case a single edge moves the verdict. That invites the natural fix tested in H5: drop the labels and read the verdict off two structural primitives, joint determination and a feedback cycle.

That fix fails, and its failure is the through-line. The two primitives, as coded from the rule bodies and the connectivity matrix, leave the verdict undetermined. Four forms carry both primitives, three of them with the same connectivity matrix, and still split across dyadic and triadic, and the cycle predicate fires on a form that factors. A directed cycle in the connectivity matrix is a different object from a closed loop in the cause-effect structure that IIT integrates over. The bidirectional-acyclic reciprocal carries the arrows, yet the `C'=C` self-loop drains the return, and the integrated structure factors despite the loop drawn on the graph. The verdict tracks closed loops in the actual cause-effect structure, finer than Thompson's labels and finer than the coarse {joint determination, feedback cycle} pair. This extends the earlier chain and pool findings (#57, #116). The structures Thompson names were already known to land on the "wrong" side of his ordering, and the matched triple now shows the ordering itself is the wrong carving.

## Limitations

Every form is n=3 except the n=4 pool in H2, and the verdict is read at a fixed grain on deterministic Boolean rules in the AND family. The size ceiling and the determination family were held fixed by design to isolate topology, so the results stay silent on OR, parity, or majority families and on larger systems. The verdict is the structure over the most-integrated reachable state at `PHI_EPS=1e-9`, and Φ magnitude is reported as an ordinal hint. "Φ=2.0 ties Φ=2.0" speaks to irreducibility structure, leaving equal coordination cost in Thompson's sense untouched. The primitive coding in H5 is one defensible operationalization of "joint determination" and "feedback cycle." A finer cause-effect-structure predicate might recover predictivity, and the failure of the connectivity-matrix version leaves that possibility open. The result holds about the model. Two Boolean models can be numerically identical and both wrong about real organizations (Axtell et al. 1996), so a passed in-silico test is evidence about the form, with the organizations Thompson's typology describes still beyond reach.
