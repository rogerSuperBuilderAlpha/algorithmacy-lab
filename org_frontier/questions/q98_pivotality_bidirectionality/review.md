# Q98 — Stage 1 review: decoupling reading from influence

## The question

The membership law ties bidirectional coupling and pivotality together, but bidirectionality has two
sides: how much a party reads, and how much it is read. Whether these are independently necessary or
whether one compensates the other is unmapped. The answer decides whether a party can enter the
coordination by influence alone.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Bidirectional coupling is necessary; pivotality is graded (Finding 8, Q90).** A node failing either
  half essentially never enters the core. The law this study takes apart.
- **Influence requires membership (Q84).** A read-only adversary cannot flip the verdict from outside the
  core. Q98 asks the structural form of that result: can strong influence without reading enter the core
  at all?
- **The pivotality gradient is moderate on the full family (Q90, rank-AUC ≈ 0.62–0.73).** A single scalar
  may under-describe pivotality; separating its two sides may sharpen it.

## The gap

The two sides of bidirectionality have never been separated. Whether reading and influence are each
independently necessary, and whether membership tracks their minimum (a conjunctive gate) or their sum (an
additive trade-off), is uncomputed.
