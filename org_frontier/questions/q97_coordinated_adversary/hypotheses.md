# Q97 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on a coalition of non-core agents trying to change the verdict that a single one cannot.
Q84 showed one read-only agent cannot flip the triad, and influence requires membership. Q97 adds two
external agents to the read-recipient triad and tests a coalition of observers, a coalition of sources, and
a bridging loop that is collectively bidirectional while each member is unidirectional with respect to the
core. Written and committed before the coalition forms are read. The single bidirectional-pivotal agent
(triadic, core {E, M, R, X}) is the instrument control.

## H1 — Two observers cannot destroy the triad

- **Claim:** With two read-only agents added (each reads the mediator, read by none), the form stays
  triadic with core {E, M, R}.
- **H0:** The two observers destroy the triad or change its core.
- **Predicted outcome:** core {E, M, R}, triadic. H0 refuted. Observation does not break the coordination,
  even by two agents.

## H2 — Two sources cannot manufacture a triad

- **Claim:** A broadcast (the mediator ignores the recipient) with two emit-only sources added stays
  dyadic.
- **H0:** The two sources manufacture a triad.
- **Predicted outcome:** dyadic. H0 refuted. Feeding a form from outside, without being read into its
  determination, does not create a core.

## H3 — A coalition gains no influence without membership

- **Claim:** In the bridging loop, where one agent reads the core and the other is read by the core, the
  coalition flips the triad only if the member the core reads is itself in the major complex; the core is
  either unchanged at {E, M, R} or contains that member.
- **H0:** The coalition flips the verdict with no coalition member in the core.
- **Predicted outcome:** no influence without membership. H0 refuted. This is the study's central and
  genuinely uncertain claim — collective bidirectionality could grant the coalition influence its members
  lack individually.

## H4 — A single bidirectional-pivotal agent flips and joins

- **Claim:** One bidirectional-pivotal agent makes the form triadic with the agent in the core.
- **H0:** It does not flip the verdict, or stays outside the core.
- **Predicted outcome:** triadic with the agent in the core. H0 refuted (the Q84 control).
