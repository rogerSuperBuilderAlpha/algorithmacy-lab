# Q102 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on the relation skeleton of coordination. Relations bind distinctions over shared purviews.
Q100 found the breadth market carries 128 relations against the read-recipient triad's 8. This reads where
those relations sit. Written and committed before the all_required, ring, and market skeletons are read.
The read-recipient skeleton (8 relations, hub the mediator with share 0.875) is the instrument control.

## H1 — Mediated triads concentrate relations on a hub

- **Claim:** In the read-recipient triad and the all-required market, one purview — the mediator — carries
  more than half the relations.
- **H0:** No single purview carries a majority.
- **Predicted outcome:** a hub above 0.5. H0 refuted. Mediated coordination relates the parties' distinctions
  through the mediator.

## H2 — The breadth market is denser in relations

- **Claim:** The all-required market's relations-per-distinction exceeds the read-recipient triad's.
- **H0:** It does not.
- **Predicted outcome:** higher density. H0 refuted. The hub creates relations combinatorially as the
  distinctions sharing it multiply.

## H3 — Symmetric triads spread their relations

- **Claim:** In the ring and the required market, no single purview carries more than half the relations.
- **H0:** A symmetric triad has a hub above 0.5.
- **Predicted outcome:** no hub. H0 refuted. Symmetric coordination relates two groups across purviews, with
  no single binder.

## H4 — The breadth hub is combinatorial

- **Claim:** In the all-required market, the hub carries more than 90% of relations, and at least one
  relation binds five or more distinctions.
- **H0:** The hub share is below 0.9, or no relation binds five distinctions.
- **Predicted outcome:** a dominant hub with high-order relations — the 128 count is every subset of the
  hub-sharing distinctions relating over the mediator.
