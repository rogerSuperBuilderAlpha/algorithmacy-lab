# Q102 literature — the shape of the relation structure

## The relation count to explain

Q100 found that relation count does not track Φ and that a mediated breadth market carries 128 relations
against a minimal triad's 8. A count is a summary; the question is the shape behind it. Relations in IIT 4.0
bind distinctions that overlap on a shared purview, so the relation structure is a hypergraph over the
distinctions, and its shape says how a coordination's parts relate.

## The expected shape

A mediated triad has a single party, the mediator, whose purview the other parties' distinctions all share.
Their relations should therefore concentrate over the mediator — a hub — and the count should grow
combinatorially as the number of distinctions sharing the hub grows, because every subset of them relates
over it. A symmetric triad has no such party: its two groups relate across several purviews, so the
relations spread. If this holds, the relation skeleton distinguishes mediated from symmetric coordination
more concretely than the counts, and explains the breadth market's explosion as a hub effect.

## Method context

The relation structure is read from `phi_structure`, with each relation's purview keyed by its node
indices, on forms already on the record.
