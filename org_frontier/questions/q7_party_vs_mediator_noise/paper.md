# Party noise versus mediator noise: which seat carries the flip changes the Φ curve

<code + data: org_frontier/questions/q7_party_vs_mediator_noise/ ; probes #145–#149 in probes/PROBES.md>

## Abstract

A triadic conjunctive mediated chain runs W' = S, S' = W∧C, C' = S. The same flip-noise of strength p that Q6 put on the mediator's commit can instead ride on a party's update. This paper sweeps p on a 51-point grid from the clean limit p=0 to the random output p=0.5 for three injection sites (the mediator S, a single party W, and both parties W and C together) and reads Φ_MIP, its clean-limit slope, the dyadic/triadic verdict, and the MIP at each step. Party noise and mediator noise trace different curves: the gap reaches 0.9237 at p=0.01, and at p=0.10 the mediator curve sits at 1.1887 against the party curve's 0.7632 (H1, confirmed). The two parties are one seat: the W-only and C-only curves coincide at all 51 grid points to numerical zero, with matching verdict-flip points (H2, confirmed). Both sites keep the same strict verdict, triadic at every interior p and dyadic only at p=0.5, so party noise holds the verdict to the same endpoint the mediator does (H3, refuted). The party curve drops far steeper near the clean corner (slope 102.44 against the mediator's 10.06), then the two curves cross, with Φ_W exceeding Φ_S at 17 of 49 interior points from p=0.33 on, so the predicted ordering fails (H4, refuted). Noising both parties at once is strongly non-separable. Φ_WC at p=0.10 is 1.3738, above both the multiplicative null 0.2912 and the single-party 0.7632, yet the effective collapse holds at p_eff(WC)=p_eff(W)=0.5 (H5, partial). The seat that carries the flip sets the curve; the two parties share one seat against the mediator's other seat; jointly noising the parties raises the interior Φ and leaves the collapse where it was.

## Introduction

Where noise enters a coordination structure may matter as much as how much noise there is. A mediated triad has two kinds of place to put it. The mediator commits: it reads the parties and binds them. The parties update by reading the mediator and carrying its commit forward. A manager who can harden one channel against noise wants to know which channel, the hub or the spoke, costs the most integration when it degrades.

Q6 fixed one half of this. It put flip-noise on the mediator's commit S' = W∧C and found a smooth exponential Φ decay with the dyadic/triadic verdict stepping only at the degenerate endpoint p=0.5. That is the curve for one site. Whether the party site gives the same curve was left open. The matched manipulation moves the same flip-noise model off the commit and onto a party's update, on the same grid, so the two sites are read against a same-run reference.

The form is the triadic conjunctive mediated chain at n=3 with labels (W, S, C) and clean rules W' = S, S' = W∧C, C' = S (probes #26, #33, #27). Flip-noise of strength p is output noise on a node's TPM column: the output lands on its clean value with reliability 1−p and flips with probability p, so the noise rides in the mechanism while the initial state stays fixed. The mediator site is column 1 (the Q6 baseline), the party site W is column 0, the party site C is column 2, and the joint-party site noises columns 0 and 2 independently with the same p on each.

The question matters for coordination theory because it separates two readings of robustness. If the curves coincide, robustness is a property of the form and the seat is irrelevant. If they diverge, robustness is seat-relative, and the structural position of a unit (hub or periphery) decides how much its noise costs. The gap from Stage 2 is that no prior probe moved the flip off the mediator and asked.

## Related work

No published source runs the Q7 experiment. Nobody has taken a triadic mediated W/S/C form, injected matched output flip-noise into the parties versus the mediator, and compared the two Φ(p) decay curves and verdict-flip points. That comparison is an open gap.

The surrounding literature is one-directional. Where noise enters a coupled system changes its effect on integration, and the effect is asymmetric across sites. The closest analog computes IIT Φ on an integrate-and-fire network under two noise placements: noise injected inside the existing units alters Φ unpredictably and can raise it, while the same noise added as a separate external element drives Φ to zero by making the system decomposable [danilczuk2026iaf]. Same magnitude, two placements, opposite outcomes. That paper also reports the IIT-3.0 measure swings widely under noise, with relative Φ variations across roughly ±400%.

The mechanism that carries such an asymmetry into Φ is the minimum information partition. System integrated information φ_s is set at the MIP, the connectivity-determined fault line, and is bounded by whichever of the cause and effect sides is more degraded [marshall2023sii; albantakis2023iit4]. A peripheral unit's coupling already governs irreducibility: in the worked example of three tightly coupled units plus a weakly coupled fourth, the MIP cuts the fourth off and integrated information falls to 10% of the system's intrinsic information [marshall2023sii]. Noise that lands on a unit the MIP already isolates moves φ_s less than noise on a unit that straddles the fault line. Adding per-unit indeterminism monotonically lowers the ceiling on φ_s, which fixes the direction of every noise effect as down without fixing the rate [marshall2023sii].

Two further results bear on the curve and the analogy. Integrated information decays approximately exponentially under uniform output noise, with 5% bit-flip noise erasing about 70% of it [mediano2021common], the published counterpart to the Q6 mediator curve under a global noise placement. In directed networks, upstream source nodes without incoming links act as an irreducible noise source that suppresses collective ordering, while the recurrent feedback core supports it, so party-like and hub-like sites carry noise through structurally distinct channels [harush2019peripheral]. A control-theory threshold lets noise enter a synchronization condition through two constants of opposite sign, so placement and design, not magnitude alone, decide whether coordination collapses [russo2016noise]. Φ_MIP also shifts under small structural changes: on five-node logic-gate networks most single perturbations cut Φ by at least half, and the effect depends strongly on the site [schwitzgebel2018phi; barrett2019phinotwelldefined]. The direction is down and the p=0.5 endpoint is fixed for any single noised unit [mayner2018pyphi], while the rate of decay and the interior verdict behavior are the free quantities the question turns on.

## Hypotheses

The five hypotheses were fixed before any computation.

H1: party noise and mediator noise trace different Φ(p) curves. Flip-noise on a party yields a Φ_MIP(p) curve that separates from the mediator-noise curve at interior p. Null: the two curves coincide pointwise within numerical tolerance across (0, 0.5).

H2: W-noise and C-noise give the same curve as each other. Flip-noise on party column W alone and on party column C alone produce identical Φ_MIP(p), identical verdict-flip points, so the party site is one curve. Null: the worker and counterpart load on Φ_MIP unequally, so the party site is itself two curves.

H3: the party verdict flips at an interior p below the degenerate endpoint. Party noise collapses the triadic/dyadic verdict at a finite p* in (0, 0.5), strictly below the p=0.5 at which mediator noise flips it. Null: the party verdict flips only at p=0.5, exactly as the mediator verdict does.

H4: party noise decays Φ faster than mediator noise. The clean-limit slope |dΦ/dp| at small p is larger for the party site than the mediator site, and the party curve sits at or below the mediator curve across the interior. Null: the mediator is at least as steep, or the ordering reverses at some interior p.

H5: two simultaneously-noised parties interact. Independent flip-noise on W and C together gives a Φ_MIP(p) curve that departs from the separable composition of the single-party curves, and its effective collapse precedes the single party's. Null: the joint curve is the separable composition and its effective collapse still sits at p=0.5.

## Methods

The measure is Φ_MIP from IIT-4.0 (`max_phi_float`, max exact Φ over reachable states with the connectivity matrix inferred numerically), and the binary verdict and MIP location from `classify`, with PHI_EPS = 1e-9 the zero threshold for the strict verdict. The verdict is the load-bearing object. Φ magnitude is an ordinal hint, and the Q7 claims rest on the shape and ordering of the curves, treating no single Φ as a true scale.

The noise model is output flip-noise applied to a node's TPM column. For a noised column c with clean values col_clean ∈ {0,1} per row, the column becomes P(out=1) = (1−p)·col_clean + p·(1−col_clean). The three sites are S (column 1, the Q6 baseline), W (column 0), C (column 2), and the joint site WC (columns 0 and 2, same p on each). The grid is 51 uniform points, p = 0.00, 0.01, …, 0.50, the same grid as Q6 so the mediator curve is directly comparable. Numerical tolerance throughout is TOL = 1e-6, the Φ-reporting precision that fixes the clean value to 2.0.

The instrument control ran first and passed for every site. The clean form at p=0 reads structure triadic, max_phi = 2.0 to 1e-6, MIP "2 parts: {W,SC}", and max_phi_float(noisy_tpm(0.0, cols)) = 2.0 for S, W, C, and WC. The four sweeps share the clean-triad left endpoint regardless of which column would be noised.

H1 reads the pointwise gap g(p) = Φ_W(p) − Φ_S(p) over the interior grid, its maximum absolute value g_max, and the p achieving it. The self-control feeds the mediator curve against itself, Φ_S − Φ_S, which must be zero at every point so any nonzero g_max is a genuine site difference. Decision rule: confirmed if g_max > 1e-3 at one or more interior p; refuted if |g(p)| ≤ 1e-6 everywhere.

H2 reads the pointwise difference d(p) = Φ_W(p) − Φ_C(p) at all 51 points, the count of points above TOL, max|d|, and each site's verdict-flip point. The positive control feeds Φ_W against the mediator curve Φ_S through the same machinery, which must flag many points so the difference statistic can resolve a real gap. Decision rule: confirmed if |d(p)| ≤ 1e-6 at every grid point and the flip points match; refuted otherwise.

H3 reads two collapse points per site. The strict-verdict collapse p_v is the smallest grid p at which the structure reads dyadic. The effective collapse p_eff is the smallest grid p at which Φ_MIP < 0.01, a fixed near-zero band declared before the run and applied identically to both sites. Decision rule on the strict register, which governs: confirmed if p_v(W) < p_v(S); refuted if both flip only at 0.5 or p_v(W) ≥ p_v(S).

H4 reads the clean-limit slope magnitude m_site = |Φ_site(0.01) − Φ_site(0.00)| / 0.01 by forward difference, and the sign of the interior ordering Φ_W − Φ_S across the 49 interior points. The slope comparison counts only if H1 holds. Self-control: m_S on repeat is the same number to TOL. Decision rule: confirmed if m_W > m_S and Φ_W(p) ≤ Φ_S(p) at every interior p; refuted if m_W ≤ m_S or the ordering reverses at any interior p.

H5 reads the residual against a separable null. The per-site Φ-drop fraction is f_site(p) = 1 − Φ_site(p)/2.0, and the multiplicative independent-flip prediction is Φ_pred(p) = 2.0·(1 − f_W(p))·(1 − f_C(p)); the residual is r(p) = Φ_WC(p) − Φ_pred(p). The test also reads p_eff(WC) against p_eff(W) at the 0.01 band. Because H2 makes f_W = f_C, the null reduces to Φ_pred = 2.0·(1 − f_W)². The positive control feeds Φ_pred against itself, which must read zero. Decision rule: confirmed if r_max > 1e-3 and p_eff(WC) < p_eff(W) by at least one grid step; refuted if both null conditions hold; partial if exactly one holds.

## Results

H1: confirmed. The party and mediator curves separate. The gap g_max reaches 0.923729 at p=0.01, three orders of magnitude above TOL. At p=0.10 the mediator curve sits at Φ_S = 1.188722 and the party curve at Φ_W = 0.763197, a gap of −0.425525, the party curve below the mediator as predicted. The self-control max|Φ_S − Φ_S| is 0.00e+00, so the gap statistic reads zero on identical curves and the real gap is a genuine site difference. The instrument passed at p=0 with Φ = 2.0, triadic, MIP {W,SC} for both sites (`results/h1_party_vs_mediator.csv`, `probe_party_vs_mediator.py`).

H2: confirmed. The two parties are one seat. The W-only and C-only curves coincide at every one of the 51 grid points: 0/51 points exceed 1e-6 and max|d| = 0.000e+00. Both sites flip the verdict at the same place, flip_W = flip_C = 0.5. The positive control resolves a real gap when one exists, with Φ_W against Φ_S flagging 49/51 points above TOL and max|W−S| = 0.923729 at p=0.01, so the W=C null holds with the test fully sighted. The instrument passed (triadic, Φ = 2.0, MIP {W,SC}) (`results/wc_symmetry.csv`, `probe_wc_symmetry.py`).

H3: refuted. Party noise holds the verdict to the same endpoint the mediator does. The strict-verdict collapse sits at p_v(S) = 0.5 and p_v(W) = 0.5; both sites hold triadic at every interior p and read dyadic only at the degenerate endpoint. The effective collapse band gives the same reading, p_eff(S) = 0.5 and p_eff(W) = 0.5. The hypothesis required p_v(W) < p_v(S), and the data put the two collapses at the same p (`results/q7_verdict_flip.csv`, `probe_verdict_flip.py`).

H4: refuted. The slope half holds while the ordering fails. The clean-limit slopes are m_S = |1.899374 − 2.0| / 0.01 = 10.062552 and m_W = |0.975645 − 2.0| / 0.01 = 102.435457, so the party curve drops about 10.2 times steeper near the clean corner. The small-p prediction matched, Φ_W(0.10) = 0.763197 < Φ_S(0.10) = 1.188722. The decision rule also requires Φ_W ≤ Φ_S at every interior p, and the curves cross: Φ_W < Φ_S at only 32 of 49 interior points, with Φ_W > Φ_S at the other 17 and the first reversal at p=0.33 (gap +0.003978 there, staying positive through p=0.49). The interior ordering reverses, so the hypothesis fails despite the steeper party slope. The H1 joint read passed (g_max = 0.923729 > 1e-3) and the self-control held (m_S repeat 10.062552, |Δ| = 0.00e+00). The instrument passed at p=0 (triadic, Φ = 2.0, MIP {W,SC}) (`results/q7_slope_asymmetry.csv`, `probe_slope_asymmetry.py`).

H5: partial. Exactly one of the two confirmation conditions holds. The non-separability condition is confirmed: r_max = 1.455836 at p=0.01 (Φ_WC = 1.931778 against the null Φ_pred = 0.475942), far above 1e-3, and the residual is positive at every interior p, so the joint curve sits above the multiplicative null throughout. At p=0.10, Φ_WC = 1.373755 runs above both Φ_pred = 0.291235 and the single-party Φ_W = 0.763197. The H2 reuse holds, max|Φ_W − Φ_C| = 0.00e+00, so the null reduces to the square as intended, and the positive self-control reads r_max = 0.00e+00. The effective-collapse condition is refuted: p_eff(WC) = 0.5 equals p_eff(W) = 0.5, holding the collapse in place. Φ_WC lies above Φ_W across the interior and stays monotone non-increasing, with no transient rise, and all three curves reach 0 at p=0.5. One condition of two holds, so the verdict is partial: the joint noise is strongly non-separable and raises the interior Φ while leaving the collapse where it was (`results/h5_joint_party.csv`, `probe_joint_party.py`).

## Discussion

The seat that carries the flip sets the curve. H1 makes the asymmetry the result: the same flip-noise costs different amounts of integration depending on whether it lands on the mediator's commit or a party's update, with the gap reaching 0.92 near the clean corner and the party curve below the mediator across most of the interior. This realizes the locus-dependent asymmetry the literature predicted [danilczuk2026iaf; marshall2023sii] for one specific contrast that no prior source had run: party-output-flip versus mediator-output-flip inside a fixed triad.

The two parties are one seat against the mediator's other seat. H2 overlays the worker and counterpart curves exactly, to numerical zero at every grid point, so the W↔C swap is an automorphism of the noise response and carries through to the full curve, beyond the clean verdict alone. That is what licenses reporting a single party-versus-mediator contrast in place of three curves. Robustness here is seat-relative, and the two party seats are the same seat.

The asymmetry lives in Φ, not in the verdict. H3 refutes the early-flip claim: both sites hold triadic at every interior p and snap to dyadic only at p=0.5, the same degenerate collapse Q6 found for the mediator. A unit XORed with a fair coin carries zero information regardless of which unit it is [mayner2018pyphi], so the strict verdict cannot separate the sites even though Φ does. This is the same verdict/Φ decoupling the Q6 reading described, now shown to survive moving the flip to a party.

H4 splits the prediction. Its steeper-slope half holds emphatically, with the party curve dropping about 10 times faster near the clean corner, which matches the worker-on-the-weak-seam reading where a flip on the separable side of the {W,SC} cut moves φ_s more than one buried in the tight {S,C} block. Its ordering half fails: the curves cross near p=0.33 and the party curve rises back above the mediator in the noisy regime. A steep early drop leaves the later ordering open; near the random-output endpoint the two sites converge and then swap. The hypothesis demanded one fixed sign across the whole interior, and the data deny it.

H5 inverts the prior on its decisive register. The joint-party noise is strongly non-separable, and it runs opposite to the conjunction-couples-and-advances-collapse prediction: noising both parties at once preserves more integration than the multiplicative null and more than a single party alone, and the effective collapse stays at p=0.5. The two party flips compound into higher interior Φ and leave the commit intact to the endpoint. The directional claim fails on its decisive register while the non-separability half is confirmed with the sign reversed, which is why the verdict is partial.

Read across the four probes: which seat is noised separates the curves (H1), the two party seats are one symmetric seat against the mediator's (H2), the separation is a Φ phenomenon and not a verdict-threshold phenomenon (H3, H4 ordering), and jointly noising the parties is non-additive in Φ while the verdict threshold holds (H5). The through-line is that the locus of the flip is a real degree of freedom for Φ and a confined one for the verdict.

## Limitations

The result is specific to this form, this noise model, and this grain. The form is the triadic conjunctive mediated chain at n=3; whether the same asymmetry holds for parity hubs, larger n, or disjunctive commits stays untested here. The noise is independent per-element output flip-noise riding in the TPM; correlated noise, input noise, or noise in the initial state are separate manipulations. The grid stops at p=0.5, the random-output endpoint for a single column; the joint-party site noises two columns, and the sweep leaves its endpoint behavior past the single-column degenerate point untouched. Φ magnitude is an ordinal hint under the classifier's own caution, so the interior crossing in H4 and the residual sign in H5 read as orderings and signs, with no claim to calibrated scale. The strict verdict is binary and could not have separated the sites at any interior p by construction, so H3's refutation describes the verdict instrument and leaves the sites' likeness open. The structural-position and synchronization analogies the related work supplies [harush2019peripheral; russo2016noise] concern collective order rather than Φ, and the mapping to the mediated triad remains the project's overlay, still unproven.
