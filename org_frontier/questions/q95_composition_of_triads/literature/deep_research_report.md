# Q95 literature — composing coordination units

## From chained mediators to chained triads

Q65 and Q66 chained mediators within a single coordination: an agent chain stays triadic at Φ = 2 at every
length, with the core a symmetric end pair. That is depth inside one unit. Composition is different: the
output of one whole triadic unit becomes a party in the next, so two complete coordinations are joined.
Whether the result is one coordination or two is the modularity question the program has not asked.

## Three possible outcomes

Composition could unify — the shared node binds the two triads into a single spanning complex, and the
coordination is one irreducible object across both units. It could fragment — the two triads stay separate
complexes joined only by a feed-forward link, so the composition is two coordinations in series. Or Φ could
add — the composed integration is the sum of the parts, scaling as the chain (Q65) and breadth (Q64) did.
The outcome bears on whether coordination composes the way functions do, or whether joining units changes
their integration.

## Method context

The forms reuse the read-recipient triad and Q94's disjoint-complex resolution. The shared node makes the
composition minimal — one party in common — so the binding is tested at its weakest coupling.
