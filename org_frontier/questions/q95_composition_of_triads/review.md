# Q95 — Stage 1 review: composing coordination units

## The question

Q65 chained mediators within one coordination. Composition joins two whole triads: the output of one
triadic unit becomes a party in the next. Whether the result is one coordination, two in series, or an
additive sum is the modularity question the program has not asked.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **Chained mediators stay triadic at Φ = 2 (Q65, Q66).** Depth inside one determination preserves the
  triad. Composition joins separate determinations, which is different.
- **Coupling two cores does not fuse them (Q94).** A one-way bridge between cores leaves them separate; a
  mutual bridge collapses one. Composition through a shared node is the matched test for whole triads.
- **Bidirectional coupling is necessary for membership (Finding 8).** A shared node that feeds the second
  triad without being fed back may bind only the first.

## The gap

No study composes whole triads. Whether joining the output of one triad to the input of another unifies
them, fragments them, or adds their integration is uncomputed.
