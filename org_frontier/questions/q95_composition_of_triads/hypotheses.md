# Q95 — Stage 3 hypotheses (fixed before computation)

Four hypotheses on composing two read-recipient triads through a shared node — the recipient of the first
is the sender of the second, so the output of one triadic unit is a party in the next. Q65 chained
mediators; this chains whole triads. Written and committed before the composed form is read. The single
triad (triadic, Φ = 2, core {E, M, R}) is the instrument control.

## H1 — The composition preserves irreducibility

- **Claim:** The composed form is triadic (whole-system Φ > 0).
- **H0:** The composition is dyadic.
- **Predicted outcome:** triadic. H0 refuted.

## H2 — The composition unifies into one spanning complex

- **Claim:** The composed form has a single disjoint complex, and its core spans both triads (contains a
  node from {E1, M1} and a node from {M2, R2}).
- **H0:** The composition fragments into two complexes, or its core lies within one triad.
- **Predicted outcome:** one spanning complex. H0 refuted. This is the study's central and genuinely
  uncertain claim — the alternative, that the shared node does not bind the two triads into one core, is
  equally plausible.

## H3 — Composition adds integration

- **Claim:** The composed major complex has Φ greater than a single triad's 2.0.
- **H0:** The composed core Φ does not exceed 2.0.
- **Predicted outcome:** greater than 2.0, as the chain (Q65) and breadth (Q64) scaled Φ with size.

## H4 — The shared node is in the core

- **Claim:** The shared bridge node S is in the composed major complex.
- **H0:** S is outside the major complex.
- **Predicted outcome:** S is in the core, being the node that joins the two triads.
