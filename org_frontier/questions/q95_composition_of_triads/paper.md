# Q95 — Composing two triads: fragmentation, not unification

## Question

Q65 chained mediators within one coordination and kept it triadic at Φ = 2 at every length. Composition is
a different operation: the output of one whole triadic unit becomes a party in the next, joining two
complete coordinations. Whether the result is one coordination spanning both units, two coordinations in
series, or an additive sum of integration, is the modularity question the program has not asked.

## Method

Two read-recipient triads (E'=M, M'=E∧R, R'=M; Φ = 2, core {E, M, R}) are composed through a shared node:
the recipient of the first triad is the sender of the second. The five nodes are E1, M1, S, M2, R2, with
E1'=M1, M1'=E1∧S, S'=M1, M2'=S∧R2, R2'=M2. The verdict, the major complex, and the disjoint-complex tiling
are read, against a single triad and two disjoint triads.

## Results

The composition is whole-system dyadic. Its major complex is the first triad {E1, M1, S} at Φ = 2.0, and it
resolves into two disjoint complexes, {E1, M1, S} and {M2, R2}, each at Φ = 2.0. The shared node S is in
the first triad's core; the second triad's core contracts to {M2, R2}, excluding S.

| configuration | whole-system | major complex (φ) | disjoint complexes |
|---|---|---|---|
| single triad | triadic Φ=2.0 | {E, M, R} (2.0) | 1 |
| two disjoint triads | dyadic Φ=0.0 | {E1, M1, R1} (2.0) | 2 |
| composed | dyadic Φ=0.0 | {E1, M1, S} (2.0) | 2 |

| | result |
|---|---|
| H1 the composition is triadic | refuted |
| H2 it unifies into one spanning complex | refuted |
| H3 composed core Φ exceeds 2.0 | refuted |
| H4 the shared node is in the core | confirmed |

## Interpretation

Composition keeps coordination units modular. Joining two triads through a shared party does not produce a
single five-node coordination; it produces two coordinations in series, joined by a one-way seam, and the whole factors across it. The composed integration is not the sum of the parts: the major complex
holds the same Φ = 2.0 as a single triad, and the second triad's core shrinks rather than merging into a
larger one.

The shared node is the seam, and it sits on one side of it. As the recipient of the first triad and the
sender of the second, S is bound into the upstream core and not the downstream one, so the handoff is
directional: the first coordination determines S, and S feeds the second without binding it back. This is
the same one-way structure Q94 found between cores, where a feed-forward link left two complexes separate,
and the same bidirectional condition of Finding 8 at the level of whole units.

Coordination does not compose the way functions do. Chaining the output of one coordinated exchange into
another does not build a larger irreducible object; each exchange keeps its own core, and the pipeline is a
series of coordinations. The program's depth results (Q65, Q66) hold within a single
determination; across a seam between determinations, composition fragments.

## Limitations

In-silico; Boolean models with exact Φ and exact complexes. The composition shares one node, the minimal
join; a composition with several shared parties or a mutual handoff could bind the two triads differently
and is untested. The composed and two-triad forms are whole-system dyadic with triadic complexes inside,
the Q74 situation, so the disjoint-complex tiling is the membership reading. The finding is shown on one
composition, not swept.
