# Q90 — Stage 1 review: does the membership law survive scale?

## The question

The two-condition account of core membership — bidirectional coupling necessary, pivotality predicting
membership among coupled parties — was found and validated on three-node systems, at rank-AUC 0.89. A law
established on the smallest systems need not hold as they grow. Q81 showed a learned surrogate for the
verdict fails to cross sizes; the matching question for the structural law is open.

## What the lab already knows that bears on this

- **The two-condition membership law (Finding 8, probe #12).** Coupling necessary, pivotality graded,
  rank-AUC 0.89 on the three-node family. The object under test, at larger n.
- **A learned surrogate does not generalize past its training size (Q81).** The cheap predictor of the
  verdict breaks across sizes. Whether the structural law breaks the same way is the question.
- **Higher-order mechanisms appear only with more elements (IIT 4.0).** At n = 4 and n = 5 a node can enter
  the core through joint constraints with no three-node analogue, which could weaken a pairwise predictor.

## The gap

The membership law has been validated only at n = 3. Its behaviour at n = 4 and n = 5 is uncomputed, and
n = 6 is beyond the exact ceiling for major-complex enumeration. Whether coupling stays necessary and
pivotality stays predictive as parties multiply is the open question.
