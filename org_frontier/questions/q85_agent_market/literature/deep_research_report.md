# Q85 — Stage 2 deep research: agent markets and interchangeability

This study scales the interchangeable-agent case and draws on the same literatures (shared

## Markets of interchangeable providers

Platform research treats multi-homing across interchangeable providers as a force that weakens any single
provider [malone1994interdisciplinary]. A market of AI agents is the limit: a worker routes through
whichever agent is cheapest or available, treating them as interchangeable. The economics of spam frames
cheap interchangeable sending as the dyadic default [rao2012economics]; costly signaling frames commitment
to a specific channel as the informative act [spence1973job; zahavi1975mate].

## Substitutability at scale

The lab's Finding 5 holds that substitutability of any role collapses the triad, and Q70 confirmed it for
two agents. Whether the collapse holds at every market size, read by exact integrated information
[oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4; mayner2018pyphi], and whether a single substitutable pair
among required agents collapses the whole, are the questions here. The framing carries from Q63-Q84
[hancock2020aimc].

## The open gap

Q70 tested N=2; the market at N=2,3,4 and the mixed case are uncomputed. The gap is open: scale the
substitutable and all-required agent sets and read the verdict. In-silico, with the validation gap
[axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong; brodeur2024preregistration].
