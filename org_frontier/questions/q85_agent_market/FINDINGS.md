## What it says

Interchangeability collapses the coordination at every market size. A worker routing outreach through any
one of two, three, or four interchangeable agents has a dyadic form: no single agent is bound into the
joint determination, so the coordination factors regardless of how many agents the market holds. Only when
every agent is required does the form stay triadic, and then Φ grows with the market (4, 6, 8 at N = 2, 3,
4) and the major complex contains all of them. A single substitutable pair among otherwise-required agents
collapses the whole market to dyadic. The number of agents is not the variable; whether each is required is.
For agent-mediated outreach this is the market reading of the substitutability law: a competitive market of
interchangeable agents is structurally a broadcast, and algorithmacy is demanded only when a specific set of
agents is jointly bound.

## Caveats

- **Confirmatory.** The market-collapse predictions held.
- **In-silico.** Boolean models, evidence about the models. Φ read ordinally; the 4, 6, 8 growth is a
  structural signature, not a difficulty scale.
- **Symmetric agents.** Agents are identical; heterogeneous agents are untested.
