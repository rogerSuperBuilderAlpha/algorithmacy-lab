# Q53 — implication Φ ceiling · Stage 1 review

**Question.** Can any back-channel topology or graded channel strength restore matched-read implication
forms to the strict-mediation complementary ceiling Φ=2.0, or is 0.830075 the absolute maximum once
outer-party coupling is added beyond the four-edge floor?

**Agenda id.** Grows from Q51 probes #155 and #159 and Q52 probe #164. No dedicated agenda slot; the
open thread is whether the back-channel equilibrium is a hard ceiling or a topology-specific local maximum.

## The gap

Q51 and Q52 fixed the worker-side AND ladder and showed symmetric AND collapses it to 0.830075 without ever
reaching Φ=2.0. Counterpart-side wiring, OR-graded gates (weaker coupling than AND), XOR parity gates, and
mixed cross-topologies have not been swept on the eight matched-read implication forms {2,4,11,13} with
matched party reads. No probe has asked whether any finite n=3 Boolean back-channel construction recovers
the strict-mediation ceiling or whether 0.830075 is the supremum across topologies. Probes #155 and #164
tested only AND gates on worker-side and symmetric panels; the impossibility claim requires a broader sweep.
