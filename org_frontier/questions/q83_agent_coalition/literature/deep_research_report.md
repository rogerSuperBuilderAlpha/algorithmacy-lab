# Q83 — Stage 2 deep research: coalitions of gatekeepers

This study builds a recipient-side gating coalition and draws on the same literatures (shared
`references.bib`).

## Coalitions and joint gating

Coordination theory treats a constraining gatekeeper as part of the structure when it gates the flow
[malone1994interdisciplinary]. A coalition of gatekeepers that jointly gate raises the question of whether
all of them belong to the irreducible coordination or only their joint effect does. Integrated information
theory decides membership by the major complex [oizumi2014phenomenology; albantakis2023iit4;
mayner2018pyphi]; the lab's regulator-coalition probe found two regulators enter only under joint gating.

## Receiving-side coalitions

The economics of spam places automated filters on the receiving side [rao2012economics], and AI-mediated
communication places agents on both sides [hancock2020aimc]. Two recipient-side agents that jointly gate
delivery are a coalition of receiving-side gatekeepers. Whether they enter the core as the mediator-side
regulators do depends on where they gate, which this study isolates.

## The open gap

The regulator coalition gates the commit; the recipient-side coalition gates the recipient's input.
Whether the receiving-side coalition enters the core the same way is uncomputed. The gap is open: build
both-required and either-suffices recipient-side gating and read the major complex. In-silico, with the
validation gap [axtell1996aligning]; hypotheses fixed before computing [chamberlin1965method; platt1964strong;
brodeur2024preregistration].
