# Q45 — Edge-floor uniqueness of the conjunctive hub · Stage 1 review

**Question.** At the lean 2(n−1) causal-edge floor of strict mediation, is the conjunctive (AND) commit the
only determination class that achieves the maximum integrated information available at that wiring budget
(Φ = n−1), or do other commit types (OR, parity, or non-mediation wirings) also saturate their respective
ceilings at the same edge count?

**Agenda id.** #48 from RESEARCH_AGENDA_50_V2.md ("Is the conjunctive hub the unique form achieving its Φ at
the 2(n−1) edge floor, or are there others (#30, #116)?").

## The gap

Probes #30 and #35 established a fixed edge budget for irreducible strict-mediation coordination, and
#115–#116 established two scaling laws for conjunctive and parity commits. The agenda question #48 asks
whether the conjunctive hub is *unique* in achieving its Φ at that floor. #113 shows two classes at the
same four-edge floor with different Φ, so uniqueness must be stated precisely: conjunctive is the only
class reaching Φ = n−1 at the floor, while parity reaches its own lower ceiling there. What remains open
is whether any non-AND commit in the 256-family or any triadic wiring in the 4096 space reaches Φ = 2.0 at
exactly four edges, and whether OR commits bind triadically at the floor at any size. No probe has tested
saturation of the Φ budget conditional on hitting the edge floor across commit classes and wiring spaces.
