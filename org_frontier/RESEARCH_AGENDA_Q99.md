# Agenda: the cause-effect structure of coordination (Q99–Q104) — done

All six questions below are on the record; each is a study. The wave showed that the dyadic/triadic verdict is one projection of a structured object with a shape (Q99: a taxonomy of triads), a fingerprint orthogonal to the scalar (Q100), a content (Q101: the joint-success state), a relation skeleton (Q102: a hub in mediated coordination), a boundary where it tracks the verdict but not membership (Q103: the Q74 split at the structure level), and a load-bearing element (Q104: the binding distinction, with no redundancy).

A new angle. The program has read coordination through the scalar verdict — Φ over the minimum-information
partition, dyadic or triadic — and through which parties join the major complex. It has used the
cause-effect structure only as a predictor: probes 36, 93, and 102 counted distinctions and relations and
found the counts classify the verdict. The structure itself, what the coordination distinguishes and how
its distinctions relate, has never been read as meaning.

IIT 4.0 unfolds more than a number. For each subset of parties (a mechanism) it specifies a cause and an
effect, what the subset constrains and is constrained by, and it specifies relations, the overlaps among
those distinctions. The read-recipient triad, for instance, carries a distinction whose mechanism is the
mediator alone but whose purview is both outer parties, and a higher-order distinction whose mechanism is
the two outer parties jointly and whose purview is the mediator. The irreducible coordination is visible in
the structure: a party that distinguishes the joint state of the others, and the others jointly
distinguishing it. This wave reads that structure.

The questions use the existing instrument (`foundations/structure_suite`, PyPhi's `phi_structure`) on the
forms already on the record, with no new tool. The format follows the prior agendas: the question, what it
reads, and the discriminator. The wave strengthens the case that integrated information is a rich
instrument and not a calculator, because the structure carries content a scalar cannot.

## Q99 — The binding distinction

**Question.** Does every triadic coordination carry a binding distinction — a single-party mechanism whose
purview spans every other party (the party that distinguishes the joint state of the others) — together
with a higher-order distinction whose mechanism is the spanned parties? Does no dyadic form carry both?

**What it reads.** The distinctions of each corpus form: their mechanisms, purviews, and φ_d. *Discriminator:*
a structural signature, read from the portrait, that is present in every triadic form and absent in every
dyadic one, and that names the third party as a specific distinction rather than a number.

## Q100 — Does the structure fingerprint the coordination type?

**Question.** The five pillars (joint determination, reciprocity, depth, breadth, delegation) are all
triadic by the scalar verdict. Do they carry distinguishable cause-effect structures, so the portrait is a
finer classifier than the binary verdict?

**What it reads.** The distinction and relation profiles of the keystone form of each pillar.
*Discriminator:* whether the structures separate the pillars, or collapse to one shape, telling whether the
verdict hides distinct kinds of coordination.

## Q101 — What the coordination distinguishes

**Question.** The binding distinction specifies cause and effect states. Which joint party-states does the
mediator tell apart? Does a triad distinguish joint states a dyad cannot?

**What it reads.** The specified cause and effect states of the binding distinction, against the broadcast.
*Discriminator:* a joint state distinguished by the triad and not the dyad — the semantic content of
coordination, not its mere presence.

## Q102 — The relation skeleton

**Question.** Relations bind distinctions over shared purviews. Is the triadic relation structure a
characteristic shape — the parties' distinctions all relating over the mediator?

**What it reads.** The relation graph: which distinctions each relation binds, over what purview, with what
φ_r. *Discriminator:* a relation motif common to triadic forms and absent from dyadic ones.

## Q103 — The structure under the operations

**Question.** How does the cause-effect structure change under the program's operations — substitution,
delegation, a lossy read, a tracking memory? Does the binding distinction degrade gracefully or
restructure?

**What it reads.** The distinction and relation profiles across the operation sweeps already studied (Q70,
Q68, Q91, Q92). *Discriminator:* whether the structure tracks the scalar verdict smoothly, or reorganizes
in ways the scalar hides.

## Q104 — The load-bearing distinction

**Question.** Which single distinction or relation, removed, collapses the triad? The cause-effect analogue
of Q93's structural margin.

**What it reads.** The φ_d and φ_r of each element against the system Φ, and the verdict after deleting the
mechanism that carries each. *Discriminator:* a load-bearing element of the structure whose removal
collapses the verdict, naming what holds the coordination together.
