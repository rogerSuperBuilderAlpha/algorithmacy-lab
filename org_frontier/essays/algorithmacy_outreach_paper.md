# Reading coordination with a consciousness measure: a structural law for agent-mediated outreach

*A synthesis of the algorithmacy lab's program through Q85.*

## Abstract

When work is mediated by an algorithmic system that interprets two parties and commits outcomes neither
controls, the competence it demands may differ in kind from reading a medium. This program borrows exact
integrated information (Φ, from IIT 4.0, computed with PyPhi) as an instrument for that difference: a
coordination form is dyadic, demanding only literacy, when Φ over the minimum-information partition is
zero, and triadic, demanding algorithmacy, when it is positive. On systems small enough to compute Φ
exactly, the verdict is exact rather than estimated. Across roughly 244 reproducible probes and a hundred
continuously-checked numbers, the program establishes a structural law (a form is triadic iff every party
is bound into a single irreducible joint determination), applies it to agent-mediated outreach through a
ten-study program that ends in a five-pillar law, and hardens that law through seven further studies on the
verdict's foundations and extensions. The work is in-silico throughout: every result is evidence about the
Boolean models, separated from claims about real organizations by a validation gap that no amount of
computing closes. The program's method, its findings, and its honest limits are the subject of this paper.

## Introduction

Two drivers work the same delivery app and end the month differently. The interesting question is not who
tried harder but what competence the situation demanded. The dominant vocabulary calls that competence
literacy: read the interface, the ratings, the terms. The proposal this lab works from is that reading the
interface is sometimes not enough, because a worker coordinates with a counterpart through a system that
interprets both sides and commits decisions neither controls. That is a different shape of problem, and it
may demand a different competence, named algorithmacy.

The difficulty is that the shape cannot be read from the surface. Three named parties can collapse to a
dyad with a wire through it; a worker alone with an app can be triadic. The instrument the program borrows
to see past the surface is a measure built to study consciousness. Integrated information theory defines Φ
as the degree to which a system's causal power exceeds the causal power of its parts taken separately, and
PyPhi computes it exactly for small systems. The borrowing is formal: Φ measures irreducibility, a fact
about the measure, and the contested status of IIT as a theory of consciousness does not bear on this use.
The same property that separates an integrated whole from a heap separates a triadic coordination from a
dyadic one.

The program began with a structural arc that established the instrument and a law, ran a long middle that
mapped the measure's own geometry, and then turned outward to a concrete application. The outward turn was
sparked by a discussion of agent-mediated ("openclaw") outreach, and it produced the body of work this
paper synthesizes: a structural law of outreach, hardened against its own foundations.

## The instrument and the method

The instrument is exact IIT-4.0 Φ over the minimum-information partition. A coordination form is modelled
as a small Boolean system whose elements update by fixed rules; the classifier reads Φ_MIP and returns the
dyadic or triadic verdict. The modelling commitment is the application-layer coupling, stated for every
form, and the verdict is read on the maximal complex when a form has spectator nodes.

The method is a six-stage protocol run per question: place the question against the logbook; research the
literature; fix five falsifiable hypotheses, each with its null, before computing anything; specify the
exact test and decision rule per hypothesis; run against the exact-Φ instrument after validating it on a
known control; and write the result as a complete paper. Two commitments carry the credibility. Hypotheses
are committed to version control before the results commit, so the order is auditable. And every number
reproduces from a committed script: a continuous-integration gate re-derives roughly a hundred registered
numbers, and a study cannot merge until the numbers it touches reproduce. The logbook holds about 244
probes, and roughly a third of the program's results are nulls, refinements, or refutations, which is the
fraction that makes the confirmations worth reading.

One methodological result deserves its own statement, because the program's deepest claims depend on it.
The whole-system verdict and the maximal complex can diverge, and Q74 gives the rule for which to report:
an element excluded from the maximal complex is bidirectionally coupled to the core if and only if the
whole system is triadic. When every excluded element is non-bidirectional (a read-only observer, an
emit-only source), the whole system is dyadic and the maximal complex is the verdict to report, because
the excluded elements are genuine spectators. When an excluded element is bidirectionally coupled but
non-pivotal, the whole system is triadic and the maximal complex is a localized core, so both must be
reported. A single connectivity check on the excluded elements selects the reading.

## The structural law and the eight findings

Before the application, the program established what makes a coordination form irreducible. Among the
strict-mediation three-node forms, only 9.4% are triadic; the surface (party count, interface, topology)
underdetermines the verdict. Eight findings reduce to one law: a coordination form is triadic, and demands
algorithmacy, if and only if every party is bound into a single irreducible joint determination.
Substitutability or optionality of any party collapses it to dyadic. Mediation depth never collapses it.
Cheap proxies cannot detect it: a time-series proxy separates the classes only near chance. And membership
in the irreducible core requires bidirectional constraining coupling, which is necessary but not
sufficient; among bidirectionally-coupled parties, the probability of membership rises with the
determination's sensitivity to the party.

## The outreach program (Q63–Q73)

The application models outreach as a coordination form: sender intent, an agent or mediator, and a
recipient. The single-mediator result (Q63) is that the form is triadic at Φ=2.0 when the agent jointly
determines a message from the sender's intent and a live recipient, and dyadic when the agent broadcasts
past the recipient. Disclosure of the agent is a label, not a read, and leaves the verdict unchanged; and
no cheap cost proxy recovers the verdict, a result sharpened in Q72, where the read-recipient triad and a
one-shot dyadic form share the same mediator in-degree and edge count yet differ in verdict, because they
differ only in whether the recipient stays live.

Ten studies extended the single-mediator case and cohered into a five-pillar law. Breadth (Q64): an
all-binding campaign stays triadic with Φ growing as n−1, while substitutable or pooled campaigns factor at
every breadth. Depth (Q65–Q66): an agent chain stays triadic at Φ=2.0 at every length, a relay-gap agent
breaks it, and the irreducible core is a symmetric end pair of the chain rather than the whole chain, with
a closed loop binding the whole structure at Φ=4.0. Reciprocity (Q67): the coordination binds at a
threshold, not by degrees, because a single reciprocal loop seeds a local core while the whole exchange
still factors. Delegation (Q68–Q70): a recipient-side triage agent joins the core only when
bidirectionally coupled, and then it displaces the sender; when both sides delegate, the two agents become
the irreducible core and the humans are displaced; but only while the agents are specific and required, as
multi-homing across interchangeable agents collapses the coordination. Robustness and the proxy frontier
(Q71–Q72): the verdict degrades gracefully under noise and never manufactures a triad, and no cheap proxy
recovers it.

The five pillars, stated in Q73, are joint determination, liveness and reciprocity, non-substitutability,
core localization under depth and delegation, and the verdict's structural rather than cheap character.
Two results in this arc are corrections the program made to itself. Q66 found that Q65's reading of the
chain core as recipient-facing was a tie-break between two symmetric ends, and the claim was corrected on
the record. And Q69's striking finding that delegation moves the core to the agents holds under a model of
delegation that freezes the humans into non-integrating source and sink nodes, which makes the whole-system
verdict of that form dyadic; the displacement is read on the maximal complex and is disclosed as
contingent on that model.

## Construct-hardening and extensions (Q74–Q85)

Seven studies tested the verdict's foundations and pushed its edges. The reporting rule (Q74) and spectator
robustness (Q75) hardened the construct: the rule says which verdict to report when the readings diverge,
and the core is stable when the coordination is surrounded by idle, observing, or emitting parties. The
verdict is a graded quantity in its stochastic limit (Q79): when the agent reads the recipient with
probability p, Φ rises 0, 0.11, 0.28, 0.54, 2.0 across p, with no flat-zero region, so the binary verdict
is the deterministic limit of a continuous emergence. The verdict survives asynchronous update (Q80): when
one element acts per step, the triadic forms stay triadic and the broadcast stays dyadic, with only the
magnitude of Φ falling, so synchronicity is not load-bearing.

The multi-agent extensions produced both a refutation and a sharp security reading. A recipient-side gating
coalition (Q83) keeps a size-three core: when delivery requires both of two symmetric agents, only one
enters the maximal complex, unlike the mediator-side regulator coalition where both enter; the location of
the gate, not the gatekeeper count, decides whether a coalition grows the core. An adversarial agent (Q84)
cannot change the verdict from outside the core: a read-only or emit-only agent leaves the core triadic and
unchanged, and only a bidirectional, pivotal agent alters it, by joining it. Influence over the
coordination requires membership in it. And a market of interchangeable agents (Q85) is structurally a
broadcast at every size: a substitutable market of two, three, or four agents is dyadic, while an
all-required set is triadic with Φ growing as 2N and every agent in the core, and a single substitutable
pair collapses the whole market.

## Limitations and the validation gap

The program is in-silico throughout. Every verdict is exact Φ on a small Boolean model, evidence about the
model and at most about a second model that reproduces it. A validation gap separates these from claims
about real organizations: cross-model agreement is internal validity, external validity is a separate and
unmet claim, and no result in the 244 probes touches data. The verdict is binary, not graded; Φ magnitude
is dominated by how a determination is encoded and is read ordinally, so the numbers that grow with breadth
or depth are structural signatures rather than a difficulty scale. The whole-system verdict and the
maximal complex diverge in ways that the Q74 rule classifies but that demand care in every claim. And exact
Φ is bounded near ten to twelve elements, while the cheap-proxy escape route fails. The program is honest
about each of these in every paper, and the self-corrections of Q65 and Q69 are part of the record rather
than hidden from it.

## Future work

The program plateaus as interesting in-silico until one result touches data, and four designs are staged
for when the anchor exists (`RESEARCH_DESIGNS_Q76plus.md`). Q76 wires the dissertation's agent-based
coordination experiment to the outreach forms, to test whether the verdict predicts a behavioural
difficulty gap. Q77 tests cheap proxies against the exact verdict on real interaction logs, where the
in-silico finding says they should fail. Q78 lifts the binary forms to multivalued parties, once
multivalued IIT is available, to test whether the verdict survives graded state. Q86 writes the formal
correspondence between the five-pillar law and the organizational constructs of literacy, algorithmacy, and
the platform as a third coordination mechanism. The first of these is the defining next chapter: crossing
the validation gap is what would turn a structural account into a claim about the world.
