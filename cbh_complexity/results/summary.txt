EXPERIMENT A — exact 4x4 Ising (the authors' own example)
  entropy H: monotone 1.00 -> 15.27 over T=0.5..6.0 (Spearman(T,H)=+1.000, i.e. monotone increasing)
  TSE complexity Cn: NON-monotone, peaks at T=2.25 (Cn=8.26; 2D Tc≈2.27) and collapses to Cn=1.84 at T=6.0 (highest entropy)
  => high-entropy disordered state has LOW complexity: high entropy does not imply complexity.
  (honest caveat) Cn is also high at low T (7.50, ordered/redundant): TSE Cn resolves the
  conundrum at the high-entropy end but conflates order with complexity at the low-entropy end.

EXPERIMENT B — Metropolis L=16 apparent-complexity grain sweep (claim C2), bootstrap 95% CIs
  T=1.00: g1=0.98[0.98,0.99]  g2=1.76[1.75,1.77]  g4=2.77[2.76,2.79]  g8=2.53[2.49,2.57]
  T=2.27: g1=0.74[0.71,0.76]  g2=1.65[1.59,1.70]  g4=2.32[2.24,2.40]  g8=2.10[2.02,2.18]
  T=6.00: g1=1.00[1.00,1.00]  g2=2.22[2.22,2.23]  g4=2.59[2.56,2.61]  g8=1.81[1.76,1.86]
  => at the coarsest grain the high-entropy (T=6) state has the lowest apparent complexity (g8=1.81 [1.76,1.86]), non-overlapping CI with the ordered/critical states: coarse-graining reveals the high-entropy state as simplest. The disordered collapse survives the bootstrap; the ordered/critical ordering is finite-size dependent.

EXPERIMENT C — parity ring (n=4), exact IIT-4.0 Φ, order->disorder by noise
  entropy H: monotone -0.00 -> 4.00 (Spearman(noise,H)=+1.000)
  TSE complexity Cn: peaks at noise=0.075 (Cn=0.308), -> 0.000 at max noise
  exact Φ_max: 0.50 (ordered) -> 0.00 (disordered); integration destroyed by disorder
  => max-entropy state (noise 0.5): H=4.00 (max), Cn=0.000, Φ=0.000 -> maximal entropy, zero complexity/integration = 'contentless'.
  Spearman(H, Cn)=-0.713, Spearman(H, Φmax)=-1.000: complexity and Φ are NOT monotone functions of entropy.
