# The Transition to Algorithmacy — Dissertation Summary

**Roger Hunt III · Bentley University**

The dissertation concerns how the structure of human coordination changes when an algorithmic system mediates between two parties, and what competency that structural change demands. It comprises three papers, ordered from the conceptual to the formal, that together develop a construct, a formal model of the condition that demands it, and a characterization of that model across the space of coordination forms.

## Paper 1 — Naming the construct (integrative review)

Equivalently positioned platform participants obtain divergent coordination outcomes through identical algorithmic systems, and the constructs available for studying work with algorithms do not explain the variance. The paper argues that these constructs—computer-mediated-communication competence, human–machine communication, AI-mediated communication, algorithmic and AI literacy, and algorithm sensemaking—share a dyadic unit of analysis: a person on one side of a medium that conveys, scores, or interprets but does not pursue objectives of its own across both human parties. Platform-mediated coordination, it contends, is structurally triadic: a worker coordinates with a counterpart she may never meet through a system that interprets both parties and commits determinations neither controls. Through an integrative review the paper represents each existing construct in its strongest form, shows where each stops short of the triad, and specifies a new construct, *algorithmacy*, to the standards of construct clarity—definition, scope conditions, dimensions, and a nomological network. The contribution is conceptual: a specification for a competency the field's existing vocabulary cannot reach, and the measurement program it opens.

## Paper 2 — Modeling whether a form is triadic (construct development and formal model)

Whether a given coordination form is triadic is not given by its surface, since the number of parties and the interface underdetermine the underlying structure. The paper adopts integrated information theory's measure, Φ, as a formal model of structural irreducibility: a coordination form is modeled as triadic when its application-layer structure does not factor into independent parties under any partition (Φ over the minimum-information partition is positive) and as dyadic when some partition factors it (Φ = 0). It is explicit that this is a modeling choice resting on stated commitments—the application-layer representation, a pre-registered state-individuation rule, and reading irreducibility over the minimum-information partition—rather than a claimed identity, and it both acknowledges the standing critiques of the borrowed measure and shows, over the complete 4,096-form family, that the binary verdict diverges from a cheap factorization test on more than 40% of cases (Φ is strictly stronger, not a heavy reimplementation). Worked examples classify against appearance in both directions—a three-party arrangement that factors to a dyad, a worker-alone-with-an-app arrangement that does not. The binary "eliminate the dyad" result is robust at its endpoints (suppressing the direct channel is what keeps the form classified as a triad), but the paper withdraws the earlier claim of a smooth magnitude decline: the intermediate Φ depends on the encoding (a conjunctive blend raises Φ where a disjunctive one lowers it), and a genuine joint determination is necessary but not sufficient—the verdict also depends on the read structure. The political-economy proposition is offered at the binary level only, as a hypothesis the model generates rather than a validated claim.

## Paper 3 — Grading and testing the model (formal model, population study, and experiment)

Paper 3 asks how far the binary classifier can be made graded, and is exact about where it can and cannot. It computes Φ across the complete family of three-node forms (a coverage check, since most wirings correspond to no recognizable organization), places a typology of named real organizations on the bands the family produces, and codes five large corporations by the structure of how they coordinate their parties. One result holds robustly: the binary dyad/triad classification, strictly stronger than a cheap factorization test over the whole form family. Φ as a fine-grained scale fails outright: its magnitude is dominated by the determination function, a disjunctive determination inflating it an order of magnitude beyond any real organization, so magnitude is read only ordinally, if at all, and the binary verdict carries the dyad/triad question. The named organizations occupy the moderate, populated bands of the family, well below the structurally-possible but organization-less tail. Paper 3 therefore presents Φ as a structural classifier, not a readability score. Its contribution is a coding result: the score is computed from structure and takes no input about the mediator's nature, so a securities exchange and a ride-hailing platform code to the same structure and the same score, and a staffing firm and a freelance marketplace do the same. The cross-technology headline—a court scored like a platform of the same structure, a human staffing agency like an algorithmic marketplace—holds as a property of the model under a shared coding (the same structure is assigned to both), not as a discovered sameness in the world; independent inter-rater coding of organizational structures is named as required work. The five cases are provisional, modeled from public documentation with fieldwork deferred, so they demonstrate the model rather than validate it.

Taken together, the three papers move the claim that coordination has become triadic from observation to formal apparatus: a specified construct, a computable model of the structural condition that demands it, and a characterization of that model across the family of coordination forms, demonstrated on real but provisional organizational cases. A fuller, outcome-anchored validation of the model, a decomposition that would make its magnitude a reliable scale, and a matched, person-level instrument of worker competency constitute the research program the dissertation opens.
