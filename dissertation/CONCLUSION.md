# Chapter 5. The Combined Contribution and the Program It Opens

**Roger Hunt III**
Bentley University

*Integrative concluding chapter of the three-paper dissertation. Synthesizes the combined contribution, its significance, the research program it opens, and the bounds the work's load-bearing decisions impose. It is a synthesis across the three papers, not a summary of each.*

## 5.1 What the three papers, together, establish

The dissertation set out from a puzzle the field's constructs could not explain, the divergence of outcomes among equivalently positioned workers passing through identical algorithmic systems, and argued that the puzzle is the surface of a structural change: coordination has become triadic, and the competency a triad demands, algorithmacy, was unnamed and unmeasured. The three papers do not each address that argument in parallel. They build on one another, and what they establish together is more than the sum of the three.

Paper 1 establishes that the object is real and that the field cannot see it. The triadic coordination form exists, the dyadic constructs are built around the wrong unit and cannot be extended to reach it, and the competency it demands therefore has to be named rather than borrowed. Paper 2 establishes that the structural condition behind the construct is decidable. Whether a coordination form is triadic is not a matter of interpretation but a computation: the integrated information of its application-layer structure is zero when the structure factors and positive when it does not, and the instrument that computes it can be validated on cases whose answers are known. Paper 3 establishes that the demand is not only decidable but measurable in degree, and that the measure travels. Calibrated against an observed coordination outcome, the formal score orders coordination forms by how hard they are to coordinate through, and it orders a courtroom and a rideshare platform together when their structures match.

Stated as one result: this dissertation turns "coordination is becoming triadic" from an observation into an apparatus. It contributes a construct, algorithmacy, defined to the discipline of construct clarity; an instrument, integrated information over the observable application layer, that decides whether a coordination form is triadic; and a calibrated, portable scale that places any organization's coordination on one continuum of triadic demand. A name, a test, and a measure, where the field had none of the three. The through-line that makes these one contribution rather than three is a single object seen three ways: the irreducibility of coordination to its parts, which Paper 1 names, Paper 2 measures, and Paper 3 calibrates.

## 5.2 The puzzle revisited

The dissertation can now say something about the puzzle it began with that it could not say at the outset, and precision about what it can say marks the boundary of the contribution. The variance among equivalently positioned workers is a fact about people: two workers, the same form, different outcomes. This dissertation has not measured people. What it has done is establish that the form those workers share makes a measurable demand, and that the demand is a property of the form and not of the technology or the industry. The forms that produce the puzzle, the platforms that dispatch and match and moderate, sit at the high end of the triadic-demand scale, and they sit there because their designs route coordination through a determination and keep the parties apart. The puzzle, in other words, is not noise. It is what one should expect when a population of workers with differing capacities meets a coordination form that makes a real and uneven demand on that capacity. The dissertation has measured the demand. The variance itself, the capacity side of the same relation, is the subject of the work that follows, and the form-level measure is its precondition: one cannot ask how well a worker meets a demand without first measuring the demand.

## 5.3 Significance

The contribution matters in three registers. For communication and organizational theory, the dissertation supplies a construct and a measure for a form of coordination the existing constructs were not built to describe, and it does so without abandoning those constructs. The triadic-demand scale is orthogonal to the schemes by which organizations are usually classified, market and hierarchy and network, and it cuts across them, placing a hierarchy like a court and a market like a platform at the same point when their determination structures agree. The dissertation therefore adds a dimension to the description of organizational coordination rather than a competitor to the existing categories: a measured property that forms share in degree, calibrated against behavior, of a kind organizational research has few of.

For the study of platform work, the dissertation makes a political-economy claim that is computed rather than asserted. A platform's structural position is most irreducible, most fully a triad through which the parties must coordinate, when the parties are kept from coordinating directly. The design choices that secure that position, forbidding off-platform contact, withholding the counterpart's identity, routing every exchange through a committed match, are not a list of separate practices but moves along one measured dimension, and the scale reads their direction. This reframes the platform not as a neutral matcher but as an actor with a structural incentive to design the dyad out of the coordination it sits inside, and it locates that incentive in a number.

For workers and those who would act on their behalf, the same result locates a lever. If a platform's position is constituted by the irreducibility of the coordination it mediates, then the interventions that would weaken that position are the ones that restore a direct channel between the parties, and they are interventions the scale can register. The dissertation takes no side, but a measure that locates the lever is something a regulator or a worker organization can use that a verdict cannot, and that is a practical contribution of measuring the demand rather than merely asserting it.

## 5.4 The program the dissertation opens

The dissertation is the first arc of a longer program, and it ends by naming the work it makes possible.

The nearest and most important next instrument is the matched, person-level measure, the supply-side sibling of the form-level scale built here. A text-difficulty score is the companion of a reading-ability test, and the two together let a school grade its materials and place its students; the triadic-demand scale is the difficulty score for coordination, and the missing companion is the instrument that would score a worker's capacity to navigate a form of known demand. Building it is harder, because it requires observing people working through forms whose difficulty is already measured, which is exactly what the form-level scale now makes possible. The variance puzzle is its subject.

The scale itself is calibrated but not yet seasoned. It is anchored in one domain, rideshare pooling, and the authority that a readability formula earns across decades and many corpora is the authority more calibration domains would give this measure. A hiring pipeline scored against the time a vacancy stays open, a content platform against the rate at which disputes escalate, a crowdwork market against the share of tasks returned unfinished: each would test whether the relation between the formal score and an observed outcome that holds in pooling holds elsewhere, and each is a study the instrument makes possible.

Two boundaries the papers pre-registered as open are handed forward with the program. Paper 2 left the continuous-stream case as the limit of its individuation rule, the platform that commits determinations without pause, and Paper 3 showed how an observed outcome can fix the window the continuous case lacks; generalizing that fix is open work. And the single scalar the instrument reports aggregates distinct modes of integration that a finer decomposition would separate, so a richer account of how triadic a form is, as against whether it is, remains to be built.

## 5.5 The bounds of the work

The dissertation names its load-bearing decisions rather than hiding them, because each is the point on which a paper stands or falls, and naming them is what makes the work defensible rather than fragile.

Paper 1 stands on the fair representation of the constructs it forecloses. The contribution is that the existing dyadic constructs cannot reach the triad, and that claim is worth nothing if any construct was represented in a weakened form before being set aside; the paper therefore represents each in its strongest version before showing where it stops short.

Paper 2 stands on the state-individuation rule. The instrument's verdict depends on how the application layer is carved into states, so the rule that carves them is fixed by definition before any computation, which is what lets two analysts reach the same verdict and keeps the answer a fact about the coordination rather than an artifact of the analyst's hand.

Paper 3 stands on the calibration anchor. A formal score becomes a scale only when it is tied to an observed outcome that varies with difficulty, so the choice of that outcome is the empirical commitment on which the calibration rests, and the paper argues the choice and reports the relation rather than assuming it.

Beyond the three named decisions, the dissertation holds to a set of bounds that keep its claims credible. It measures the coordination form and not the person, and it says so wherever the distinction could be blurred. Its formal claim is the precise one and no more: that the cause-effect structure of an observable application-layer matrix is irreducible over its minimum-information partition, a computable fact distinct from the analyst's inability to see the mechanism and from any metaphysical claim about emergence. Its instrument applies to systems that can be put in the form of a transition matrix, not to all systems. And its calibration is an observational association argued with care, not a causal claim. These are the edges of the contribution, and stating them is part of making it.

## 5.6 The transition

The title of the dissertation is the transition to algorithmacy, and the three papers are an account of what that transition is and how to measure its demand. Literacy became a competency when writing displaced speech as the medium through which a culture coordinated, and the competency was new because the structure of coordination had changed, not because people had changed. The claim of this dissertation is that an analogous change is underway in the structure of everyday coordination, that it runs through the algorithmic third party that now stands between the parties to most platform-mediated work, and that the competency this structure demands is as new, and as worth naming and measuring, as literacy once was. The dissertation names the competency, builds the test for the condition that demands it, and measures how strongly the coordination forms ordinary working life now runs on make that demand. What it does not do is measure the worker, and that is the right place for it to end, because the worker is where the next arc of the program begins. Coordination has a readability now, and it is a property of the form. The question the program turns to next is the reader.
