# Chapter 5. The Combined Contribution and the Program It Opens

**Roger Hunt III**
Bentley University

*Integrative concluding chapter of the three-paper dissertation. Synthesizes the combined contribution, its significance, the research program it opens, and the bounds the work's load-bearing decisions impose. It is a synthesis across the three papers, not a summary of each.*

## 5.1 What the three papers, together, establish

The dissertation set out from a puzzle the field's constructs could not explain, the divergence of outcomes among equivalently positioned workers passing through identical algorithmic systems, and argued that the puzzle is the surface of a structural change. Coordination has become triadic, and the competency a triad demands, algorithmacy, was unnamed and unmeasured. The three papers do not each address that argument in parallel. They build on one another, and what they establish together is more than the sum of the three.

Paper 1 establishes that the object is real and that the field cannot see it. The triadic coordination form exists, the dyadic constructs are built around the wrong unit and cannot be extended to reach it, and the competency it demands therefore has to be named rather than borrowed. Paper 2 establishes that the structural condition behind the construct is decidable. Whether a coordination form is triadic is a computation rather than a matter of interpretation. The integrated information of its application-layer structure is zero when the structure factors and positive when it does not, and the instrument that computes it can be validated on cases whose answers are known. Paper 3 establishes how far the model can be made graded, and is exact about where the answer is "not far." Two results hold. The binary classification is clean across the whole family of forms and is strictly stronger than a cheap factorization test, not a costly reimplementation of one. A controlled agent-based experiment confirms the construct's central prediction behaviorally: the form the model scores as most demanding, strict mediation with no direct channel, is the hardest for independent agents to coordinate through, a large effect that survives extended training. A third result is weaker than an earlier draft claimed. Across the simulated population the finer ordering of regimes by Φ *magnitude* is not robustly supported. With proper standard errors only the determination function moves Φ significantly, and strict and partial mediation are indistinguishable once size is held fixed, so the regime ordering rests on the behavioral experiment, not the population, and the experiment is a consistency check (Φ and the agents' difficulty share a structural cause) rather than an independent validation. The graded magnitude fails as a scale. It is dominated by the determination function, which inflates it beyond any real organization for reasons orthogonal to coordination demand, and the named cases occupy only the moderate band of the population. Paper 3 therefore delivers a structural classifier and, at most, an ordinal indicator anchored behaviorally at one tested contrast, rather than a finished scale, and it names the fuller validation and a decomposition of the measure as the work that remains. Its cross-technology headline, a court scored like a platform of the same structure, it holds as a property of the model under a shared coding, not as a discovered sameness of court and platform in the world.

Stated as one result: this dissertation turns "coordination is becoming triadic" from an observation into a formal apparatus. It contributes a construct, algorithmacy, defined to the discipline of construct clarity; a formal model, integrated information over the observable application layer, that classifies whether a coordination form is triadic; and a structural classifier that orders any organization's coordination by triadic demand at the level of the mediation regime, its central prediction tested behaviorally. A name, a formal model, and a tested classifier, where the field had none of the three, with a fuller validation, and a refinement of the measure that would make its magnitude a reliable scale, named as the program's next arc. The through-line that makes these one contribution rather than three is a single object seen three ways: the irreducibility of coordination to its parts, which Paper 1 names, Paper 2 models, and Paper 3 grades and tests.

## 5.2 The puzzle revisited

The dissertation can now say something about the puzzle it began with that it could not say at the outset, and the boundary of the contribution lies in what it can say precisely. The variance among equivalently positioned workers is a fact about people: two workers, the same form, different outcomes. This dissertation has not measured people, and it has not validated its model against behavior either. It has established that the form those workers share makes a demand that can be *modeled* as a property of structure, and that the modeled demand depends on the determination structure and not on the technology or the industry. The forms that produce the puzzle, the platforms that dispatch and match and moderate, sit at the high end of the triadic-demand scale, and they sit there because their designs route coordination through a determination and keep the parties apart. The puzzle is not noise. It is what one should expect when a population of workers with differing capacities meets a coordination form that makes a real and uneven demand on that capacity. The dissertation has modeled the demand structure. The variance itself, the capacity side of the same relation, is the subject of the work that follows, and the form-level model is its precondition. One cannot ask how well a worker meets a demand without first having a model of how demanding the form is.

## 5.3 Significance

The contribution matters in three registers. For communication and organizational theory, the dissertation supplies a construct and a measure for a form of coordination the existing constructs were not built to describe, and it does so without abandoning those constructs. The triadic-demand scale is orthogonal to the schemes by which organizations are usually classified, market and hierarchy and network, and it cuts across them, placing a hierarchy like a court and a market like a platform at the same point when their determination structures agree. The dissertation therefore adds a dimension to the description of organizational coordination rather than a competitor to the existing categories: a modeled property that forms share in degree, computed from structure with its modeling choices on the table, of a kind organizational research has few of.

For the study of platform work, the dissertation makes a political-economy claim that is derived within the model rather than asserted, a hypothesis the model generates, not a fact it validates. In the model, a platform's structural position is most irreducible, most fully a triad through which the parties must coordinate, when the parties are kept from coordinating directly. The design choices that secure that position, forbidding off-platform contact, withholding the counterpart's identity, routing every exchange through a committed match, are moves along one measured dimension rather than a list of separate practices, and the scale reads their direction. This reframes the platform as an actor with a structural incentive to design the dyad out of the coordination it sits inside, rather than a neutral matcher, and it locates that incentive in a number.

For workers and those who would act on their behalf, the same result locates a lever. If a platform's position is constituted by the irreducibility of the coordination it mediates, then the interventions that would weaken that position are the ones that restore a direct channel between the parties, and they are interventions the scale can register. The dissertation takes no side, but a measure that locates the lever is something a regulator or a worker organization can use that a verdict cannot, and that is a practical contribution of measuring the demand rather than merely asserting it.

## 5.4 The program the dissertation opens

The dissertation is the first arc of a longer program, and it ends by naming the work it makes possible.

The nearest and most important next instrument is the matched, person-level measure, the supply-side sibling of the form-level scale built here. A text-difficulty score is the companion of a reading-ability test, and the two together let a school grade its materials and place its students. The triadic-demand scale is the difficulty score for coordination, and the missing companion is the instrument that would score a worker's capacity to navigate a form of known demand. Building it is harder, because it requires observing people working through forms whose difficulty is already measured, which is exactly what the form-level scale now makes possible. The variance puzzle is its subject.

The model itself is built but not yet validated. It has been characterized across the whole family of forms, but it has not been tied to a single observed coordination outcome, and the authority that a readability formula earns by validation against comprehension is authority this model has not yet earned. The next arc is that validation. A hiring pipeline scored against the time a vacancy stays open, a content platform against the rate at which disputes escalate, a crowdwork market against the share of tasks returned unfinished: each would test whether a higher modeled score goes with a harder coordination, and each is a study the model makes possible. Such a test must vary determination structure at a fixed number of parties, because that is the model's novel content. An earlier attempt to validate against rideshare pooling was cut because pool size confounded the score with party count, leaving only the trivial axis tested.

Two boundaries the papers pre-registered as open are handed forward with the program. Paper 2 left the continuous-stream case as the limit of its individuation rule, the platform that commits determinations without pause, where the analyst must choose a window and Φ depends on the choice. An observed outcome could one day fix that window, and finding a domain in which to do so is open work. And the single scalar the instrument reports aggregates distinct modes of integration that a finer decomposition would separate, so a richer account of how triadic a form is, as against whether it is, remains to be built.

## 5.5 The bounds of the work

The dissertation names its load-bearing decisions rather than hiding them. Each is the point on which a paper stands or falls.

Paper 1 stands on the fair representation of the constructs it forecloses. The contribution is that the existing dyadic constructs cannot reach the triad, and that claim is worth nothing if any construct was represented in a weakened form before being set aside. The paper therefore represents each in its strongest version before showing where it stops short.

Paper 2 stands on the state-individuation rule and, equally, on the node update functions. The instrument's verdict depends on how the application layer is carved into states, so the rule that carves them is fixed by definition before any computation. The carving rule does not fix which Boolean function each node computes, how each party reads the mediator and how the mediator combines the parties, and Paper 2 is now explicit that the update functions, not the state boundaries, are where the magnitude is decided, and in some read structures the binary verdict itself. The binary classification is robust across the faithful update functions. The magnitude is not, which is why Paper 2 reads it only ordinally and Paper 3 declines to treat it as a scale. Fixing both the carving and the update functions before computing is what keeps the answer a fact about the modeled coordination rather than an artifact of the analyst's hand.

Paper 3 stands on the modeling choices that translate each organization's coordination into a determination structure. The graded score is a property of the model those choices define, so the paper fixes each structure before computing and reports what the model yields rather than asserting it. It makes no claim of validation against an observed outcome, and it says where such validation would have to begin.

Beyond the three named decisions, the dissertation holds to a set of bounds that keep its claims credible. It measures the coordination form and not the person, and it says so wherever the distinction could be blurred. Its formal claim is the precise one and no more: that the cause-effect structure of an observable application-layer matrix is irreducible over its minimum-information partition, a computable fact distinct from the analyst's inability to see the mechanism and from any metaphysical claim about emergence. Its model applies to systems that can be put in the form of a transition matrix, not to all systems. And it claims no validation against an observed outcome. The model is characterized across its domain and its assumptions are named, not confirmed against behavior. These are the edges of the contribution, and stating them is part of making it.

## 5.6 The transition

The title of the dissertation is the transition to algorithmacy, and the three papers are an account of what that transition is and how to measure its demand. Literacy became a competency when writing displaced speech as the medium through which a culture coordinated, and the competency was new because the structure of coordination had changed, not because people had changed. The claim of this dissertation is that an analogous change is underway in the structure of everyday coordination, that it runs through the algorithmic third party that now stands between the parties to most platform-mediated work, and that the competency this structure demands is as new, and as worth naming and measuring, as literacy once was. The dissertation names the competency, builds the formal model for the condition that demands it, and grades how strongly the coordination forms ordinary working life now runs on make that demand, as the model scores them. It does not validate that model against behavior, or measure the worker, and that is the right place for it to end, because the validation and the worker are where the next arcs of the program begin. The dissertation can now model coordination's readability as a property of the form. Validating that model against how coordination actually goes, and then measuring the reader, is the work still ahead.
