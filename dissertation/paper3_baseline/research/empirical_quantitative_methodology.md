# How to write Paper 3: the empirical / quantitative-analysis paper (craft reference)

Genre research to calibrate how Paper 3 is written — the analogue of Paper 1's
`integrative_review_methodology.md` and Paper 2's `methods_paper_methodology.md`. This does **not** go
into the paper; it sets the structure and reporting standard the paper must hit. Sources from the
academic connectors (Consensus, citation-ranked) plus a web deep-research harness (`w8k0fxi0c`) for
journal/structure color (folded in below). Citation integrity is deferred to the paper's bibliography

**The governing decision (author):** lean the section structure and prose voice on the **readability /
text-difficulty measurement papers especially**, because Paper 3's contribution is exactly analogous —
a score that is a *property of the form* (the text / the coordination form), validated against an
*observed outcome* (comprehension / coordination success), saying nothing about any individual. Plus the

## Cluster 1 — the canonical quantitative empirical-paper structure (IMRaD)

The empirical paper has a rigid, conventional structure (unlike the bespoke Papers 1–2). From
`RESEARCH_PLAYBOOK.md` Phase 7 and the cited papers' own organization:

**Standard section sequence** (short **noun-phrase** titles, never declarative sentences):
> Abstract · Introduction · Background/Theory · **Data and Methods** · **Results** · Discussion ·
> Limitations · Conclusion · Data and Code Availability · References.

- **~8,000 words**, with **Methods + Results ≈ half** the paper (the center of gravity of a quantitative
  paper is the design and the numbers, not the framing).
- **Hypotheses fixed before computation** (state H1 and the informative structural H0); call it "claims
  fixed before computation," not "pre-registered," absent a registry record.
- **Report effect size, not just significance** — with large N everything is significant; report the
  correlation, the variance explained, and the effect's magnitude, and say significance is uninformative
  at this N.
- **Match the register of the papers you cite** — read them first; argue through content and
  concession-then-claim, not adjectives. Run a de-slop pass with mechanical checks (em-dash count, the
  meta-narration phrase list, the section-title list read on its own).

## Cluster 2 — the readability paper as the structural template (the one to copy)

This is the load-bearing cluster. Readability papers validate a **form-level score** by **correlating it
against an observed outcome** — exactly Paper 3's anchor logic (Φ vs coordination friction/achievement).
Their shared skeleton, distilled from the exemplars below:

> **(1)** difficulty matters & is hard to judge by eye → **(2)** a *formula* scores it from features of
> the form → **(3)** a *corpus/sample* of forms + an *observed outcome* (comprehension, perceived
> difficulty, test scores, grade level) → **(4)** *compute* the formula and *correlate* it against the
> outcome → **(5)** *results*: the correlation / which features predict → **(6)** *discussion*: validity,
> where the formula holds and where it breaks, recommendations.

Map to Paper 3: formula = **Φ**; corpus = the **organization typology** + the **Chicago pooling** forms;
observed outcome = **friction / achievement**; correlate; results; discussion. The structure transfers
almost line-for-line.

- **[the closest template — form-score vs observed performance at scale] Rafatbakhsh, E., et al. (2023).**
  Predicting the difficulty of EFL reading comprehension tests based on linguistic indices. *Asian-Pacific
  J. of Second and Foreign Language Education.* 63 passages scored by linguistic indices (incl.
  Flesch-Kincaid), correlated against the **observed difficulty of 17,900 test-takers**. The exemplar for
  "a form-level score validated against a behavioral outcome at scale" — Paper 3's anchor in another
  domain.
- **[corpus + correlate-against-outcome; honest null] Zheng, J., et al. (2017).** Readability Formulas and
  User Perceptions of EHR Difficulty: A Corpus Study. *JMIR* 19(3):e59. Corpus (140 Wikipedia + 242 EHR
  notes), recruit raters, compute formulas, **correlate against perceived difficulty** — and report a
  *weak* correlation honestly. The template for the anchor section *and* for reporting a limit candidly.
- **[modern validation, methods register] Crossley, S. A., et al. (2017).** Predicting Text Comprehension,
  Processing, and Familiarity in Adult Readers. *Discourse Processes* 54(5–6). Validates readability
  against **measured comprehension/processing**, benchmarking new models vs the traditional formula. The
  "is the simple formula good enough?" structure.
- **[validation-as-test, methods journal] Lenzner, T. (2014).** Are Readability Formulas Valid Tools for
  Assessing Survey Question Difficulty? *Sociological Methods & Research* 43(4). A readability *validation*
  paper in a top methods journal — tests whether the formula correctly orders 71 difficult/improved
  question pairs. The register and the "does the form-score recover the known ordering?" logic (cf. Paper
  3's positive control: does Φ separate forms party-count cannot?).
- **[robustness/consistency precedent] Zhou, S., et al. (2017).** How Consistent Are the Best-Known
  Readability Equations…? *IEEE Trans. Professional Communication* 60(1). Studies how the score varies with
  tool, counting rules, and **passage length** — a worked robustness analysis (stability of a form-level
  score), the template for Paper 3's robustness battery.
- **[application + recommendation structure] Wang, L.-W., et al. (2013).** Assessing readability formula
  differences with written health information. *Research in Social and Administrative Pharmacy* 9(5).
  (417 cites.) Apply multiple formulas, compare, recommend — the "which measure, used how" discussion.
- *(Foundations to cite as the lineage: Flesch, R. (1948), A new readability yardstick, *J. Applied
  Psychology* 32(3); Kincaid, J. P., et al. (1975), Derivation of new readability formulas, Navy training
  report; Gunning Fog; McLaughlin SMOG (1969); DuBay, W. H. (2004), The Principles of Readability — the
  survey of the field and its validation history. Confirm exact details in the bibliography pass.)*

**Why this template fits and what it licenses:** readability scores are *sample-free properties of the
text* validated against an outcome and carrying no claim about a given reader — the exact stance of Claim
A (the unit is the form, not the person). Citing this lineage is what lets Paper 3 say "this is a
readability score for coordination" and have it land as a recognized move, not a metaphor.

## Cluster 3 — the validation framing: concurrent criterion validity

The anchor is, in measurement terms, a **concurrent criterion-validity** demonstration: a new measure
(Φ) correlated with an external **criterion** (the observed coordination outcome) measured on the same
units. Name it as such — it gives the anchor a recognized methodological standing.

- **[cornerstone] Cronbach, L. J., & Meehl, P. E. (1955).** Construct validity in psychological tests.
  *Psychological Bulletin* 52(4). (Already in Paper 2's bibliography.) Criterion validity is the mode of
  justification when a measure is checked against an external referent; validation embeds the measure in a
  network that yields testable predictions. The root of "calibrate the score against an outcome."
- **[validation IS hypothesis testing] Landy, F. J. (1986).** Stamp collecting versus science: Validation
  as hypothesis testing. *American Psychologist* 41(11). (263 cites.) Recasts validation as ordinary
  hypothesis testing — the direct warrant for stating **H1/H0** in Methods and reading the anchor
  correlation as a test, not a description.
- **[concurrent = correlation; the mechanics] Alavi, M., et al. (2023).** Considerations in the use of
  concurrent or predictive validity. *Nursing & Health Sciences.* Concurrent validity = both measures
  taken together, assessed by **correlation**; "a high positive correlation suggests robust concurrent
  validity." Names exactly what the anchor does (Φ and the outcome, same units, correlated).
- **[the near-precedent — a complexity measure vs a work outcome] Schlick, C., et al. (2015).** Validity
  Analysis of … Effective Measure Complexity. Validates an **information-theoretic complexity measure**
  (EMC) against a **coordination/work criterion** (total time to complete development activities) in
  cooperative project work, explicitly via criterion-related validity. The closest existing thing to Paper
  3's move — an information measure of a *work structure* validated against a coordination outcome. Cite to
  show the move has precedent and to position Paper 3's contribution against it.
- *(Methodological grounding: Schmitt, N., et al. (1984), Personnel Psychology — concurrent ≈ predictive
  validity coefficients; the criterion-validity reporting norms.)*

**What this licenses for Paper 3:** frame Stage 1 as concurrent criterion validation (Φ vs the observed
outcome on the same pooling forms), state it as a hypothesis test (H1/H0), and report the validity
coefficient (the correlation) as the evidence — the standard, recognized apparatus.

## Cluster 4 — robustness & honesty conventions (the credibility battery)

From `RESEARCH_PLAYBOOK.md` Phase 5 + the readability robustness precedent (Zhou 2017) + the large-N
reporting norm:

- **Effect size vs significance** (large N): report the correlation, **r²/variance explained**, and the
  multiplicative effect (the log-slope), and state significance is uninformative at N≈48k.
- **Stability across samples/time windows** — re-run the anchor on a second window; report whether the
  Φ→outcome relation holds (the readability analogue: score stability across corpora/lengths, Zhou 2017).
- **Within-stratum (Simpson's-paradox) check** — does the monotone friction trend hold within area×time
  strata, not only pooled?
- **Power / minimum-detectable-effect** — so a thin cell (e.g., the n≈2,884 pool-4) is interpretable.
- **Positive and negative controls, labeled** — the dyadic baselines (Φ=0) are the **negative control**
  (forms that should and do score zero); "Φ separates forms at fixed party count" (0.83 vs 2.0 at n=3) is
  the **positive control** that Φ adds signal beyond raw party count.
- **Disclose the confound** — the Φ↔friction relation runs partly through party count; name it in Methods
  (the fixed-n control answers it) and in Limitations.

## What this means for Paper 3 (synthesis)

1. **Structure the paper as a readability-measurement paper** wearing IMRaD: Introduction · The
   Coordination Form as Unit (Background) · Data and Methods · Results · Discussion · Limitations ·
   Conclusion · Availability. Short noun-phrase titles. ~8,000 words, Methods+Results ≈ half.
2. **The anchor section copies the readability-validation skeleton** (Rafatbakhsh/Zheng): the form-level
   score (Φ), the sample (pooling forms; the typology), the observed outcome (friction/achievement),
   the correlation, the limits — and names it **concurrent criterion validity** (Cronbach & Meehl; Landy;
   Alavi).
3. **State H1/H0 in Methods** (validation = hypothesis testing, Landy); H0 = "Φ tracks only party count,
   not determination structure," which the typology refutes.
4. **Run and report the full robustness battery** with effect sizes and labeled controls; disclose the
   party-count confound in the open.
5. **Position against Schlick 2015** as the nearest precedent (an information measure validated against a
   work-coordination outcome) — and against the readability lineage as the genre Paper 3 imports.
