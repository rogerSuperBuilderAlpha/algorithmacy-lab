## 1. Tradition: theory-driven, not theory-building

Paper 3 applies an already-specified instrument (the Paper 2 Φ classifier) to real organizations.
That is the **Yin "gaps and holes" theory-driven register** (Ridder 2017), where the research
question is strongly tied to existing theory and the cases instantiate, illustrate, develop, and
(weakly) test it. It is **not** Eisenhardt's inductive "no theory first" theory-building — whose
"singular aim is theory building" (Eisenhardt 2021) — because the model is prior, not generated
from the cases. Stake (social construction) and the extended case method (anomalies) also do not
fit a pre-specified-instrument demonstration.

Implication for framing: the cases are a **demonstration plus replication test**, not a definitive
validation. Ridder cautions case-based theory testing is "restricted to specific events" and is
weaker than experimental/quantitative testing. Calibrate the contribution statement accordingly —
the cases show the tool does analytic work and behaves as the model predicts across real forms;
they do not statistically validate Φ against an outcome (that stays future work, the cut anchor).

## 2. Case selection: theoretical sampling, diverse-case logic

Select by **theoretical (not statistical) sampling**. The citable logic for a set built to span one
dimension at full range is **Seawright & Gerring (2008) diverse-case method**: "maximum variance
along relevant dimensions," representing "the full range of values." Reinforced by **Burnham et al.
(2008)**: maximize comparative variance, minimize error variance, **control extraneous variance** —
which is exactly the design move of holding party count fixed while varying determination structure.
Eisenhardt's polar/extreme-type logic ("the process of interest is transparently observable") adds
support.

The design move (the cut-anchor lesson made into method): **vary determination structure; control
party count.** Determination structure is the X we span; party count is the nuisance variable we
hold fixed so the score's variation is attributable to structure, not size.

## 3. Replication logic (Yin) mapped onto our five cases

- **Literal replication** — cases predicted to produce the *same* classification because they share
  determination structure. Each treated as an experiment that confirms or disconfirms the prediction.
- **Theoretical replication** — cases predicted to produce *contrasting* classifications for
  model-predicted reasons (different determination structure).
- External validity in case work is **analytic generalization** via replication, not statistical
  generalization.

Our slate as a replication design (party count fixed at the two-party core for #1–#4; #5 extends n):

| Case | Structure | Predicted Φ | Replication role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Uber | strict mediation | 2.00 | literal-replication pair with the exchange (same structure) … |
| Securities exchange (Nasdaq/NYSE) | strict mediation | 2.00 | … **and** theoretical contrast on the *seat* (institution vs algorithm) at equal Φ |
| Upwork | partial mediation | 0.83 | literal-replication pair with ManpowerGroup … |
| ManpowerGroup | partial mediation | 0.83 | … **and** theoretical contrast on the seat (human vs algorithm) at equal Φ |
| Amazon Mechanical Turk | higher-order strict | 3.00 (n=4) | theoretical replication: structure binding >3 parties extends the score upward |

The headline (structure sets the score, not the mediator's seat) is carried by the two equal-Φ
pairs that differ on seat: a literal replication on Φ *and* a theoretical contrast on seat, in one.
Note this equality is true by construction once both are coded to the same structure — report it as
a modeling property (the model is indifferent to the seat once structure is fixed), not as an
empirical discovery, exactly as Paper 2's posture requires.

## 4. Case count

Five is well-supported. Diverse-case minimum is two (Seawright & Gerring); Eisenhardt's classic
heuristic is 4–10 (1989); Eisenhardt (2021) clarifies there is **no mandated count** — it flows
from the aim. For a demonstration paper the binding rationale is **full-range coverage of the
determination-structure dimension**, which five cases (three bands + two seat-contrasts + the
higher-order extension) satisfy.

## 5. Analytic structure: within-case first, then cross-case

The standard two coupled steps (Eisenhardt 1989; Creswell/Yin): **within-case analysis first** (each
organization as a stand-alone entity, its own patterns allowed to emerge), then **cross-case
synthesis**. So Paper 3 has five within-case write-ups, then one cross-case section.

## 6. Per-case anatomy (template)

A rigid nested 5-section template was **refuted** by the research (do not adopt one). The defensible
per-case anatomy combines Yin's standard components with the verified within-case step:

1. **Bounded unit of analysis and context** — what coordination, between whom, at what layer; the
   organization and its setting. State the boundary explicitly.
2. **Data sources and chain of evidence** — what the model's inputs are drawn from (filings, terms
   of service, public reporting, documented mechanism), so a reader can trace organization → evidence
   → classifier inputs. For provisional cases, mark each input's status (see §8).
3. **Within-case description** — how this organization actually coordinates its parties: who reads
   what, what the mediator commits, whether the parties keep a direct channel.
4. **The analytic operation** — model the coordination as a W–S–C (or W–S–C–D) system under Paper 2's
   pre-registered individuation rule; pre-register the determination structure *before* computing;
   run the classifier; report Φ, the band, and the MIP.
5. **Alternative explanations** — rival codings of the same organization and what they would yield
   (the sensitivity check), and why the chosen coding is faithful. This is where the "reads are a
   second declared choice" honesty from Paper 2 §5 lives.

## 7. Cross-case synthesis: a display matrix

Operationalize the cross-case section as a **Miles & Huberman (1994) display matrix**: themes/
dimensions as **rows**, the five cases as **columns ordered along the determination-structure
dimension** (dyadic → partial → strict → higher-order), filled by explicit decision rules. Reading
across rows shows the predicted Φ-classification pattern and the seat-invariance result. Tie the
matrix back to the Stage-1 catalog: the five cases sit on **populated bands of the 4,144-form
family**, so they are not cherry-picked.

## 8. Rigor, the ORM register, and provisional status

- **Rigor as reasoning, not template (Harley & Cornelissen 2022, ORM).** Rigor "emanates from the
  way researchers engage in a deliberate reasoning process of inferring theoretical claims from
  their data… exists outside of templates." The methods section must justify the **inferential
  chain** — organization observed → evidence → classifier inputs → Φ classification → coordination-
  form claim — not present a checklist as proof.
- **Transparency (Pratt 2008, via the ORM "Tumult over Transparency" essay).** Disclose by answering:
  Why was this study done? Why in this context? What is studied and why? How was it conducted and
  analyzed? Reveal design, sampling, data collection, analysis.
- **Yin's four tests** (construct/internal/external validity, reliability) and **Lincoln & Guba (1985)
  trustworthiness** (credibility, transferability, dependability, confirmability) were in scope but
  no standalone claim individually verified the full mappings — **cite Yin (2018) and Lincoln & Guba
  (1985) directly** when writing. External validity via analytic generalization/replication is
  confirmed.
- **Provisional/pre-fieldwork cases: no established convention exists.** Handle the provisional
  status as an honest **transparency/limitation disclosure**, not a recognized methodological
  category. State plainly which inputs are public-documentation provisionals to be replaced/
  supplemented with field data, per case, and in the methods section.

## 9. Methods-section skeleton (assembled from the above)

1. Register and aim — Yin theory-driven demonstration of the Paper 2 instrument; not theory-building,
   not outcome-validation. Calibrate the claim (demonstration + replication, not definitive test).
2. The unit of analysis — the coordination form, not the person (the readability move, carried from
   the recast).
3. Case selection — theoretical/diverse-case sampling; vary determination structure, control party
   count; the five cases and their replication roles (§3).
4. Modeling procedure — Paper 2's individuation rule; pre-register structure before computing; the
   classifier; what is reported (Φ, band, MIP).
5. The inferential chain and its rigor (Harley & Cornelissen); transparency disclosures (Pratt).
6. Provisional-status disclosure and the field-data plan.
7. Cross-case method — the ordered display matrix; link to the catalog's populated bands.
