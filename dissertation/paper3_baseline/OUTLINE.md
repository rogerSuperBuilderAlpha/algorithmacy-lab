# Paper 3 outline: a portable model for analyzing triadic coordination across organizations

Roger Hunt III
Bentley University

Working outline, v2 (reframed: the contribution is the *model* across organization types, anchored in one
data-rich domain — not a single-platform study).

The paper produces a portable model. It takes any organization's coordination structure, maps it to the
Worker–System–Counterpart application layer, computes Φ, and places the organization on a single scale of
triadic demand — the way a readability score places a text by how much reading competency it demands. The
scale is calibrated against an observed coordination outcome in one data-rich domain (rideshare pooling),
then the model is demonstrated across a typology of organization types. The measure is a property of the
coordination form, not of any individual. The instrument is Paper 2's contribution; the calibrated,
portable model is this paper's.

---

## 1. Introduction: from a decision procedure to a model

Paper 2 decided whether a coordination form is triadic; an instrument is not yet a model for analyzing
organizations until its output is anchored to something that varies with difficulty and shown to apply
across kinds. The introduction states the move from decision procedure to calibrated, portable model, and
commits to the readability analogy: a number that places an organization on a continuum of demand,
validated against how people actually coordinate through it, and general enough to place organizations of
very different types.

Moves: state what Paper 2 delivered and withheld. Declare the contribution as a portable model that
yields a calibrated difficulty scale across organization types. Commit to the coordination form as the
unit and to outcome validation as the anchor. Preview the typology the model will place.

## 2. The unit: the coordination form, not the person

The scale measures the organization's coordination form, and the dissertation should say so without
flinching. A difficulty score is a property of the form, validated against how participants perform on
it, and carries no claim about any individual. The person-level competency is the matched sibling the
program builds later, exactly as a reading-ability measure is the sibling of a text-difficulty measure.
Calibrating the form comes before matching participants to it.

Moves: state the unit and defend it. Draw the readability parallel: the text score is validated against
measured comprehension and says nothing about a given reader. Locate the variance puzzle (Paper 1's
opening) as the program's destination and explain why it does not belong here. Answer in advance the
reviewer who asks where the individuals are.

## 3. The model: mapping any organization to a comparable score

The heart of the paper. The model is a procedure: represent an organization's coordination as a W–S–C
application-layer system, individuate states by Paper 2's pre-registered rule, and compute Φ. Applied
*uniformly* — the same rule, the same node convention, the same granularity discipline — it yields scores
that are comparable across organizations that share no common vocabulary. The section specifies the
procedure precisely enough that another analyst could place a new organization, which is what makes it a
model rather than a set of examples.

Moves: state the procedure step by step. Show that uniform application is what licenses comparison. State
the granularity discipline the tractability limit imposes and how it is held constant across cases.
Establish that the model takes an organization in and returns a single comparable number.

## 4. The anchor: validating the scale in one data-rich domain

The scale's credibility rests on the anchor, because Φ gives a number and a number is not a difficulty
scale until it is tied to an observable outcome that varies with difficulty. The section names the anchor
and the validation logic. Rideshare pooling is the calibration domain: the platform coordinates two
riders who never meet plus a driver entirely through its matching determination, Φ varies with that
structure (solo vs pooled, by area and time), and the public record carries the outcome — whether the
match happened and what it cost. A high-Φ configuration must demonstrably produce the coordination
behavior a demanding form should, so the formal measure and the behavioral record move together.

Moves: state that the anchor is the empirical load-bearing decision, the way the state alphabet was Paper
2's. Specify the observed coordination outcome (pooled-match success and detour cost). Argue the dataset
(Chicago TNC Trips: large-scale interaction records with an outcome, not self-report) as the calibration
weight, explicitly *not* the subject of the paper. Report the Φ→outcome fit. Concede the single-domain
limit in one sentence and return: a calibrated scale in one domain is the precondition for porting the
model.

## 5. The baseline: the typology of organizations placed on the scale

The substance. With the scale calibrated, the model places a typology of organization types by their
computed Φ. The section reports where each falls and what the spread establishes: that organizations
differ in measurable degree of triadic demand, that one model places them all, and that the placements
track how coordination through each actually goes. The dyadic baselines anchor the floor; the strongly
triadic platforms and pipelines sit high; the **human-mediated contrast class** (a court, a staffing
agency, a broker) is the decisive test — if the model measures triadic coordination and not algorithms, a
human-mediated form with the same determination structure scores as triadic as an algorithmic one.

Moves: report the placements across the four classes (dyadic baselines; algorithmic platforms;
algorithmic-institutional gatekeepers; human-mediated intermediaries). Show the dyadic-limit cases sit
low and the irreducible cases sit high, as Paper 2 predicts. Make the human-mediated contrast carry its
weight: the model's prediction is that the seat of the mediator (algorithm vs person) does not set the
score; the determination structure does. State the baseline as the calibrated reference future work
measures against.

## 6. What the model establishes

The model establishes that the demand for algorithmacy is measurable, comparable across organization
types, and that it varies across the coordination forms ordinary working life now runs on. The section
states the contribution as a portable, calibrated instrument for placing any organization's coordination
on a scale of triadic demand, names the matched supply-side instrument as the next work, and positions
the baseline as the empirical foundation the program builds on.

Moves: state the contribution. Name the open extensions: the supply-side individual scale, additional
calibration domains, the within-form variance the matched pair will eventually explain. Close on the
program the model opens.

---

## Two load-bearing decisions, stated honestly

Paper 2 stands or falls on the state-individuation rule. Paper 3 stands or falls on the calibration
anchor (Claim C) and on the model's portability across organization types (Claim B). None is a doubt
about algorithmacy. All are questions about the instrument that measures it, and naming them as such is
what makes the dissertation defensible rather than fragile.
