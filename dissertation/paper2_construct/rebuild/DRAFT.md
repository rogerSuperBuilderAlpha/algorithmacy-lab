# Classifying Coordination Forms: An Integrated-Information Instrument for the Moderated Dyad and the Mediated Triad

Roger Hunt III · Bentley University

*Paper 2 of the dissertation. Methods and formal model. Draft. Every numerical result is reproducible from a committed script in `rebuild/`, named at the point of use. Bibliography follows the body, and every in-text citation resolves to it.*

---

## Abstract

Paper 1 named a competency, algorithmacy, and placed it in a coordination form the existing constructs do not reach. That form is the mediated triad. A third party constitutes the coordination between two others instead of carrying messages between them. Paper 1 left a question open. Handed a particular coordination form, how does an analyst decide whether it is a moderated dyad, which reduces to the pair, or a mediated triad, which does not? Inspection does not settle it. A worker can sit inside a triad she experiences as a two-party app. Paper 2 supplies the decision procedure. In supplying it, the paper establishes something the dissertation needs. Given a fully specified model of the coordination's mechanism, whether the form is triadic is a structural fact about that mechanism rather than the analyst's framing of it. The form is a mediated triad when its application-layer cause-effect power is irreducible. It is a moderated dyad when that power factors. Integrated information theory's Φ, a measure built in another field to quantify irreducibility, computes which. Specifying the mechanism is the analyst's job. The four-question criterion from Paper 1 constrains that job but does not fix it, so the objectivity is bounded. Given a declared mechanism the verdict is fixed and auditable, and a different declaration can be argued in the open. The procedure models a coordination form as a three-node Boolean system over worker, system, and counterpart, and reads the exact integrated information Φ over the minimum-information partition. Φ equal to zero classifies the form a moderated dyad. Φ greater than zero classifies it a mediated triad. The paper borrows the measure under the standards organization theory sets for importing a formalism, defends the import against the obvious cheaper alternatives, and checks the instrument on controls and worked cases before applying it. The contribution has two layers. The first is the classifier. The second is a warrant, and for the dissertation it is the deeper one. Once a coordination form is modeled faithfully, the verdict is fixed by the mechanism rather than chosen by the analyst, and this converts Paper 1's asserted distinctness into a demonstrated one. The existing competency constructs, modeled from what each says its own unit is, are structures that factor, and Φ scores them zero. The platform interaction, modeled with the counterpart constitutive of the determination, does not factor. A new construct is therefore required, not merely preferred. The paper builds the instrument and checks it on controls and on alternative faithful encodings of the same case. Calibration against coordination outcomes is Paper 3.

*Keywords:* mediated triad; moderated dyad; integrated information; Φ; coordination; formal model; construct validity; algorithmic management; theory borrowing

## 1. Introduction: the form question and why it has a formal answer

Paper 1 ends with a question it does not answer. It names two coordination forms. A moderated dyad is two parties whose exchange a third element shapes but does not constitute, so the coordination still reduces to the pair. A mediated triad is two parties whose coordination a third element constitutes, on terms of its own, so the relation no longer reduces to the pair. Paper 1 argues that platform work is increasingly the second form and that the existing competency constructs all describe the first. Then it hands the reader a particular network and asks which form it is. Paper 1 gives a provisional, four-question answer and defers the rigorous one. This paper is the rigorous one.

The question is not idle, because the surface does not settle it. Counting parties does not settle it. A setting with three visible parties is a moderated dyad if the third only carries what the other two send. Reading the interface does not settle it either. A driver who experiences nothing but an app may sit inside a three-party system she cannot see, where a rider she never chose is constitutive of the match she is handed. Paper 1 called this the false dyad and treated it as a warning. A coordination form can present as a dyad and be a triad. If appearance can mislead in this way, then the analyst needs something other than appearance.

The something is a structural fact about a specified mechanism. Triadicity, in the sense Paper 1 needs, is structural irreducibility. A coordination form is a mediated triad when its cause-effect power does not factor into independent parts along the line between the parties. It is a moderated dyad when that power factors. Irreducibility of this kind is not a matter of interpretation once the mechanism is fixed. It is a property of the mechanism, and it is computable. The mechanism still has to be written down, and writing it down is the analyst's job, so the objectivity claimed here is conditional. Given a fully specified mechanism, the verdict is the mechanism's, not the modeler's. The four-question criterion keeps the modeling honest. It says what a faithful model of a coordination form must contain. The criterion is necessary but not sufficient. It governs the mediator's determination and the channel between the parties. It leaves a second choice open. How the parties read the mediator also moves the verdict, and the criterion does not fix the reads. Section 5 shows both the discipline and the residual. So the claim is bounded. A faithful model needs the criterion met and the reads declared in advance. The verdict is then fixed by the model and auditable against the declaration. Integrated information theory built a measure for the irreducibility property. The measure is Φ, the integrated information a system generates over and above its parts, evaluated over the partition that makes the least difference. Φ equal to zero means the mechanism factors. Φ greater than zero means it does not. This paper imports that measure, defends the import, and turns it into a classifier of coordination forms.

The instrument runs over a small model. A coordination form is represented as a three-node system. One node is the worker, W. One is the system or mediator, S. One is the counterpart, C, the other human party the worker is coordinating with. Each node updates as a Boolean function of the current state of all three. The functions encode the mechanism of the form: what the mediator reads, what it commits, whether the two parties can reach each other directly. Exact IIT-4.0 system Φ over the minimum-information partition reads off whether that mechanism is irreducible. The model is deliberately small. Three nodes is enough to hold a worker, a counterpart, and a mediator, and small enough that the exact computation, which is expensive in general, is trivial here.

The paper has a second purpose beyond the classifier, and it is the one that earns the instrument its place in the dissertation. The existing competency constructs are accounts of how a worker engages a platform dyadically. A worker and a channel. A worker and a partner. A worker and a system. Each of these is a structure that factors. Each is a Φ-equal-zero form, and the zero is read from the construct's own account of its unit, not from a caricature. Take a construct that places the third element in the context the two parties read, not in a party that commits a joint determination. It has said its third element does not couple the pair. A mechanism built to that specification factors. So the dyadic verdict on the rivals follows from what the rivals claim, not from a hostile encoding. If the existing constructs all describe structures that factor, and the platform interaction, faithfully modeled, does not, then a new construct is not a preference. It is a requirement, because a construct built for a structure that reduces cannot reach a structure that does not. This is what keeps the dissertation's argument from circling. Paper 1 named the triad and asserted the rivals were dyadic. Paper 2 derives both verdicts from the mechanisms the constructs themselves specify.

The rest of the paper imports a foreign formalism and defends the import on the merits, not on the authority of its home field. Section 2 sets the standards organization theory holds borrowed models to and accepts them. Section 3 states what Φ measures, separates it from the consciousness theory it is usually attached to, and maps Paper 1's four-question criterion onto the structural conditions under which the three-party system fails to factor. Section 4 fixes where the measure runs, the application layer, and what that buys. Section 5 treats the one genuine empirical commitment, how the states are individuated, and shows the verdict's behavior under alternative carvings. Section 6 builds the instrument and runs its correctness controls. Section 7 applies it to worked cases and shows the classifier cutting against appearance in both directions. Section 8 answers the strongest objection, that a cheaper test would do the same work, with an exhibit. Section 9 reports what the topology alone does and does not fix. Section 10 states what the instrument establishes and what it does not. The contribution is a decision procedure that establishes, for a faithfully modeled form, the structural fact of the triad, and warrants the construct. It is not a calibrated scale, and the paper does not claim one.

## 2. The borrowing, and the discipline it requires

Organization theory borrows formal models from other fields. It always has. Population biology gave it organizational ecology, where Hannan and Freeman took the logic of selection among populations and applied it to populations of organizations (Hannan & Freeman, 1977). Kauffman's NK fitness landscapes from theoretical biology gave it a model of strategic complexity, where Rivkin parametrized a strategy as a set of decisions with interactions and read off how hard it is to imitate (Rivkin, 2000). Microeconomics gave it transaction-cost theory. The practice is old and it is respectable. Integrated information is the next thing to borrow, and this paper claims its place in that line.

Borrowing is respectable only under discipline, and the discipline has been written down. Whetten, Felin, and King set out what it takes to borrow a construct well, and the central demand is that the borrowed object carry its explanatory work in the new setting, not just its name (Whetten, Felin, & King, 2009). Oswick, Fleming, and Hanlon distinguish borrowing that imports a model wholesale from blending that adapts it, and they warn that one-way import has to be checked by reasoning about where the analogy breaks (Oswick, Fleming, & Hanlon, 2011). Ketokivi, Mantere, and Cornelissen give the sharpest standard. An analogy can decorate an argument or it can do the argument's reasoning. When it reasons, it has to be evaluated as an argument, with its inferential structure exposed and tested, not invoked as a suggestive image (Ketokivi, Mantere, & Cornelissen, 2017). This paper's analogy reasons. The whole classifier rests on the claim that organizational irreducibility is the same property that Φ measures. That claim is an argument, and Sections 3 through 8 expose and test it.

Two guards come with the standard, and the paper adopts both. The first is the disanalogy check. Hannan and Freeman, importing population biology, warned in the same breath against literal application, because a literal reading of the biological model would miss the organizational phenomenon it was meant to illuminate. They wrote of an analogue, not a homology. The distinction matters and a reviewer will press on it. A homology is a shared structure with a shared causal origin, two things that are the same because they descend from one thing. An analogy is a shared structure without a shared origin, two things that have the same shape for different reasons. The claim here is an analogy. Coordination irreducibility and informational irreducibility have the same formal shape. They do not have a common cause, and the paper does not say a platform is conscious or that a market is a brain. The second guard is the counterfactual check that Oswick and colleagues require. At each step the paper asks what would have to be false for the analogy to fail, and shows the case where it does fail. Section 8 is built around such a case.

The borrowing has to state exactly what it takes and what it leaves. What it takes is one thing: the integrated-information measure of irreducibility, Φ over the minimum-information partition, as formalized in IIT 4.0 and computed for a system as a whole (Albantakis et al., 2023; Marshall et al., 2023). What it leaves behind is everything else attached to that measure in its home field. It leaves the consciousness identity, the postulate that a system's cause-effect structure is its experience. It leaves the exclusion and maximization machinery that finds, among all candidate substrates, the one that maximizes Φ and calls that the locus of experience. None of that is needed to classify a fixed three-party system as reducible or not. The classifier needs the binary verdict at the system level and nothing above it. Dropping the rest is not a concession forced by critics. It is a consequence of using only the part that does the organizational work.

The borrowing cannot lean on the authority of integrated information theory to justify the measure's organizational meaning. The theory is contested in its home field, and the contest is about the consciousness identity, the part this paper does not import. The measure's meaning here has to be re-earned in organizational terms. That is what Section 3 does. It says, in the language of coordination, what it means for a three-party structure to factor, and shows that the factoring is the moderated dyad and the failure to factor is the mediated triad.

## 3. What Φ measures, separated from consciousness, and the construct mapping

Φ measures one thing. It measures how much a system's cause-effect power is lost when the system is cut into parts. Take a system in a state. It constrains its own past and future, which is to say the current state makes some prior states more likely and some next states more likely. Now cut the system along a partition, severing the connections that cross the cut, and ask how much the constrained probabilities change. Do this for every partition and find the one that changes them least. That partition is the minimum-information partition, the MIP, the cut that does the least damage. Φ is the damage that cut still does. If some cut does no damage at all, the system's behavior is fully accounted for by its parts acting separately, and Φ is zero. The formal statement is exact. The system-level integrated information is zero if and only if the system's intrinsic cause-effect power factors over some partition (Albantakis et al., 2023; Marshall et al., 2023). "Cause-effect power" here is the system's constraint on its own past and future. It is not the unfolded cause-effect structure, the set of distinctions and relations that IIT builds on top of the system measure, which Section 2 set aside.

The formalism is separable from the theory built on it. Integrated information theory adds a postulate to the measure, that the cause-effect structure a system specifies is identical to its experience, and from that postulate it derives claims about consciousness (Tononi, Boly, Massimini, & Koch, 2016). The hard disputes about the theory are disputes about that postulate. The measure underneath does not depend on it. Φ is a function of a transition probability matrix and a state. It returns a number whether or not anything is conscious. This paper uses the function and declines the postulate, and the declining costs nothing, because the postulate was never doing organizational work.

The measure maps onto Paper 1's four-question criterion. Paper 1 gave four questions for telling a moderated dyad from a mediated triad, and deferred the rigorous version. Each question turns out to name a structural condition under which the three-party cause-effect power fails to factor. The mapping is the analogy doing its argumentative work, so it is stated as an argument and checked case by case in Sections 6 and 7.

The first question is whether the mediator commits a determination or only carries one. In the model this is whether the system node S updates as a genuine joint function of both other parties. A mediator that commits reads both sides, so its rule is `S' = f(W, C)` with f depending on both arguments. The résumé system that scores an applicant against a posting reads the applicant and the posting and commits a score that is a function of both. A mediator that only carries does not. Its rule ignores one side, `S' = W`, passing the worker's input through untouched. A pass-through factors. The counterpart drops out of the system's behavior, and the structure reduces to the worker and the channel. Commit makes Φ positive. Carry makes it zero.

The second question is whether the parties are kept apart or can route around the mediator. In the model this is whether there is a direct edge between W and C. If the only path from the worker to the counterpart runs through S, the structure cannot bypass the mediator, and the mediator's determination is load-bearing. Restore a direct channel, an edge from C to W or W to C that does not pass through S, and the structure can route around the middle. When it can, the cut that severs the mediator stops doing damage, because the parties still reach each other, and Φ falls. Kept apart preserves the triad. A route-around collapses it.

The third question is whether the unseen party is assigned or chosen. In the model this is whether C is constitutive of the mediator's determination even when the worker never sees or selects C. This is the false dyad. The edge that carries it is `S' = f(W, C)` with C present in f, while the worker's own rule never reads C directly. The worker experiences a two-party app. The structure is a three-party system, because the determination she acts on is a function of a party she did not choose. The mediator assigns the counterpart into its own determination. That is the C-into-S edge, and it keeps Φ positive.

The fourth question is whether the mediator holds its own interest or passes input through neutrally. In the model this is whether S commits a determination with a form of its own, a non-trivial function f, rather than a pass-through. A neutral conduit applies the identity. A mediator with an interest applies a function that serves ends of its own, and that function is what couples the two parties through the middle. Interest, in the model, is a committed determination rather than a relay. The committed determination is what fails to factor.

The four conditions line up into one statement. The cause-effect power of a coordination form is irreducible when the mediator commits a joint determination over both parties, the parties cannot reach each other except through it, the unseen party is constitutive of that determination, and the determination has a form of its own. When all four hold, no cut along the party line is free, and Φ is positive. The form is a mediated triad, and it demands the competency Paper 1 calls algorithmacy. When any of them fails, some cut is free, and Φ is zero. The form is a moderated dyad, navigable with the literacies the existing constructs describe. Reducible structure, factoring along party lines, moderated dyad, literacy. Irreducible structure, no free cut, mediated triad, algorithmacy. The mapping is the paper's load-bearing claim, and the rest of the paper tests it.

## 4. The application layer: where the measure runs

The instrument runs over the application layer, not the machine. A platform's internal mechanism is proprietary and mostly hidden. Its ranking model, its matching solver, its pricing engine are not observable, and the instrument does not pretend to read them. What is observable is the interface the parties move through: the states they occupy, the inputs they supply, and the determinations the mediator commits back to them. A driver goes available, gets matched, drives, goes available again. A rider requests, waits, is served. The system commits a match, a fare, a route. These are application-layer states and application-layer determinations. The model is built from them.

Two kinds of not-knowing must be kept apart, because the instrument addresses one and not the other. The first is epistemic opacity. The worker cannot see the mechanism. She does not know how the match was computed, and the firm will not tell her. This is the condition Christin documents in algorithmic workplaces, where the rules are hidden and shifting and workers reason about them from the outside (Christin, 2017). Epistemic opacity is about access to the mechanism. The second is compositional irreducibility. The whole does not reduce to its parts even when the parts are fully known. Φ is about the second. It is computed from a specified mechanism, with nothing hidden, and it asks whether that mechanism factors. The instrument does not measure how opaque the platform is. It measures whether the coordination form, specified at the application layer, is irreducible. A form can be irreducible and transparent, or opaque and reducible. The two properties are independent, and conflating them would be a mistake.

The model is built from a specified mechanism, so its formal properties hold by construction. PyPhi, the reference implementation of the measure, is valid for systems that are Markovian and satisfy conditional independence, meaning each node's next state depends only on the current state and, given the current state, the nodes update independently of one another (Mayner et al., 2018). When the transition matrix is built from declared Boolean update rules, as it is here, both properties hold automatically. The rules are functions of the current state, so the process is Markov. The nodes are separate functions evaluated on the same input, so they are conditionally independent given that input. Nothing has to be assumed or checked. The properties are built in.

This is also where the empirical extension parts company from the formal model, and the division is clean. Inferring a transition matrix from real interaction logs, rather than declaring it, is a different task, and there the Markov and conditional-independence properties are not free. The PyPhi authors warn that matrices estimated from observed time series are not guaranteed to satisfy conditional independence and must be checked (Mayner et al., 2018). For the formal instrument this paper builds, the warning does not bind, because the matrix is specified, not estimated. For the empirical program that estimates a matrix from logs, the warning binds hard, and the check becomes a precondition rather than an afterthought. That program is Paper 3. Paper 2 builds and validates the instrument on specified mechanisms. Paper 3 takes it to data, where the conditional-independence check is the first gate.

## 5. The state alphabet: the entire empirical commitment

Φ is not invariant to how the states are carved. The same coordination form can be modeled with a coarse alphabet, available versus busy, or a fine one, available, matched, en route, arrived, and the integrated information will generally differ between them. This is the one place where a real choice enters. Carving the states is the entire empirical commitment of the method. Everything else follows from the carving and the declared mechanism.

The commitment is handled the way measurement-dependent choices are handled elsewhere. The individuation rule is fixed by definition and declared before any computation, the way a coding scheme is pre-registered before a content analysis. The rule for this paper is one node per party, with each node taking a binary application-layer state that marks the party's status in the coordination, available or not, committed or not, waiting or not. The choice is stated up front so that no result depends on a carving selected after seeing the answer. Pre-registration prevents fishing. It does not by itself show the verdict is stable, so the stability is checked directly.

The check re-encodes the rideshare case several ways and reads the verdict each time. This is in `review_response.py`, and it shows both what the criterion fixes and what it leaves open. The baseline carving gives a mediated triad. A carving that re-derives the driver's availability from both the system and the counterpart, a genuinely different worker-state rule, still gives a mediated triad, with maximum Φ of 0.415. Two of the re-encodings flip the verdict to moderated dyad for reasons the criterion names. One leaves the counterpart's state to hold rather than clear, so the counterpart stops being a live party and the form becomes a dyad with a spectator. The other softens the dispatch from a conjunction, which fires only when a driver and a rider are both present, to a disjunction, which fires on either side alone, so the mediator no longer commits a joint determination. Both of those flips break a condition the four-question criterion states, so the criterion is doing real work.

The criterion does not do all the work. The four questions govern the mediator's determination and the channel between the parties. They do not fix how the worker and the counterpart read the mediator's state, and that read is a second modeling choice. Hold the dispatch fixed at the joint conjunction `S' = W ∧ C` with no direct channel, so that every condition the criterion names is met, and vary only the reads. The verdict still moves. A pure bottleneck read, where both parties simply track the system, gives a triad. A realistic feedback read, where the counterpart accumulates rather than clearing (`C' = C ∨ ¬S`), gives a moderated dyad, at Φ of zero, even though the mediator still commits the joint conjunction over both live parties. Section 9 reports the same thing across the whole read space. Only a minority of strict-mediation reads preserve the triad. So the criterion is necessary, not sufficient. A faithful model needs the criterion met and the reads declared, and reasonable analysts could declare the reads differently and reach different verdicts.

This bounds the objectivity claim rather than dissolving it. Given a fully specified mechanism, the determination and the reads both written down, the verdict is the mechanism's and not the modeler's, because the computation is fixed once the mechanism is. What the modeler chooses is the mechanism, and the criterion narrows that choice without closing it. The method makes the choice explicit and checkable. The reads are declared in advance, the way the state alphabet is, and they are defended on the substance of the case rather than selected after the verdict appears. The verdict is then auditable against the declaration. The reads have no rule as tight as the one that fixes the state alphabet. The alphabet is pinned by a stated rule, one node per party with a binary status. The reads are disciplined only by argument about the case. That is the method's thinnest joint, and the model has to meet it in the open, by declaring the reads and defending them on the case. That is weaker than a verdict no analyst could move, and it is what a formal model of this kind can actually deliver. The full sensitivity analysis, over many carvings and reads of real logs, is Paper 3's, where the alternatives can be compared against outcomes at scale.

The hardest case for any individuation rule is the continuous platform. A ride-hailing app does not move through a few discrete states. It runs a stream of micro-determinations, each a small update to position, price, and match probability. Carving that stream into states is a real difficulty. The continuous case is the rule's hardest test, and it is handed to the empirical program as such. The formal instrument works on the discrete model, where the states are declared. Whether a given continuous platform is faithfully captured by a particular discrete carving is an empirical question about that platform, and it belongs to Paper 3.

## 6. The instrument and its correctness controls

The instrument is a three-node Boolean network and a classifier on top of it. The three nodes are the worker W, the system S, and the counterpart C. The network is specified by three update rules, one per node, each a Boolean function of the current state of all three. From the rules the instrument builds the state-by-node transition matrix and the connectivity matrix, the latter by testing, for each pair of nodes, whether flipping one changes the other's update. For each reachable state it computes the exact IIT-4.0 system Φ over the minimum-information partition. The test ranges over reachable states, the states that have at least one predecessor, the states some other state maps into. This is the predecessor sense of reachable, not reachability from a fixed starting state. A coordination form's verdict should rest on configurations the form can actually occupy, not on states it can never reach. The classifier reads the binary verdict. If Φ is zero at every reachable state, the structure factors, and the form is a moderated dyad. If Φ is greater than zero at some reachable state, the structure does not factor, and the form is a mediated triad. The computation uses PyPhi's system-integrated-information routine, which implements the IIT-4.0 system measure directly (Mayner et al., 2018; Marshall et al., 2023). At three nodes the exact computation, which scales exponentially and becomes infeasible past roughly a dozen nodes, runs in well under a second. The implementation is `instrument.py` in the `rebuild/` directory, and every number below is reproducible by running it and the scripts that import it.

No worked example runs before two controls. The controls are a correctness check, not a validation. They test that the instrument computes the verdict correctly on cases whose answer is known by construction. They do not test the mapping from a coordination form to a dyad or triad against anything outside the model. That mapping is argued in Section 3. The factoring control decouples the counterpart: the worker reads the system, the system reads the worker, and the counterpart reads only itself (`W' = S`, `S' = W`, `C' = C`). This is a dyad with a spectator attached, and it must score zero. It does. Φ is zero at every reachable state, and the verdict is moderated dyad. The irreducible control couples all three: the worker reads the system or the counterpart, the system reads the worker and the counterpart, and the counterpart reads the parity of the worker and the system (`W' = S ∨ C`, `S' = W ∧ C`, `C' = W ⊕ S`). This must score positive. It does. The maximum Φ across reachable states is 0.830, the mean is 0.484, and the minimum-information partition at the maximum is the full tripartition that separates all three nodes. The verdict is mediated triad. The two controls separate cleanly, zero against positive, which shows the computation returns the right value when the answer is known. The controls are in `instrument.py`.

The partition the test uses is the minimum-information partition, and the choice of partition matters. One might think the natural cut for a three-party question is the complete cut that separates each party from the others, `{W}{S}{C}`. That cut over-calls. It severs every connection, so it finds positive integration in structures that in fact reduce along a coarser line, and it would label moderated dyads triadic. The test is therefore not the complete cut. It is Φ over the minimum-information partition, the cut that does the least damage. The MIP is party-respecting where the structure permits. In the worked triads below it lands on partitions like `{W, SC}`, which keeps the system and the counterpart together and separates the worker, rather than on the complete three-way cut. The instrument reports the MIP at the maximum-Φ state for every case, so the reader can see which cut carried each verdict.

## 7. Worked applications: the classifier cuts against appearance, both ways

The dyadic limit is not a toy. It is the structure the existing competency constructs assume, and putting it through the instrument is the foreclosure the dissertation needs. The encoding is not a caricature chosen to score zero. It is read off what the construct says its own unit is. Computer-mediated-communication competence and human-machine communication place the third element in the channel, or in the context the two parties read while they deal with each other. A channel conveys. It does not commit a determination that couples a third party into the relation. So a model faithful to the construct's own claim gives a counterpart node that does not enter the mediator's determination, which is the decoupled `C' = C`. Take chat with a language model, the human-machine-communication case at its cleanest. A worker and a model exchange turns. The worker reads the model and the model reads the worker, and the model commits nothing that couples a third party into the exchange (`W' = S`, `S' = W`, `C' = C`). The instrument scores it. Φ is zero at every reachable state, and the verdict is moderated dyad. The construct that models this case as a worker relating to a channel is an accurate account of a structure that factors, and the zero follows from the construct's own commitment that its third element is a channel. The foreclosure is not that the construct is wrong about its own case. It is that its case is dyadic, by the method, and so the construct cannot be the account of a case that is not. This and the cases below are in `worked_examples.py`.

The irreducible triad scores the other way. Take a résumé sent to an applicant-tracking system that scores it for a hiring manager. The applicant reads the system's determination and the manager reads it, and the system commits a determination that is a joint function of the applicant and the manager's posting (`W' = S`, `S' = f(W, C)`, `C' = S`). Sweeping the determination f across the standard Boolean functions sorts the cases. When the system commits a conjunctive or disjunctive function, AND, OR, NAND, or NOR, the maximum Φ is 2.0, the mean across reachable states is 0.5, and the minimum-information partition is `{W, SC}`. The verdict is mediated triad. When the system commits parity, XOR or XNOR, Φ is 0.5 and the MIP is the full tripartition. Still a triad. When the system ignores one party, committing `f = W` so the manager's posting drops out, or `f = C` so the applicant drops out, Φ is zero and the verdict is moderated dyad. The last case is the false triad below. The point of the sweep is that a system reading both parties and committing a joint determination produces an irreducible structure across a range of determinations, and the dyadic construct applied here scores the case zero by leaving the third party out. It is wrong about this case, by the method.

The false dyad is the centerpiece, because it is where appearance and structure come apart most sharply. Rideshare presents to the driver as a two-party app. She interacts with a screen. The structure is a three-party system, because the rider she never chose is constitutive of the dispatch she is handed. The model makes the difference one edge. In both versions the driver's availability is consumed by a dispatch and the rider keeps waiting until served (`W' = ¬S`, `C' = C ∧ ¬S`). The versions differ only in what the dispatch reads. In the triad the dispatch reads both sides, `S' = W ∧ C`, because a match requires an available driver and a waiting rider. In the dyadic model the dispatch reads the driver only, `S' = W`. The instrument scores them. The triad reaches Φ of 2.0 at the all-on state, with a mean of 0.400 over its five reachable states and a MIP of `{W, SC}`. The dyadic model scores zero everywhere. One edge, the connection from the counterpart into the dispatch, carries the entire verdict. "A worker may sit inside a mediated triad she cannot see" is no longer a slogan. It is the difference between two models that differ by a single edge, and the instrument finds the edge.

The single edge tempts an easy dismissal. The author added a connection that reads the rider, so of course the rider matters, and Φ only reports back the coupling that was put in. The dismissal would be right if reading the rider were enough to make the form a triad. It is not, and the instrument is needed because it is not. A dispatch that fires when a driver and a rider are both present, the conjunction `S' = W ∧ C`, reads both parties and gives a triad. A dispatch that fires when either is present, the disjunction `S' = W ∨ C`, also reads both parties and gives a moderated dyad, as Section 5 reports. Both edges are there. Both read the rider. One form is irreducible and one factors, and which is which cannot be seen by checking whether the edge exists. The exhibit in Section 8 makes the same point at full strength, with every node reading both others and the whole structure still factoring. So the verdict is not a restatement of the edge. It is a fact about whether the determination is a constitutive joint function, which the conjunction is and the disjunction is not, and that fact is what the instrument computes and the eye does not. A dyadic construct applied to rideshare scores it zero by omitting the rider. A casual three-party model that reads the rider through a disjunction scores it zero too. The conjunction is the faithful model, and on it the case is a triad.

Appearance misleads in the other direction too, which is why the instrument is needed rather than a head-count. The false triad has three visible parties and still reduces. A mediator that ignores one party, committing a determination that reads only the worker, presents as a three-party arrangement and is structurally a dyad with a spectator. This is the `f = W` case from the sweep. Φ is zero, and the verdict is moderated dyad, because the third party, though present on the screen, does nothing the structure depends on. Three parties present, structure factors. Two parties present, structure may or may not factor. The count does not decide it. The mechanism does, and the instrument reads the mechanism.

The per-state profiles matter. They are in `review_response.py`. The triad verdicts above rest on the state where both parties are simultaneously live. The ATS triad carries Φ above zero at one of its four reachable states, the all-on state. The false-dyad triad carries it at one of five, again the all-on state. The irreducible control, by contrast, carries Φ above zero at five of its six reachable states. The thin triads are not a defect. They are what the classifier's rule means. Irreducibility appears exactly when the worker and the counterpart are both engaged and the mediator must resolve the two together, which is the moment the three-party coordination actually occurs. At states where one party is idle, there is nothing for the mediator to integrate, and the structure factors there. The rule "Φ above zero at some reachable state" therefore reads as "there is a configuration the form can occupy in which the coordination does not reduce to the pair." One such configuration is enough to make the form a triad, by design, because a form that is irreducible only when fully engaged is still irreducible.

The single carrying state is not a knife-edge of exact arithmetic. Mixing the false-dyad triad's transition matrix toward uniform, a crude way to add noise, degrades the integrated information at the all-on state smoothly rather than collapsing it. Φ at the all-on state runs 2.0 with no noise, 1.90 at two percent, 1.75 at five percent, and 1.53 at ten percent. The verdict survives perturbation. The triad is carried by one state, and that state is robust. The mixing is a crude noise model, not a calibrated process, so it shows robustness rather than a realistic decay rate. This is in `review_response.py`.

## 8. Why Φ, and not a cheaper test

The strongest objection to the whole apparatus is that a cheaper test would do the same job. Φ is expensive in general and exotic by background. If a simple structural check sorted dyads from triads, importing integrated information would be ornament. The objection has real force, and it comes from inside the measure's home field. The PyPhi authors note that a system that is not strongly connected necessarily has Φ of zero, because a one-directional cut through it removes no edges and so does no damage (Mayner et al., 2018). Connectivity is therefore a sufficient detector of reducibility. If a structure is not strongly connected, it factors, and no expensive computation is needed to know it. So a connectivity test, or a conditional-independence test, or a factorization test catches the easy reducible cases for free.

The objection is answered by showing where the cheap tests fail, and the answer is an exhibit, not an aggregate. Sufficiency runs one way. Not strongly connected implies Φ of zero. The converse is false. Strongly connected does not imply Φ positive. There are structures that are fully connected and still reduce, and on those the cheap tests over-call, because they key on edges and the reduction is not visible in the edges. The exhibit is one such structure: `W' = NOR(S, C)`, `S' = ¬W ∧ C`, `C' = NAND(W, S)`. It has all six off-diagonal edges. Every node reads both others. No node is constant. It is strongly connected. By every cheap structural test it is triadic. The connectivity test calls it a triad. The factorization test, which looks for a clean split of the party graph into non-interacting blocks, finds none and calls it a triad. Both over-call. The exact computation disagrees. Φ is zero at every reachable state. The reachable states are `(0,0,0)`, `(0,0,1)`, `(1,0,1)`, and `(0,1,1)`, four of the eight, and the all-on state `(1,1,1)` is not among them. The exhibit cannot enter the all-on state, the configuration that carries the triad verdict in every worked case above. The dyad verdict rests on the reachable set just as the triad verdicts rest on the all-on state, and the reachable-only rule is applied to both. The exhibit's reduction is a fact about the half of the state space the form can occupy, which is the half the verdict should rest on. The structure factors despite being fully connected, because the dependence among the reachable nodes is conditional, and the minimum-information partition finds the factoring that the edges hide. The verdict is moderated dyad, and it is correct. This is `why_phi.py` and `review_response.py`.

The exhibit is not only an automaton. It is a coordination pattern with a recognizable kin in platform-work research. Picture a mediator that reads both parties and commits a determination back to each, with all the apparatus of a constitutive third party, and yet whose determination is fixed by what the two parties already are. The system looks like it makes the match. In fact, on the configurations it can occupy, its output is redundant given the two parties' own states. Knowing the worker and the counterpart fixes the mediator, so the mediator adds no constraint the pair does not already supply, and the coordination reduces to the pair despite the screen full of mediation. The kin is the documented pattern of platform apparatus that presents as constitutive and is not. Hamblin describes fauxtomation, the charade in which a system is dressed in the authority of an algorithm while the real work runs elsewhere (Hamblin, 2022). Öborn and colleagues describe a gig triangle whose algorithmic allocation the parties route past while the three-party form persists on the screen (Öborn et al., 2025). The exhibit makes the structure of that pattern exact. A third party can present every sign of constituting the coordination and add no constraint to it.

The exhibit does not yet have a tight empirical instance, and this paper does not claim one. Its closest documented relatives, fauxtomation and routed-past mediation, are kin by appearance rather than proven matches to the exhibit's exact wiring, where the mediator is fully connected to both parties, there is no direct channel between them, and the determination still reduces. Finding a platform that takes that exact shape, and showing an analyst would misread it, is a measurement task for Paper 3, not a claim made here. The exhibit's present job is narrower and it does it. It shows that reading the update functions by eye does not settle the verdict. Every node reads both others, so an analyst checking for couplings sees a triad, and counting parties sees a triad, and only the minimum-information partition sees that the apparatus is redundant. That is where the cheap tests fail, and it is the discipline the instrument enforces against calling a form triadic on sight. The false dyad is a triad that looks like a dyad. The exhibit is a dyad that looks like a triad. The instrument is needed for both.

The exhibit is not a one-off. An analogous phenomenon appears in the reference implementation's own documentation, in the Rule 110 example, where a fully connected automaton has a component with Φ of zero because knowing two of its elements fixes the third, so a cut that looks destructive removes no real constraint. The mechanism in the Rule 110 case is a deterministic logical identity, and the mechanism in the exhibit is a state-dependent collapse of constraint, so the two are kin rather than the same. What they share is the lesson. Conditional dependence makes a strongly connected structure reducible. The cheap tests cannot see it, and Φ over the minimum-information partition can. That residual zone, strongly connected yet reducible, is where Φ earns its weight. The cheap test handles the easy zone. The expensive test is needed exactly where appearance and structure part, which is the zone the false dyad lives in too.

The closest alternative measure can be checked directly, and it fails. Effective information and the causal-emergence measures built on it are the nearest relatives from the same lineage (Hoel, Albantakis, & Tononi, 2013; Comolatti & Hoel, 2025). Effective information has no partition term. It is a function of a system's determinism and degeneracy, computed over a maximum-entropy intervention, without minimizing over any cut. Because it never minimizes over a partition, it cannot detect the factoring the classifier turns on. Computed on the exhibit, effective information is 2.0 bits, while Φ over the minimum-information partition is zero. The contrast is sharper than a tie. Effective information does not even order the cases correctly. The factoring control, a genuine dyad, has the highest effective information of the three at 3.0 bits, because it is fully deterministic and effective information rewards determinism. The irreducible control, a genuine triad, has 2.5 bits. The exhibit, a dyad that every cheap test over-calls, has 2.0. Effective information runs opposite to the dyad-triad verdict here. The worked cases show the same inversion. On the false-dyad pair, the dyadic model scores 2.5 bits of effective information and the triad scores 2.16, so effective information rates the dyad higher than the triad it is supposed to detect. The applicant-tracking triad scores 1.81, the lowest of all the cases, dyads included. Effective information does not order the cases by the dyad-triad line, and on the centerpiece pair it reverses it. It is not a substitute, and only a measure that minimizes over partitions draws the line the classifier needs. These computations are in `review_response.py`.

Other measures in the same partition-minimizing family are not tested here, and the paper does not claim they agree. Geometric Φ and the practical integrated-information measures are partition-based irreducibility measures built to detect the same kind of factoring (Oizumi, Tsuchiya, & Amari, 2016; Barrett & Seth, 2011). They use different baselines and different normalizations, and they are known to disagree on some systems at the boundary where Φ sits near zero, which is where the controls and the exhibit sit. Whether geometric Φ and Φ* assign the same zero-set as the IIT-4.0 measure on these boundary cases is an open question this paper does not settle. The IIT-4.0 system measure is the one adopted, for practical reasons. It is canonical, exactly computable at this size, and implemented in a maintained reference toolbox (Marshall et al., 2023; Mayner et al., 2018; Kitazono, Kanai, & Oizumi, 2018). The verdict reported here is the verdict of the adopted measure. Computing geometric Φ and Φ* on the controls and the exhibit, to check whether the line is robust across the family, is an open task.

One competitor is left open. Partial information decomposition, and its dynamical extension into integrated information decomposition, splits the information several sources carry about a target into redundant, unique, and synergistic parts (Williams & Beer, 2010; Mediano et al., 2025). Synergy is a candidate signature of irreducibility, and whether a synergy measure performs a partition-minimization equivalent to the MIP, or merely correlates with Φ being positive and over-calls the hard case, is not settled here. This is the single open comparison. Computing a synergy measure on the exhibit would settle it, and that computation is not done in this paper. It is a limitation and an obvious next analysis.

Cost is not an issue at the size the paper works. Exact Φ scales steeply, and at scale a cheaper measure would have a real advantage. At three nodes the exact computation is trivial in absolute terms, so the cost argument for substituting a cheaper measure has nothing to bite on. The classifier's problem is a three-party problem, and three parties is small. The aggregate comparison counts, how often Φ and a given cheap test agree across some catalog of forms, are comparator-dependent and are not the headline. The headline is the exhibit. One fully connected structure that reduces is enough to show that the cheap tests are not sufficient and that Φ does work they cannot.

## 9. What the topology fixes, and what it does not

Three parties in a row do not make a triad. The result cuts against an easy reading of Paper 1. One might think that putting a mediator between two parties, with no direct channel, is enough to make the form irreducible. It is not. The verdict turns on what the mediator commits and on what the parties can do, not on the topology alone. Two findings show it, both at the binary level, and both withholding any claim about magnitude.

The first finding is that the reads decide the verdict, not just the presence of a mediator. Hold the mediator's determination fixed at a joint function, `S' = W ∧ C`, and sweep the reads, the functions by which the worker and the counterpart take in the system's state, across all strict-mediation forms with no direct worker-counterpart channel. Most of these do not preserve the triad. Under the fully specified read space, every Boolean read function on each side, only 12.5 percent of the read pairs, 32 of 256, keep Φ positive. The fraction depends on how the read space is defined, and a narrower admissible set raises it, so the percentage is reported as read-space-dependent rather than as a constant of nature. The qualitative claim is robust across the definitions. Only a minority of strict-mediation reads preserve the triad, and the reads that look like realistic feedback collapse it. The two anchors make this concrete. A pure bottleneck, where both parties simply read the system's state (`W' = S`, `C' = S`), reaches Φ of 2.0 and is a triad. A realistic feedback structure, where the worker reads against the system and the counterpart accumulates (`W' = ¬S`, `C' = S ∨ C`), scores zero and is a dyad. Same mediator, same fixed determination, opposite verdicts, decided by the reads. This is `sweeps.py`.

The second finding is the eliminate-the-dyad result, and it is stated at the binary level only, because the magnitudes do not behave like a scale. Hold the determination fixed at `S' = W ∧ C` again and vary the direct worker-counterpart channel, the edge that would let the parties route around the mediator. With no direct channel the form is a triad, maximum Φ of 2.0. With a full bypass, where the worker and counterpart simply read each other, the form is a dyad, Φ of zero. The two endpoints are robust and they point the way the argument needs. Removing the direct channel keeps the form classified a triad, so a platform that wants the interaction to stay triadic has a structural reason to design the direct channel out. That is the political-economy reading, and it holds at the binary level. The converse is documented in platform-work research. When workers and clients open a direct channel off the app, the coordination collapses toward the pair, and platforms act to prevent it. Öborn and colleagues found all three parties in a Swedish gig triangle stepping outside the app to coordinate directly, with the platform working to keep the bilateral side deals from forming so it could hold its place in the middle (Öborn, MacKenzie, Örnebring, & Van Couvering, 2025). Maffie documented ride-hail drivers building off-app private operations that disintermediate the platform, using the app only to source clients they then coordinate with directly (Maffie, 2023). The model's edge is their phenomenon. The direct channel is the thing the platform has a reason to suppress, and the parties have a reason to build. These cases document one axis, the channel that opens between the parties. They are not the harder zone where a mediator stays wired in to both sides and the form still reduces. That zone is Section 8's, and it is the one without a documented instance. The magnitudes in between do not support more. Across the intermediate encodings of a direct channel the maximum Φ runs 2.0 with no channel, 0.83 with a disjunctive channel, 6.0 with a conjunctive channel, 2.0 with a parity channel, and 0.0 with a full bypass. The middle is non-monotone. A conjunctive channel scores higher than no channel at all. So the number is not a dose. The reported Φ is the integration at the partition the measure's normalization selects, not the smallest integration any cut leaves. At the conjunctive channel the selected cut leaves 6.0, while another cut leaves 2.0. The zero is the same whichever cut is read, so the classifier turns on the zero, not the size. There is no reading on which more direct channel means steadily less integration. The binary verdict is the claim. The magnitude is not a coordination scale, and Section 10 says so.

## 10. What the instrument establishes, and what it does not

The contribution has two layers. The first is a formal decision procedure for triadicity. Paper 1 gave a four-question criterion and called it provisional. This paper makes it rigorous. The four questions become structural conditions on a specified mechanism, and the integrated information over the minimum-information partition reads off whether the conditions are met. Handed a coordination form, modeled at the application layer, an analyst can now compute whether it is a moderated dyad or a mediated triad, rather than argue it. The procedure replaces a judgment with a calculation.

The second layer is the one the dissertation rests on, and it is a warrant. The warrant is conditional. Once a coordination form is modeled faithfully, the verdict is fixed by the mechanism, not chosen by the analyst, and this converts Paper 1's asserted distinctness into a demonstrated one. Paper 1 argued that the existing competency constructs describe a dyad and that platform work is a triad, and a skeptic could call that a framing the author preferred. The instrument moves the framing to a place where it can be checked. The encodings are not free. The rival constructs are modeled from what each says its own unit is, a channel or a context that conveys rather than commits, and a mechanism faithful to that claim factors and scores zero. The platform interaction is modeled with the counterpart constitutive of the determination, and a mechanism faithful to that claim does not factor and scores positive. The faithfulness is disciplined by the four-question criterion, which is necessary but not sufficient, and by the declared reads, which the criterion does not fix. Section 5 showed both. The verdict holds across re-encodings that keep the criterion and the declared read structure, and it can move when the reads are changed, so the model has to declare the reads as well as meet the criterion. The warrant does not rest on the analyst's hidden preference. It rests on the criterion, which is public, and on a mechanism that is declared in full and fixed once declared. A different analyst who declares different reads can reach a different verdict, and the disagreement is then a visible disagreement about the model, not a hidden one about the framing. The construct-proliferation bar is the standard that a new construct is warranted only when the existing ones cannot reach the phenomenon (Suddaby, 2010; Podsakoff, MacKenzie, & Podsakoff, 2016; Larsen & Bong, 2016). That bar is cleared by a procedure rather than a claim. The existing constructs, faithfully modeled, are Φ-equal-zero forms. The phenomenon, faithfully modeled, is a Φ-greater-than-zero form. The gap is real, and a triadic construct is required to cover it. The structural fact the paper establishes is that the dyadic and the triadic carve a real joint, determinable by a procedure, for any form modeled to the criterion.

The limits are as important as the contribution. The verdict is binary, not a difficulty scale. The classifier separates zero from positive reliably, and the magnitude above zero is read only ordinally, if at all. The eliminate-the-dyad sweep showed the magnitude is non-monotone in an obvious structural variable, so it is not a measure of how hard coordination is. The node update functions, not the state-individuation rule, drive the magnitude, and reading them as a scale would over-claim. The instrument is state-alphabet dependent, and that dependence is the method's one real empirical commitment, handled by declaring the individuation rule in advance, checking the verdict across faithful re-encodings of a case (Section 5), and handing the full sensitivity analysis over real logs to Paper 3. The instrument is not validated against the world. No claim here connects a Φ value to an observed coordination outcome, a worker's earnings, a task's success, or a dyad's stability. That connection, Φ as a coordination-difficulty scale anchored to outcomes, is what this paper declines to assert and Paper 3 takes up. One part of the not-yet-validated is sharper than the rest. The zone where Φ uniquely beats the cheap tests, the strongly-connected-yet-reducible structure of the Section 8 exhibit, is the one zone with no documented platform instance. Every case with a tight real-world anchor, the false dyad and the route-around result, is a case a factorization or connectivity test would also classify. So the claim that Φ is required, rather than a cheaper test, rests for its unique part on a zone that is so far occupied by a constructed exhibit and a conceptual kin. Confirming a platform takes that exact shape is a measurement task, and it belongs to Paper 3. The tractability of the exact computation is a discipline on how finely the states may be carved, not a defect, because at the three-party scale the model targets, the computation is cheap. And the import is an analogy, not a homology. Coordination irreducibility and informational irreducibility share a form, not a cause, and nothing in the paper says a platform is a mind.

The structural reality the paper claims is therefore precise and bounded. It is the claim that, for a coordination form modeled to the criterion, the moderated-dyad and mediated-triad distinction is a structural fact about the mechanism, that the line between them is computable, and that the existing constructs sit on one side of it while platform work sits on the other. It is conditional on a faithful model, and the condition is doing real work. It is not the claim that the verdict is independent of all modeling, which the carving check refutes, and it is not the claim that Φ predicts how coordination goes. The first claim warrants the construct. The second would calibrate it, and the second is Paper 3's.

## 11. Conclusion

Paper 1 named a competency and left a question. Which coordination forms demand it, and how would anyone tell? This paper answers the second half with a procedure. A coordination form is modeled at the application layer as a three-node system over worker, system, and counterpart. The exact integrated information over the minimum-information partition reads whether that system's cause-effect power factors. Zero means a moderated dyad, navigable with the literacies the field already has. Positive means a mediated triad, the form that demands algorithmacy. The procedure is borrowed, under the standards organization theory holds borrowed models to, and defended against the cheaper tests by an exhibit they cannot handle. It is checked on controls, checked across faithful re-encodings of the centerpiece case, applied to worked cases where it cuts against appearance in both directions, and held to stated limits. It is a classifier and a warrant. It does not yet measure difficulty. The instrument established here is the object Paper 3 calibrates, where the binary verdict meets coordination outcomes and the analyst asks not only whether a form is a triad but how much the triad costs the people inside it.
