## 1. Introduction: the form question

Paper 1 drew a line through the constructs that describe how people work with algorithms. On one side sit the constructs we already have — algorithmic competence, platform literacy, the technology-acceptance family — and every one of them measures a *dyad*: a worker and the system in front of her, a person and the model she queries, a user and the interface she has learned to read. On the other side sits the form Paper 1 named algorithmacy, which measures a *triad*: a worker coordinating with a counterpart she may never meet, *through* a determination the system commits between them. The argument of Paper 1 was that the second form is real, that the dyadic constructs cannot see it, and that it asks something of a worker the dyadic constructs never name.

That argument carries a debt. It only does work if an analyst, handed a particular case, can say which form she is looking at. And here the argument runs out of road, because the dyad and the triad do not announce themselves. Counting the parties will not settle it: a setting with three people in it is still a dyad if the third is a passive conduit, or if the system between them ignores one side, because then the structure collapses back onto the pair and the third party was never load-bearing. Reading the interface will not settle it either: a worker who experiences nothing but an app — who taps a screen and is dispatched, and never sees the person on the other end — may sit inside an irreducible triad, the dyadic picture an artifact of what she happens to see. The distinction between the two forms is not given by the surface of the interaction. It has to be *classified*, and the classification can run against appearances in both directions.

So Paper 1's contribution generates a question it cannot answer from inside its own argument: given an object of study, is the coordination dyadic or triadic? This paper answers it. We map the object and its relationships into a transition matrix and run integrated information theory over it. The measure returns a number, and the number decides: **Φ = 0 means the structure factors along the lines between the parties (a dyad, whatever it looked like); Φ > 0 means it does not (a triad, whatever it looked like).** What looked like three parties and scores zero was a dyad with an extra body in the room. What looked like a worker alone with an app and scores positive was a triad the interface hid.

We want to be exact about what kind of contribution this is. It is not a new measure of "triadicity," invented for the occasion and asking to be trusted. It is the import of a measure built and tested in another field, brought to bear on a question organizational research has not had a tool for — what methodologists call a method-introduction contribution, the kind whose value is the method itself rather than any single finding it produces (Bergh et al., 2022). The decision procedure is the deliverable. Without it, "this case is triadic" is an assertion an analyst makes and a reviewer takes on faith; with it, the verdict is computed, reproducible, and wrong in ways that can be caught.

We build the instrument and we validate it, and we run it on cases whose answers the construct already fixes, to show it agrees. We do not yet calibrate it — a Φ value is not a difficulty scale until it is anchored to an observed coordination outcome, and that anchoring is Paper 3. The claim of this paper is narrower and, we think, prior: that whether a coordination form is triadic is not a matter of interpretation but of computation, and that the instrument that computes it can be built from a tool already sitting in a neighboring field.

## 2. The borrowing

The instrument is integrated information theory, and importing it asks for a defense on two fronts. The first is that reaching into another field for a formal tool is a disciplined move with standing in organizational research, not an ornament. The second is that *this* tool, in particular, is not a metaphor stretched over the problem but the same formal object the problem is made of. We take them in turn.

### 2.1 Borrowing is what the field does

Organizational and strategy research has imported formal apparatus from the natural sciences before, and the most heavily traveled case is the one closest to ours. Kauffman built the NK fitness landscape to model rugged adaptation in evolutionary biology; Levinthal (1997) carried it into strategy, Rivkin (2000) used it to explain why a complex strategy resists imitation even when rivals can see it whole, and a generation of simulation studies followed (Baumann et al., 2019). The landscape did not arrive housebroken. What made the transfer disciplined rather than decorative was a rule the methodologists who studied it stated plainly: in moving a formal model from one domain to another, the syntax can travel unchanged while the semantics must be reworked for the new domain (Marks et al., 2019; Robertson & Caldart, 2008). Keep the mathematics; rebuild what it refers to.

That is precisely our move. We take integrated information theory's mathematics of irreducibility, re-read what its terms denote in the setting of coordination, and leave the rest of the theory at the door. Stated in the methodologists' own vocabulary, this is a method at the early phase of its life cycle in our field — mature where it came from, new here (Bergh et al., 2022). The contribution is to bring it across and show it earns its place.

### 2.2 The tool is the object, not an analogy for it

The fit is not rhetorical. It holds because the tool and the phenomenon are the same formal thing seen twice.

Consider what integrated information theory measures. Its quantity, Φ, is the degree to which a system's cause-effect structure is irreducible to its parts. The theory's integration postulate puts it in terms that read, for our purposes, like a definition handed over from the other side: a system's cause-effect power "must specify its cause-effect state as a whole set of units, irreducible to separate subsets of units" (Albantakis et al., 2023). When the structure can be split along the lines between its parts with nothing lost, Φ is zero; a strictly modular system carries no integrated information at all (Balduzzi & Tononi, 2008; Oizumi et al., 2014). Φ is exactly zero when the whole is its parts and positive when it is not.

Now consider what makes a coordination form non-dyadic. A form is triadic, in Paper 1's sense, precisely when it does not reduce to the pairwise dealings of the parties — when they must coordinate *through* a determination that binds them rather than around it. The triad is the case that does not factor into its pairs.

These are one object in two languages. The property that makes a coordination form irreducibly triadic is the property Φ was built to quantify, and the value Φ already returns is the value the construct needs. We do not have to invent a measure of triadicity and argue for its validity from scratch; the imported tool delivers it. And the irreducibility it delivers is not an artifact of one formula — across more than a dozen independently developed measures of causation, the same irreducibility recurs, because they share a few underlying primitives (Comolatti & Hoel, 2022). We are not betting the construct on a quirk of one equation.

### 2.3 What we do not import, and one objection answered

Integrated information theory was built as a theory of consciousness, and as a theory of consciousness it is contested; we import none of that, only the irreducibility mathematics, which stands on its own as a formal account of when a system exceeds its parts (Negro, 2024; the consciousness identity we set aside is Tononi & Koch, 2015).

That one-sentence concession invites a sharper objection, and we would rather meet it than let it sit. The methodological literature on importing research methods holds that one should develop theory and method together, not bolt a ready-made instrument onto a question it was never shaped for (Lê & Schmid, 2022). On its face our move looks like the opposite — we are taking a tool off the shelf and calling it theory-neutral. The appearance is misleading. The theory this method is theory-neutral *toward* is its origin theory, consciousness, which we have set down. The theory it is being co-developed *with* is the organizational one — Paper 1's account of triadic coordination, which is what tells the instrument where to run, how to carve its states, and what a positive verdict means. With respect to the theory it is here to serve, method and theory are developed together exactly as the principle asks. The tool is borrowed; the construct it measures is ours, and the two were built to fit.

One thing follows that we will hold to throughout. The warrant for using Φ on coordination is not that it has been validated in neuroscience. Borrowed credibility is no credibility (Øby, 2025). The warrant is that the instrument behaves correctly on coordination cases whose structure we know in advance — which is a claim we have to earn in §5, not assume here.

## 3. The application layer

Before the instrument can run, we have to say what it runs *over*, and the answer resolves a problem that otherwise sinks any attempt to measure algorithmic coordination: the algorithm is a black box.

The measure does not run over the algorithm's internal mechanism. It runs over the **application layer** — the states the two parties move through and the determinations the mediator commits between them. A driver logs in, holds a position, accepts or declines; the platform returns a dispatch or a price. A résumé is submitted; the system commits a forward or a reject; a manager calls back or does not. All of this is visible in ordinary interaction logs, and none of it requires seeing inside the model that produced it.

This is what dissolves the opacity problem rather than fighting it. The mechanism is opaque, and the analyst neither can see it nor needs to. Φ computed over the application-layer transition matrix is a property of the dynamics the parties actually operate in — the moves they make and the determinations that constrain them — not of the hidden process that generates those determinations. The distinction matters enough to draw it precisely, because three things that are easy to run together are genuinely different. *Epistemic opacity* is the analyst's inability to see the mechanism. *Formal irreducibility* is the structure's failure to factor. *Metaphysical emergence* is a claim about the world that we are not making. The emergence literature has learned to keep these apart, and to flag where a macro-level description buys real causal traction and where it only looks like it does (Bedau, 1997; Hoel, 2017; the cautionary case is Eberhardt & Lee, 2022).

So we state the claim and stop there: the cause-effect structure of the observed application-layer transition matrix is irreducible over its minimum-information partition. That is a computable fact about the dynamics the parties move through. It is not a claim that the analyst has seen into the algorithm, and it is not a claim that the coordination is metaphysically more than its parts in some inflationary sense. It is the narrow, checkable thing, and the narrowness is what makes it defensible.

The matrix that carries this claim is built from the logs alone. A platform engineers the very feature that makes the construction clean: it keeps the worker and the counterpart from reaching each other directly, routing everything through its own determinations. Rideshare drivers cannot contact riders off the app; the platform sits between them by design, and disintermediation is the point of the design, not an accident of it (Rosenblat & Stark, 2016). One participatory-design study with drivers caught the resulting phenomenology in its title — "you are you and the app, there's nobody else" (Stein et al., 2023). The worker experiences a dyad. Whether that is what she is in is the question the next sections answer.

## 4. The state alphabet

Everything the instrument reports rests on one prior decision, and we make it in the open. Φ is not invariant to how the application-layer states are individuated — carve the states one way and a form looks irreducible, carve them another and it factors. If the carving is done after the computation, to taste, the dyad/triad verdict is an artifact of the analyst's hand and worth nothing. So we fix the carving rule by definition, before any Φ is computed, and we defend it.

The rule is this: **a new application-layer state begins when the mediator commits a determination that alters its causal disposition toward the parties.** A determination is a commitment the mediator makes — a forward or a reject, a match or no match, a price, a rank, a score — that changes how it will afterward constrain what passes between the two parties. States are individuated by these commitments and by nothing else.

In the imported vocabulary the rule has an exact reading, and the reading is why it is the right rule. A determination is a transition that moves the mediator's cause repertoire and its effect repertoire together: it changes both what could have brought the system to this point and what the system now constrains downstream. That joint movement of cause and effect is what makes a transition irreducible rather than cosmetic, and it is exactly the quantity Φ is built to register. A change that moves neither repertoire — a re-rendering of a value the mediator has already committed — is not a new state, however much the interface dresses it up as one.

Two rival criteria feel like state boundaries, and the rule turns both down. The first is *action availability*: the intuition that a new state begins whenever a party *could* act differently. But an option that commits nothing — a button she may or may not press — leaves the mediator's disposition toward the parties exactly where it was, and availability is a fact about the interface, not about the causal structure. The second is *visibility*: the intuition that a new state begins when a value becomes visible to a party. But disclosing a value the mediator has already committed changes who can read it, not what it constrains; the repertoires do not move, so the state does not begin. Both criteria mistake the surface of the interaction for its structure. The rule individuates on the structure, which is why two analysts applying it to the same logs carve the same states and reach the same Φ. That reproducibility is the whole point of fixing the rule in advance.

The rule also keeps the problem computable, and this is not a separate constraint bolted on but the same discipline seen from the other side. Φ grows super-exponentially in the number of states, so the state alphabet must stay small. The individuation rule keeps it small in a principled way: only determination-bearing transitions count, so the alphabet is governed by how often the mediator actually commits, not by every flicker on the screen. The rule that makes the verdict well-defined is the rule that makes it tractable.

One case strains the rule, and we name it now rather than let it ambush the calibration work. A platform that commits in a near-continuous stream — Uber re-pricing every surge tick, re-evaluating every match, re-weighting every repositioning — has no natural discrete boundary at which one application-layer state ends and the next begins. The rule says "a new state per committed determination," but when determinations arrive without pause the analyst must choose a window, and Φ depends on the choice. We do not resolve this. We pre-register it as the rule's open boundary and hand it to Paper 3, where an observed coordination outcome can supply the window the continuous case lacks, fixing the granularity by the scale at which Φ best tracks the outcome it is meant to predict rather than by fiat. Until that anchor is in hand, the instrument is honest about where the rule is sharp (discrete, log-reconstructible determinations) and where it is not yet.

## 5. The instrument and its validation

We now build the instrument and, before running it on anything in dispute, make it prove itself on cases whose answers we already know.

A coordination form is modeled as a three-node application-layer system: the Worker (W), the System or mediator (S), and the Counterpart (C). Its state-by-node transition matrix records how each party's next determination-bearing value follows from the current state of all three. Over that matrix the instrument computes exact integrated information — specifically the IIT-4.0 system integrated information, the irreducibility of the system's cause-effect state over its minimum-information partition — by way of the repository's exact-Φ oracle, which wraps the standard PyPhi implementation (Marshall et al., 2023; Mayner et al., 2018). We name the quantity precisely because the theory offers more than one: this is the system-level φ over the minimum-information partition, not the structure-level sum over distinctions and relations, and the paper's claims attach to the former.

A word on what "validation" means here, because the methods genre has a default this paper deliberately departs from. The canonical sequence for building a measure is define, operationalize, confirm (Lambert & Newman, 2023), and "confirm" ordinarily means demonstrating reliability and validity across a sample of respondents. Our instrument is formal, not psychometric, and there is no sampling step to run. "Confirm," for a formal instrument, means something we can hold it to just as strictly: the instrument must return the answers we already know on cases whose structure is fixed in advance. We adapt the genre's third step rather than skip it, and we say so plainly so that a reader does not read the adaptation as an omission.

Two controls fix the ends of the scale.

The first is a **factoring** form, in which the counterpart is causally decoupled — the worker and mediator are coupled to each other, but nothing reads or writes the counterpart (W′ = S, S′ = W, C′ = C). The system splits cleanly into {W, S} and {C}, and there is nothing for an integration measure to find. The instrument returns Φ = 0.000 at every reachable state. This is the result the theory demands: a strictly modular system carries no integrated information (Balduzzi & Tononi, 2008).

The second is an **irreducible** form, in which every party reads the other two (W′ = S ∨ C, S′ = W ∧ C, C′ = W ⊕ S). No partition splits this without loss. The instrument returns Φ as high as 0.830, and the minimum-information partition is the full tripartition {W, S, C} — the system cannot be reduced along any line, including the line between the parties.

Zero where the structure factors, positive where it cannot be factored at all. Only once the instrument clears these two controls do we let it classify anything contested. And because the controls and everything after them are computed, not argued, the published instrument code regenerates every number in this paper from the rules that define each case.

## 6. The classifier at work

With the instrument validated, we run it on coordination forms whose verdicts the construct fixes in advance. These are not illustrations chosen to flatter the measure. Each is a case where Paper 1 already tells us the answer, and the question is whether the instrument agrees — including the two cases where agreement means overruling what the situation looks like.

**The dyadic limit.** A worker in conversation with a language model is the clean two-party case: she prompts, the model answers, she responds to the answer, and the model commits nothing that couples a third party (W′ = S, S′ = W, C′ = C). The instrument returns Φ = 0.000 at every state. The form factors to the two parties already visible in it. It is a dyad, and the measure says so.

**The irreducible triad.** A résumé passes through an applicant-tracking system to a hiring manager. The applicant and the manager never deal with each other directly; each reaches the other only through the determination the system commits — the forward or the reject (W′ = S, C′ = S), which the system sets as a joint function of the résumé's signal and the manager's configured profile (S′ = W ∧ C). Across every determination that genuinely reads both sides — and, or, nand, nor — the instrument returns Φ = 2.000, with the minimum-information partition falling between the worker and the system-counterpart pair, {W, SC}. The structure does not factor, even though the two human parties never touch. Already this is the more interesting direction of misclassification in miniature: the configuration that most invites a two-separate-dyads model, two parties with no link between them, is the one the measure flags as a single irreducible triad.

**The false triad.** Now the first overruling. Take the same three-party setup, but let the mediator's determination ignore one side — the system forwards on the résumé's signal alone, indifferent to the manager's profile (S′ = W). Three parties are present, on the interface and in the room. The instrument returns Φ = 0.000. The determination reads only one party, so the structure collapses onto the pair, and the third is a body without a causal role. Three parties on the surface, two in the structure; the measure overrules the count.

**The false dyad.** And the second, which is the one the instrument exists for. A rideshare driver works a system that presents to her as nothing but an app — she taps, she is dispatched, and she can neither see nor choose the rider. Modeled honestly, though, the rider is constitutive of the dispatch the platform commits: the platform dispatches only when an available driver and a waiting rider both exist (S′ = W ∧ C), the driver's availability is consumed by the dispatch (W′ = ¬S), and the rider waits until a dispatch serves her (C′ = C ∧ ¬S). The driver and rider never interact directly; they are bound only through the platform. The instrument returns Φ = 2.000, with the partition again at {W, SC}. The structure the driver cannot see is an irreducible triad.

The sharpness of the result is worth holding still for. Strip the rider out of the determination — model the dispatch as a function of the driver alone, S′ = W, which is exactly what a dyadic construct does when it studies "the driver's relationship with the app" — and with every other rule unchanged the instrument returns Φ = 0.000. The verdict turns on a single dependency: whether the platform's determination reads the third party the worker cannot see. Include her and the form is an irreducible triad; drop her and it is a dyad. The misclassification a dyadic construct makes is not a misreading of the data; it is the modeling choice of leaving the constitutive third party out, and the instrument is what catches it.

This is not a toy. The triangular structure of platform work — platform, worker, and a third party who is the customer — is by now well established: scholars have theorized it as a triangle directly (Stark & Pais, 2020; Azaïs & Dieuaide, 2020), shown that the third party mediates the worker-platform relationship rather than decorating it (Healy & Pekarek, 2025), and demonstrated in the formal economics that a three-sided platform does not reduce to the two-sided model its strategists reach for (Sun et al., 2024; the foundational two-sided result is Rochet & Tirole, 2003, with the managed-intermediary boundary in Hagiu & Wright, 2015). What this literature has described, the instrument computes: the third party is not context, it is structure.

**The incentive, computed.** One result remains, and it is the one that turns the classifier into a source of theory rather than a referee of cases. Hold the determination fixed (S′ = W ∧ C) and vary one thing — how much the worker and counterpart can deal with each other directly. With no direct channel, the parties reaching each other only through the system, Φ is 2.00. Add a weak direct channel and it falls to 0.83. Make the direct channel strong enough that the parties can coordinate without the system and it falls to 0.00 — the triad reduces to a dyad. Coordination is most irreducible when the dyad between the parties is eliminated.

We did not assert this; we computed it, and the computation does what a formal instrument is supposed to do in this field — it generates a proposition rather than merely testing one (Davis et al., 2007). The proposition is about political economy: a platform's position is constituted by the irreducibility of the coordination it sits inside, direct contact between the parties destroys that irreducibility, and so the platform has a structural incentive to design the dyad out — to keep the worker and the counterpart from ever coordinating except through its own committed determinations. This is what platforms in fact do, and the measure says why doing it pays. It is also exactly the kind of claim that wants calibrating against an outcome, which is where Paper 3 takes it.

## 7. What the instrument establishes, and what it does not

The instrument decides one question and leaves another open by design, and the contribution is sharpest when the two are kept apart.

What it establishes is a decision procedure. Φ over the observable application-layer transition matrix is a formal test of whether a coordination form is triadic — whether its cause-effect structure is irreducible to the parties — and therefore of whether the form demands the competency Paper 1 called algorithmacy rather than the literacy a dyad asks for. This is the instrument Paper 1's construct required and could not supply from within its own argument. A reducible form is navigable one hop at a time; an irreducible form forces a worker to reason forward and backward through a determination she does not control and cannot route around, and the measure is what tells the two apart before a single worker is studied.

We should be candid about where this sits in a longer arc, because it is easy to overstate the novelty. The toolkit of irreducibility has already reached social coordination. Engel and Malone (2018) used Φ itself to measure how far a human group's behavior exceeds its individual members; Mediano and colleagues (2025) developed the decomposition of integration into its synergistic and redundant parts; others have turned information-theoretic synergy on multi-agent collectives. No one, though, has pointed an irreducibility measure at the mediator-bound platform triad: the structure in which a committed determination binds a worker and a third party who never directly interact, and where the open question is whether that third party is load-bearing or incidental. Our contribution is the application and the constitutive-mediator framing, not the invention of the measure, and saying so is what keeps the claim defensible.

Three boundaries bound it, and we state them up front because they are what make the rest credible. First, the tool applies to discrete systems specified by a transition matrix (the class PyPhi computes), and our application-layer matrices are exactly that, so we are within scope; but we claim nothing about systems outside it. Second, a single scalar Φ aggregates several distinct modes of integration that a finer decomposition would separate (Mediano et al., 2025); the binary verdict we lean on, Φ = 0 against Φ > 0, is robust to this, but one number simplifies a heterogeneous quantity and we do not pretend otherwise. Third, the irreducibility is a formal property of the observable dynamics — distinct from the analyst's inability to see the mechanism, and carrying no metaphysical freight about emergence.

Two things are deliberately unfinished. The verdict depends on the state alphabet, which is why §4 pre-registers the individuation rule rather than hiding the dependence; and a Φ value is not yet a difficulty scale. A number that separates triads from dyads does not yet say how hard a given triad is to navigate, and it cannot until it is anchored to an observed coordination outcome. That anchoring — together with the continuous-stream granularity that §4 left open — is the work of Paper 3.

This paper sits deliberately between the other two, and a three-paper dissertation earns its coherence or fails for the lack of it. Paper 1 develops the construct and argues that some coordination is triadic. Paper 3 will calibrate the measure against an outcome. This paper is the bridge: it operationalizes the distinction Paper 1 asserts and hands Paper 3 the instrument it will calibrate. The object that runs through all three is one and the same — the thing Paper 1 names, this paper measures, and Paper 3 will anchor: the irreducibility of coordination to its parts. Φ over the application layer decides whether a coordination form has it. How hard such a form is to navigate is, by design, where Paper 3 begins.
