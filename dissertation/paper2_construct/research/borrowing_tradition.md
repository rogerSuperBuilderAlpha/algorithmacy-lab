# Research note — org theory's tradition of borrowing formal models, and the argumentative template for importing IIT/Φ

**Purpose.** Strengthen Paper 2 §2 ("The borrowing") against the committee's "intimidation by formalism" charge by showing IIT/Φ is the next legitimate entry in organization theory's long tradition of principled cross-disciplinary model-borrowing — and by copying the *proven argumentative moves* those imports used. Built from a Stage-1 landscape map (four source disciplines) + a Stage-2 deep-research dive into how the exemplars argued their imports, with the load-bearing primaries verified verbatim. Confidence is marked per item; quotes marked **(verbatim, p. X)** were confirmed against a primary source.

---

## The seven-move template (each move + the exemplar that supplies it + verified quote)

### Move 1 — Name the tradition of principled borrowing; position the new import as its next legitimate entry
Org theory has repeatedly imported formal models from other disciplines and made them respectable: **biology** → organizational ecology (Hannan & Freeman 1977), evolutionary economics (Nelson & Winter 1982); **sociology** → neo-institutionalism (Meyer & Rowan 1977; DiMaggio & Powell 1983), embeddedness/structural holes (Granovetter 1985; Burt 1992); **economics** → transaction-cost economics (Williamson 1975), agency theory (Jensen & Meckling 1976; Eisenhardt 1989); **physics/complexity** → NK landscapes (Levinthal 1997), complex adaptive systems (Anderson 1999). IIT/Φ is one more import in this lineage, not an exotic outlier.

### Move 2 — Position the import *between* two flawed extremes that share a hidden defect
**Granovetter (1985), AJS 91(3) — high confidence.** Legitimizes importing social structure by placing embeddedness between the "undersocialized" atomized-actor account (neoclassical economics) and the "oversocialized" norm-following account (Parsonian sociology), then showing both share one defect: **(verbatim)** "Under- and oversocialized resolutions of the problem of order thus merge in their atomization of actors from immediate social context." He then proves the lens does *real work* (not decoration) by deploying it "as a critique of Oliver Williamson's markets and hierarchies research program."
→ *Paper 2 application:* position Φ-for-coordination between a pure-dyadic-contract view and an everything-is-culture view, and use Φ to out-explain a rival formalism (dyadic agency / transaction-cost models).

### Move 3 — Establish a structural homology to *canonical org-theory constructs* (not just to the source theory)
**Engel & Malone (2018), PLOS ONE 13(10):e0205335 — high confidence.** They ground Φ's cross-disciplinary bridge in familiar constructs: **(verbatim)** Adam Smith's observation that systems are productive when "(a) division of labor leads different people to specialize … and (b) the invisible hand of the market integrates their diverse efforts," and "Lawrence and Lorsch discussed the importance of differentiation and integration in large, hierarchical human organizations." The legitimating logic: differentiation+integration is essential to consciousness but *also* recurs across economics, organization design, and engineering — which licenses applying Φ outside neuroscience.
→ *Paper 2 application:* anchor Φ's irreducibility to the **Lawrence & Lorsch differentiation/integration** pairing so the import lands on familiar ground. (Caveat: L&L is one of several parallel examples in their paper, not a privileged anchor; the actual move is "this pairing recurs across many disciplines.")

### Move 4 — Re-semanticize for the new domain; explicitly bound what does NOT transfer
**Nelson & Winter (1982), *An Evolutionary Theory of Economic Change* — high confidence, all verbatim from primary source.**
- Routines-as-genes homology: **(verbatim, p. 14)** "In our evolutionary theory, these routines play the role that genes play in biological evolutionary theory. They are a persistent feature of the organism … they are heritable … and they are selectable."
- The disciplined-importation posture: **(verbatim, p. 11)** "We emphatically disavow any intention to pursue biological analogies for their own sake … We are pleased to exploit any idea from biology that seems helpful … but we are equally prepared to pass over anything that seems awkward, or to modify accepted biological theories radically in the interest of getting better economic theory (witness our espousal of Lamarckianism)."
- Bounding what doesn't transfer: **(verbatim, p. 11)** "Relatedly, our theory is unabashedly Lamarckian: it contemplates both the 'inheritance' of acquired characteristics and the timely appearance of variation under the stimulus of adversity." (i.e., they keep the selection logic but openly reject the strictly-Darwinian, blind-mutation reading.)
- The entitlement framing: **(verbatim, p. 9)** "we have borrowed basic ideas from biology, thus exercising an option to which economists are entitled in perpetuity by virtue of the stimulus our predecessor Malthus provided to Darwin's thinking."
- **Do NOT attribute to N&W** a clean "genotype/phenotype is blurred / no sexual reproduction" disclaimer — that is **Hodgson's (2003) critique** ("Sometimes [routines] are treated as genotypes, sometimes … as behaviours or phenotypes"), not their own admission.

The transfer-methodology rule that codifies this move: **Marks, Gerrits & Marx (2019), *Biology & Philosophy* 34(1), Art. 7 — high confidence.** **(verbatim)** "the syntactic structure of the model can remain unaltered, whilst the semantic dimension requires considerable modification in order to fit the specific phenomena in the social sciences," and "a new justification is needed why the model represents the target domain adequately."
→ *Paper 2 application:* keep Φ's mathematics (syntax), rebuild its referents for coordination (a node = worker/system/counterpart; a state = a determination-bearing configuration), and **enumerate what is dropped**: the consciousness/phenomenology identity, the intrinsic-existence metaphysics, the maximally-irreducible-complex apparatus.

### Move 5 — Position as a *complement* that captures what rivals miss, not a replacement
**Eisenhardt (1989), AMR 14(1):57–74 — high confidence.** She adopts agency theory as a partial-but-valid complement: it is "empirically valid … particularly when coupled with complementary perspectives," and openly concedes it "ignores a good bit of the complexity of organizations," while legitimizing it by what it uniquely illuminates — "information systems, outcome uncertainty, incentives, and risk."
→ *Paper 2 application:* present Φ as a complement that uniquely captures the **irreducibility/coordination-structure** dimension other lenses leave implicit, while conceding it does not capture meaning, power, or culture.

### Move 6 — Make the import accountable: computable + outcome-predictive (not decorative)
**Anderson (1999), Org. Science 10(3):216–232 — high confidence.** Frames CAS as **(verbatim)** "a genuinely new way of simplifying the complex," reduces it to four named, specifiable elements ("agents with schemata, self-organizing networks sustained by importing energy, coevolution to the edge of chaos, and system evolution based on recombination"), and ties it to a program "merging empirical observation with computational agent-based simulation."
**Engel & Malone (2018) — high confidence.** Compute Φ on three real datasets (68 four-person groups; Wikipedia editor groups; internet traffic) and tie it to *independent* outcomes: Φ correlated with a measured collective-intelligence factor (r = 0.370, p = 0.003, on 61 of 68 groups), and higher-Φ Wikipedia groups produced higher-quality articles (F = 3.6847, p = 0.0053). They frame Φ as "a certain kind of interactional complexity that, at least sometimes, predicts group performance" — an empirical predictor, not an assertion of group cognition.
→ *Paper 2 application:* the committed scripts (exact-Φ instrument, the 4,096-form catalog, the Φ-vs-separability comparator) and the validation protocol **are** this move; lean on them as the answer to "does the formalism do real work."

### Move 7 — Pre-empt the metaphor/physics-envy charge; decouple from the source theory's contested commitments
**McKelvey (1997), Org. Science 8(4):351–380 — high confidence.** Licenses natural-science imports via a **dual-causation** premise: **(verbatim)** "organizational phenomena result from both individual human intentionality and natural causes independent of individuals' intended behavior" — so formal measurement does not deny agency. And he turns the physics-envy charge around: **(verbatim)** "mathematically, computationally, and experimentally intense twentieth century natural sciences all have microstate idiosyncrasy assumptions similar to those postmodernists suggest are true of organizational phenomena" — i.e., the borrowed apparatus already tolerates idiosyncrasy, so the import is appropriate, not a category error.
**Engel & Malone (2018) — the consciousness-decoupling move (closest precedent, high confidence).** Concede-then-reframe: **(verbatim)** "there is still considerable debate among researchers about whether phi actually measures consciousness," then "Phi was originally developed by neuroscientists as a measure of consciousness in brains, but it captures, in a single mathematical quantity, two properties that are important in many other kinds of groups as well: differentiated information and integration," with the clincher: **(verbatim)** "Whether phi is measuring consciousness or not, it is clearly measuring something that is of potential interest to many different disciplines."
→ *Paper 2 application:* this is exactly §2.4's move ("we import the irreducibility mathematics, not the consciousness identity"). The research shows it is the **load-bearing** move and should be foregrounded, not buried. **Adopt only the bracketing half of Engel & Malone — NOT their speculative re-engagement** (they later add a "Measuring the consciousness of groups" section that speculates groups may have "a kind of consciousness"; the dissertation must not follow them there).

## The standard the borrowing must clear (and the failure modes the critiques exploit)

**The analogical-transfer bar — Ketokivi & Mahoney (2016), *Acad. Mgmt. Learning & Education* 15(1):123–138 — high confidence, verbatim.** (Note: this paper is about *analogical reasoning quality*, applied to TCE; cite it for the standard, not as a generic transfer protocol.)
- **(verbatim, p. 125)** "The Achilles heel of analogical reasoning is that if it fails in any single relevant aspect, the entire analogy collapses. In this sense, analogies do not have the property of being 'sort of right'—they either work or they do not."
- **(verbatim, p. 125)** "One dissimilarity can trump a dozen similarities."
This is *why* Move 4 (bound the analogy) is non-negotiable: the homology must hold on the *relevant* axis (irreducibility-under-partition ↔ triadic-vs-dyadic), and the dissertation must show the dis-analogies (consciousness, phenomenology) are on *irrelevant* axes. The standards reference for evaluating such transfers is **Ketokivi, Mantere & Cornelissen (2017), AMR 42(4):637–658, "Reasoning by Analogy and the Progress of Theory."**

**Failure modes to pre-empt (from the critiques):**
1. **Metaphor mistaken for mechanism** → answered by Move 6 (computable + outcome-predictive).
2. **Obscuring human agency** — **Penrose (1952), AER 42(5):804–819 (verbatim, p. 808):** "The characteristic of biological analogies in economics is to suggest explanations of events that do not depend upon the conscious willed decisions of human beings." → answered by McKelvey's dual-causation (Move 7) and by re-semanticizing nodes as human/system actors. (The "Social Darwinism" framing is **Levallois 2011**, not Penrose's own words.)
3. **Source-originators reject the extension** (cf. Maturana & Varela repudiating Luhmann's social autopoiesis) → answered by decoupling from the contested consciousness commitment (Move 7), claiming only the irreducibility mathematics.
4. **Self-fulfilling / prescriptively harmful assumptions** — **Ghoshal & Moran (1996), AMR 21(1):13–47 (verbatim abstract, p. 13):** TCE prescriptions are "not only wrong but also dangerous … because of the assumptions and logic on which it is grounded" (the opportunism assumption can create the opportunism it assumes; argument at pp. 21–25). → answered by treating Φ **descriptively/predictively, never prescriptively**.
5. **Un-testability** → answered by pre-registering Φ against independent coordination outcomes (the validation protocol).

**The methodological-contribution bar to position against:** **Bergh, Boyd, Byron, Gove & Ketchen (2022), *J. Management* 48(7):1835–1848 — high confidence on structure.** A methodological contribution is typed on two dimensions — *extent of change* (minor↔major) × *potential audience* (limited↔large) — across a method's life-cycle phases. Paper 2 should claim its contribution explicitly on this map (a new-to-the-field method, early life-cycle phase, broad audience).

## The closest precedent is also the sharpest open problem (flag for §2.4)

Engel & Malone (2018) is both the model to copy and the rival to distinguish. Two seams:

1. **Φ's formal content already *is* the dissertation's distinction (high confidence, verbatim).** Engel & Malone: "if the different parts were completely independent of each other, then the information would not be integrated at all, and the value of phi would be 0," and Φ is high when there is entropy "that cannot be explained by looking at the subsystems separately but … by looking at the system as a whole" — "more than just the sum of its parts." This is *exactly* factorable (dyadic) = low Φ, irreducible (triadic) = positive Φ. The homology is faithful, not decorative — the strongest single rebuttal to "intimidation by formalism."

2. **The IIT-4.0-vs-practical-Φ problem (open, important).** Engel & Malone used a *practical, auto-regressive* Φ (ΦAR) that **diverges from the theoretical definition** — and paid for it with the instability the committee flagged (they dropped low-variance nodes; ~19.5% of Study 2 cases, ~67% initially in Study 3). The dissertation uses *exact IIT-4.0 Φ*. This cuts both ways and §2.4 should meet it head-on:
   - **Pro:** exact Φ has no estimator divergence — more rigorous than the precedent, and the binary verdict is reproducible (no node-dropping).
   - **Con:** exact IIT-4.0 carries the full axiomatic edifice (intrinsic existence, the maximally-irreducible complex), which is *more* exposed to "you imported the contested metaphysics." The decoupling move (Move 7) must therefore be stronger for IIT-4.0 than Engel & Malone needed it to be: claim only the system-level Φ over the minimum-information partition (a partition-based irreducibility computation), explicitly disclaim the structure-level/complex apparatus and the intrinsic-existence reading.
