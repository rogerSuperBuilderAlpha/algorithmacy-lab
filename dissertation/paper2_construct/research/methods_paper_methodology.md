# How to write Paper 2: the methods/construct paper of a three-paper dissertation (craft reference)

Genre research to calibrate how Paper 2 is written — the analogue of Paper 1's
`integrative_review_methodology.md`. This does **not** go into the paper; it sets the standard the
paper must hit. The anchors below come from two passes that agree: the academic connectors (Consensus, citation-ranked)
and a web-wide deep-research harness (`wl1uknb25`, 104 agents, 22 sources, 25 claims adversarially
verified 3-vote, 0 killed). The harness corroborated the connector anchors (Bergh et al. 2022;
MacKenzie/Podsakoff/Podsakoff 2011; Cronbach & Meehl 1955) and added three the connectors missed —
Lê & Schmid 2022, Davis/Eisenhardt/Bingham 2007, Fioretti 2013 — plus the institutional
connective-tissue guidelines for Cluster 1. The construct-development canon (Suddaby
2010; Cronbach & Meehl 1955; Lambert & Newman 2023; MacKenzie, Podsakoff & Podsakoff 2011;
Shaffer/Lawson & Robins) is already gathered in Paper 1's `connector_finds.md` and applies here too;
this file adds what is *new* for a methods/construct paper that is the **middle** of a three-paper
dissertation.

## Cluster 1 — the three-paper / manuscript-style dissertation format and the middle paper

The format is established and increasingly standard in the social sciences, and its defining challenge
is **coherence**: three stand-alone, publishable papers that nonetheless "cohesively address an
overarching research problem."

- **[anchor] Matteson, S. M., & Lincoln, Y. S. (2021).** Insights into undertaking a three-article
  dissertation. In *Methodological Innovations in Research and Academic Writing.* Defines the format:
  three publication-ready articles that **cohesively address an overarching problem**; phases,
  advantages, challenges. The clean reference for what the format is.
- **[coherence — key] Mason, S., & Merga, M. (2018).** Integrating publications in the social science
  doctoral thesis by publication. *Higher Education Research & Development*, 37(7), 1454–1471. DOI
  10.1080/07294360.2018.1498461. The integration problem stated directly: norms are emergent, and the
  risks are **over-dependence on imitation of the traditional thesis and compounding repetition**
  (especially methods sections). Identifies 11 structural choices. *The paper to cite on how the three
  papers connect.*
- **[the warning] Morse, J. M. (2005).** Feigning independence: the article dissertation. *Qualitative
  Health Research*, 15(2), 155–156. The critical counterpoint: splitting one project into three thin
  articles can **violate holism** and scatter an integrated argument. Read as the failure mode to
  avoid — each paper must earn its independence, not merely be a slice.
- **[empirical norms] Anderson, T., Saunders, G., & Alexander, I. (2021).** Alternative dissertation
  formats in education-based doctorates. *Higher Education Research & Development.* (and Anderson et
  al. 2021, *English for Specific Purposes*, on macrostructures.) Evidence on how manuscript-style
  dissertations are actually organized (chapter counts, connective framing, co-authorship).
- **[payoff] Asanov, A.-M., et al. (2024).** Patterns of dissertation dissemination. *Scientometrics.*
  Cumulative (article-based) dissertations yield ~3× the publications and citations of monographs —
  the rationale for the format.
- *(Format-mechanics references — Freeman 2018, IJDS; Smaldone et al. 2019, J. Nursing Scholarship;
  Robinson & Dracup — on the manuscript-option mechanics, committee/authorship, copyright.)*
- **[institutional norms — harness-verified] The structural requirements, stated identically across
  doctoral guidelines** (De Jong, Moser & Hall 2005, *Nurse Author & Editor* 15(3), DOI
  10.1111/j.1750-4910.2005.tb00554.x; U. Hawaii Bauer MIS three-article guideline; Indiana U.
  multi-article guideline): three **free-standing, independently readable** papers, each a **substantive
  distinct contribution** ("not minor tweaks of a work more appropriately reported in one or two
  papers"), all held to the **same publishability standard** — *no lower bar for the middle paper* —
  bound by a Chapter 1 that establishes "conceptual and theoretical linkages" and "collective meaning"
  and a conclusion that makes the combined contribution explicit. "A collection of unrelated published
  papers is not acceptable" — **coherence is itself an evaluation criterion.** (These are
  single-institution doctoral guidelines and vary by program; cite for the *principle*, not as
  field-universal law.)

**What this means for Paper 2 (the middle paper).** Its job in the arc: Paper 1 develops the construct
(algorithmacy) and asserts that some coordination forms are triadic; Paper 3 calibrates an outcome.
Paper 2 is the **bridge** — it must (a) stand alone as a methods/construct-measurement contribution
(an instrument and its validation), and (b) connect backward (it operationalizes Paper 1's
triadic/dyadic distinction) and forward (it hands the calibration anchor to Paper 3). The coherence
device is the shared object across all three: *irreducibility of coordination to its parts.* Guard
against Morse's failure mode — a thin slice — by making Paper 2 a genuine standalone contribution (a
validated instrument + a found-tool argument), not merely "the measurement chapter." Guard against
Mason & Merga's repetition risk by keeping the methods/construct apparatus here and only *referencing*
it from Papers 1 and 3, not re-deriving it.

## Cluster 2 — the construct-development & measurement-paper genre

Already in hand (Paper 1): Suddaby 2010 (construct clarity); Cronbach & Meehl 1955 (nomological
network / construct validity); Lambert & Newman 2023 (Define–Operationalize–Confirm); MacKenzie,
Podsakoff & Podsakoff 2011 (construct measurement in MIS/behavioral research); Shaffer, DeGeest & Li
2016 and Lawson & Robins 2021 (discriminant validity / construct proliferation). The connector pass
adds the ORM-genre anchors that name the *steps a measurement paper is expected to walk*:

- **[anchor — the ORM 3-step] Lambert, L. S., & Newman, D. A. (2023).** Construct development and
  validation in three practical steps. *Organizational Research Methods*, 26(4). The canonical
  **Define → Operationalize → Confirm** sequence and a reviewer checklist. *This is the genre template
  Paper 2 must visibly honor* — define the construct (triadic/non-dyadic coordination), operationalize
  it (Φ over the application-layer TPM via the individuation rule), confirm (validate on controls,
  then demonstrate). Note for Paper 2: it walks the *logic* of the three steps, but the "confirm"
  evidence is formal-validation-on-controls (factoring → Φ=0; modular → Φ=0), not psychometric
  reliability — flag that substitution explicitly so a reviewer reads it as a deliberate genre
  adaptation, not an omission.
- **[scale-construction canon] Clark, L. A., & Watson, D. (2019).** Constructing validity: New
  developments in creating objective measuring instruments. *Psychological Assessment*, 31(12),
  1412–1427. The 2019 update of the 1995 classic: clear construct conceptualization first, then item
  generation, then construct validity. The deep statement of *why definition precedes measurement* —
  the discipline behind pre-registering the state alphabet before computing Φ.
- **[org-measurement state of the art] Zickar, M. J. (2020).** Measurement development and evaluation.
  *Annual Review of Organizational Psychology and Organizational Behavior*, 7, 213–232. Stresses that
  **theory and discovery should guide scale development** and statistical tools are chosen to serve the
  theoretical underpinnings — directly licenses Paper 2's stance that the *construct* (irreducibility)
  selects the *tool* (Φ), not the reverse.
- **[primer] Boateng, G. O., et al. (2018).** Best practices for developing and validating scales.
  *Frontiers in Public Health*, 6, 149. The three-phase / nine-step process — useful as the
  conventional backdrop Paper 2 deviates from (it builds a *formal* instrument, not a survey scale), so
  citing it lets the paper name what it is and is not doing.

**What this means for Paper 2.** Honor the Define–Operationalize–Confirm spine *visibly* (it is what a
measurement reviewer looks for), but state once, up front, that "Confirm" here means
formal-validation-on-controls (the instrument returns Φ=0 exactly where the construct says it must —
the dyadic limit, the modular system, the party-ignoring mediator) rather than psychometric reliability
across a sample. That reframing is the bridge between the measurement genre and a formal/computational
instrument, and it is honest about which evidentiary moves transfer and which do not.

## Cluster 3 — the methodological-contribution / new-tool paper genre

This is the genre Paper 2 most centrally occupies: a paper whose contribution *is* a method/tool, not a
substantive finding. The connector pass surfaced the precise anchor.

- **[anchor — the genre's defining statement] Bergh, D. D., et al. (2022). What constitutes a
  methodological contribution?** *Journal of Management*, 48(7). Sets out to "eliminate the mystery"
  around what counts as a methodological contribution in premier management journals: a **typology of
  methodological advancements**, contributions located **across a method's life-cycle phases**
  (introduction → refinement → established use), and recommendations for avoiding common failure modes.
  *Load-bearing for Paper 2's framing*: it legitimizes a paper whose entire value-add is a method, and
  its life-cycle frame is exactly how to position Φ — *introducing* an established tool from another
  field into organizational measurement (early-life-cycle in the target domain, mature in its origin).
  The two-pillar argument (borrowing tradition + natural fit) is, in Bergh et al.'s terms, the warrant
  for a *method-introduction* contribution.
- **[method-validation logic] Eisenmann, M., et al. (2021).** Design method validation — an
  investigation of current practice in design research. *Journal of Engineering Design*, 32(11–12).
  Surveys how a field validates *methods* (not findings) and finds a preponderance of non-comparative,
  controlled demonstrations — the precedent for Paper 2's "validate on controls, then demonstrate on a
  worked case" structure, and for being explicit that there is no single agreed validation protocol for
  an imported formal method (so the burden is to show the tool behaves correctly on cases with known
  answers).
- **[importing-a-method craft + a tension to confront] Lê, J. K., & Schmid, T. (2022).** The practice
  of innovating research methods. *Organizational Research Methods*, 25(2). DOI
  10.1177/1094428120935498. The ORM statement on how to introduce/adapt a method: **holistic
  innovation** (changes cascade across data generation, analysis, *and* presentation, not one stage),
  **develop theory and method together**, and be **"excruciatingly clear"** in presenting the procedure
  so a reader can reproduce it. The first two principles set the bar for §3–§5; the transparency
  principle is satisfied by the published `phi_instrument.py` / `worked_examples.py`.
  > **The tension this raises (the harness flagged it; confront it, don't bury it).** Lê & Schmid say
  > *develop theory and method together* — yet Paper 2's whole move is to import Φ as a **theory-neutral
  > formal tool** and validate it as a natural fit, deliberately *leaving the consciousness theory at the
  > door* (Marks & Gerrits' keep-syntax / rework-semantics). A reviewer steeped in Lê & Schmid will read
  > that as a contradiction. The resolution to state explicitly: what we borrow is the **syntax** (the
  > irreducibility-over-MIP mathematics), and we *do* co-develop theory and method — but the theory we
  > co-develop it with is the *organizational* one (Paper 1's triadic-coordination construct), not IIT's
  > consciousness theory. The method is theory-neutral only with respect to its *origin* theory; with
  > respect to its *target* theory it is co-developed exactly as Lê & Schmid require. Saying this turns a
  > latent objection into a strength.
- **[the caution to pre-empt] Øby, E. (2024).** Assessing transparency and methodological precision in
  variable measurement within organizational research. *Quality & Quantity*. Documents
  **"misplaced claims of prior validation"** and elusive construct–measure links as a recurring rigor
  failure. The direct warning for Paper 2: do **not** wave at IIT's neuroscience validation as if it
  certified the organizational use. State plainly that what transfers is the *formal property*
  (irreducibility of a TPM over its MIP), validated here on organizational controls — not borrowed
  credibility from consciousness science. (Reinforces the consciousness concession already in
  `ARGUMENT.md` and the three caveats in `technical_background.md`.)

**What this means for Paper 2.** Frame the contribution in Bergh et al.'s vocabulary — a
*method-introduction* contribution, importing a tool mature in its origin field into an early phase of
its organizational life cycle — and discharge the validation burden Eisenmann et al. describe by
showing correct behavior on known-answer controls *before* the worked case. Pre-empt Øby's failure mode
by never importing IIT's neuroscientific validation as warrant; the warrant is the on-controls behavior,
computed here.

## Cluster 4 — computational / formal-modeling method papers

The harness added the two org-science anchors that frame *what a formal/computational method is for* in
this field, and the connector pass confirmed the rest is governed by the NK formal-import precedent.

- **[anchor — what a formal method is *for*] Davis, J. P., Eisenhardt, K. M., & Bingham, C. B. (2007).**
  Developing theory through simulation methods. *Academy of Management Review*, 32(2), 480–499. DOI
  10.5465/amr.2007.24351453. Positions simulation/formal methods in the **"sweet spot" between
  theory-creating and theory-testing** methods; their primary value is **generative** — producing novel
  theory through controlled experimentation. *Frames Paper 2 correctly*: the instrument is not a
  hypothesis test, it is a theory-development engine — the worked sweep (2.00 → 0.83 → 0.00)
  *generates* the eliminate-the-dyad proposition that Paper 3 then calibrates.
- **[org-science formal-method precedent] Fioretti, G. (2013).** Agent-based simulation models in
  organization science. *Organizational Research Methods*, 16(2). DOI 10.1177/1094428112470006. The ORM
  statement that a **formal/computational instrument deriving organizational outcomes from
  agent-level interactions** is an established org-science method — the reporting template for putting a
  computational instrument in a management venue.
- The cross-field formal-import canon already in `technical_background.md` still governs the
  *transfer discipline*: the NK fitness-landscape lineage (Levinthal 1997; Rivkin 2000; Baumann,
  Schmidt & Stieglitz 2019) and the keep-syntax/rework-semantics rule (Marks & Gerrits 2019; Robertson
  & Caldart 2008). (Harness caveat: it did **not** surface explicit reproducibility/robustness reporting
  *standards* for computational method papers — so the conventions below are drawn from the NK precedent
  and the repo's own compute-don't-assert posture, not from a single editorial source.)

Those precedents converge on the way Paper 2 must report: state the formalism, run it on constructed
cases with known structure, report the numbers (not assertions), and bound the claims. The reporting
conventions Paper 2 inherits:

1. **Compute, don't assert** — every Φ value in the paper is produced by the instrument
   (`proxy_audit.exact_phi`) and reproducible from the published `phi_instrument.py` /
   `worked_examples.py`, not stated rhetorically. (Matches the repo's transparency posture.)
2. **Validate on controls before demonstrating** — the factoring control (Φ=0), the modular/party-
   ignoring control (Φ=0), and the irreducible control (Φ>0, MIP = full tripartition) are reported
   first, so the worked case is read against a calibrated instrument.
3. **Worked example as the demonstration, not the proof** — one fully traced case (résumé → ATS →
   manager) carries the "the fit holds where the construct says it should" claim; the eliminate-the-dyad
   sweep (2.00 → 0.83 → 0.00) is the substantive demonstration.
4. **Bound the claims in the open** — the three caveats (discrete-TPM only; single Φ aggregates ΦID
   modes; formal-property-not-metaphysics) are stated up front, the way a disciplined formal-import
   paper states where its tool does and does not apply.
