## §1 Introduction — the Puzzle

**Thesis.** Coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is *algorithmacy*; the visible
surface is an unexplained variance puzzle.

**What we argue (five-Cs):**
- *Common ground.* Coordinating through a third party that pursues its own objectives is a real, old,
  structurally distinct form (lawyers through a judge; traders through a market maker; driver↔rider through a
  matching engine; applicant↔hiring manager through an ATS). In each, **the third party's objectives, not the
  parties' intentions, determine what passes between them.** (Simmel; Krackhardt; Latour; Stark & Pais; Stark
  & Vanden Broeck.)
- *Complication (the puzzle, foregrounded).* **Equivalently positioned participants on identical algorithmic
  systems achieve divergent coordination outcomes, and no current construct explains the divergence** — a
  persistent within-platform earnings gap among >1M Uber drivers that discrimination/differential treatment
  cannot account for (Cook et al. 2021); divergent reactivity under identical opaque evaluation (Rahman 2021).
  The existing competency vocabulary (CMC competence, HMC, AI-MC, the literacies, sensemaking) assumes *a
  worker on one side, a system on the other* — it cannot describe a worker coordinating *through* a third
  party that acts on both parties.
- *Concern.* A new coordination form has produced a new competency before, and it was not a refinement of the
  old (oracy→literacy). The institutions now forming (bootcamps, certifications, workforce programs) reach for
  "algorithmic skill / AI fluency / platform literacy"; **the vocabulary the field hands them will determine
  whether they train workers for the form they face or the one prior theory was built around.**
- *Course of action.* Name and specify the competency, at the individual level, integrating knowledge, skill,
  motivation, and situated practice; it develops through participation in the form, not instruction about it.
- *Contribution.* A competency-level construct shaped to the structural form, with dimensions, discriminant
  position, and nomological network (the four elements of construct clarity), plus the measurement program it
  opens (Papers 2–3).

**Lands on:** *coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is algorithmacy* — stated once,
apparatus deferred.

## §2 Method — the Warrant for the review

**Thesis.** An integrative review is the right and only genre to recover the missing construct, and the
corpus/search/synthesis is auditable.

**What we argue:**
- *Genre warrant.* Integrative, not systematic — the contribution is a new framework primary studies cannot
  yield (Torraco case (b), an emerging topic needing a construct; the clarifying-constructs function, Cronin &
  George; Post et al.; Rousseau 2024). Align purpose with method (Kunisch et al. 2023).
- *Level-of-analysis anchor.* The corpus is the corpus of **competency-level constructs** in human-algorithm
  interaction — not the empirical platform literature (which motivates the puzzle but cannot change what a
  competency construct accommodates) and not platform-form theory (one level above competency).
- *Search.* **Construct-by-construct**, not keyword: constructs travel through citation networks; forward/
  backward citation tracing from each of the seven foundational specifications (CMC competence, HMC, AI-MC,
  algorithmic literacy, AI literacy, algorithm sensemaking, algorithmic competency). A second, selective
  corpus supplies the structural/historical adjacents (Simmel/Krackhardt; Latour; Stark; literacy studies).
- *Inclusion/exclusion.* In: sources that specify a competency-level construct, or supply structural/historical
  foundations. Out: empirical applications that don't extend a construct's domain; label-uses with no engageable
  definition.
- *Synthesis logic.* The **dyadic-inheritance argument** — read each construct against the shared structural
  form; the points where each stops short are where the inheritance shows.

**Lands on:** the corpus and synthesis logic are auditable (systematicity: transparency, coverage, saturation,
connectedness, universalism, coherence; Simsek, Fox & Heavey 2023 = the Annals "Review scope and process"),
and the integrating logic is the dyadic inheritance. *(Fill the `[N]` corpus count.)*

## §3 The Object — the triadic form and its three conditions

**Thesis.** The coordination form that now governs ordinary life is triadic and **irreducible**; co-optation is
the mechanism that makes it so; it imposes three constitutive conditions; and the older coordination mechanisms
cannot secure coordination inside it.

**What we argue:**
- *Irreducibility.* A triad is not a long dyad. The third party can stand outside a party's exit threats,
  broker between two who cannot reach each other, and profit from disagreement — none reducible to dyadic
  relations (Simmel; Krackhardt's Simmelian ties; Peirce's reduction thesis — triadic relations don't decompose
  into dyads; Burch 1991). Confirmed independently by higher-order network theory (pairwise projection discards
  dynamically consequential information; Battiston et al. 2020/2021) and by mechanism design (a correlation
  device produces equilibria the parties can't reach by direct communication; Aumann 1987; Myerson 1982).
- *Co-optation as the mechanism (outline's sharpened framing).* The platform **enrolls** providers and users
  and routes their activity through systems optimizing the platform's objectives, without delegating authority
  and without making the optimization visible — co-optation, the fourth coordination mechanism after hierarchy,
  market, network (Stark & Vanden Broeck 2024; Stark & Pais's "rules but not bureaucracy; rankings but not
  ranks; accounts but no accountability"). This is what converts a brokerable pair into a triad the third party
  governs, and it is distinct from the human brokerage tradition (which acts on *connectivity*, not content).
- *Three constitutive conditions.* **Signal asymmetry** — each party sends an input the third party transforms;
  neither sees what reached the other or what the third party did between (Möhlmann et al. 2021; Curchod et al.
  2020; Rahman 2021; the older market-microstructure and ATS analogues). **Intent compression** — the third
  party accepts inputs only in its own vocabulary; situated intent translates lossily (Bowker & Star; Scott's
  legibility; Suchman's situated action; the encoding work of Cotter, Bishop, DeVito). **Opaque mediation** —
  the rules are unpublished, unstable, adaptive; the third (constitutive) source of opacity is intrinsic to ML,
  not remediable by disclosure (Burrell 2016; Strathern's "measure becomes target"; Rahman's invisible cage).
- *Integrating-conditions test (Okhuysen & Bechky).* Any coordination form must secure accountability,
  predictability, common understanding. Hierarchy (no manager to escalate to), market (prices/contracts not
  observable/enforceable), and network (counterparts rotate; relational history runs through the third party's
  ratings) each fail **inside** the form. Yet coordination still works when work goes well — so **something is
  securing the integrating conditions that the older mechanisms no longer can.**

**Lands on:** the form is irreducible to worker-system + system-counterpart links, and the integrating conditions
hold only insofar as workers exercise *something* under the three conditions — the worker-level competency the
literature has not specified. *(Scope: the formal irreducibility test belongs to Paper 2.)*

## §4 The Foreclosure — the existing construct landscape

**Thesis.** Every construct built for human-algorithm interaction inherits a dyadic ontology; none can be
charitably extended to the triad; the inheritance is a field-level pattern, not a per-construct accident.

**What we argue (each construct in its strongest form, then where it stops short):**
- *CMC competence (Spitzberg 2006).* Most fully specified; its integrated architecture (motivation, knowledge,
  skill, context, outcomes) is what algorithmacy will inherit. But it sits inside a **channel premise** — the
  medium is constraints/affordances, "more similar than different" to face-to-face — so the medium pursues no
  objectives across the parties. *Architecture transports; channel premise does not.*
- *Human-machine communication (Guzman & Lewis 2020).* Promotes the algorithm from channel to **communicating
  partner** (functional/relational/metaphysical) — the philosophical groundwork for an active intermediary. But
  the dyad survives with one term reassigned to the machine; **the human counterpart disappears.**
- *AI-mediated communication (Hancock et al. 2020).* Restores a counterpart but the agency clause places the AI
  **on the sender's side**, modifying messages on one party's behalf (five dimensions); empirical effects cross
  the channel (moral crumple zone) without dissolving the clause. The platform algorithm operates on inputs from
  **both** parties on the *platform's* behalf — outside what the dimensions parametrize.
- *The literacy frameworks (Long & Magerko 2020; DeVito 2021; Zarouali; Dogruel; Carolus).* Measure what a user
  **knows about** the system — a one-place predicate over a single human. Knowledge ≠ action (Lomborg & Kapsch);
  pragmatically effective folk theories can be mechanistically wrong. **The counterpart and the coordination are
  outside the frame.**
- *Algorithmic competency (Zhou et al. 2025) — the hardest case, closest by label.* Four dimensions (understand,
  embrace, leverage, remediate) of worker proficiency *with respect to the management system*; validated across
  five samples; discriminant from the Algorithmic Management Questionnaire (r=.17). But the **counterpart appears
  only instrumentally** (politeness to customers = leveraging the algorithm); its domain is worker↔system. The
  jingle/jangle warrant (Larsen & Bong 2016): same label, different unit of analysis — algorithmic competency is
  **dyadic** (worker, algorithm), algorithmacy is **triadic** (worker, algorithm, counterpart).
- *Algorithm sensemaking (Möhlmann et al. 2023) — closest by cognitive process.* How a worker reaches a working
  model of an opaque adaptive system through participation (focused enactment / selection / retention). But it is
  located **inside the worker**; the riders/customers are absent. Tellingly, the **same author network is now
  publishing in explicit three-party terms** (Stelmaszak, Möhlmann & Sørensen 2025) — the worker-level structural
  extension is acknowledged open territory.

**The field-level synthesis:** all seven share the commitment *a worker on one side of a medium/channel/partner/
system/knowledge-object/process; none on one side of a third party acting on inputs from both.* The shared unit
holds across the cluster, so the limit holds across the cluster — and **measurement refinement cannot reach it**
(refining a dyadic-unit construct yields a more sophisticated dyadic measure). The recent Annals/AMR integrative
reviews of human-AI interaction (Hillebrand et al. 2025; Murray et al. 2021; Raisch & Krakowski 2021) confirm the
absence at the level of theoretical synthesis — none names the worker-counterpart-through-algorithm axis as a
structural object.

**Lands on:** the construct the form requires is the one the dyadic inheritance has prevented the field from
naming. **(Carries the synthesis table from `STRUCTURE.md`.)**

## §5 The Warrant for a new construct — historical & theoretical adjacents

**Thesis.** In-kind coordination transitions have produced new competencies before; the closest theories
(literacy, Latour/ANT, Stark) leave the same worker-level gap; so naming a new competency is warranted, and the
slot is real and empty for a principled reason.

**What we argue:**
- *Oracy→literacy is in-kind, not incremental.* Writing produced cognitive operations oral culture could not
  sustain (Havelock; Ong; Goody; confirmed neurally — Dehaene's neuronal recycling) **and** new coordination at
  scale impossible without it — codified law, double-entry bookkeeping, the bureaucratic file — via three
  properties speech lacks: **persistence, portability, comparability** (Goody; Kafka; Vismann; Poovey; Soll).
- *Print shows an in-kind transition need not be sudden or universal.* Standardized texts enabled collation,
  cumulative critique, authoritative reference (Eisenstein); print was constructed, contested, slow (Johns).
  *Strengthens the structural argument: in-kind ≠ abrupt.*
- *The coordinative claim survives the New Literacy Studies objection.* Street's ideological model, Scribner &
  Cole (Vai), Heath reject autonomous-cognitive literacy — but **the coordinative claim rests on material/
  institutional enablement, not cognitive universalism.** Writing makes codified law/bookkeeping/the file
  possible; these are historically locatable and asymmetrically distributed (which NLS itself documents). The
  mechanism is preserved across practice variation. *This is the load-bearing rebuttal.*
- *Contemporary scholarship treats the algorithmic shift as the latest such transition* (Stiegler; Floridi;
  Hui; Hansen; Chun; Mackenzie; Bucher; Striphas; Burrell & Fourcade) — broad convergence on the structural-
  analogy claim, **but none specifies what individual workers must do.**
- *Latour and the fractal-dyads limit.* The intermediary/mediator distinction names a property channel
  vocabularies miss, but ANT's symmetry premise obscures asymmetry, and post-ANT's fractality is **dyads-of-
  dyads** — pairwise translations chaining into networks of pairwise translations, never entering structural
  triadicity (Whittle & Spicer; Gad & Jensen; Mol). And platforms are **not generic Latourian mediators**: they
  are corporate-authored, unprecedented-scale, accountability-vacant, continuously-updating — a four-feature
  mediator with a corporate principal (Burrell & Fourcade; Kockelman's triadic semiotic agent).
- *Stark and the structural-form theory.* The platform is a distinct organizational form (co-optation/algocracy/
  triangular geometry), grounded in a longer post-Powell lineage (heterarchy; post-bureaucracy; hybrids) and
  distinct from rival fourth-mechanism candidates (community, gift, commons-based peer production, platform
  co-ops). But the structural account answers *what a platform does to participants*, not *what participants must
  do to coordinate inside it.*
- *What competency-level theory adds.* Network theory (geometry), form theory (governance), and the historical/
  philosophical accounts (why a new vocabulary is needed) **do not supply the vocabulary itself** — that is the
  work of competency literatures (literacy-as-competency; dynamic capabilities; algorithm sensemaking), which
  specify what an individual does in a structural position. Algorithmacy adds the specification at the unit a
  platform actually imposes: triadic, adaptive, opaque, corporate-authored.

**Lands on:** algorithmacy stands on three foundations (platform-as-distinct-form; in-kind coordination
transitions produce new competencies; platform-third-party ≠ generic mediator, with formal triadic
irreducibility) and supplies the worker-level competency the structural account presupposes but does not provide.

## §6 The Construct — algorithmacy (the generative core)

**Thesis.** Algorithmacy is the integrated communication competency through which a worker coordinates with
another human party through an algorithmic third party — specified to construct-clarity discipline.

**What we argue:**
- *Definition.* Communicative in content (infer the other side from observed outcomes; translate intent into
  the system's vocabulary; track shifting rules over time), integrated in form (motivation, knowledge, skill,
  situated practice into one capacity; Spitzberg; Campion et al.). **The worker is the unit of analysis** —
  because the variance it explains is variance *between workers facing the same conditions*; a dyad- or
  platform-level construct cannot capture it. It integrates what existing constructs split (knowledge / process
  / behavior). Develops through participation, not instruction.
- *Scope / boundary conditions.* Applies exactly where the three conditions hold (signal asymmetry, intent
  compression, opaque mediation) — jointly sufficient, individually necessary. Where the third party is
  transparent/accountable, an older capacity suffices; where any condition fails, it is a different form.
- *Dimensions (formative, one per condition).* (1) **Inferential** — read outcomes back to inputs (folk
  theories, algorithmic gossip; Bishop; Cotter; DeVito). (2) **Translational** — compress situated intent into
  the system's schema with awareness of the loss (a pragmatic-boundary problem; Carlile; articulation work;
  boundary objects). (3) **Temporal-tracking** — detect rule shifts and update (the four properties of learning
  algorithms; shadow learning; Beane). The three **compose** the construct; the integration is what makes it a
  construct (high on one, low on another ⇒ different outcomes).
- *Discriminant position (by structural commitment, not label).* CMC (opposite a conveying medium); HMC
  (opposite an algorithmic partner); AI-MC (inside her own message production); literacies (knowledge-state, a
  different level); sensemaking (process *inside* the worker, vs capacity *between* parties — closest cousin,
  directional relation open); algorithmic competency (closest by name — three differences: communication-for-
  triadic-coordination vs adaptation-to-management; dimensions-from-the-form vs inductive-from-one-context;
  broad scope vs explicit on-demand framing). No prior study has run the jingle/jangle test across the family;
  algorithmacy is the first to start from construct-clarity discipline (Suddaby 2010; Podsakoff et al. 2016).
- *Nomological network.* **Antecedent:** exposure to the triadic form (frequency, duration, architecture
  variety), moderated by reflective revision and motivational engagement (thinner evidence — flag honestly).
  **Consequents:** coordination success, earnings, retention through algorithmic disruption (Cook et al.; Hall &
  Krueger; Cameron). **Relations:** strongest shared variance with sensemaking and on-demand algorithmic
  competency; weaker with CMC competence and the literacies; transfer across platforms is open (the thinnest
  link — name it as what the program must fill).

**Lands on:** algorithmacy is a single, bounded, measurable construct distinct from its neighbors — the framework
the review crystallizes into, and the answer to the variance puzzle.

## §7 Future research agenda — the program the construct opens

**Thesis.** The construct opens a measurement program the existing landscape was not positioned to undertake.

**What we argue (motivate Papers 2–3 without importing their method):**
- *The matched-instrument structure.* A **demand-side** scale (how much algorithmacy a platform's coordination
  form demands — Papers 2–3) and a **supply-side** scale (an individual's competency — later work). Calibrating
  the form precedes matching participants to it (the readability analogy).
- *Open questions as the agenda.* **Distribution** (uniform or unequal across worker populations — who succeeds);
  **transferability** (one capacity across architectures, or one per architecture — whether platform-mobile work
  is a coherent category); **relation to algorithm sensemaking** (antecedent / consequence / independent — decides
  whether IS-sensemaking and management-competency describe one phenomenon); **aggregation** (does an
  individual-level competency aggregate to teams/institutions — whether org-level interventions are possible).
- *Destination.* The variance puzzle (§1) is where the program lands: a measured, explained account of why
  equivalently positioned workers diverge.

**Lands on:** the construct opens a research program, and the variance puzzle is its destination.

## §8 Conclusion — close with the foreclosure demonstrated

**Thesis.** The transition to triadic coordination has already happened, and the field's constructs have not
caught up.

**What we argue:** restate the transition claim with the foreclosure now **demonstrated** (not asserted) and the
construct specified; the institutional stakes (the vocabulary the field hands its training institutions); the next
moves in the dissertation's measurement program.

**Lands on:** a coordination structure now governs ordinary life that the discipline cannot describe with what it
has, and algorithmacy is the construct that describes it.
