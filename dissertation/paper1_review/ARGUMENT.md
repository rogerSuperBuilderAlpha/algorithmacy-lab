## §1 Introduction — the Puzzle

**Thesis.** Coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is *algorithmacy*. The visible
surface of the transition is a variance puzzle the field cannot currently explain.

### The technical argument

**1. The puzzle, stated as a controlled contrast.** Hold worker behaviour constant and route it through an
algorithmic intermediary, and the intermediary still distributes divergent outcomes. This is not a rhetorical
claim — it has been demonstrated *in silico*: in a calibrated ride-hailing simulation, "small changes in the
system parameters … often distribute vastly different incomes to identically performing drivers," producing
an effectively unpredictable system **[recent: Bokányi & Hannák 2020, *Sci. Rep.*]**. Performance is held
fixed; the divergence is manufactured downstream, by the matching mechanism. The same structural inequality
in the driver income distribution is documented on real platform data **[recent: Sühr et al. 2019, *KDD*]**
and reproduced under independent agent-based models **[recent: de Ruijter et al. 2024, *Sci. Rep.*]**.

**2. The puzzle is not reducible to precarity or to control.** The two dominant accounts of platform-work
outcomes — precarity (employment classification, insecurity) and algorithmic control — both predict *common*
worker experience. The largest qualitative test of that prediction finds the opposite: "worker satisfaction,
autonomy, and earnings vary significantly across **and within** platforms," and the variance survives after
classification and control are accounted for **[recent: Schor et al. 2020, *Theory & Society*, n=112]**. A
field-level mechanism is missing. The large-N correlational anchor is consistent: a within-platform earnings
gap across >1M Uber drivers that is *not* discrimination but a function of how drivers operate the system
**[cornerstone: Cook et al. 2021, *Rev. Econ. Stud.*]** — i.e. of something the worker does, unnamed.

**3. Why no existing competency construct closes it.** Every competency vocabulary the field could hand this
phenomenon — CMC competence, human-machine and AI-mediated communication, the algorithmic/AI literacies,
algorithm sensemaking, algorithmic competency — is built on the same picture: *a worker on one side, a system
on the other.* None describes a worker coordinating **through** a third party that acts, on its own
objectives, on inputs from both parties (the foreclosure of §4). So the variance among equivalently
positioned workers has no construct to attach to.

**4. Why the gap matters now (the concern).** A new coordination form has produced a new competency before,
and it was a restructuring, not a refinement (oracy→literacy; §5). Training institutions — bootcamps,
certifications, workforce programs — are already reaching for "algorithmic skill / AI fluency / platform
literacy." **The vocabulary the field supplies will decide whether they train workers for the form they face
or the one prior theory was built around.**

**5. The move and the contribution.** Name and specify the competency at the individual level — integrating
knowledge, skill, motivation, and situated practice — and open the measurement program it implies (Papers
2–3).

**Common ground (the form is old, not novel):** coordinating through a third party that pursues its own
objectives is structurally distinct and long-standing — litigants through a judge, traders through a market
maker, rider↔driver through a matching engine, applicant↔hiring manager through an ATS. In each, *the third
party's objectives, not the parties' intentions, determine what passes between them* **[cornerstone: Simmel
1908/1950; Stark & Pais 2020; Stark & Vanden Broeck 2024]**.

**Lands on:** *coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is algorithmacy* — stated once,
apparatus deferred. The variance puzzle is the surface; the structural transition is the cause.

## §2 Method — the Warrant for the review

**Thesis.** An integrative review is the right — and only — genre to recover the missing construct, and the
corpus, search, and synthesis are auditable without PRISMA.

### The technical argument

**1. Genre warrant.** The contribution is a new framework that primary studies cannot yield; that is exactly
Torraco's case (b) — an emerging topic requiring a construct — and the clarifying-constructs function of the
integrative review **[cornerstone: Torraco 2005/2016; Cronin & George 2023; Post et al. 2020]**. The review's
purpose (generate a construct) is aligned to its method (synthesis/critique of prior work as data), per the
align-purpose-with-method discipline **[recent: Kunisch et al. 2023]**; the point of view is an *output* of
the synthesis, not an a priori input **[recent: Rousseau 2024; Grønstad 2025]**.

**2. Level-of-analysis anchor.** The corpus is the set of **competency-level constructs** in human-algorithm
interaction — deliberately *not* the empirical platform literature (which motivates the puzzle but cannot
change what a competency construct accommodates) and *not* platform-form theory (one level above competency).
This anchoring is what makes the foreclosure a fair test rather than a category error.

**3. Search procedure.** Constructs travel through citation networks, not keyword strings, so the search is
**construct-by-construct**: forward/backward citation tracing from each of the seven foundational
specifications (CMC competence, HMC, AI-MC, algorithmic literacy, AI literacy, algorithm sensemaking,
algorithmic competency). A second, selective corpus supplies the structural/historical adjacents (Simmel and
the brokerage tradition; Latour/ANT; Stark; literacy studies). Recent integrative reviews in the family are
treated as coverage checks — algorithmic-management reviews **[recent: Kadolkar et al. 2024, *JOB*; Keegan et
al. 2025, *Annual Rev. OP/OB*]**, the multilevel AI-in-organizations review **[recent: Bankins et al. 2023,
*JOB*]**, the algorithm-literacy integrative review **[recent: Gagrčin et al. 2024, *New Media & Society*]**,
and the socio-technical SLR **[recent: Dedema et al. 2024, *JASIST*]** — none of which names the
worker-counterpart-through-algorithm axis, which is the saturation evidence for the gap.

**4. Inclusion / exclusion.** *In:* sources that specify a competency-level construct, or supply
structural/historical foundations. *Out:* empirical applications that do not extend a construct's domain;
label-uses with no engageable definition.

**5. Synthesis logic.** The integrating logic is the **dyadic-inheritance argument** — read each construct
against the shared structural form; the points where each stops short are where the inheritance shows. This is
made auditable through systematicity-as-orientation (transparency, coverage, saturation, connectedness,
universalism, coherence) **[recent: Simsek, Fox & Heavey 2023]** — which *is* the Annals "Review scope and
process" disclosure.

**Lands on:** the corpus and synthesis logic are auditable, and the integrating logic is the dyadic
inheritance. *(Fill the `[N]` corpus count.)*

## §3 The Object — the triadic form and its three conditions

**Thesis.** The coordination form that now governs ordinary life is triadic and **irreducible**; co-optation
is the mechanism that makes it so; it imposes three constitutive conditions; and the older coordination
mechanisms cannot secure coordination inside it.

### The technical argument

**1. The triad is the minimal irreducible unit — three independent warrants.** A triad is not a long dyad,
and three distinct literatures converge on why:
- *Social-structural.* The third party can stand outside a party's exit threats, bridge two who cannot reach
  each other, and profit from disagreement — none of these reduces to dyadic relations **[cornerstone: Simmel
  1908/1950; Krackhardt 1999 (Simmelian ties)]**. The cleanest cross-disciplinary statement that *the triad
  is the smallest unit of a network* — its indirect ties are what dyadic analysis cannot see — is **[recent:
  Siltaloppi & Vargo 2017, *Marketing Theory*]**; the bridge from Simmel's 1908 treatise into
  organization/innovation studies is **[recent: Nooteboom 2006, *J. Inst. Econ.*]**.
- *Formal/logical.* Triadic relations do not decompose into conjunctions of dyadic ones (Peirce's reduction
  thesis) **[cornerstone: Peirce; Burch 1991]**; and pairwise projection of a higher-order interaction
  discards dynamically consequential information — group-level mechanisms are "invisible in dyadic models"
  **[recent: Battiston et al. 2025, *Nature Human Behaviour*]** (forward-references Paper 2's formal
  model of irreducibility without importing it).
- *Mechanism-design.* A correlation device (here, the platform) produces equilibria the parties cannot reach
  by direct communication **[cornerstone: Aumann 1987; Myerson 1982]**.

**2. The algorithmic third party is a distinct case — and naming the contrast is load-bearing.** The
brokerage tradition is the natural prior, and it must be both invoked and bounded. Classical brokerage is a
**human** *tertius* acting on *connectivity* — conduit, tertius gaudens, tertius iungens — i.e. on whether
two alters are connected **[cornerstone/recent: Obstfeld, Borgatti & Davis 2014; Halevy, Halali & Zlatev
2019, *Acad. Manag. Annals* — the canonical third-party-influence synthesis, and a register model since it is
the target venue]**. The platform tertius is categorically different: it is non-human, it acts on **content**
(it transforms each party's input), and it pursues **its own objectives across both parties**. So the
brokerage literature supplies the irreducibility pedigree *and a precise contrast case* — it is not the
construct.

**3. Co-optation is the mechanism that converts a brokerable pair into a governed triad.** The platform
*enrolls* providers and users and routes their activity through systems optimizing the platform's objectives —
without delegating authority and without making the optimization visible. This is co-optation, the fourth
coordination mechanism after hierarchy, market, and network **[recent/cornerstone: Stark & Vanden Broeck
2024; Stark & Pais 2020 — "rules but not bureaucracy; rankings but not ranks; accounts but no
accountability"]**. It is what distinguishes the algorithmic tertius from the human broker, who acts on
connectivity rather than governing content.

**4. The three constitutive conditions — each given a mechanism and a citation base.**
- **Signal asymmetry** — each party sends an input the third party transforms; neither sees what reached the
  other or what the third party did between. This is information asymmetry in the strict management sense — one
  party holds better information, and the structural condition (not the parties) creates it **[cornerstone:
  Spence 1973 (signaling); Bergh et al. 2018, *J. Management* — the canonical management review of 223
  asymmetry articles]**, instantiated on platforms by **[cornerstone: Möhlmann et al. 2021; Curchod et al.
  2020; Rahman 2021]**.
- **Intent compression** — the third party accepts inputs only in its own vocabulary, so situated intent
  translates lossily. The mechanism is the legibility/standardization tax on situated action **[cornerstone:
  Bowker & Star 1999; Scott 1998; Suchman 1987]**, with the worker-side encoding work documented by
  **[cornerstone: Cotter 2019; Bishop 2019; DeVito 2021]**.
- **Opaque mediation** — the rules are unpublished, unstable, adaptive; the third (constitutive) source of
  opacity is intrinsic to machine learning and is *not* remediable by disclosure **[cornerstone: Burrell
  2016; Strathern 1997 ("when a measure becomes a target"); Rahman 2021 (the invisible cage)]**.

**5. The integrating-conditions test — why older mechanisms fail inside the form.** Any coordination form must
secure accountability, predictability, and common understanding **[cornerstone: Okhuysen & Bechky 2009]**. The
three classical mechanisms each fail *inside* the triad: **hierarchy** has no manager to escalate to;
**market** has prices/contracts that are neither observable nor enforceable across the intermediary — and the
governance properties that distinguish market from hierarchy (information aggregation, matching) are precisely
the ones the platform internalizes and hides **[recent: Felin & Zenger 2011, *Strategic Organization*]**;
**network** fails because counterparts rotate and relational history runs through the third party's ratings,
not between the parties **[cornerstone: Powell 1990; Granovetter 1985]**. The "common understanding" leg is
the grounding problem of communication — the shared basis interlocutors build to coordinate **[cornerstone:
Clark & Brennan 1991]** — and it is exactly what the triad cannot secure directly. Yet coordination still
*works* when work goes well, so **something is securing the integrating conditions that the older mechanisms
no longer can.**

**Lands on:** the form is irreducible to worker-system + system-counterpart links, and the integrating
conditions hold only insofar as workers exercise *something* under the three conditions — the worker-level
competency the literature has not specified. *(Scope: the formal model of irreducibility belongs to Paper 2.)*

## §4 The Foreclosure — the existing construct landscape

**Thesis.** Every construct built for human-algorithm interaction inherits a dyadic ontology; none can be
charitably extended to the triad; the inheritance is a field-level pattern, not a per-construct accident.

### The technical argument — each construct in its strongest form, then where it stops short

- **CMC competence [cornerstone: Spitzberg 2006].** The most fully specified competency in the family; its
  integrated architecture (motivation, knowledge, skill, context, outcomes) is exactly what algorithmacy will
  inherit, and recent work extends it to managerial technology-mediated communication **[recent: Koponen et
  al. 2025]** and reaffirms that mediated and face-to-face competence are distinct constructs **[recent:
  Schulze et al. 2016]**. *But* it sits inside a **channel premise** — the medium is constraints/affordances,
  "more similar than different" to face-to-face — so the medium pursues no objectives across the parties.
  *Architecture transports; channel premise does not.*
- **Human-machine communication [cornerstone: Guzman & Lewis 2020].** Promotes the algorithm from channel to
  **communicating partner** (functional/relational/metaphysical) — the philosophical groundwork for an active
  intermediary. *But* the dyad survives with one term reassigned to the machine; **the human counterpart
  disappears.**
- **AI-mediated communication [cornerstone: Hancock, Naaman & Levy 2020].** Restores a counterpart, but the
  agency clause places the AI **on the sender's side**, modifying one party's messages (five dimensions);
  empirical effects cross the channel without dissolving the clause. The platform algorithm operates on inputs
  from **both** parties on the *platform's* behalf — outside what the dimensions parametrize.
- **The algorithmic / AI literacies [cornerstone: Long & Magerko 2020; DeVito 2021].** Measure what a user
  **knows about** the system — a one-place predicate over a single human. The recent integrative review of the
  algorithm-literacy family confirms the construct is organized around the *user's* knowledge/awareness, with
  no counterpart and no coordination in frame **[recent: Gagrčin et al. 2024, *New Media & Society*, n=169]**.
  And knowledge ≠ action: pragmatically effective folk theories can be mechanistically wrong **[cornerstone:
  Lomborg & Kapsch 2020]**, a point the recent folk-theory evidence base reinforces **[recent: Heiland 2023;
  Low et al. 2023; Lin 2025; Ridley 2024; Starks et al. 2024]**. **The counterpart and the coordination are
  outside the frame.**
- **Algorithmic competency [cornerstone/recent: Zhou et al. 2025] — the hardest case, closest by label.** Four
  dimensions (understand, embrace, leverage, remediate) of worker proficiency *with respect to the management
  system*; validated across five samples; discriminant from the Algorithmic Management Questionnaire (r=.17),
  whose perceived-AM companion is **[recent: Jiang et al. 2025 (PAMQ)]**. *But* the **counterpart appears only
  instrumentally** (politeness to customers = leveraging the algorithm); its domain is worker↔system. The
  jingle/jangle hazard is explicit and now method-backed: same label, different unit of analysis — algorithmic
  competency is **dyadic** (worker, algorithm), algorithmacy is **triadic** (worker, algorithm, counterpart)
  **[cornerstone: Lawson & Robins 2021; Shaffer, DeGeest & Li 2016 (construct proliferation / discriminant
  validity)]**.
- **Algorithm sensemaking [cornerstone: Möhlmann et al. 2023, *JAIS*] — closest by cognitive process.** How a
  worker reaches a working model of an opaque adaptive system through participation (enactment / selection /
  retention). *But* it is located **inside the worker**; the riders/customers are absent. Tellingly, the same
  author network is now publishing in explicit three-party terms **[recent: Stelmaszak, Möhlmann & Sørensen
  2025]** — the worker-level structural extension is acknowledged open territory.

**The field-level synthesis.** All seven share one commitment: *a worker on one side of a
medium/channel/partner/system/knowledge-object/process; none on one side of a third party acting on inputs
from both.* Because the shared unit holds across the cluster, the limit holds across the cluster — and
**measurement refinement cannot reach it** (refining a dyadic-unit construct yields a more sophisticated
dyadic measure, not a triadic one). The recent integrative reviews of algorithmic management and AI-in-
organizations confirm the absence at the level of theoretical synthesis: none names the
worker-counterpart-through-algorithm axis as a structural object **[recent: Kadolkar et al. 2024; Keegan et
al. 2025; Bankins et al. 2023]**.

**Lands on:** the construct the form requires is the one the dyadic inheritance has prevented the field from
naming. **(Carries the synthesis table from `STRUCTURE.md`.)**

## §5 The Warrant for a new construct — historical & theoretical adjacents

**Thesis.** In-kind coordination transitions have produced new competencies before; the closest theories
(literacy, Latour/ANT, Stark) leave the same worker-level gap; so naming a new competency is warranted, and
the slot is real and empty for a principled reason.

### The technical argument

**1. Oracy→literacy is in-kind, not incremental.** Writing produced cognitive operations oral culture could
not sustain **[cornerstone: Havelock 1963; Ong 1982; Goody 1977]**, *and* new coordination at scale that
speech cannot support — codified law, double-entry bookkeeping, the bureaucratic file — through three
properties speech lacks: **persistence, portability, comparability** **[cornerstone: Goody 1986; Vismann
2008; Poovey 1998; Soll 2014]**. The transition restructured what individuals had to be able to do; it did
not add a skill to the oral repertoire.

**2. Print shows an in-kind transition need not be sudden or universal.** Standardized texts enabled
collation, cumulative critique, and authoritative reference **[cornerstone: Eisenstein 1979]**, but print was
constructed, contested, and slow **[cornerstone: Johns 1998]**. *This strengthens the structural argument:
in-kind ≠ abrupt — so the algorithmic transition's unevenness is not evidence against it.*

**3. The coordinative claim survives the New Literacy Studies objection (the load-bearing rebuttal).** The
ideological model of literacy rejects autonomous-cognitive "great divide" claims — literacy's effects are
practice-specific, not universal **[cornerstone: Street 1984; Scribner & Cole 1981 (the Vai study); Heath
1983]**, a debate whose resolution is cleanly reviewed in **[recent: Reder & Davila 2005, *Annual Rev. Applied
Linguistics*]**. The rebuttal: algorithmacy's coordinative claim rests on **material/institutional
enablement, not cognitive universalism**. Writing makes codified law, bookkeeping, and the file *possible*;
these are historically locatable and asymmetrically distributed — which NLS itself documents. The mechanism is
preserved across practice variation, so the relativist critique does not bite.

**4. Contemporary scholarship already frames the algorithmic shift as the latest such transition** —
**[cornerstone: Stiegler; Floridi 2014; Hui; Hayles; Chun; Bucher 2018; Striphas 2015; Burrell & Fourcade
2021]** — broad convergence on the structural-analogy claim, **but none specifies what individual workers must
do**, and the most advanced critical-literacy statement of the algorithmic case stops at critical
consciousness rather than a worker competency **[recent: Leander & Boldt 2020, *BJET* — computational agents
as text-producing actants; Mills 2010 (the NLS digital turn)]**.

**5. Latour and the fractal-dyads limit.** The intermediary/mediator distinction names a property channel
vocabularies miss **[cornerstone: Latour 2005; 1992]**, but ANT's symmetry premise obscures asymmetry, and
post-ANT fractality is **dyads-of-dyads** — pairwise translations chaining into networks of pairwise
translations, never entering structural triadicity **[cornerstone: Mol 2002; Gad & Jensen 2010]**; the
ANT×NLS symmetry move is explicit in **[recent: Clarke 2002]**. And platforms are **not generic Latourian
mediators**: they are corporate-authored, unprecedented-scale, accountability-vacant, and continuously
updating — a four-feature mediator with a corporate principal **[cornerstone: Burrell & Fourcade 2021;
Kockelman 2013 (the triadic semiotic agent)]**.

**6. Stark and the structural-form theory.** The platform is a distinct organizational form
(co-optation/algocracy/triangular geometry), grounded in the post-Powell lineage (heterarchy; post-
bureaucracy; hybrids) and distinct from rival fourth-mechanism candidates (community, gift, commons-based peer
production, platform co-ops) **[cornerstone: Stark & Vanden Broeck 2024; Powell 1990; Adler 2001]**. *But* the
structural account answers *what a platform does to participants*, not *what participants must do to coordinate
inside it.*

**7. What competency-level theory adds.** Network theory (geometry), form theory (governance), and the
historical/philosophical accounts (why a new vocabulary is needed) **do not supply the vocabulary itself** —
that is the work of competency literatures, which specify what an individual does in a structural position.
Algorithmacy adds the specification at the unit a platform actually imposes: triadic, adaptive, opaque,
corporate-authored.

**Lands on:** algorithmacy stands on three foundations (platform-as-distinct-form; in-kind coordination
transitions produce new competencies; platform-third-party ≠ generic mediator, with formal triadic
irreducibility) and supplies the worker-level competency the structural account presupposes but does not
provide.

## §6 The Construct — algorithmacy (the generative core)

**Thesis.** Algorithmacy is the integrated communication competency through which a worker coordinates with
another human party *through* an algorithmic third party — specified to construct-clarity discipline.

### The technical argument — built to the Define→Operationalize→Confirm standard

The construct is specified against an explicit best-practice template — definition, scope conditions,
dimensions, discriminant position, nomological network — i.e. the four elements of construct clarity and the
three steps of construct development **[cornerstone: Suddaby 2010; Cronbach & Meehl 1955; recent: Lambert &
Newman 2023 (Define→Operationalize→Confirm); Podsakoff, MacKenzie & Podsakoff 2016]**.

- **Definition.** Communicative in content (infer the other side from observed outcomes; translate intent into
  the system's vocabulary; track shifting rules over time), integrated in form (motivation, knowledge, skill,
  and situated practice fused into one capacity) **[cornerstone: Spitzberg 2006; Campion et al. 2011]**.
  **The worker is the unit of analysis** — because the variance the construct explains is variance *between
  workers facing the same conditions* (§1); a dyad- or platform-level construct cannot capture it. It
  integrates what existing constructs split (knowledge / process / behaviour) and develops through
  participation, not instruction.
- **Scope / boundary conditions.** Applies exactly where the three conditions hold (signal asymmetry, intent
  compression, opaque mediation) — jointly sufficient, individually necessary. Where the third party is
  transparent/accountable, an older capacity suffices; where any condition fails, it is a different form.
- **Dimensions (formative, one per condition; the integration is what makes it a construct).**
  1. **Inferential** — read outcomes back to inputs (folk theories, algorithmic gossip) **[cornerstone:
     Bishop 2019; Cotter 2019; DeVito 2021]**, with the recent empirical folk-theory base showing the
     inference is real, effortful, and outcome-bearing **[recent: Heiland 2023; Low et al. 2023; Lin 2025]**.
  2. **Translational** — compress situated intent into the system's schema with awareness of the loss (a
     pragmatic-boundary problem) **[cornerstone: Carlile 2004; Bowker & Star 1999]**.
  3. **Temporal-tracking** — detect rule shifts and update against an adaptive learner **[cornerstone:
     Rahman 2021; Beane 2019 (shadow learning)]**.
  The three **compose**: high on one and low on another yields different coordination outcomes, which is why
  the construct is the *integration*, not any one dimension.
- **Discriminant position (by structural commitment, not label).** Against CMC competence (opposite a
  conveying medium); HMC (opposite an algorithmic partner); AI-MC (inside her own message production); the
  literacies (a knowledge-state at a different level); sensemaking (a process *inside* the worker vs a
  capacity *between* parties — closest cousin, directional relation open); algorithmic competency (closest by
  name — three structural differences: communication-for-triadic-coordination vs adaptation-to-management;
  dimensions-derived-from-the-form vs induced-from-one-context; broad scope vs explicit on-demand framing). No
  prior study has run the jingle/jangle test across this family; algorithmacy is the first specified from
  construct-clarity discipline, and the discriminant claim is to be confirmed by the same discriminant-
  validity machinery the field requires **[cornerstone: Lawson & Robins 2021; Shaffer et al. 2016]**.
- **Nomological network.** *Antecedent:* exposure to the triadic form (frequency, duration, architecture
  variety), moderated by reflective revision and motivational engagement (thinner evidence — flagged
  honestly). *Consequents:* coordination success, earnings, and retention through algorithmic disruption — the
  outcome side now has a concrete evidentiary spine in the platform-variance literature **[recent: Schor et
  al. 2020; Bokányi & Hannák 2020; cornerstone: Cook et al. 2021; Cameron 2024]**. *Relations:* strongest
  shared variance with sensemaking and on-demand algorithmic competency; weaker with CMC competence and the
  literacies; **transfer across platforms is the thinnest link — named as what the program must fill.**

**Lands on:** algorithmacy is a single, bounded, measurable construct distinct from its neighbours — the
framework the review crystallizes into, and the construct that lets the variance puzzle be posed precisely
(the program it opens, not this review, answers it).

## §7 Future research agenda — the program the construct opens

**Thesis.** The construct opens a measurement program the existing landscape was not positioned to undertake.

### The technical argument (motivate Papers 2–3 without importing their method)

- **The matched-instrument structure.** A **demand-side** model (how much algorithmacy a platform's
  coordination form demands — Papers 2–3 build and grade the model; validating it against an outcome is later
  work) and a **supply-side** scale (an individual's competency — later work). Modelling the *form* precedes
  matching participants to it — the readability analogy: a text's grade level is a property of the text,
  scored before any reader is matched to it.
- **Open questions as the agenda.** *Distribution* — uniform or unequal across worker populations (who
  succeeds, and whether the variance is structured by who can exercise the competency). *Transferability* —
  one capacity across architectures or one per architecture (whether "platform-mobile work" is a coherent
  category). *Relation to algorithm sensemaking* — antecedent / consequence / independent (decides whether
  IS-sensemaking and management-competency describe one phenomenon; the same-author three-party turn,
  Stelmaszak et al. 2025, makes this live). *Aggregation* — whether an individual-level competency aggregates
  to teams/institutions (whether org-level interventions are possible).
- **Destination.** The variance puzzle (§1) is where the program lands: a measured, explained account of why
  equivalently positioned workers diverge — i.e. an answer to Bokányi & Hannák's "identically performing
  drivers, vastly different incomes" that is located in the worker's competency, not only the matching
  mechanism.

**Lands on:** the construct opens a research program, and the variance puzzle is its destination.

## §8 Conclusion — close with the foreclosure demonstrated

**Thesis.** The transition to triadic coordination has already happened, and the field's constructs have not
caught up.

**What we argue:** restate the transition claim with the foreclosure now **demonstrated** (§4) rather than
asserted, and the construct specified (§6); name the institutional stakes (the vocabulary the field hands its
training institutions — §1's concern, now earned); point to the next moves in the dissertation's measurement
program (Papers 2–3).

**Lands on:** a coordination structure now governs ordinary life that the discipline cannot describe with what
it has, and algorithmacy is the construct that describes it.
