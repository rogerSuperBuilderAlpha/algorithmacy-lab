## What an integrative review *is* (and is not)

An integrative review **moves beyond describing a body of evidence to derive new insights through synthesis
and/or critique** — it offers a new theoretical or conceptual contribution, not a summary (Cronin & George,
2023; Breslin & Gatrell, 2023, DOI 10.1111/joms.12581). Torraco's foundational definition: it "reviews,
critiques, and synthesizes representative literature on a topic in an integrated way such that new
frameworks and perspectives are generated" (Torraco, 2005, 2016). It is distinct from the systematic review
(aggregates findings under one unit of analysis, PRISMA-style), the narrative review (descriptive), and
meta-analysis (statistical pooling). Integrative is the right choice **only because the contribution is a
new framework primary studies cannot yield** — which is exactly Paper 1's situation.

**Torraco's two justifications for an integrative review** (Torraco, 2005, 2016; via Breslin & Gatrell,
2023): (a) a **mature** literature that has become fragmented/unwieldy and needs reconceptualizing; or (b) a
**new or emerging** topic that needs an initial conceptualization. **Paper 1 is case (b)** — an emerging
coordination form that needs a worker-level construct. State this explicitly in §2; it is the methodological
warrant.

## The contribution must be "beyond a review" — a theoretical contribution

Review articles make a theoretical contribution by **identifying, challenging, and advancing the building
blocks of a theory** (Post, Sarala, Gatrell & Prescott, 2020, DOI 10.1111/joms.12549). The recognised ways
a review advances theory include: exposing emerging perspectives, analyzing assumptions, **clarifying
constructs**, establishing boundary conditions, and theorizing with mechanisms (Post et al., 2020). Reviews
can classify, represent, **problematize**, configure, aggregate, integrate, interpret, and explain a
literature (Kunisch et al., 2023). Paper 1's contribution is **construct-clarifying / framework-building**:
it foregrounds a new construct (algorithmacy) by foreclosing the existing ones.

A theory-driven review is **both integrative and generative** (Post et al., 2020; Breslin & Gatrell, 2023):
*integrative* = analyze and synthesize prior work; *generative* = produce new ideas from that synthesis. The
review must **simultaneously align with prior work and diverge from it** — connect the literature in an
original way to present it in a new light. Six of ten high-impact integrative reviews studied by Breslin &
Gatrell (2023) produced a **new conceptual framework** as their payload. Paper 1's payload is the
algorithmacy construct + its nomological position.

## Structure and craft

- **Frame by problematization, not gap-spotting** (Alvesson & Sandberg, 2011, 2020): critically interrogate
  and *reimagine* the literature rather than note an empty cell. Paper 1's v1 outline already does this — it
  opens on the **variance puzzle** as the surface of a structural change, not on "no one has studied X."
- **Construct the research opportunity** rather than assert a gap (Locke & Golden-Biddle, 1997).
- **Five-Cs introduction** (Lange & Pfarrer, 2017): common ground → complication → concern → course of
  action → contribution. (The existing draft uses this; keep it.)
- **Breadth + bridging across disciplines** (Cronin & George, 2023): the contribution lives *across* fields
  no single one produced. Break down themes within each disciplinary group, then synthesize across them.
  Paper 1 spans communication, IS, organization studies, and literacy studies — bridge them explicitly.
- **Synthesis tables/matrices** are expected: organize the foreclosed constructs in a comparison table
  (construct × structural commitment × where it stops short of the triad). This makes the charitable-
  extension foreclosure auditable at a glance.
- **Future-research agenda** as a genuine contribution, not a coda (Elsbach & van Knippenberg, 2020): it
  should open the measurement program (Papers 2–3) and locate the variance puzzle as its destination.

## Rigor and auditability even though it is *not* systematic

Integrative ≠ unsystematic. Make the values and assumptions explicit; specify and justify the search
strategy, the inclusion/exclusion criteria, and the synthesis logic so a reviewer can audit them (Snyder,
2019; Tranfield, Denyer & Smart, 2003; Sauer et al., 2023, the 6-steps/14-decisions; Rousseau, 2024,
"reviews as research: developing trustworthy synthesis"). For Paper 1 this means: **anchor the corpus at the
construct's level of analysis** (competency-level constructs, not the empirical platform literature);
**construct-by-construct search** (constructs travel through citation networks, not keywords —
forward/backward citation tracing from each foundational paper); explicit inclusion criterion (bears on
whether the existing constructs can extend to the triad); explicit exclusions (empirical applications that
don't change a construct's domain; label-uses with no engageable definition). The existing draft's §2
already does most of this — fill the `[N]` corpus count and keep the criteria explicit.

## Failure modes top reviews avoid (the reviewer's rejection list)

1. **Descriptive, not synthetic** — summarizes instead of deriving new insight. Fix: every section ends in a
   synthesized claim, not a catalogue.
2. **No theoretical contribution** — "adds value beyond a review" is the bar (Post et al., 2020). Fix: the
   construct + framework is the payload, stated up front and delivered.
3. **Unfair representation of foreclosed work** — straw-manning the constructs you reject. Fix: the
   **charitable-extension test** — represent each construct in its strongest form, then show it cannot reach
   the triad. This is Paper 1's integrity check (a straw-manned foreclosure invalidates the contribution).
4. **Opaque method** — no auditable search/criteria. Fix: §2 specifics above.
5. **No payoff / weak agenda** — Fix: §7 opens a concrete measurement program (the dissertation's Papers 2–3).

## Ordered process + quality checklist (folds into PROCESS.md Mode A)

1. State the **methodological warrant** (Torraco case (b): emerging topic needing a construct).
2. **Anchor the corpus** at the construct's level of analysis; construct-by-construct citation search;
   explicit inclusion/exclusion. (Deep research / the harness supports this; verify every cite.)
3. **Frame by problematization** (variance puzzle), five-Cs intro.
4. Build the **comparison/synthesis table** of foreclosed constructs (× structural commitment × stop-short).
5. Run the **charitable-extension foreclosure** construct by construct — strongest form first.
6. **Generate** the construct to construct-clarity discipline (definition, domain, dimensions, discriminant
   position, nomological network; Suddaby, 2010; Cronbach & Meehl, 1955).
7. **Future-research agenda** that opens the measurement program.
8. **Auditability + citation-integrity pass**; bridge across disciplines explicitly; every claim synthesized.
9. **Register**: *Annals* voice (claim-first paragraphs; the existing draft is the anchor); length = venue
   norm (~12–20k words).

Quality bar (one line): *Does the review present the literature in a new light and leave the reader with a
construct and framework they did not have before, derived auditably and fairly from a synthesis they can
check?* (Post et al., 2020; Cronin & George, 2023; Breslin & Gatrell, 2023.)
