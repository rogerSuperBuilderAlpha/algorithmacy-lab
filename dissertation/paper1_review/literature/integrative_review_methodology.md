## The governing principle: align review PURPOSE with review METHOD

A review is **primary scientific inquiry that uses prior research as its data source to develop new
knowledge** — not a summary (Kunisch, Denyer, Bartunek, Menz & Cardinal, 2023, *ORM*, **OA**). Quality comes
from **deliberate alignment of the review's purpose with its method**, judged across six aspects: design,
execution, analysis, synthesis, contribution, utilization. Pick the genre from the purpose, not from habit.

## What an integrative review *is* (and why it is the right genre here)

An integrative review **synthesizes "representative" literature to GENERATE new theoretical frameworks and
perspectives** — a "review with an attitude," balancing narrative generativity with systematic rigor (Fan,
Breslin, Callahan & Iszatt-White, 2022, *IJMR*; Elsbach & van Knippenberg, 2020). It **moves beyond
describing evidence to derive new insight through synthesis and/or critique** (Cronin & George, 2023;
Torraco, 2005, 2016). It is distinct from the **scoping** review (maps a field, illuminates gaps), the
**systematic** review (adjudicates effects, PRISMA), and **meta-analysis** (statistical pooling).

Critically, an integrative review carries **no PRISMA, no a priori study protocol, and no risk-of-bias
requirement, and no fixed article format**, and **its perspective/new knowledge is an OUTPUT of the review,
not an a priori input that guides it** (Grønstad, 2025, *Review of Managerial Science*, Table 1; Torraco,
2016; Elsbach & van Knippenberg, 2020). Purpose = **redirection / reconceptualizing** an area; payoff = a
**taxonomy, conceptual classification, or theoretical model/framework**.

**Torraco's two justifications** (2005, 2016): (a) a *mature* literature that has fragmented and needs
reconceptualizing; or (b) a *new/emerging* topic needing initial conceptualization. **Paper 1 is case (b)**
— state this in §2 as the methodological warrant.

## Top-journal standards (verified from official guidance)

**Academy of Management Annals** (AOM author resources — the target venue):
- Annals' **evidence base IS the literature**; its target genre is the **integrative review *with a point of
  view* that sets the future-research agenda**.
- Reviews must take a **critical, potentially provocative position**: "summarize and challenge established
  concepts, pinpoint problems and factual errors." A **"traditional review that lacks a perspective" is
  explicitly NOT acceptable**, and Annals is **not** a theory-development venue (avoid writing a theory
  paper).
- **Submission is a 5-page PROPOSAL, not a full manuscript** (single-spaced, 1-inch margins, 12-pt;
  references/tables/figures excluded). **Four mandated section headings:** *Focus of the review* (why is
  this important?), *Related reviews* (value beyond prior reviews), *Review scope and process* (the
  search/inclusion-exclusion/analysis logic), *New insights* (how the review redirects research).
  **Non-conforming proposals are rejected without consideration.** → *Practical sequencing for the
  dissertation: the chapter is the full review; the Annals submission is a 5-page proposal derived from it,
  written to these four headings.*

**Academy of Management Review** (if a theory-paper route is ever taken instead): AMR is a
theory-development journal that explicitly sanctions **"synthesizing recent advances and ideas into current
theory"** and **"delineating a novel type of problem"** as legitimate contributions (AOM guidelines;
Thatcher & Fisher, 2022, *AMR*). A **contribution-to-length ratio is assessed** — length must be earned by
theoretical payoff. (Annals, not AMR, is Paper 1's venue, but AMR's standards confirm the integrative/
construct contribution is recognised.)

## The contribution must be "beyond a review" — a theoretical/construct contribution

The bar is **a contribution beyond a review** (Post, Sarala, Gatrell & Prescott, 2020, *JMS*). Reviews can
classify, represent, **problematize**, configure, aggregate, integrate, interpret, explain (Kunisch et al.,
2023); they advance theory by exposing emerging perspectives, analyzing assumptions, **clarifying
constructs**, and establishing boundary conditions (Post et al., 2020). A theory-driven review is **both
integrative** (synthesize prior work) **and generative** (produce the new construct/framework), aligning
with prior work while diverging from it; the payoff is a **new conceptual framework** (Breslin & Gatrell,
2023; Fan et al., 2022). Paper 1's payoff is the **algorithmacy construct + its nomological position**,
specified to construct-clarity discipline (Suddaby, 2010; Cronbach & Meehl, 1955).

## Framing and structure

- **Frame by problematization, not gap-spotting** (Alvesson & Sandberg, 2011): critically interrogate and
  *reimagine* the literature. Paper 1's v1 outline already does this — open on the **variance puzzle** as
  the surface of a structural change, not "no one studied X." (Alvesson & Sandberg's "problematizing review"
  even contests whether *integration* is the best strategy — answer by making the integration generative.)
- **Construct the research opportunity** (Locke & Golden-Biddle, 1997); **five-Cs intro** (Lange & Pfarrer,
  2017): common ground → complication → concern → course of action → contribution.
- **Bridge across disciplines explicitly** (Cronin & George, 2023): the contribution lives across fields no
  single one produced (communication / IS / org studies / literacy studies).
- **Synthesis table/matrix** of the foreclosed constructs (construct × structural commitment × where each
  stops short of the triad) — makes the foreclosure auditable at a glance.
- **Future-research agenda is a real contribution** (Elsbach & van Knippenberg, 2020): it opens the
  measurement program (Papers 2–3) and locates the variance puzzle as its destination.

## Rigor and auditability without PRISMA: systematicity as an orientation

Integrative ≠ unsystematic. Auditability is achieved through **systematicity as an encompassing orientation
toward explicit methods**, constituted by six principles — **transparency, coverage, saturation,
connectedness, universalism, coherence** (Simsek, Fox & Heavey, 2023, *ORM*, empirically grounded in 165
organizational reviews). Make values/assumptions explicit; specify and justify the search strategy,
inclusion/exclusion criteria, and synthesis logic (Snyder, 2019; Rousseau, 2024, "trustworthy synthesis";
Sauer & Seuring, 2023). For Paper 1: anchor the corpus at the construct's level of analysis (competency-
level constructs, not the empirical platform literature); **construct-by-construct citation search**
(constructs travel through citation networks — forward/backward tracing from each foundational paper, not
keywords); explicit inclusion criterion (bears on whether the existing constructs extend to the triad);
explicit exclusions. This is exactly the Annals "Review scope and process" disclosure.

## Failure modes top reviews avoid (the rejection list)

1. **Over-descriptive without theoretical payoff** — the dominant failure (Breslin & Bailey, 2020, *IJMR*,
   **OA**); bibliometric/co-citation reviews are especially prone. The most valuable move is **"crystallizing"
   the synthesis into a conceptual/theoretical contribution.** Fix: every section ends in a synthesized
   claim; the construct is the payload.
2. **No "point of view"** — Annals rejects perspective-less reviews. Fix: a critical, provocative stance.
3. **Unfair representation of foreclosed work** (straw-manning what you reject). Fix: the
   **charitable-extension test** — represent each construct in its strongest form, then show it cannot reach
   the triad. A straw-manned foreclosure invalidates the contribution. *(This is the review's analogue of
   "validate the instrument on its own controls before comparing.")*
4. **Opaque method** — Fix: the systematicity disclosure above.
5. **Length not earned** — contribution-to-length ratio. Fix: cut anything that does not advance the construct.

## Ordered process + quality checklist (folds into PROCESS.md "Mode A")

1. State the **methodological warrant** (Torraco case (b): emerging topic needing a construct) and **align
   purpose with method** (Kunisch et al.).
2. **Anchor the corpus** at the construct's level; construct-by-construct citation search; explicit
   inclusion/exclusion; record it as the Annals "Review scope and process."
3. **Frame by problematization** (variance puzzle), five-Cs intro, with a **point of view**.
4. Build the **comparison/synthesis table** of foreclosed constructs.
5. Run the **charitable-extension foreclosure** construct by construct — strongest form first.
6. **Generate** the construct to construct-clarity discipline (definition, domain, dimensions, discriminant
   position, nomological network).
7. **Crystallize** the synthesis into the framework; **future-research agenda** opening the measurement program.
8. **Systematicity + citation-integrity pass**; bridge disciplines explicitly; every claim synthesized.
9. **Register**: *Annals* voice (claim-first paragraphs; existing draft is the anchor); length earned by
   contribution; chapter = full review, Annals submission = 5-page proposal to the four headings.

**Quality bar (one line):** *Does the review take a point of view, fairly synthesize across disciplines, and
leave the reader with a construct and framework they did not have before — derived auditably and crystallized
into a theoretical contribution that earns its length?* (Kunisch et al. 2023; Post et al. 2020; Elsbach &
van Knippenberg 2020; Annals guidance.)
