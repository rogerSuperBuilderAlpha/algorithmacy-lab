# Paper 1 — adversarial committee review and adjudication

Three hostile reviewers read the rebuilt draft (`DRAFT.md`) independently: a construct-clarity
methodologist (R1), a platform-work / algorithmic-management domain expert (R2), and a
theory / communication-history skeptic (R3). Votes: **R1 pass-with-major-revisions (bordering
reject); R2 reject; R3 reject.** This file synthesizes the findings and adjudicates which are
real, which are fixable, and which are reviewer overreach.

## The two structural objections that actually threaten the paper

### A. The standalone / deferral problem (R1, R3 — both name it the viva-killer)
Paper 1 defers the decision procedure (how to tell a moderated dyad from a mediated triad) to
Paper 2, and all measurement to Papers 2–3. §6 concedes the form "does not announce itself,"
counting parties fails, reading the interface fails, and "the construct alone does not determine
it." The reviewers' sharp distinction: deferring *measurement of degree* (how much algorithmacy
a worker has) is legitimate for a conceptual paper; deferring *membership* (whether the
phenomenon is even present in a case) is not, because membership is the existence claim the whole
paper makes. As written, nothing in Paper 1 could confirm or disconfirm that a given case is a
mediated triad. **This is real.** It is also partly self-inflicted: our "conceptual + defer"
choice for the in-kind criterion (gap 1) may be too thin. Fix: supply a *provisional, defeasible*
decision criterion in Paper 1 — even a qualitative checklist an analyst could run — so the
distinction is applicable, not only promised.

### B. The structure→competency leap is unearned, and novelty rides entirely on it (R2 — viva-killer)
The paper concedes the *structure* is already named (Stark & Pais, Curchod, Latour, Stark &
Vanden Broeck — §1, §3.5). So the whole contribution is the claim that this structure *demands a
new worker competency*. That inference is carried by the oracy→literacy analogy (§3.3) and §3.9's
assertion of cognitive demand — and it is contradicted by the findings of the very sources cited
for the structure: Rahman's invisible cage (reactivity, entrapment), Cameron's manufactured
consent (control by *removing* worker discretion), Rosenblat & Stark (asymmetry as domination).
The strongest rival framing — algorithmic management as *control* (Kellogg, Valentine & Christin)
— is not cited. The paper's own §4.8 Weberian boundary half-concedes it ("equips her to comply…
not to challenge"). **This is real and substantive.** Fix: engage the control literature head-on
and argue why the triad *rewards* a coordinative competency rather than merely compelling
compliance — or scope the claim down.

### B′. Two-sided-market theory is absent (R2 — rated fatal)
Rochet–Tirole, Parker–Van Alstyne, Hagiu — the economics of multi-sided platforms — appear
nowhere. The paper's four defining properties of the mediator (constitutes coordination, commits
determinations, holds an interest across both sides, keeps parties apart / irreducible) are, line
for line, a two-sided platform. §3.8 dispatches principal-agent, brokerage, and triangulation —
three beatable near-neighbors — and omits the one rival that already has the platform's own
interest and the irreducibility built in. **This is real.** Fix: add engagement and say what
algorithmacy adds beyond it. (A legitimate answer exists: two-sided-market theory is about the
*platform's* pricing/structure; algorithmacy is a *worker-side competency*. But the paper must
make that argument, not skip the literature.)

## Concrete, fixable errors (should fix regardless of direction)

- **C. The r = .17 anchor is a category error (R1, R2 — independently caught).** The .17 is
  Zhou's correlation between algorithmic *competency* and *perceived algorithmic management* — a
  construct-to-context correlation — NOT a competency-to-competency correlation. Using it to
  anchor the expected algorithmacy↔algorithmic-competency discriminant correlation (§4.7, §5.4) is
  a non-sequitur, and self-defeating (if the two constructs are "one word away" and engage "the
  same object," a .17 expected correlation contradicts that). Clearest, most embarrassing,
  most fixable error. Drop the false precision or reframe honestly.
- **D. Scope conditions contradict the definition (R1).** The definition (§5.1) requires "another
  human party," but the three scope conditions (§5.2) don't include it — so they are not jointly
  sufficient: an opaque recommender feed (one human + system, no counterpart) satisfies all three,
  yet that is a dyad the paper excludes. And intent compression may not be necessary (the driver
  who signals "I'm available" compresses almost nothing). The "jointly sufficient and individually
  necessary" claim is overstated. Fix: add the human-counterpart condition to the domain and argue
  (or soften) necessity against near-cases.
- **E. The AI-MC concession undercuts the universal claim (R1, R2).** §4.3 concedes AI-MC "gets
  the structure right" and is "genuinely three-place," then forecloses it on *level* (message vs
  system) and on its authors' own scope boundary. That concedes the *structure* is not novel,
  contradicting the abstract's "all describe a moderated dyad." Fix: the foreclosure claim should
  be about *level/unit*, not pure structure; qualify the abstract's universal.
- **F. Sensemaking is both inside and outside the construct (R1).** §5.5 makes it an antecedent;
  §5.3/§5.4 make it a component of the inferential dimension ("sits inside algorithmacy"). A
  formative construct cannot leave a facet's membership "an open question." Resolve it.
- **G. The market-maker example contradicts the accountability premise (R2).** The gap-2 electronic
  exchange (§5.2) has a professional accountability community (SEC, FINRA, exchange rules,
  best-execution/price-time priority) — which contradicts §3.5/§3.8's claim that the algorithmic
  mediator uniquely lacks one. The paper's own showcase non-gig example refutes its
  accountability-vacuum premise. Fix: pick a cleaner example or rescope the accountability claim to
  the opacity of the matching logic, not total absence of oversight.
- **H. Source-claim mismatch / unsourced functionalism (R2).** Curchod et al. (2020) studied eBay
  sellers but is cited for the ATS/hiring case (§5.2). The ATS "opacity is a design feature, not a
  flaw" claim (§3.5, §5.2) is an unsourced functionalist just-so story. Fix the cite; source or
  soften the opacity-as-design claim.

## Soften, don't capitulate (reviewers partly overreach)

- **Peirce is oversold (R1, R3).** Transposing Peirce's logic-of-relations reduction thesis onto
  coordination structures equivocates on "reduce," and "removing the third leaves no residual pair"
  is close to true-by-definition (R3's pen-pal counterexample is sharp). The underlying point — a
  constitutive triad is not a dyad-plus-conduit — survives without Peirce's formal apparatus. Fix:
  downgrade Peirce from "grounds/proves" to a conceptual analogy, or cut the load-bearing framing.
  Not fatal; oversold.
- **The Latour stable-type vs unstable-relation "contradiction" (R3) is answerable.** The
  classification of a *given case* can be act-relative (Latour) while the two *roles* (moderate vs
  constitute) are stable analytic categories — which is how Williamson's types work, and what §3.2
  already says. A tension to state more explicitly, not a contradiction. Reviewer overreach.
- **"Seriality is decorative" (R1) is too strong; the literacy-seating critique (R3) is fair.**
  The seriality does real work — the literacy line is about a competency *becoming a general
  condition of participation* (the democratization arc), which is the paper's point. But R3 is
  right that the paper should make explicit it is seating algorithmacy in the literacy line for the
  *generalization* claim, not a *structural-descent* claim (the structural ancestors are the
  market maker and judge). Clarify; don't cut.
- **Simmel "all four roles at once" proves too much (R2, R3).** Fair minor: soften the "all of
  them at once" overreach to a disciplined claim.

## The honest bottom line
The triadic-structure description (§3.5, §3.8) is the strongest part and is close to publishable.
The paper's vulnerabilities are concentrated in two places: (1) it defers the existence test that
would make its central distinction usable, and (2) its one novel move — structure demands a
competency — is asserted via analogy and runs against the control literature it doesn't engage.
Everything else is a cluster of concrete, fixable errors (C–H) that should be corrected regardless.
The reviewers' hostility is calibrated to a viva; the core objections (A, B, B′, C, D) are real and
would be raised by a real committee.

## Response applied (revision pass)
Direction chosen: strengthen Paper 1 now, revise to completion.

- **A (deferred existence test):** §6 now states a provisional, defeasible four-question decision
  criterion an analyst can run (assign-vs-choose; commit-vs-carry; kept-apart-vs-route-around;
  interest-vs-neutral); the rigorous version stays Paper 2's. Abstract, §1, §3.4 reconciled from
  "cannot answer / does not supply a method" to "answers in part / supplies a provisional one."
- **B (structure→competency leap):** §3.9 adds a control-vs-competency passage confronting Rahman,
  Cameron, Rosenblat & Stark, and Kellogg, Valentine & Christin (now cited) head-on — control and
  competency are not exclusive; the documented variation in how workers adapt is the competency.
- **B′ (two-sided markets absent):** §3.8 adds two-sided-market theory (Rochet & Tirole; Hagiu &
  Wright) and the worker-platform-customer triangle (Vallas & Schor) as the near-neighbor that does
  capture the structure — at the level of platform economics, not worker competency. Used to ground
  the irreducibility claim in cross-side externalities, reducing the Peirce dependence.
- **C (r=.17 category error):** removed from §4.7 and §5.4; replaced with an honest statement that
  no study has measured the two competencies together, so the association must be estimated.
- **D (scope conditions vs definition):** §5.2 adds the second-human-party requirement and drops the
  overstated "jointly sufficient and individually necessary," handling the opaque-recommender case.
- **E (AI-MC concession):** §4.3 reframed — the dyad/triad distinction tracks constitution, not
  body-count; AI-MC has three bodies but reduces to the pair, so it stays a moderated dyad.
- **F (sensemaking inside/outside):** resolved consistently as an antecedent/input across §5.4, §5.5.
- **G (market-maker accountability contradiction):** §5.2 example swapped to algorithmic credit
  scoring (cleanly non-gig, genuinely opaque); §3.5 accountability claim rescoped from "no outside
  body" to "reasoning sealed to the parties"; Peirce downgraded from proof to conceptual shape.
- **H (Curchod source mismatch):** §5.2 ATS cite changed Curchod (eBay study) → Pasquale (black box).

Deliberately NOT changed (judged overreach): the Latour stable-type/unstable-relation "contradiction"
(answerable, and §3.2 already states it); the claim that the seriality is "decorative" (it carries the
generalization arc). The Simmel "all four roles at once" overreach and the literacy-seating clarity
point remain minor open items, not addressed in this pass.
