# Paper 1 — outline (approved)

Integrative review. Construct contribution. Framing-light: Paper 1 carries the conceptual
distinction, not the formal model. Style: CLAUDE.md (plain, no first person). Register: Academy of
Management Annals. Load-bearing decision: fair representation of each foreclosed construct.

## The spine
- The arc: oracy → literacy → algorithmacy. Each names a competency a coordination shift demanded.
  Oracy = the direct dyad. Literacy = the **moderated dyad** (a medium shapes the channel; the
  relation still reduces to two parties). Algorithmacy = the **mediated triad** (a third party
  mediates, commits binding determinations, holds its own interest; the relation does not reduce).
- The foreclosure: every existing construct describes a moderated dyad. They are literacies.
  Algorithmacy names the mediated-triad competency none of them reaches.
- The open question that divides the three papers: does a given network's coordination protocol
  require moderated-dyad or mediated-triad interactions? Inspection cannot settle it. Paper 1 draws
  the distinction and specifies the competency. Paper 2 builds a model that catalogs which a form is.
  Paper 3 runs experiments with the model.
