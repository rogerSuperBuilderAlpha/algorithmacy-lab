# Paper 1 — ideal structure, section by section (drafting blueprint)

An abstraction of the ideal section structure for an integrative review whose contribution is **a new
construct established by foreclosing existing constructs**, instantiated to Paper 1 (the transition to
algorithmacy). Derived from `literature/integrative_review_methodology.md` and `OUTLINE.md`. Read with
`../PROCESS.md` "Mode A."

## The argument spine (the abstract form)

> **Puzzle → Method → Object → Foreclosure → Warrant → Construct → Agenda → Close.**

A construct-by-foreclosure integrative review is a single argument: *here is a phenomenon (Puzzle) that the
field cannot explain; the reason is a structural object (Object) the existing constructs were not built for;
none of them can be charitably extended to it (Foreclosure); transitions of this kind have produced new
competencies before, so naming a new one is warranted (Warrant); here is the construct, specified to
clarity (Construct); here is the program it opens (Agenda).* Each section advances the spine and **ends on a
crystallized claim** — a synthesized assertion, not a catalogue. Nothing that does not advance the spine
survives (contribution-to-length discipline).

Two outputs from one spine: the **dissertation chapter** is the full review below; the **Annals submission**
is a 5-page proposal mapping the spine onto four headings (see the mapping at the end).

## §1 Introduction — the Puzzle (problematization + point of view)

- **Function:** establish the phenomenon the field cannot explain, and stake the point of view. This is where
  the review earns its "attitude."
- **Moves (five-Cs; Lange & Pfarrer 2017):** common ground (coordination through a third party is real and
  old) → complication (equivalently positioned participants get divergent outcomes through identical systems;
  no construct explains it) → concern (a new coordination form has produced a new competency before, and the
  vocabulary the field hands institutions will mis-train workers) → course of action (name and specify the
  competency) → contribution (the construct + the program it opens).
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** *coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is
  algorithmacy* — stated once, in the author's voice, with the apparatus deferred.
- **Genre rule:** problematization, not gap-spotting (Alvesson & Sandberg 2011); a review with a point of
  view (Annals). Open on the **variance puzzle**, not "no one has studied X."
- **Failure mode avoided:** a literature-gap opening; a perspective-less framing.

## §2 Method — the Warrant for the review (systematicity disclosure)

- **Function:** justify *why an integrative review*, *what corpus*, and *how it was searched and synthesized*
  — auditable without PRISMA.
- **Moves:** state the methodological warrant (Torraco case (b): an **emerging** topic needing a construct;
  align purpose with method, Kunisch et al. 2023); anchor the corpus at the construct's **level of analysis**
  (competency-level constructs, not the empirical platform literature); **construct-by-construct** citation
  search (forward/backward tracing, not keywords); explicit inclusion criterion (bears on whether existing
  constructs extend to the triad) and exclusions; commit to representing competing constructs **fairly**.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** the corpus and synthesis logic are specified precisely enough to
  audit, and the **dyadic-inheritance argument** is named as the integrating logic.
- **Genre rule:** systematicity as an orientation — transparency, coverage, saturation, connectedness,
  universalism, coherence (Simsek, Fox & Heavey 2023). This section *is* the Annals "Review scope and
  process."
- **Failure mode avoided:** opaque method; an unsystematic "narrative" feel; fill the `[N]` corpus count.

## §3 The Object — the triadic form (what the existing constructs miss)

- **Function:** establish the structural object the rest of the paper measures every construct against. The
  foreclosure has no force until the object is fixed.
- **Moves:** define the triadic form and its **irreducibility** (a triad is not a long dyad; Simmel; Peirce's
  interpretant; Stark & Vanden Broeck's co-optation as the fourth mechanism that makes the triad
  irreducible); specify its three constitutive conditions — **signal asymmetry, intent compression, opaque
  mediation**; draw the everyday cases (résumé→ATS→hiring manager; rider→matching engine→driver); run the
  **integrating-conditions test** (hierarchy/market/network each fail to secure accountability,
  predictability, common understanding inside the form).
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** the form is irreducible to worker-system + system-counterpart links,
  and the older coordination mechanisms cannot secure coordination inside it — so something else must.
- **Genre rule:** the object is the fixed yardstick; specify it before foreclosing. (Forward-references Paper
  2's formal irreducibility test without importing it — scope discipline.)
- **Failure mode avoided:** treating the triad as a complicated dyad; loading the measurement formalism here.

## §4 The Foreclosure — the existing construct landscape (the integrative-critical core)

- **Function:** the heart of the review. Show that **every** existing construct inherits a dyadic ontology
  and none can be charitably extended to the object.
- **Moves:** take each construct in turn — CMC competence (channel) → HMC (partner, counterpart erased) →
  AI-MC (counterpart restored but algorithm a modifier-in-transit) → algorithmic/AI literacy (one-place
  predicate over a single human) → algorithm sensemaking (a worker interpreting a system) → **algorithmic
  competency** (the hardest case, closest by label; counterpart outside its domain). For each, **state its
  strongest form, then show where it stops short of the triad** (the charitable-extension test). Close on the
  shared structural commitment and name the jingle-jangle hazard.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** the dyadic inheritance is a field-level pattern, not a per-construct
  accident; the competency the form requires is the one the inheritance has prevented the field from naming.
- **Genre rule:** **charitable-extension test** = the review's analogue of "validate the instrument on its
  own controls before comparing." A straw-manned foreclosure invalidates the contribution.
- **Carries a synthesis table** (see below).
- **Failure mode avoided:** straw-manning; a construct-by-construct catalogue with no field-level synthesis.

## §5 The Warrant for a new construct — historical & theoretical adjacents

- **Function:** legitimize naming a **new** competency (not a refinement) by showing in-kind coordination
  transitions have produced new competencies before, and that the closest theories leave the worker-level gap.
- **Moves:** oracy→literacy and manuscript→print as **in-kind restructurings**, not skill increments (Goody;
  Olson; Eisenstein; Johns); secure the coordinative claim against the **New Literacy Studies / ideological-
  model objection** (material enablement, not cognitive universalism; Street; Scribner & Cole); show the
  three closest adjacents (Latour's transforming media; Stark's platform form; algorithmic-culture
  scholarship) each stop short of specifying **what the individual must do**.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** a new coordination form warrants a new competency in kind, and no
  adjacent tradition has named the worker-level one — the slot is real and empty for a principled reason.
- **Genre rule:** bridge across disciplines (Cronin & George 2023); the warrant must survive its strongest
  counter-objection.
- **Failure mode avoided:** analogy as decoration; leaving the literacy claim exposed to the relativist critique.

## §6 The Construct — algorithmacy (the generative core; construct clarity)

- **Function:** deliver the payload. Specify the construct to construct-clarity discipline.
- **Moves (Suddaby 2010; Cronbach & Meehl 1955):** **definition** (with a standard of success — coordinating
  with a counterpart through the mediating party to reach the outcomes direct coordination would have
  produced); **conceptual domain & dimensionality** (the dimensions through which the competency operates,
  one per constitutive condition); **discriminant position** against the four adjacent constructs by
  **structural commitment, not label**; **nomological network** of antecedents and consequents, with the
  thinnest links flagged honestly as what the program must fill.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** algorithmacy is a single, bounded, measurable construct distinct from
  its neighbors — this is the framework the review crystallizes into.
- **Genre rule:** the "beyond a review" theoretical contribution (Post et al. 2020); the **generative** half
  of the integrative/generative pair. This is the crystallization point.
- **Failure mode avoided:** definition-by-label; a fuzzy construct that re-imports a foreclosed one.

## §7 Future research agenda — the program the construct opens

- **Function:** a genuine contribution, not a coda. Convert the construct into a research program.
- **Moves:** state the measurement program at a level that motivates Papers 2–3 **without importing their
  method**; identify the **matched-instrument structure** (a demand-side scale for platforms, a supply-side
  scale for individuals); locate the variance puzzle as the program's destination; flag distribution,
  transferability, relation to algorithm sensemaking, and aggregation as the open questions.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** the construct opens a measurement program the existing landscape was
  not positioned to undertake, and the variance puzzle is where it lands.
- **Genre rule:** the agenda *is* part of the contribution (Elsbach & van Knippenberg 2020).
- **Failure mode avoided:** a generic "more research needed" list; importing Paper 2's formalism.

## §8 Conclusion — close with the foreclosure now demonstrated

- **Function:** restate the spine with the foreclosure **shown**, not asserted, and earn the consequence the
  introduction could not yet claim.
- **Deliverable / crystallized claim:** a coordination structure now governs ordinary life that the field
  cannot describe with what it has, and algorithmacy is the construct that describes it.
- **Failure mode avoided:** summarizing sections rather than landing the transition claim.

## The synthesis table (lives in §4; the foreclosure made auditable at a glance)

| Construct | Where the human sits | What the algorithm is | The counterpart | Structural commitment | Where it stops short of the triad |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CMC competence | one side of a channel | passive channel | present, passive | sender–medium–receiver | algorithm conveys; pursues no objectives |
| Human-machine communication | partner to the machine | a communicating partner | erased | human ↔ machine dyad | no third human party |
| AI-mediated communication | sender whose message AI shaped | a modifier in transit | restored | sender-side modification | algorithm doesn't pursue objectives across both |
| Algorithmic / AI literacy | a knower of the system | object of knowledge | absent | one-place predicate over one human | no coordination, no counterpart |
| Algorithm sensemaking | an interpreter of the system | object of interpretation | absent | worker ↔ system | counterpart outside the frame |
| **Algorithmic competency** (hardest case) | proficient user of the mgmt system | the management system | outside its domain | worker ↔ management system | counterpart and triad outside the construct |
| **Algorithmacy** (the construct) | one side of an active intermediary | a third party with its own objectives across both parties | **constitutive** | **worker–system–counterpart triad** | — (this is the form) |

The table is the field-level pattern §4 crystallizes: one shared structural commitment forecloses the whole
family; the last row is what the form requires.
