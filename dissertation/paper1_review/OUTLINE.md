# Paper 1 outline: the transition to algorithmacy

Roger Hunt III
Bentley University
Target: *Academy of Management Annals*

Working outline, v1.

The paper argues, through integrative review, that everyday coordination has become triadic, that no existing construct for human-algorithm interaction can describe a triad because each inherits a dyadic ontology, and that the competency a triad demands is new in the way literacy was new against oracy. The construct is algorithmacy. The paper establishes that it exists and that the field cannot reach it with what it has.

---

## 1. Introduction: the variance puzzle and the transition it reveals

Equivalently positioned platform participants achieve divergent coordination outcomes through identical algorithmic systems, and no current construct explains the divergence. The introduction opens on that puzzle, not on a literature gap, and treats the puzzle as the visible surface of a structural change in how coordination happens. It states the transition claim once, in the author's voice: coordination has become triadic, and the competency it requires is algorithmacy. It commits to the contribution and defers the apparatus.

Moves: state the variance puzzle as a real, measured, unexplained phenomenon (Cook et al., 2021; Rahman, 2021). Name the structural condition the puzzle reveals. Declare the construct. Follow the Lange and Pfarrer (2017) five-Cs sequence so the introduction reads as common ground, complication, concern, course of action, contribution.

## 2. Review method

The review is integrative rather than systematic, because the contribution is a new framework that primary studies cannot yield rather than a tally of what exists. The section specifies the synthesis logic and the inclusion criteria with enough precision that a reviewer can audit the search, following the protocol *Annals* expects (Cronin & George, 2023; Rousseau, 2024). It commits to representing competing constructs fairly before foreclosing them.

Moves: state the review type and why it fits. Specify search strategy, inclusion and exclusion criteria, and the integrating logic. Name the prior reviews of the human-AI interaction space and say what algorithmacy adds to them.

## 3. The triadic form

The coordination structure that now governs ordinary life is triadic, and a triad is not a long dyad. Two parties coordinate through a third that interprets both, acts on both, and holds its own interest in the outcome, and the third party makes the structure irreducible to any decomposition into worker-system and system-counterpart links. The section establishes the irreducibility as a structural fact, draws the everyday cases, and identifies co-optation as the mechanism by which the third party converts a brokerable pair into a triad it governs.

Moves: define the triadic form and its three structural features (signal asymmetry, intent compression, opaque mediation). Show co-optation as the fourth coordination mechanism after hierarchy, markets, and networks, and the one that makes the triad irreducible (Stark & Vanden Broeck, 2024). Draw the cases where former dyads are now triads: résumé through applicant tracking system to hiring manager, rider through matching engine to driver.

## 4. The existing construct landscape

Every construct built to explain human-algorithm interaction inherits a dyadic ontology, and the inheritance is not incidental to any of them. The section takes each construct in turn and shows the same structural limit: one human on one side, the system on the other, the counterpart absent or demoted. It runs the charitable-extension test, demonstrating why each construct cannot stretch to the triad rather than asserting that it cannot, and treats algorithmic competency as the hardest case because it is closest by label.

Moves: computer-mediated communication keeps the algorithm as channel; human-machine communication promotes it to partner and erases the counterpart; AI-mediated communication restores the counterpart but models the algorithm as a modifier in transit (Hancock et al., 2020; Guzman & Lewis, 2020; Spitzberg, 2006). Algorithmic and AI literacy measure knowledge of the system, a one-place predicate over a single human (Long & Magerko, 2020; DeVito, 2021). Algorithm sensemaking captures a worker interpreting a system (Möhlmann et al., 2023). Algorithmic competency describes worker proficiency with respect to the management system, with the counterpart outside its domain (Zhou et al., 2025). Close on the shared structural commitment that forecloses extension, and name the jingle-jangle hazard the family creates.

## 5. The historical and theoretical adjacents

Each major change in how humans coordinate has produced competencies that prior practice could not produce, and the algorithmic shift is the latest such change. The section establishes the oracy-to-literacy and manuscript-to-print transitions as in-kind restructurings rather than skill increments, secures the coordinative claim against the New Literacy Studies objection, and shows that three traditions came close to algorithmacy without naming the worker-level competency a triad requires.

Moves: literacy changes coordination in kind through persistence, portability, and comparability (Goody, 1986; Olson, 1994). The print case shows an in-kind transition need not be sudden or universal (Eisenstein, 1979; Johns, 1998). The coordinative claim survives the ideological-model critique because it rests on material enablement rather than cognitive universalism (Street, 1984; Scribner & Cole, 1981). Latour's transforming media, Stark's platform form, and contemporary algorithmic-culture scholarship each leave the same gap: none specifies what the individual must do.

## 6. Algorithmacy as a construct

Algorithmacy is the coordination competency a triadic algorithmic structure demands, and the section specifies it to construct-clarity discipline. It defines the construct, fixes its conceptual domain and dimensionality, distinguishes it from the four adjacent constructs by structural commitment rather than by label, and places it in a nomological network of antecedents and consequents. The definition commits to a standard of success: coordinating with a counterpart through the mediating party in a way that produces the outcomes the parties would have reached under direct coordination.

Moves: state the definition. Specify the dimensions through which the competency operates. Establish discriminant position against algorithmic literacy, AI literacy, algorithm sensemaking, and algorithmic competency (Cronbach & Meehl, 1955; Suddaby, 2010). Lay the nomological network and flag the thinnest links honestly as the network the program must fill.

## 7. Future research agenda

The construct opens a measurement program rather than closing a debate, and the section names the program it opens. It points forward to the question of how a coordination form's triadicity can be measured and how a platform's demand for algorithmacy can be scaled, and it positions the construct to support work across organizational, communication, and information systems scholarship.

Moves: state the measurement program at a level that motivates Paper 2 without importing its method. Identify the matched-instrument structure: a demand-side scale for platforms and a supply-side scale for individuals. Locate the variance puzzle as the program's destination.

## 8. Conclusion

The transition to triadic coordination has already happened, and the field's constructs have not caught up. The conclusion restates the transition claim with the foreclosure now demonstrated rather than asserted, and closes on the consequence the introduction could not yet earn: a coordination structure now governs ordinary life that the discipline cannot describe with what it has, and algorithmacy is the construct that describes it.

---

## Scope discipline

Paper 1 carries the transition argument and the dyadic-inheritance foreclosure. It does not load the measurement formalism. The construct stands on the structure of the triad alone, and the method that proves the structure is triadic belongs to Paper 2.
